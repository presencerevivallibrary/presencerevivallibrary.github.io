890-08 A Greater Than Solomon Is Here

Go to 2 Chronicles, chapter 1, and then go to 1 Kings, chapter 10, and mark that.

There are great things in My presence, saith the Lord, that you only need to enter into. Thank You, Jesus. For the key is in My Spirit, in My power. And the preparation time for this hour is the ministry of the Holy Spirit at all times. So find the path and yield, and you will be blessed indeed.

Hallelujah.

The Call to Riches Begins With Becoming Last in This Kingdom

A few months ago, maybe a month and a half, He pulled me in the room and said, "I am going to teach you some things about riches."

I think it was on a Wednesday night that I began to express it. As He continued to pull me in this room, I did not know what to call it. Some of the terms He started passing through my spirit were, "The first shall be last, and the last shall be first," and, "Many are called, but few are chosen."

I understood then that many, many, many are called to this kind of ministry, to this kind of operation in God, the kind He is talking about, but very, very, very few are chosen.

We found out that when He said the first will be last, He meant those who make themselves first in this kingdom will be last in the next one. But those who make themselves last here, which is servant of all, become first in the next. Many are called. Few are chosen.

So He pulled me in this room and started giving me glimpses into several kinds of ministries. The one He wanted to teach me on was riches.

He said, "I want you to understand some things about riches."

When He pulled me in, I did not know what to call it except "gospel entrepreneurship," maybe the separation in Romans 12 to the simplicity of giving. I know now that the simplicity of giving is when the Holy Spirit is able to get the trumpet out of your hand when you give.

Gospel Entrepreneurship Stands Equal in Reward With the Preaching Gift

I understand that God, by His grace, is able to finish up with my ministry when He does it. I am going to see what I have seen in my spirit through this whole progression.

Even though I have the privilege of tearing hell up with my gift, forcing Satan to turn loose of people through deliverances and wheelchairs and all of that, I know this is inbred into me by my new birth. I know. I am not wondering. I know.

But in this ministry of entrepreneurship, the devil would be just as intensely mad at the person who has entered into the ministry of gospel entrepreneurship and can actually pay for the meeting as he would be at me for being able to bring revival anywhere on that scale.

Because in eternity, you and I will stand shoulder to shoulder, equal to equal, in the reward. There are many members in the body, and not all members have the same office. Because all the members are part of one body, every member is a measure, but it is part of a whole. The closer these measures function together as one, the more they make one whole. That causes the greatest effectiveness.

We will stand shoulder to shoulder in the reward. It will be a day to be reckoned with. It will be an incredible day.

The Rich Young Ruler Could Not Let His Whole Life Become Seed

The first place He brought me to, of course, was, "The first will be last, and the last will be first. Many are called, but few are chosen."

So I backed up far enough to get the whole context, according to the laws of meditation, of what He was trying to get over to me about riches.

When the rich young ruler came to Him, and I am reiterating this because you must understand it for the next step, he threw himself down and said, "What must I do to inherit eternal life?"

Jesus said, "You know the commandments."

He named them.

The young man said, "I have kept these from my youth up."

Jesus said, "You are old covenant. You are saved. You have it. How would you like to be perfect? How would you like to be perfect?"

He said, "Go and sell what you have, give to the poor, and lay up for yourselves treasures in heaven that wax not old. Then you come, pick up your cross, and follow Me."

He went away exceedingly sorrowful, exceedingly, because he could not do this. He could not do it.

The apostles were so amazed at what had just happened that they cried, "My God, who can be saved?"

Jesus said, "With God all things are possible."

Peter spoke up, "Well, we left all. We left it all to follow You. What do we receive?"

This is when He told him that anyone who has forsaken houses and lands and mothers and brothers and sisters for His sake and the gospel's will receive in this lifetime a hundredfold of the same. Innumerable multitudes of families, mothers, brothers, sisters, and children will be inherited into the kingdom because of your ministry. Innumerable. Hundreds and hundreds of folds.

He said, "Peter, you twelve, in the regeneration, in the Millennium, when I sit upon the throne of My glory, you will sit on twelve thrones, judging the twelve tribes of Israel."

But for everyone else who has forsaken all, they will inherit this. Hundreds of folds. They will so incredibly increase the kingdom in houses and lands because they forsook all to follow Jesus.

Then at the end of that statement, He made this incredible statement: "Many that are first will be last, and the last will be first."

He was saying that those who have made themselves first in this kingdom, like the rich young ruler, just cannot do it. He just could not do it. He wanted the wealth to serve himself. He just could not do it.

Those who make themselves first in this kingdom will be last in the next, meaning the Millennium. But those who make themselves last in this kingdom, which means servants, literal slaves, will be first in the next. What makes them first in the next? They literally forsook all to increase the kingdom by hundreds of thousands of folds of families, mothers, fathers, children, houses, and lands. That procured them an incredible place in the kingdom to come.

But the rich young ruler had great wealth, and he could not.

To help you understand a slave, being young and incredibly wealthy, he probably inherited from his family. Jesus did not just ask for wealth he would have earned. He asked for all the wealth he had. That meant every hour, every business decision, every act of wisdom leading up to that point. Suddenly he would use it to increase the family of God. Every hour he had committed in his whole life would now serve the gospel.

He literally became a slave, but he could not do it. He could not do it.

The following instruction was, "Pick up your cross and follow Me." And you might as well know, the cross is where the old man is put to death.

Now follow Me.

God's Prosperity Starts Where You Are Willing to Lose It

Walking in the carnal man, where most of us walk, we cannot see past this life. We cannot even see into it.

The rich young ruler did not know that if he had gone ahead, picked up his cross, and started following Jesus, that mortification would have taken place. When it began to take place, it would not have been a matter of what he gave up. They would have had to use a team of horses just to try to hold a person like that back from running toward anything he could do to inherit more of the kingdom, not giving it up, but gaining more of it.

Then the startling revelation hit me that God's prosperity really does not begin for you but at that level.

People say, "Well, those verses were designed to make us rich. Look, when you get a hundredfold return..."

The rich young ruler was not looking for wealth. He already had it. What he did not have was God's prosperity.

God's prosperity starts at the level where you can lose it. That is when you can gain it. When you come to the place where you are willing to give it for the increase of the kingdom of God, that is the level where God's kind of prosperity begins.

And, buddy, His prosperity will hold up under any circumstance. I do not care if it is depression, oppression, or recession. I do not care what comes. When you have God's prosperity, it will hold up under any circumstance.

I do not mind telling you, I began to seek Him for the wisdom to learn to walk that way. Just last night He told me my mind was very narrow and lived in a box.

I said, "Well, You are very subtle. You are very subtle, Lord."

I began to seek Him on what would qualify this, and He began to come back with wisdom. He began to talk to me about wisdom.

David and Solomon Wanted Wisdom to Judge God's People and Wealth to Build God's House

I knew Solomon was wise, but then I began to notice that whenever prosperity or needs were mentioned, God seemed to link Solomon there somehow with His glory and used Solomon's wealth to tell you that you really do not need to be concerned about what you eat, wear, or drink.

He used him so many times. The Queen of Sheba came to hear the wisdom of Solomon, and Jesus said, "A greater than Solomon is here."

I thought, I guess I am going to have to have another look at this. If the keys to gospel entrepreneurship are in wisdom, then I have got to know what causes it to increase.

Let us begin here, with the first verse. I do not know how long I am going to make it.

King David and his son, King Solomon, both did two things, two things, that caused David to go from a shepherd boy herding sheep to one of the greatest kings Israel had ever seen, and Solomon to go, in only two generations from shepherding, to the wisest, wealthiest, most powerful king that had ever been in this earth.

Two things. These are the two things.

First, they wanted God's wisdom to judge the people of God righteously.

Second, they wanted to build a house for God to dwell in.

Now, running ahead just for a moment, I do not mind telling you the wisdom that comes down from above is pure, peaceable, gentle, easy to be entreated, without hypocrisy, and yields the peaceable fruits of righteousness in those who are exercised thereby. I do not mind telling you that wisdom comes from His Word.

But I also do not mind telling you that the house you and I are building, the house you and I are building for God to dwell in, is the body of Christ.

It is no wonder He says that when you can come to the place where you can forsake all to increase the family of God, then you have sought His wisdom, and He will give you His wealth.

Solomon's Thousand Offerings Opened the Door to Wisdom and Wealth

Let us begin with the first verse:

And Solomon the son of David was strengthened in his kingdom, and the Lord his God was with him, and magnified him exceedingly.

Then Solomon spoke unto all Israel, to the captains of thousands and hundreds, to the judges, and to every governor in all Israel, the chief of the fathers. Solomon and all the congregation with him went to the high place that was at Gibeon, for there was the tabernacle of the congregation of God, which Moses the servant of the Lord had made in the wilderness.

But the ark of God David had brought up from Kirjath-jearim to the place David had prepared for it, for he had pitched a tent for it in Jerusalem.

Moreover, the brazen altar that Bezalel the son of Uri, the son of Hur, had made, he put before the tabernacle of the Lord, and Solomon and the congregation sought unto it.

Solomon went up there to the brazen altar before the Lord, which was at the tabernacle of the congregation, and offered, now notice this, a thousand burnt offerings upon it.

A thousand burnt offerings. I can hardly imagine that. But I can say this: all you have to do is say, "In the name of Jesus." I can hardly stand it.

A thousand burnt offerings upon it.

In the night, God appeared unto Solomon and said unto him, "Ask what I shall give thee."

The Holy Ghost, who was with him, interceded very quickly for him and said, "No, no. Do not ask for the gifts. Tell Him you want Jesus. Tell Him you want Jesus."

"What do you want? What shall I give thee?"

Give me now wisdom and knowledge, that I may go out and come in before this people, for who can judge Thy people that are so great?"

God said to Solomon, "Because this was in thine heart, and thou hast not asked for riches, wealth, or honor, nor for the life of thine enemies, neither yet hast thou asked for long life, but hast asked wisdom and knowledge for thyself, that thou mayest judge My people over whom I have made thee king, wisdom and knowledge is granted unto thee. I will give thee riches, wealth, and honor, such as none of the kings have had that have been before thee, neither shall there any after thee have the like."

Then Solomon came from his journey to the high place at Gibeon, to Jerusalem, from before the tabernacle of the congregation, and reigned over Israel.

Now notice the second chapter, first verse. God made him wealthy, but notice what he wanted to do with this wealth:

And Solomon determined to build a house for the name of the Lord, and a house for his kingdom.

Then he begins into the wealth, the building, and the specifications that God had communed in his heart for the house he was going to build Him.

It amazes me that this house is only a type. When Jesus said, "A greater than Solomon is here," the house we are building for Him is a greater house.

Tell me, if you ask for the same things, what makes you think He would not pour the same kind of things out upon you? When I began to get into the wealth of Solomon, that is when God said, "Son, your mind is in a narrow box."

Solomon's Temple Foreshadows a House Built From All Nations

Go on over to 1 Kings, chapter 10, because I am laying a foundation.

Here is something interesting I want you to keep in your heart. This is a shadow and a type. When Solomon began to build his tabernacle, he contracted with the artisans and the children of strangers. They actually came and did the work on the house. That tells you that the house we are building, we are also building with the children of all nations.

Something else may interest you. After twenty years, when he completed the tabernacle, Solomon and the whole congregation prayed over its dedication. They sacrificed twenty-two thousand oxen and twenty thousand sheep. Forty-two thousand sacrifices they burned on the altars that day.

The glory of the Lord fell, and the fire fell into the temple. Not even the priests could go into the temple by reason of the glory that was there.

When Solomon prayed the dedication for this temple, sometime I would like to take that prayer apart. But when I read it and understood the first layer of it by the power of the Holy Spirit, I fainted inside and got weak.

One of the things he said so moved me was this verse...

Hang in with me, folks. I am fading. Hallelujah, Jesus. Worship with me for a minute.

See, I determined as I enter into this place with Him that I would follow Him as closely as I could in the services. If I could hear Him when He was way back here instead of here, then I could make ready for the change before He got here.

But I can say this: yes, sir.

In one year of Solomon's ministry, twenty-five tons of gold came in, which is roughly three hundred twenty million dollars at today's prices. That was just the gold.

He asked wisdom, and God gave it to him. With it, He gave him riches to build His house. Three hundred twenty million dollars, just the gold, not counting the spices, the ivory, or the trade.

In fact, silver was so plentiful that they esteemed it as stones and piled it up. Gold was so plentiful that they did not even inventory the cups in the king's house. All the world came to him to seek out his wisdom. God said there was none like him.

If it is God's house I am building, and I seek His wisdom, then it is through His wisdom that the wealth comes.

It really looks like there was a stipulation here: the wealth comes to build His house. Now that is their covenant. This is mine.

Suddenly He says:

No man, when he hath lighted a candle, putteth it in a secret place, neither under a bushel, but on a candlestick, that they which come in may see the light.

You can rest assured salvation came to her country, as it did to all the countries of the earth that came up to hear the wisdom of Solomon.

"You evil generation, you want a sign? Here is your sign. She came to hear his wisdom, and I am right here with you, and you will not hear Me."

He says that a greater than Solomon is here.

This is what I understood in the first pass: no man, when he has lighted a candle, puts it under a bushel, but on a tabletop. Why is a man given the Word? Is it not to understand? Is a candle brought to be put under a bushel?

I am here, greater than Solomon, and I am giving you the Word. Is the Word given to you not to understand? Or is it like a candle that you put on top of a table and ignite? When you ignite it, it shines light in the whole room, and then you can walk by it.

He said, "A greater than Solomon is here."

I can tell you one thing: hello, praying in tongues.

Suddenly He says, "The light of the body is the eye. Therefore, if thine eye be single, thy whole body is full of light; but if thine eye be evil..."

The light of the body is the eye. What do I use these two organs for? They let light into my body. Without these two organs, I would be walking in darkness. These two organs let light into my body. Therefore I can walk according to that light. I can cross the room.

But He says if your eye is evil, which is impaired all the way to blind, then it lets no light into your body, so you have nothing to walk by.

Of course, I pulled this over into the spiritual realm where we always know it exists. I said, "Look here. The Word is the lamp. That was the first pass I made, and it was a good pass. Every bit of it.

But on this next pass, I was lying back there in my place of aloneness. Sometimes it amazes me how revelation knowledge jumps on a guy. I will be there and not know a thing about it, and the next moment I know everything about it and do not know how I knew. Then I have to go start digging.

Boy, He said something to me.

He said, "Do you want to know what made Me greater than Solomon?"

I am thinking, Son of God, yes. The message You preached, yes. All these things, yes.

But this is what He is talking about that made Him greater: He gave us something Solomon could not. He gave us something King David could not. This is what made Him greater than anyone who had ever lived: the access He gave us to God.

When He said, "The light of the body is the eye," yes, this organ lets light into my body. There would not be any light in my body if this organ was not functioning. Yet He went on to say that if your eye is single, your whole body shall be full of light; but if it is evil, it will be full of darkness.

I thought, if I get a dark eye, I am void of revelation knowledge and cannot see where I am going. If I have eyes that let light in, then I can walk around anything and see exactly where I am going.

I kept going over it and over it, just letting it set in my spirit.

So I am saying, now, do You mean, is the candle brought to be put under a bushel? No. The light of the body is the eye. He is saying, "A greater than Solomon is here. Is a candle brought to be put under a bushel? No. I have come to light your candle. When I light your candle on the inside, your spirit is going to start to see and be able to commune with God one-on-one through that new nature, because you will have revelation knowledge and understanding that no generations before you have ever had."

Of John, He said there was no greater prophet, yet the least in the kingdom of God is greater than he. Why? Because your candle has been lit. Because you have a new nature. Because you have access to revelation knowledge that exceeds any wisdom Solomon had, because Solomon was unable to communicate with or understand God through a new nature.
