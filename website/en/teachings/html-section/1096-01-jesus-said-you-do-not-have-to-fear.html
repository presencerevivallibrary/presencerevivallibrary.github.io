1096-01 Jesus Said You Do Not Have to Fear

John 17 Opened When the Glory of Jesus Became Accountability

Open to John 17 as we get ready. Hallelujah. Then we are going to be in a few places, like Luke 11, Mark 4, and would you believe we are even going to shoot for the book of Acts.

When I got a look at that girl, she had been a prisoner of her own body for the twelve or thirteen years she had lived in Palm Springs. As I stood there, He preceded a vision I had inside with the mystery that began to help me understand what the glory of God was. Then I got to see God, or Jesus, in His glory for just a minute.

That moment was the kind of revelation that begins to come forward when you know He is about to give you something, about to do something. It ended up with Him saying things in my spirit like, "I am holding you accountable," and, "No longer will you pass children in that condition without accountability."

He started saying things like that as He began to open up from His Word what I had seen in verses I had read many times.

John 17 is a prayer He prayed for us. He prayed for His disciples and for everyone who would believe on Him through them, which is us. I had read this prayer many times, and I had heard parts of it mentioned. People will quote a statement He made out of this prayer and use it to confirm something. But the whole prayer was hard for me to understand.

He made very difficult statements: "I in them," "Thou in Me," and that we would be "made perfect in one." It was a very difficult prayer to understand.

The one degree I was off, which kept me from understanding it, was that I was trying to link the prayer too much with our oneness with Him in deity and not in His humanity. When I understood that the oneness He was talking about, Him being with the Father and the Father in Him and us in Them, was the position He held with the Father in His glorified humanity, it began to open up.

The Life From the Father Makes Us One With Jesus in His Humanity

Through the born-again trail I had already traced that new life you and I received. It began with the Father. As the Father had life within Himself, He gave to the Son to have life within Himself. Then the Son turned around and gave it to us.

The life Jesus had in His spiritually alive spirit is now the same life we have in our spiritually alive spirit, and it came from the Father.

The oneness He is talking about, "I in them, and Thou in Me," is the same kind of oneness you and I had with the first Adam when we were baptized into his spiritual death and the whole human race inherited his nature. Paul said that even those who had not sinned after the similitude of Adam's transgression still inherited it. We did not commit the sin that caused us to have that nature, but since we were born of him, we had that nature. We were all baptized into Adam's death. We were all one with Adam in that nature.

Thank God the second Adam came along, the first Man to stand on this planet in four thousand years spiritually alive.

In Him was life; and the life was the light of men.

That light shined in the spiritual darkness, the spiritual death of the human race. Now that we have been baptized into Jesus' death, that we might also be like Him in His resurrection, all of us who are born again have been baptized into that new nature. That new nature has made us one with Jesus and also one with the Father.

This is why, when Jesus described going away, He said:

In My Father's house are many rooms. If it were not so, I would have told you. I go to prepare a place for you, that where I am, there ye may be also.

When He said, "Where I am," He was talking about that place, that room, that dwelling He held with the Father in His humanity, and as it stands now, His glorified humanity.

We know what makes Him different from us is that in eternity past, before His glorified humanity, He was the second Person in the Godhead as deity. This is where people's brains begin to lock up. We have been made one with Him in His humanity. We will never touch His deity. But we are one with Him in His humanity, not His deity.

The room He holds in His humanity is the one He was talking about when He said, "I go away to prepare a place for you." The way He went was by way of the cross. He went to prepare a place for us, that He would come again and receive us unto Himself, and that where He is, that place He occupied with the Father in His humanity, we may be also.

Hallelujah.

Philip spoke up, "We do not know the way. How do we know how to get there?"

Jesus said:

I am the way, and the truth, and the life.

That life is in you. No man can come to the Father but by that life, but by Him.

In John 5, tracing that life is tremendous. He says that as the Father raises up the dead and makes them alive, the Son turns around and quickens, or makes alive, whom He will. As the Father had life within Himself, He passed it on to the Son, and the Son passed it on to us.

I am literally born of God. I do not mind telling you, I am excited. I understand my position more and more because I have gotten to see Jesus in His glorified humanity and am beginning to understand it.

Born From Above Means God Is Now Our Father in the New Man

Jesus made statements in Matthew like, "Call no man on this earth master," as in lord, as in final say-so. He said you only have one Master who has the final say-so, and that is Jesus, and you are all brethren.

Then He made another interesting statement: "Call no man father upon the earth." He said you only have one Father, and that is your heavenly Father. He also said, "Call no man rabbi." He was talking about exalted titles and positions. That is why the next verse says that he who is greatest among you will be servant of all, which means slave. That is how you obtain a lofty, exalted position in the kingdom of God: by taking the position of servant and slave.

It is interesting for Him to say, "You are all brethren," and, "You only have one Father." He clarified it: call no man on this earth your father.

People try to explain that away as only religious titles. They say Abraham is the father of faith, or someone fathered a move, so we call men on earth father. There are all kinds of religious titles, and they try to pass that on. In modern commentaries they will use something like a Catholic priest to demonstrate their point.

But when He said, "Call no man on this earth father," I can tell you right now: your natural father is no more your father than Joseph was Jesus' father. Right there, it was broken off. Jesus was born from above. It was broken off right there, the forty-first person from Abraham. Your natural father is no more your father than Joseph was Jesus' father, although Joseph was recorded as His father.

Jesus' only connection with a human being was with Mary, and she was not even in the bloodline. Hallelujah.

Joseph just had to stand by and let the old man die. He was a just man, but he would have put her away. He was willing not to, and of course he started being visited by angels and dreams.

Of course I have a father and mother, because that which is born of flesh is flesh. My natural father and mother caused the birth of my flesh to come about, and in that I inherited a spiritually dead spirit.

But one day, when I bowed my knee to the Lord Jesus Christ and was quickened, I was born from above. I was born again. The old man passed away. Even the law lost its hold over that spiritually dead Jew, because the man it was holding on to died, and it had no nature to hold on to anymore. A new man came into existence, and in that new man I only have one Father, and we are all brethren.

Hallelujah.

I thank my natural father and mother for bringing me into existence in that birth of flesh. There is a big argument about whether, once we are born again, our children are born spiritually alive. You get into a mess there. What if one parent is not saved? Is the child half-saved? You have a merger of DNA. Where would half-saved come from?

I understand this much: what is born of flesh is flesh. The human spirit came into existence at that moment of conception and grew as the body grew. I also know I have to have a birth from above, and that birth from above has to be my decision.

My natural father is no more my father than Joseph was Jesus' father, even though he was recorded in the genealogy as His father.

Hallelujah. I have been born again. My Father is God. Jesus prepared a place for me and received me to Himself, that where He is, I will be also in His glorified humanity. I hold that place now with the Father that He holds as a Son of God in His humanity.

Hallelujah.

The Glory He Gave Us Is the Finished Redemption Work

Let us pick it up in John 17:16:

They are not of the world, even as I am not of the world.

Sanctify them through Thy truth: Thy word is truth.

As Thou hast sent Me into the world, even so have I also sent them into the world.

And for their sakes I sanctify Myself, that they also might be sanctified through the truth.

Then verse 20:

Neither pray I for these alone, but for them also which shall believe on Me through their word;

That they all may be one; as Thou, Father, art in Me, and I in Thee, that they also may be one in Us: that the world may believe that Thou hast sent Me.

And the glory which Thou gavest Me I have given them; that they may be one, even as We are one:

I in them, and Thou in Me, that they may be made perfect in one; and that the world may know that Thou hast sent Me, and hast loved them, as Thou hast loved Me.

Back up for a moment to verse 22:

And the glory which Thou gavest Me I have given them.

That is me. He gave it to me. If the glory He gave me made us one with Him as He is one with the Father, then to understand that glory just a little more, remember: Arnold Schwarzenegger's glory is his body. He takes care of it. If you are going to build your body to the place where it is your glory, you are going to get those shirts that show it off. If you put all that time in, and it is your glory, you might as well show it off a little bit.

Jesus has another glory. He gave His to us.

Usually the cheerleaders marry the football players, and he ends up working in the shoe store while she hangs around a weight-loss gym. Then that marriage collapses, and she usually marries the nerd who made millions, the one nobody would marry until he made them.

For a computer fellow, his glory is that he can crash files, get secret information out of almost any computer, design hardware and new equipment, and finally become absolutely rich. That is his glory.

Jesus said, "My glory I give unto them." The glory He had was what God sent Him to finish. He was the first Man to be born spiritually alive on this planet, and He finished the work His Father gave Him to do. He glorified His Father, and then that glory, "My glory," He gave to us.

He said that the world may know the Father loved us with the love He loved Him.

The glory Jesus gave to you and me was the finished work of the cross in the presence of His Father. That glory demonstrated the power of the Father. It was the whole finished redemption work, so that we can go into this earth now and glorify Jesus like He glorified His Father, and finish the work He has given us to do.

That glory includes all those things He did on this earth. The more I see Him in His glory, the more excited I am getting.

How is the world going to know the Father sent Him and loved us as He loved Jesus? Because the glory God gave Him, He gave to us. That glory is the whole redemption work. It equips us. It is the same Spirit, the same rights, the same everything that was His glory. I get to walk around this earth with His glory, what He has given to me.

The world is supposed to look at you and me and say, "The Father loves this group as He loved Jesus."

You bet I am excited. That glory included power over disease, power over devils, power over poverty, power over everything. It is His glory, and He gave it to me. The world is supposed to look at me when I am operating in His glory and know the Father loves me like He loves Jesus.

Hallelujah.

Matthew 10 Answers Persecution by Saying It Is Enough to Be Like Him

Let us go to Matthew 10. I have been working on Matthew 10, but I still have not quite gotten it over to you because I have been tracing that new life He has given me.

I cannot tell you how excited I am about John 14, when I finally understood that in His Father's house are many rooms, and "where I am, there ye may be also." He was talking about the position He held with the Father in His glorified humanity. He went by way of the cross, and He is receiving me unto Himself, that where He is, I may be also.

I get to walk in the glory He walked in here. He said, "My glory I give unto you." Everything He received in His humanity as He walked with His Father, I get to walk in with Him. Now He is sitting at the right hand of the Father with a glorified body, and when redemption is finished, I will have one just like Him. But right now I get to walk in the glory He walked in here.

In Matthew 10, He said if Beelzebub persecuted the Master of the house, he will persecute the servant. He gave the whole answer to Satan's attacks. He did not just name a principality; He named the lord of the flies himself. He named Satan.

He said Satan attacked the Master and Lord of the household, and if he attacks and persecutes the Lord and Master of the household, he will persecute you because the servant or disciple is not above his lord.

Then He gave the whole answer to all persecution and everything the devil could ever attempt to do to you and me. He said:

It is enough for the disciple that he be as his master, and the servant as his lord.

In answer to all of Satan's persecution, He says, "It is enough. It is enough for you that you can be like your Lord."

That was His answer to you for everything Satan could possibly throw at you for the rest of your life.

"Would You give me this? Would You give me that?"

It is enough for you to be like Him, because if you ever understand what it means to be like Him, you will understand that He already gave you His glory.

Matthew 10:24 says:

The disciple is not above his master, nor the servant above his lord.

It is enough for the disciple that he be as his master, and the servant as his lord.

If they have called the master of the house Beelzebub, how much more shall they call them of his household?

Backing up to verse 23:

But when they persecute you in this city, flee ye into another: for verily I say unto you, Ye shall not have gone over the cities of Israel, till the Son of man be come.

Then:

The disciple is not above his master, nor the servant above his lord.

It is enough to be as your Master. If they persecuted the Master of the house, how much more will Beelzebub persecute those who are of His household? His whole answer was: it is enough for you to be as Him.

What Jesus Taught in Darkness Would Be Preached in Light

In verse 26 He said:

Fear them not therefore: for there is nothing covered, that shall not be revealed; and hid, that shall not be known.

What I tell you in darkness, that speak ye in light: and what ye hear in the ear, that preach ye upon the housetops.

You have to understand that right here He was imparting His power to the twelve in the form of a mantle. They were not born again yet. They went out with His mantle on them, raised the dead, cast devils out, and came back excited, saying, "Even the devils are subject unto us through Thy name."

Jesus said, in effect, "Yes, I know. But rather rejoice that your names are written in heaven."

These men were not regenerated yet. All He could do was put a mantle on them. He said, "The Holy Spirit is with you, but in that day He shall be in you."

I like what He taught about John the Baptist. He said that though John was the greatest prophet born of women, he who is least in the kingdom would be greater than John. The fact that the least in the kingdom was greater than him meant that John did not belong to the kingdom yet.

I also like what He told Peter: "Satan desires to sift you as wheat, but I have prayed for you, that your faith fail not." He was talking about the period between His death and resurrection, and the receiving by the Church of the new nature and the baptism of the Holy Spirit. "When you are converted, strengthen your brethren."

Jesus was fulfilling the old covenant by preaching the principles of the new. That is why He said the law and the prophets were until John; since that time the kingdom of God is preached, and every man presses into it.

He was telling them, "They persecuted Me, and in your ministry of this commission they are going to persecute you. Since I am preaching the principles of the kingdom, they are going to persecute every generation that follows. But you do not have to fear. It is enough to be as the Master."

There is nothing here that will not be uncovered to you, and nothing kept secret or hidden in darkness that will not be revealed. In other words, "You are not born again yet, so you do not have a nature to understand what I am saying to you. But soon you will be born again, and your spirit will come alive and receive a capacity to understand these things. In that day, there is absolutely nothing I taught you that was hidden which you will not understand, and nothing kept secret on the other side of spiritual death that you will not have a capacity to understand. When you receive this, you will become as your Master, and you will know it is enough to be like Me."

What He was teaching them while they were spiritually dead, in darkness, one day they would proclaim in the light of the new nature and in the fullness of understanding who they were in Him.

"What I taught you in the ear, in the closet, proclaim from the housetops. You do not have to fear. You will be born again. You will understand the full revelation. You will become just like your Master. Therefore, you do not have to fear."

Hallelujah.

Fear God Alone, Not the Devil, the Flesh, or Persecution

Verse 27 says:

What I tell you in darkness, that speak ye in light.

All these parables, all these things He taught them while they could not understand, He said to speak in the light, in the illumination and teaching of the Holy Spirit through that new nature. "In Him was life; and the life was the light of men." In that day, you will be born again, and you will teach it out of the life of that new nature.

Then:

And what ye hear in the ear, that preach ye upon the housetops.

And fear not them which kill the body, but are not able to kill the soul.

But rather fear Him which is able to destroy both soul and body in hell.

The devil does not have that power. His cohorts do not have that power. The flesh does not have that power. Nobody has that power to pass that judgment but God. Therefore, if I am going to fear anything, I will fear Him, because He is the only One who has the power in the final judgment to destroy both soul and body in hell.

He is the only One I fear, not the devil. If I fear anything, it is not transforming totally into Jesus' glory through the understanding of the new nature with the power of the Holy Spirit as my Teacher. If I am going to fear anything, it is not becoming like Him.

Glory.

So notice: I have grounds for not being afraid. I do not have to fear those who can kill my body but cannot kill my soul.

Sparrows, Hair, and Lilies Teach the Worth of Sons

Look at the example He gave to these spiritually dead men:

Are not two sparrows sold for a farthing? and one of them shall not fall on the ground without your Father.

But the very hairs of your head are all numbered.

That is about one-sixteenth of a day's wage. If you do not believe the hairs of your head are numbered, when you get to heaven, just ask Jesus how many you had in your heyday. If you get a real close relationship with Him, where He can fellowship with you on a friend's level, and you wash your hair and lose four or five, He may even keep count for you: "That is number five million six hundred seventy-eight."

Can you imagine Him using something as basic as this to teach you not to be afraid and to teach you who you are?

The servant can be as the Master because you are born again, and there is nothing hidden from you now that will keep you from becoming like Him. Hairs on your head are numbered. "Fear not therefore."

What kind of teaching is that?

Most preachers I know operate in a certain amount of fear. Most of what they do is motivated by being sure the lack is supplied in their ministry. But here Jesus is using the most basic fundamentals: the worth of a sparrow.

How do you like being compared to a sparrow as the son of God? The hairs on your head are numbered. He is trying to use things like that. How would you magnify the worth of a sparrow that just perishes against your worth, when He sent His Son for you? How are you going to figure that out with your head?

You have no basis for fear. Satan came after the Lord of the house; he is going to come after you. You can remain unilluminated from those things once hidden by spiritual death. Yes, you can live a life unilluminated from those things and never, outside of just being born again, become as your Master. But He wants you to know that it is enough to be like Him. If you let Him take you and transform you to be like your Master, He wants you to know that is enough.

He also compared the lilies of the field to Solomon in all his glory. Can you imagine the majesty of Solomon literally stunning the Queen of Sheba until there was no spirit left in her? She said, "The half was not told me in my own country." She was stunned to see his apparel, the utensils of worship, the majesty, and the way he ascended to the temple to worship God.

Can you imagine the majesty and beauty of that? Yet Jesus says that Solomon in all his glory was not clothed like one of these.

He is right. One time I wondered what was the closest thing to the beauty of heaven that I could look at on earth. He let me know: have you ever seen a fresh, beautiful flower arrangement with all the different kinds of flowers? That is getting pretty close. It is absolutely beautiful. I do not think anything is clothed like nature.

Yet Solomon in all his glory was not clothed like one of these. Then Jesus talked about how temporary it was. Today it is in the field; tomorrow it is baking bread in a woman's oven.

He said, "How is it that you can have no faith? How is it that you have no faith for provision?"

If you are going to become like your Master, your Lord, your Teacher, Jesus, when the veil of spiritual death is taken off and those hidden things are revealed, then all the things hidden in secret can be proclaimed out of light. There is enough in there to make you just like your Lord.

He said it is enough to be like Him.

Inheritance Means the Father Wants to Supply the Gospel

I know God is my Father now. My natural father is no more my father in the new man than Joseph was Jesus' father. He is my genealogy, but not my Father anymore. That man died. God is my Father, and we are all brethren.

I want you to know I have a great inheritance. He looks at the sparrow when it falls. He counts the hairs on my head. He looks at my eternal existence and wants to clothe me beyond what Solomon was clothed with, beyond the lily which was so temporary, though He took painstaking time to clothe it and tomorrow it is baking bread.

How is it, as a son with the full inheritance, that I can have no faith?

I think I belong to a kingdom on this earth as a son of God. I think I am an extension of the kingdom of heaven, and my Father is my Father. There is one thing in my Father's heart: He would not that any should perish. He wants to carry out the salvation of the world, and the only thing He has to use to do that is the body of Christ.

For me to think, even for a fleeting moment, that a Father who numbers the hairs on my head, notices the sparrow, and clothes the lily would not tend to all the billions needed to carry this gospel as far as it needs to be carried, shows how I once limited myself to men's limited thinking.

Thank God for my Father and the way He does things. I know He wants to get in and pick the bill up. He wants to get in and pay the bill before I even approach it, for the salvation of the world.

Hallelujah, Jesus.

How could I have feared? How could I ever have thought I would have to beg men for money to preach and win the lost? How could I have thought my Father would not pay for what He wanted done?

What confidence did I instill in people about God's ability to give provision by being reduced to a beggar, while He is counting the hairs on my head, noticing the sparrow, clothing the lily, and I am a son of God with a full inheritance wandering around begging to get a little work done?

I am sorry. I am through with that stuff. It is enough to be like Him. I am going to major on getting like Him instead of running around begging.

I preach to people, and I want them to know I serve a God who supplies every need.

"The Word does not work for me."

How do you know unless you try it? Are you like your Master yet, turning the other cheek and walking the second mile? How are you doing?

"It does not work for me."

Yes, it does. You just have not tried it.

I think you are starting to get this.

Luke 11 Shows the New Nature as the Candle That Removes Hidden Darkness

Notice the wording now:

There is nothing covered, that shall not be revealed; and hid, that shall not be known.

What I tell you in darkness, that speak ye in light.

Now go to Luke 11. It is sounding the same.

Beginning with Luke 11:27:

And it came to pass, as He spake these things, a certain woman of the company lifted up her voice, and said unto Him, Blessed is the womb that bare Thee, and the paps which Thou hast sucked.

Notice the correction Jesus brought:

But He said, Yea rather, blessed are they that hear the word of God, and keep it.

Why would we be more blessed than Mary herself from hearing the Word of God and keeping it?

Then when the people were thick together, He began to say:

This is an evil generation: they seek a sign; and there shall no sign be given it, but the sign of Jonah the prophet.

For as Jonah was a sign unto the Ninevites, so shall also the Son of man be to this generation.

You know how Jonah was a sign to the Ninevites. He was thrown overboard out of the ship when he revealed to the men that he was the problem and why the boat was sinking. He said, "Throw me overboard, and it will be okay." They threw him overboard. They sacrificed him for their life, just like Jesus was thrown over for the whole human race and went into the deep. Jonah called it the belly of hell. He was swallowed by the whale, spit up, and then came and preached to the Ninevites, who repented in sackcloth and ashes. They even put their animals on a fast.

That was a sign given to that generation.

Then:

The queen of the south shall rise up in the judgment with the men of this generation, and condemn them: for she came from the utmost parts of the earth to hear the wisdom of Solomon; and, behold, a greater than Solomon is here.

Solomon led us up to the edge of the rebirth, but he could not lead us in. Jesus is saying plainly that Solomon operated in the shadow of the rebirth, but He is greater than Solomon and will bring us into it.

This is what He was talking about in verse 33:

No man, when he hath lighted a candle, putteth it in a secret place, neither under a bushel, but on a candlestick, that they which come in may see the light.

That is a funny verse just to stick in there, but that is why He says, "Blessed are they that hear the Word of God and keep it." From keeping it, they were going to be born again. That is why they would be more blessed.

Solomon operated in the shadow. The wisdom he operated in was a type of what your new nature is to your spirit. When a candle is lit, what is it lit for? Is it lit to be hidden under a bushel? No, the candle illuminates the entire room.

Your new nature is to your spirit what that candle was to that dark room. It illuminates you. Nothing will be hidden anymore because you are going to be born again. You will receive a new nature, and everything hidden in darkness will give way to the light, because your new nature is to your spirit what that burning candle was to that dark room.

Hallelujah, Jesus.

Mark 4 Says the Parables Were Hidden Until the Candle Was Lit

Now we have another one. Go to Mark 4.

Hallelujah. Glory be to God.

As you know, He gave the parable of the sower in parable form. Up until verse 10, He gave it that way. He said the sower sowed, the fowls of the air came, and this was wayside. Then He spoke about thorny ground.

In verse 9, right in the middle of the parable, He said:

He that hath ears to hear, let him hear.

Then:

And when He was alone, they that were about Him with the twelve asked of Him the parable.

And He said unto them, Unto you it is given to know the mystery of the kingdom of God: but unto them that are without, all these things are done in parables:

That seeing they may see, and not perceive; and hearing they may hear, and not understand; lest at any time they should be converted, and their sins should be forgiven them.

Most teachers leave that verse alone. They jump over the parable part, go to the answer, and just mumble when they read through those verses.

To the multitudes He gave it in parable form. But when the twelve and those gathered around Him came, He began to expound it. Can you tell me the mystery of why He hid it from the multitude?

Certainly. It is not difficult.

That nation had to fulfill the law and offer Jesus up as the final sacrifice. I can tell you one thing: if He raised my child from the dead, you could not get me to offer Him. Yet the priesthood had to offer Him up as the final sacrifice.

Peter talked about that after the healing of the lame man on Solomon's porch. In Acts 3:12 he said:

Ye men of Israel, why marvel ye at this? or why look ye so earnestly on us, as though by our own power or holiness we had made this man to walk?

The God of Abraham, and of Isaac, and of Jacob, the God of our fathers, hath glorified His Son Jesus; whom ye delivered up, and denied Him in the presence of Pilate, when he was determined to let Him go.

But ye denied the Holy One and the Just, and desired a murderer to be granted unto you;

And killed the Prince of life, whom God hath raised from the dead; whereof we are witnesses.

And His name through faith in His name hath made this man strong, whom ye see and know.

Then:

And now, brethren, I wot that through ignorance ye did it, as did also your rulers.

But those things, which God before had shewed by the mouth of all His prophets, that Christ should suffer, He hath so fulfilled.

Repent ye therefore, and be converted, that your sins may be blotted out...

He said they did it in ignorance. But now is the time to turn and repent. Every person up to the time of Jesus' resurrection was covered by the Abrahamic covenant of accounted righteousness. The day He was resurrected, revival immediately broke out.

Hallelujah.

Going back to Mark 4, to those without, it was not given. It was spoken in parable form. But He was grooming those within and explaining the parables against the day that the candle in them, their spirit, would be lit and nothing would be hidden. He was grooming them so that what He taught them in darkness, they would speak forth in light.

Good Ground Can Choose Thirty, Sixty, or a Hundredfold

Let us take up the good ground for the next few minutes, and then we are done.

Mark 4:20 says:

And these are they which are sown on good ground; such as hear the word, and receive it, and bring forth fruit, some thirtyfold, some sixty, and some an hundred.

Then He said:

Is a candle brought to be put under a bushel, or under a bed? and not to be set on a candlestick?

For there is nothing hid, which shall not be manifested; neither was any thing kept secret, but that it should come abroad.

If any man have ears to hear, let him hear.

Take heed what ye hear: with what measure ye mete, it shall be measured to you: and unto you that hear shall more be given.

For he that hath, to him shall be given: and he that hath not, from him shall be taken even that which he hath.

The same verses again. He took them aside and expounded the parables while they lived in darkness. He was telling them the day would come when nothing would be covered, nothing would be hidden. You can walk in thirtyfold. You can walk in sixtyfold. You can walk in a hundredfold. The measure you measure out is what you will get.

He said, "When your candle is lit, and everything that was once hidden in darkness gives way, you will be able to understand who I am and what I am. You will be a born-again son of the living God. In that day, nothing will be hidden to keep you from producing thirty, sixty, or a hundredfold. It is your choice."

It is enough for the slave to be as his Master.

Hallelujah.
