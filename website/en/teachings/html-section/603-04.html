603-04 Understanding a Place Called Done

The Born Again Trail Leads Into Done

Open to Romans 2, and then we will go along as we get there.

I know that on this part of the Born Again Trail, He is bringing different facets together to establish that place called done more severely.

In the first visitation, when I looked down the Born Again Trail, I remember the anointing was on me so strongly it felt like the gift of faith. I had God's faith. Nothing was impossible. Any question I asked with my heart, He answered instantly.

One of the questions was about all those verses:

Nothing shall be impossible unto you.

And:

The works that I do shall he do also; and greater works than these shall he do.

And:

Whosoever shall say unto this mountain, Be thou removed...

And:

If ye had faith as a grain of mustard seed...

I posed this question to Him, and He knew what I was saying. Although those are our promises, the Church does not reflect much of that. Hardly any churches I know do. It seems as though the journey from here to there is longer than most people's lives.

I said, "Can You show me?"

He said, "You will find your answer on this trail."

He was right.

At that time, the thing I knew most about was in the book, The Walk of the Spirit, The Walk of Power. Little did I know I had reached out and grabbed the right key, the one Jesus talked about when He said:

He shall receive of mine, and shall shew it unto you.

The key I latched onto was the revelation key, and it saved my life.

Tongues, Worship, Fasting, and Meditation Become Keys to the Finished Place

As the Born Again Trail began to unfold, I found out that praying in tongues is a revelation gift. In the position I have in the body of Christ, one thing it does regularly is open mysteries to me. He will do that for you too, but He will also give you what is necessary for the qualification of your call.

If you wanted to define compassion, compassion is something everybody is supposed to have. But when you find your passion, that is something else. Paul found his. That is why people were so nourished when he fed them the Word of God. He sat at the feet of Gamaliel and had everything from history, from one of the best teachers who ever lived. Then he sat under the apostles and got the best of the future.

When Paul went into a town and into the synagogue, he nourished believers, but he had such a passion that he always caused persecution. Sometimes I think they were glad when he left town because they helped him leave.

Passion is what gives your life life in the journey.

As the Born Again Trail unfolded, when I needed private worship, He revealed that key and unlocked the principles and power in it. He unlocked the fasting key, which was not the most popular. Then when I had maxed out what I had taken in from the Word, He unlocked the meditation and assimilation key. I already knew basic meditation, but He showed me meditation and assimilation. He showed me how my soul and mind worked, and I was able to use what He showed me.

Now He is taking these different facets of the Born Again Trail and bringing them together to establish the place we have been calling done. It is the place God visited us with through the cross. It is the position, the standing, the life. It is what He has given you, and what you need to find out is how final He has given it to you.

Confession Changes Us, Not God

Like anybody, I thought I knew about faith. Most of the time, when I got something to work on purpose, it was more accident than purpose. But I did what would cause me eventually to believe it, and then God did it.

When I was trying to understand confession, I thought, "Since You already know my petition before I pray, how is me saying it 9,999 times going to make You do it?"

Some say, "You have to pressure God. You have to talk Him into it. You have to pray with hard faith."

It is not Him you are changing. Quit yelling at Him.

I remember those verses where Jesus said:

Use not vain repetitions, as the heathen do: for they think that they shall be heard for their much speaking.

I thought, "There is hardly anything more like that than confessing something 9,999 times, like your healing."

The heathen chant. You think the devil does not have a good copy? They chant to immerse themselves in what they are getting into.

I remember, on blatant faith, taking what somebody told me and deciding, "I am going to use confession to get myself healed according to the mighty verses. I can say to the mountain, and if I doubt not in my heart, I will have what I say."

Not too many read down to the part about having ought against someone. You need to get rid of it, or it will not work.

When I first began to apprehend something, I had a tilted vertebra that tried to paralyze one leg. Before I would go out to do a healing line, I would have to sit down in a chair and push that thing hard. It was nice to have a big twin-engine airplane, but I could hardly get out of it.

It was not faith that got me a twin-engine airplane and then could not get me out of it.

My back does not hurt now, though I have had a few bouts. I hated that burning right at the vertebra, right where the weight-bearing mathematics takes place. It made me uncomfortable all the time. The only thing that helped was getting the weight off it.

It is gone now. The closer I get to that place called done, the more I see it.

Confession played its part also. But because I did not understand confession, it took longer to work for me. I thought I was basically talking God into it. Trust me, He is not the One with the problem.

Repetition Works When It Becomes Assimilation of Truth

When I began confession, I would walk and say, "I call myself healed. I call myself healed. No, you do not, devil. You cannot have me. You do not have a right to keep that thing in my body."

I knew it here, but there was a mystery of knowing it here. You walk around wondering, "Why will it not go?" We come up with good names to cover that, like mental assent.

I would walk back and forth: "I call myself healed." Then the devil would say, "You think you will be heard for your much speaking?"

He started in on me: "What is magical about 9,999? Does God move on thousands? Maybe there is a code in here."

The devil would love to get you into anything that keeps you from receiving. Sometimes you think it is something in the natural, besides corresponding actions, that produces it. You think it is a formula.

The timing is mostly when you can believe it, and it has everything to do with what you know about it. Sometimes in the timing, He has you in school. That still does not mean He is not going to give it to you as quickly as He can.

I learned a word that worked well for the devil: "Shut up."

It was not my much speaking that did it. It was what that much speaking did to me. That is part of meditation and assimilation. It is how you are made.

You can offer your soul on the altar of information. Stand next to somebody who speaks Chinese and realize they have symbols for every sound they make, just as we have an alphabet with symbols for every sound we make. They can flow with that and understand each other, and to them we sound like that.

Your mind can assess language as fast as it is thrown at you. The only reason you would not understand what I am saying is if I did not say it clearly, put words in the wrong place, or was too undescriptive. You have that ability. You were created like that.

When you learn how to put that to work for you on purpose, you can walk the floor and call yourself something, especially one of the major redemptive identification truths, like the curse of sin or the curse of sickness and disease being removed.

Now that He is bringing more together, I am finding out how final that judgment was. When I walk and call myself something, I see that final place. I do not have trouble calling other people healed on the phone or anywhere else. I am having trouble not believing it. That place is forming in me now.

You cannot walk and call yourself healed without eventually starting to affect yourself. Eventually you start believing it because it starts transforming you into what you are giving yourself to the most. If you are giving yourself to a truth that says you are healed, somewhere down the line you will end up believing it.

The Finished Place Makes Impossible Promises Receivable

When I was attacked with an infection that ran through my system for about a year, I am not sure what the devil was trying to do. I think he was trying to kill me.

A woman I know who did cultures for doctors asked me what it was like. I explained how that infection ran through me. She said, "It is possible your appendix burst. Did you get yourself checked out?"

I said, "No."

She said, "What did you do?"

I said, "I hurt and believed."

It was right on this platform that I got healed coming down the steps. He asked me a question on this step. It was something He had already been dealing with me on. He said, "Are you going to do this?"

I said, "Yes, I am going to do it."

He knew I meant it. That was all He needed. Right there, the pain left. By the end of the day, the growth, knot, bulge, or whatever was sticking out was gone.

Hallelujah, Jesus.

This place called done is where a work done by heaven was so final that when scattered verses in different subjects refer to what you are capable of, we look across the void and then look at believers we have known all our lives and say, "This has to be impossible."

It is impossible. That is where God likes to begin.

You were impossibly lost, and He had a remedy. You were on the outside, and now you are inside.

We read those impossible verses. Usually the closest we see is people coming into a healing anointing to some degree, and it is often selective. It hardly ever approaches the revivals in the book of Acts, when neighboring cities brought their sick and tormented, and Scripture says they were healed, every one.

Someone says, "Brother Roberson, they were trained by Jesus Himself."

Yes. And who came to walk with Jesus after He was baptized into your death by John and pulled back up into the life you were going to walk in? He had to be empowered by the third Person of the Godhead, the Holy Spirit, who taught Him.

Jesus taught the disciples, but who taught them what He said? It was not until they were born again that they started to catch on.

I love Peter on the Mount of Transfiguration. He looked up and said, "Let us build three tabernacles." Jesus probably felt like saying, "Have you been listening? The law and prophets were until John. Since that time the kingdom of God is preached, and every man presses into it. I am preaching principles that belong to the kingdom while fulfilling the old covenant."

Peter saw Moses and Elijah and wanted to build three tabernacles. They might as well have built one for themselves too after seeing something like that.

Who is your Teacher?

The Nature Change Gives the Spirit Something to Work With

I am not going to stand before Him and be judged guilty for letting my lifetime in this town slip by without affecting it with what Jesus said I could affect it with. I am just not going to do it.

The ride is rough at times, and who can guarantee it will get better? I do not need to ask Paul. It might get rougher. Who cares? You will be happy about this before a thousand years is over.

What will you be happy about? That you got in and fought like Jesus meant what He said, even if it was impossible.

All things are possible to him that believes. He is not talking outside the spirit of His Word. He is talking about everything He promised you. What He said He would and could do is possible to you who believe.

When He added the worship key, it made it possible for the capacity of my new nature to begin receiving more and more of the love He always loved me with. He has no favorite kids.

The more I received His love, the more I could look over the fence at Him and understand why He does things the way He does. It is His nature. I am getting freer and freer in His grace because I am starting to understand. I can discern things more and more because He is heightening the presence of His love in my nature.

I have taught you in detail about Gospel Duck, the duck raised as a chicken. I wore you out with it because I had to get across the nature change and the role it plays. It is the only thing the Holy Spirit has to work with.

You ask, "Why does He not go around me and do it?"

What releases Him to do it is getting you to believe. So He schools you, gets you to believe, and you move the mountains.

Do you think heaven is standing back saying, "I do not want to do it yet. Run, Holy Ghost, do something"? No. The Holy Ghost is the One who gets you to believe it. Heaven is delighted when they find the substance they need to move to the salvation of the masses, the power to unfold mysteries that confound and harvest where there was no harvest.

He is delighted when the Holy Ghost does such a work with somebody that He actually brings forward who Jesus made them.

The Holy Ghost is not lacking in power. The Father is not the problem. The Word, the Lord Jesus Christ your Redeemer, is not the problem.

Then who does that leave?

Gospel Duck took his place. It was more fun for chickens to raise a duck because he did not know he could swim and did not know he could fly. He snuck away to do those things, but the reason he knew he could do them was that they were in his nature.

He said, "Where are you going, son?"

"Mom, I am going south. I have heard there is a place called done there, and I will see you in the spring. I love you, Mom, but I cannot live as a chicken anymore."

You have a nature with full capacity to understand, receive, reservoir, and pool the love of God. What a powerful teaching tool: to increase the love in the nature of love, look over at God, understand more about His love, and then take a difficult verse and understand what He meant when He said it.

You had better keep praying in the Holy Ghost. You are activating the Teacher in you, the Holy Spirit. He is not speaking those tongues for His benefit. He is trying to make a champion out of you.

God Takes Pleasure When We Come Within Range of Done

We are heading for a place called done. At times God is excited, though I am not sure we can describe His emotional process that way. When I was groping for the right word, it jumped out at me: He takes great pleasure.

He takes great pleasure when He sees us getting close, when He sees us coming within range of possibility. It brings Him great pleasure when He sees people on the border or edge of operating in some place in Him that is very seldom touched.

When He gets like that, I can feel it. When I catch something He said and give His agenda instead of mine, sometimes I am the only one who goes back feeling His pleasure, because I took my beating the day before. But I can feel His pleasure strongly when I please Him.

These revelations are coming together. Faith comes by hearing, and hearing by what the Word said about it. My understanding is that in that place, He is absolutely loosed with no restraint as faith and power genuinely come under the judgment Jesus bore. Paul describes it: once, at the end of the world, He was so final about this thing.

If my sin was judged, so were your diseases, once, two thousand years ago. He called two thousand years ago the end of the world.

When all these principles merge and put us on that rock, everything God funneled through the cross and gave us, everything Jesus paid for, is what we get to see turned loose.

That is where revival is. I am not only talking about an outpouring, a move, a season with God where we touched something and got whatever He was in that area for a while until everybody drank of it, partook of it, and then it subsided like a wave.

I am talking about a place, not a move. A place.

Joy Carries the Power of Done Before the Deposit Manifests

I think touching God's joy, the joy of the Lord as your strength, is part of this. We have been through this several times, and I have been trying to explain the difference between joy and peace.

I have had peace for some time. All hell can turn loose, and I will not turn loose of my peace. I do not like it when hell turns loose, but I will not turn loose of the presence of knowing it is done. How long it takes, what it takes, I do not care. I know it, so I will maintain my peace.

But joy is different.

When I looked at that place called done, I started getting His joy, feeling His pleasure that He would take somebody who had never walked, never heard, never seen, and let me be there when they gain consciousness with all their senses. I am talking about three- and four-day-dead people. A place called done.

I am talking about mapping out every children's hospital in the United States. I am talking about stirring up some trouble. You want to change a nation? Give it revival. You want to change a nation? Stir it up. Turn the bars into churches. Change spirits.

Peace maintains. I remember having peace after a long fast. I came out of it and said, "This is a strange feeling, not to be troubled about these things. This must be peace."

Jesus said, "Not as the world giveth." What kind of peace does the world give? If the world has the resources, it can resolve something. I do not even need that.

I am still trying to come up with something to explain that kind of joy. I had to revert to the most powerful thing on this planet outside of God, which is mammon.

Suppose somebody says, "I am going to give you five million dollars. It is coming through. The deposit is set up next month." I got excited the first seven or eight times people came to me with things like that. After a while, I still had joy over the possibility, but I waited.

But this is how joy feels: somebody puts the cash in your hand, and you feel the power surge. "I could have that car. I could have that house." You feel the power of that deposit.

That is how far joy pulls ahead of peace. Not only do I have peace, but I have joy, and I feel the power as though it has already been deposited.

So forgive me when I see that place called done and break down with joy at what He can do there.

Romans Separates Finished Righteousness From Law-Based Perfection

Go to Romans 3 and let us at least set up where we are going.

Two things were happening at Rome. One, the Jewish community was having trouble understanding why swine-eating Gentiles who had never kept a day of the law were suddenly getting their inheritance. Two, even when Gentiles were converted, Judaizers said they could not be perfected outside the law. They would start naming things they had to keep, like the Sabbath, and once the list starts, it never stops.

When Paul appealed to Rome on his missionary journey and turned to Jerusalem, they had him take a vow of purification and shave his head. James said, "Look at all these Jews who have been saved and are zealous for the law. Please do this to keep from causing trouble."

Paul did not mind becoming all things to all men so he could win some. But he also had a way of nurturing, because he sat at the feet of Gamaliel. That man was legendary in his knowledge of the law and Scripture. Paul had the best of both worlds. When he went into a synagogue, he could prove why Jesus was indeed the Christ.

But Paul also confronted Peter once about eating with Gentiles. Peter was not only spending time with them; he was eating the food they ate, food that was not kosher, until certain men came down from Jerusalem. When they came, Peter got caught up in dissimulation, another word for hypocrisy, and he and the other Jews withdrew.

This is where Paul continually got into trouble. He brought such nurturing to the Jew by proving from the past, and to the Gentile also. But he also caused persecution. The last time he went to Jerusalem, he had to be taken by force and put on a ship headed for Rome.

When he got to Rome, this great apostle, after all the trials were over, had a rented house. Everyone who came to him, he received. How many people do you know who would receive every single person who came for two years and minister as much of the law and Word as they needed every time?

Paul had something to him. But he caused so much persecution because he opened his mouth. He totally surrendered, and he got in trouble again and again.

So with Judaizers and Jews having trouble believing the Gentiles were saved after the great revivals under Peter and at Antioch, Paul wrote a masterpiece separating the two. There is so much power in Romans that you hardly know how to deal with it.

If you assimilate that book, I can guarantee your feet will start walking toward the capacity you have as a born-again believer.

Faith Receives What Christ Will Not Come Back to Provide Again

In Romans 10, Paul is still defining the difference between the righteousness of the law and the righteousness of the Spirit. He is still explaining why the Gentiles can be saved.

He says:

Say not in thine heart, Who shall ascend into heaven? (that is, to bring Christ down from above:)

Or, Who shall descend into the deep? (that is, to bring up Christ again from the dead.)

He is not going to go and provide salvation for you again. He is not going to go and provide even a flake-of-dust proportion of healing for you again. At one time, it was settled forever.

You need to stop and find out how to receive.

How many, after you stop and think about it for a minute, feel edified? How many feel like there is hope for you? How many have the kind of hope being charged with the substance of faith?

Now faith is the substance of the thing I am hoping for. It is all the evidence I need of the thing I cannot see.

Do you need anything else?

Nothing.
