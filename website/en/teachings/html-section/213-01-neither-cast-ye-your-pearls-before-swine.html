213-01 Neither Cast Ye Your Pearls Before Swine

The House That Stands Is Built by Hearing and Doing Jesus' Sayings

Matthew 7:24 says:

Therefore whosoever heareth these sayings of mine,

and doeth them,

I will liken him unto a wise man,

which built his house upon a rock:

And the rain descended,

and the floods came,

and the winds blew,

and beat upon that house;

and it fell not:

for it was founded upon a rock.

I want you to notice something here. Jesus said not only did this man hear the Word, but he did the Word.

Notice something else: Jesus never guaranteed that a storm would not come. In fact, quite the contrary. This verse almost guarantees you that storms will come.

But here is the good part. He said if you are like this wise man who built his house upon the understanding of His Word, then hell or high water, no matter how bad the storm gets, you have a personal guarantee from Jesus Christ Himself that your house will not go down the river.

Would it not behoove you and me to find out why the word "therefore" is there?

"Therefore" means, "Based upon the foundation of what I have already said, hear this."

What is He saying? He is saying that if I would be a doer of His Word, I would build my house on a rock so solid that hell or high water, it would not go down the river.

Then it would behoove us to find out exactly what sayings Jesus is talking about that we are supposed to build our house on.

I know what I used to preach His sayings were:

The works that I do shall ye do also;

and greater works than these shall ye do;

because I go unto my Father.

Now that is one saying I can have a cup of tea over. I like that one.

But it just so happens that is not the saying He is talking about.

How many of you would do the sayings Jesus is talking about if you knew that in doing them, hell or high water, there is not a thing this side of hell the devil could do about your house?

We do not have to back up very far to find out why He put "therefore" there. In fact, to get ourselves in real deep water, we only have to back up to the first verse of Matthew 7.

The Rock Begins With Refusing to Judge

Matthew 7:1 says:

Judge not,

that ye be not judged.

How many of you would rather go back to "The works that I do shall ye do also"?

Jesus said, "Judge not, and you will not be judged."

Verse 2 says:

For with what judgment ye judge,

ye shall be judged:

and with what measure ye mete,

it shall be measured to you again.

When I first was turned on to the Word, I kept it right down to the letter. When I ran into "Judge not, and you will not be judged," I thought, "It would be real nice to have people treat me the way I want to be treated. It seems the way to get people to treat me the way I want to be treated is to treat them likewise."

So I made up my mind that if He said, "Judge not, and you will not be judged," then Mr. Goody-Two-Shoes was born. You would have had to tie me up and pull my fingernails out to get me to say anything ugly about anyone.

I thought in doing that, people would quit being ugly to me.

Wrong.

In fact, Mark 4 guarantees quite the contrary. It says Satan comes immediately to steal the Word.

The better I got, the worse people got. It seemed like they lined up just to have their turn.

I said, "Good God, You said if I do not judge, then I will not be judged. I quit judging, and it seems like people are coming in crowds to judge me."

But He did not say people would not be ugly to me. He was talking about building your house on the rock. He guaranteed that storms would come, the stream would rise, and the wind would beat on your house. All He guaranteed was that your house would not go down the river.

Therefore, the judgment He is talking about is in the spirit, not in the natural.

As long as there are people running around in this world, and as long as there is a devil on the loose, people will seek you out for the sole purpose of being ugly to you.

The judgment He was talking about is spiritual judgment.

If I refuse to judge you, Satan does not have the power, no matter what it looks like, to judge my house and cause it to go down the river.

He did not say you would not be ugly to me. He said that no matter how ugly you were, or anybody else was, or circumstances were, or money was, or praise was, Satan does not have the power to judge my house.

Doing Jesus' sayings is the only refuge and the only house that will not go down the river.

Judging Another Person's Mote While Keeping Your Beam Is Hypocrisy

Matthew 7:3 says:

And why beholdest thou the mote

that is in thy brother's eye,

but considerest not the beam

that is in thine own eye?

Verse 4:

Or how wilt thou say to thy brother,

Let me pull out the mote out of thine eye;

and, behold,

a beam is in thine own eye?

Jesus brings one strong conclusion to this:

Thou hypocrite,

first cast out the beam out of thine own eye;

and then shalt thou see clearly

to cast out the mote

out of thy brother's eye.

What in the world is a hypocrite?

Jesus called this man a hypocrite, the one who refused to get the beam out of his own eye but judged another man for having a mote in his eye.

A hypocrite cannot be a hypocrite without knowing he is one. A hypocrite is someone who says one thing and does another. A hypocrite is someone who preaches one thing and lives another. A hypocrite is somebody whose life speaks so loudly you cannot hear what he is saying.

Jesus said a hypocrite is someone who, with a beam in his own eye, continues to judge his brother for the mote that is in his.

Pearls Before Swine Belongs to the Same Warning About Judging

Now we are going to look at another verse that it took me eleven years to understand.

Matthew 7:6 says:

Give not that which is holy unto the dogs,

neither cast ye your pearls before swine,

lest they trample them under their feet,

and turn again and rend you.

I never really understood what that meant because I broke the cardinal rule: never take a verse out of its context.

We tend to create man-made doctrines out of verses we pull out of context, then wonder why they do not carry any power.

By the time we are done, if you will take heed and have ears to hear, you will have one more solid row of foundation on that rock that hell can do nothing about.

In my King James and in your King James, they have that little mark that says Jesus changed the subject. But just because King James says Jesus changed the subject does not necessarily mean Jesus changed the subject.

I said, "Lord, if I quit taking 'cast not that which is holy before the dogs, and your pearls before swine' out of context, what in the world does it mean?"

I asked those who seemed to be somewhat among them. The most common answer I got was this: when God reveals a precious truth to you, do not carry it over and give it to the sinner, because he will trample it under his feet and turn around and mock it.

Then that same preacher will preach that one time, and out of the other side of his mouth he will say, "Bring the sinner. Set him in church. Set him under the Word." In half an hour, he will bring forth more precious pearls and holy things than you can shake a stick at.

Do you think sinners do not go out laughing and mocking? Do you know how many times you have been laughed at for speaking with tongues?

I said, "Lord, is this what it means to cast pearls before swine and holy things before dogs?"

I could not get clearance in my spirit, because if there is anything Dave Roberson wants, it is to build a house on God's rock that hell cannot do anything about. To do that, I must understand His Word.

The Pearl Is the Truth You Refuse to Keep While Judging Your Brother

Verses 5 and 6 are one thought:

Thou hypocrite,

first cast out the beam out of thine own eye;

and then shalt thou see clearly

to cast out the mote

out of thy brother's eye.

Give not that which is holy unto the dogs.

Think about this for a minute.

What is a hypocrite? A hypocrite is somebody whose life speaks louder than what he is saying. A hypocrite is someone who says one thing and does another.

Did you know God knew you and I would have faults?

Is there a faultless one among us? Is there anyone who does not get angry, who is not short-tempered at times, who always goes the second mile, who always prays for those who despitefully use him, who always loves those who hate him, who always has a reaction according to the Word of God in every incident?

No.

Jesus classified as a hypocrite the one who has faults but judges his brother of the same faults.

In human nature, we tend to destroy in other people what we hate in ourselves.

He did not say you and I would not have faults. God knows that we do. But He was saying that when you judge your brother for the same fault you have, you cast your pearls before swine.

You empower the devil to take that judgment you were guilty of when you judged him, and it empowers him to turn around and judge you back with the same judgment, the same measure you measured to him.

The only way you can cast your pearls before swine is by judging your brother for the holy thing you yourself are guilty of not keeping. When you do, you empower the devil with that truth you would not keep to judge you with because you judged your brother.

When you judged your brother of faults you possess, you are the one who cast the pearl before swine. You are the one who cast the holy thing before dogs.

By refusing to live what you were judging him for, you empowered the devil to pick up that pearl. The same measure you measured out, he turns around and measures back on your house, causing your house to go down the river in the middle of the storm.

Ask God for Good Things to Help Your Brother Instead of Judging Him

It is better for me to walk in the fullness of His Spirit. As Matthew's account of the parable of the sower says, good ground not only heard the Word, but understood it.

What should I do rather than judge you? What would I like God to judge back on me in the spirit?

I would like to do to you what I would like God to do to me.

That is what Matthew 7:7 is about:

Ask,

and it shall be given you;

seek,

and ye shall find;

knock,

and it shall be opened unto you:

For every one that asketh receiveth;

and he that seeketh findeth;

and to him that knocketh it shall be opened.

Ask who?

Ask God, and He will give me good things for my brother whom I refuse to judge.

Verse 9 says:

Or what man is there of you,

whom if his son ask bread,

will he give him a stone?

Or if he ask a fish,

will he give him a serpent?

Verse 11:

If ye then,

being evil,

know how to give good gifts unto your children,

how much more shall your Father which is in heaven

give good things to them that ask him?

Verse 12:

Therefore all things whatsoever ye would

that men should do to you,

do ye even so to them:

for this is the law and the prophets.

If I am not going to judge you and cast my pearls before swine, what should I do?

He said to ask. Ask God, and He will give me good things for my brother whom I refuse to judge. I am going to do to him what I would like him to do to me, and I will pray that God gives me some good thing to give him to help get the mote out of his eye, because I refuse to let a beam enter mine.

If I pray and ask good things for you instead of judging, then God is going to turn around and do to me and my house as I was doing to you and your house. In doing good, I cast my pearls before God, and He picks them up and blesses me with them.

The Storm Cannot Remove a House Built on This Kind of Mercy

Now we understand Matthew 7:24 better:

Therefore whosoever heareth these sayings of mine,

and doeth them,

I will liken him unto a wise man,

which built his house upon a rock:

And the rain descended,

and the floods came,

and the winds blew,

and beat upon that house;

and it fell not:

for it was founded upon a rock.

Based on what He has already said, whoever hears these sayings and does them is like a wise man who built his house on a rock.

The storm still comes. The flood still rises. The wind still beats upon the house. But the house does not fall, because it is founded upon a rock.

I can hardly wait until tomorrow night as we continue the Spirit's probe.
