434-04-Why People Fall Out Of Prayer Why People Fall Out of Prayer

Romans 12 Points Back to How the Body Is Offered

Let us look at Romans 12 again as we enter into this next phase.

Romans 12 says:

I beseech you therefore,

brethren,

by the mercies of God,

that ye present your bodies

a living sacrifice,

holy,

acceptable unto God,

which is your reasonable service.

Of course, "therefore" means, "Based on what I have already said, I am about to say this." What he is about to say in Romans 12 is based on something he has already said.

Verse 2 says:

And be not conformed to this world:

but be ye transformed

by the renewing of your mind,

that ye may prove what is that good,

and acceptable,

and perfect,

will of God.

Verse 3 says:

For I say,

through the grace given unto me,

to every man that is among you,

not to think of himself more highly than he ought to think;

but to think soberly,

according as God hath dealt to every man

the measure of faith.

Then he says we have many members in one body, and all members do not have the same office. We are one body in Christ and members one of another, having gifts differing according to the grace given to us.

Is he not talking about a many-membered body called the body of Christ, of which you and I are individual members in particular?

We all have grace differing according to the measure of faith or grace God separated us to. I hold the office I hold, and you hold the office and calling you hold, not by your own choice, but by God's measure and call upon your life.

That is why Paul said you ought not think of yourself more highly than you ought, but soberly, according as God has dealt to every man the measure of faith.

Somehow, by offering myself as a living sacrifice, I am not conformed to the world, but transformed to find not only the good, not only the acceptable, but the absolute perfect will of God for my life.

That good, acceptable, and perfect will is not just the revelation knowledge of His Word. It is the good, acceptable, and perfect will of God for your individual life in the body of Christ.

When God began to open this chapter, my prayer was not because I was afraid of what He meant by offering my body a living sacrifice. All I wanted to know was how to do it. If offering my body a living sacrifice resulted in finding the perfect will of God for my life, the question was not whether I would do it. The question was, "Please tell me how."

Fleshly Self-Denial Cannot Produce a Living Sacrifice

It was not until the word "therefore" opened up to me that I understood how. It means that in the previous chapters, he gave the information for how to offer our bodies.

I found out that offering my body the way the holiness people taught me did not do any good. Through extreme discipline, I quit smoking, quit chewing, quit going with the girls who did, dressed the way they wanted, took the jewelry off, got rid of the television, and did everything they wanted me to do because they told me that was offering myself as a living sacrifice.

Let me tell you something about works of the flesh. Once you begin that program of self-mortification through extreme discipline, there is nowhere to stop. As soon as you put something else off, the devil will come along and condemn you about something else.

There is no place to stop until finally you end up in a monastery somewhere dressed in a spiked girdle and a hairy shirt, sleeping sitting up and not able to speak to anybody because you took a vow of silence.

In holiness, you can deny your flesh all the way down the line until there is nothing left, and then you have to go a step further into asceticism. When self-denial is not enough, you torture the flesh.

When you get into programs of flesh, there is nowhere to stop. You cannot make yourself good enough. I do not care what you deny yourself. You cannot make yourself good enough.

So if there was a way to offer my body a living sacrifice according to God's plan, I wanted to know how.

Romans 8 and Jude Give the Same Key: The Spirit Mortifies Through Prayer

I dug into the previous chapters and found that Romans 8 held the key:

If ye through the Spirit

do mortify the deeds of the body,

ye shall live.

Then he went further and gave me the same key Jude gave me. Jude says you do not have to be sense-ruled; you can build yourself up above that by praying in the Holy Ghost.

Romans 8 says:

Likewise the Spirit also helpeth our infirmities:

for we know not what we should pray for as we ought:

but the Spirit itself maketh intercession for us

with groanings which cannot be uttered.

This is all concerning the weaknesses and mortification of the flesh. He that searches the heart knows the mind of the Spirit, the plan of God, and can successfully make intercession for you according to the will of God.

By offering ourselves as a living sacrifice, we offer ourselves to the Holy Spirit. He is the One who can turn us over to the death of the flesh to work two things: the revelation knowledge of His Word in our spirit, and the supernatural working of the plan of God inside our hearts.

That is true mortification.

The Measure of Faith Contains the Call and All the Cross Provided

God has delivered to every man the measure of faith, and I would be the last one to call God a liar. If He said He has given you a measure of faith, He has given you one.

A measure is part of a whole. These measures are all the different offices in the body of Christ. Each possesses its own measure, and all the measures working together make one complete, devil-stomping, mountain-moving body of Christ.

I only possess one of those measures. You only possess one. Yet He has instilled into every born-again person's spirit the measure of faith.

Contained within that measure is enough faith to appropriate everything the cross says you are, your healings, blessings, ministry, call, all of it. To stand in God's presence and say, "I do not have enough faith," is to call God a liar because He delivered you the measure to fulfill your ministry and appropriate everything the cross redeemed for you.

"I would have gone to Mexico if I had enough money."

Did He tell you to go?

If you stand before Jesus and ask, "Was I supposed to go that year?" and He says yes, then why did the money not come in?

He says, "I sent it, but you did not receive it."

Are you saying you did not have enough faith?

The measure comes in the form of a seed, deposited inside your spirit when you are born again, not of corruptible seed, but incorruptible, by the Word of God.

Your seed is different than mine. A corn seed produces a corn stalk, a wheat seed produces wheat, and a cantaloupe seed produces cantaloupe. The diagramming and programming inside my seed is different from the diagramming and programming inside your seed.

When you pray in the Holy Ghost, out of your innermost being flow rivers of living water. You cannot spend an hour praying in the Holy Ghost without throwing water on that seed. Inside that seed is the diagram and program for your entire life, your ministry, every branch, every facet, every harvest.

You cannot pray hour after hour without that seed beginning to germinate. The stalk comes out, limbs start shooting, leaves start collecting, and pretty soon your first harvest comes. Direction becomes so settled inside your spirit that someone asks, "How do you know that is your call?"

You say, "Does a dog in Oklahoma have fleas? Can a fish swim?"

When the incorruptible seed begins to sprout and produce, it has not only the understanding of faith but also the diagramming and programming for your call and what is to unfold in your life.

Praying in Tongues Begins Transformation and the Unfolding of the Call

This comes forth in two phases.

First is transformation to His Word:

Be not conformed to this world:

but be ye transformed

by the renewing of your mind.

The word transformation carries a supernatural sense, like a transformer on a telephone pole. The voltage comes in at one degree and goes out at another. The Word of God comes into you at one voltage, transforms your life, and comes out at another.

He is talking about something supernatural happening to you as you are transformed, not conformed to the world, but transformed by the renewing of your mind to prove good, acceptable, and perfect will.

Second, the Holy Ghost plants His call in your life, and that begins to unfold.

If transformation takes place from offering your body a living sacrifice, and praying in tongues is definitely one of the keys to offering your body, then what happens when you stand before God and say, "Father, I am going to turn myself over to the Holy Spirit for the next couple of hours for the sole purpose of offering my body a living sacrifice"?

What is really taking place in the world of the Spirit?

In the Old Testament, a living sacrifice started out living and ended up dead. It gave itself all the way to death.

We know He does not want us to die. He wants us to be a living sacrifice. Yet He wants us dead to the things of the world and alive unto God.

You cannot pray five minutes in the Holy Ghost without Him attempting to turn a little bit of your life over as a living sacrifice, so you become more dead to the world and alive to God.

If you spend hours, He begins the mortification process. He begins to turn you over as a living sacrifice, which results in transformation and the finding of His perfect will for your life.

Stammering Lips Bring the Rest and the Weaning From Milk

Isaiah 28:9 says:

Whom shall he teach knowledge?

and whom shall he make to understand doctrine?

them that are weaned from the milk,

and drawn from the breasts.

Verse 10:

For precept must be upon precept,

precept upon precept;

line upon line,

line upon line;

here a little,

and there a little.

Verse 11:

For with stammering lips

and another tongue

will he speak to this people.

Verse 12:

To whom he said,

This is the rest

wherewith ye may cause the weary to rest;

and this is the refreshing.

He asks, "Whom shall we teach knowledge? Whom shall we make understand doctrine?" He answers: those who are weaned from the breast.

Strong meat belongs to those who by reason of use have their senses exercised to discern both good and evil. The man on milk is unskillful in the Word of righteousness or right standing, because he is a baby. He does not know how to use the Word.

He may understand healing is in the Word and belongs to him. He may know prosperity belongs to him. But he cannot get the damnable disease off his body because he is unskillful in the use of the Word of God.

Who can receive line upon line, precept upon precept? Those who are drawn off milk. These are the ones to whom He speaks with stammering lips and another tongue, the supernatural manifestation and gift of the Holy Spirit, through whom He causes the weary to rest.

When I pray in tongues, I speak mysteries before the Father. Because I speak these mysteries before Him, He can pull me aside and teach me line upon line, here a little and there a little, precept upon precept. He can wean me from milk onto strong meat because transformation is taking place by the power of the Holy Spirit instead of through men.

Prayer Purges What Keeps the Soul From Rest

Did you notice He says:

This is the rest...

this is the refreshing.

Are you tired? Are you weary? Do some of you need rest in your souls?

You cannot pray two hours in the Holy Ghost without actively offering your body as a living sacrifice. That means some kind of death process is involved.

It is impossible to grow in God without leaving the world behind. When line upon line and precept upon precept begin, and this weaning from the breast begins, what accompanies it is rest and refreshing.

When I was born again, I entered the kingdom of God, created after God in righteousness and true holiness, but my outer man had not yet conformed to the inner man. I entered the kingdom with boatloads of problems.

I started praying in the Holy Ghost, and He started purging me from things that kept me from rest. It was not until two or three years ago that the final things in my life began to die, and God did for me personally what I did not dream possible. He ushered me into peace.

Outside of receiving the baptism of the Holy Spirit, the most powerful thing I have received from the Lord Jesus Christ is that incredible peace. Nothing has ever come into my life that has made me more unmanageable by the devil.

The devil comes and threatens with everything under the sun, but I have so much peace in my spirit that it makes me unmanageable by him.

Somehow, with stammering lips and another tongue, those hours praying in the Holy Ghost offered my body as a living sacrifice and purged me from the things keeping me from rest.

The First Rest Came Through Long, Uncomfortable Prayer

I first noticed it when I was praying long hours on the riverbed in Oakridge, Oregon. I was so bored praying that I would kick green rocks. Then I started picking red leaves off trees. I just kept praying hour after hour in those days because I was inexperienced in it.

After six or seven months, I started getting lost in a place in Him. It was incredible.

A long-term effect of constant pressure on your mind will begin to affect it. Doctors say a large percentage of ailments in the body are caused by pressures, worries, cares, torments, and fears. Your body will go haywire. Your mind, being submitted to pressure over a long term, will start breaking down.

One time I had a $400 tax refund check, the biggest amount of money I had ever seen in my life. I went to the bank to sign my name on the back and forgot my name.

But I noticed something peaceful beginning to insert an influence on my mind. I kept praying in the Holy Ghost.

The more I prayed in the Holy Ghost, the more I entered into that rest and refreshing.

Through praying the mysteries and offering our bodies as living sacrifices by the power of the Holy Spirit, purging begins to take place.

Jesus said:

Every branch that beareth fruit,

he purgeth it,

that it may bring forth more fruit.

Are you saved? Then you are bearing fruit. I have good news for you: He will purge you so you bear more fruit.

He went on to say:

Now ye are clean

through the word which I have spoken unto you.

The purging process is through the Word. You cannot pray in the Holy Ghost and speak the mysteries contained within the Word without the Word of God coming alive in you when God starts answering your prayer in the Holy Ghost. Then the purging process starts.

Many Fall Out Because Prayer Begins to Touch What They Want to Keep

In those early days, I prayed myself to a pretty good place of peace. I found myself sleeping in halls and in my car during the first few months of ministry. I spent so much time ministering that I probably did not have time to worry much.

Because I prayed myself to a place of peace, I wanted everybody to have it. When God started using me somewhat in miracles, people were attracted to the ministry. They said, "We would like to have what you have."

I said, "I can tell you what I do. I play Kenneth Hagin and Kenneth Copeland tapes, spend the day praying in tongues, then walk out and pray for people, and things start happening."

They asked, "How much time do you spend in prayer?"

I told them, "Sometimes an hour a day, sometimes six hours, sometimes all day, depending on what I am doing."

The next two, three, four, five years around Oregon, I had a whole bunch of people praying in the Holy Ghost. I thought every one of them I launched into prayer would walk out as a spiritual giant working miracles. But fewer did that than did not.

If ten people called to ministry got turned on to prayer, perhaps three would continue with it, and seven would fall out and choose some other avenue to try to grow in God.

The way they fell out was what I could not understand. One day they were testifying, "I have never seen anything like this," and we were all shouting. Then suddenly they disappeared. The next thing I heard, they were still preaching, but they were preaching about how fanatical I was over praying in tongues.

Some even said, "I did what Roberson said, and do you think my anointing got better? It got worse. You can pray too much in tongues."

They were right in one sense. Their anointing did get less, and they turned on me.

For years, I did not understand why they were getting so strange. I knew I had rough times, but I stuck it out because the Word says if you build yourself up on your most holy faith, praying in the Holy Ghost, then you build yourself up.

I do not care what my emotions say. If the Word says it, I choose to believe the Word.

The Spirit Purges What We Do Not Want to Release

I was with Rick Renner when a man came to the tape table and said, "I want to be a mighty intercessor, but I know a lady who prayed too much in tongues and got weird. What do I do to keep from getting weird?"

I could not take it anymore. I stepped between them and said, "Look, friend, if you get weird, it is because you already had a capacity to be weird."

When we turn ourselves over as a living sacrifice, including this key of prayer, what makes you think everything God wants to purge out of you is something you want to get rid of?

Most of us want God's great anointing. We want our pie and want to eat it too.

"God, leave me alone. Just give me Your glory to bring more glory to what I am doing. You can purge this, but leave these areas alone."

In this process of offering yourself as a living sacrifice, you turn yourself over to the Holy Spirit. He is not conforming you to the world. Transformation is taking place to find the good, acceptable, and perfect will of God for your personal life.

The Holy Ghost will attempt to purge everything out of you as He turns you over, not only the things you want to get rid of, but the things you do not want to get rid of.

The Word Lights the Ground So Stones and Thorns Can Be Removed

Mark 4 says the Word was sown on wayside ground, stony ground, thorny ground, and good ground.

In the wayside ground, Satan came immediately and took it away. In stony ground, it took root for a little while, but the roots went outward instead of down because they hit stones. The stalk shot up, and when persecutions and afflictions came, it fell because it had no root.

The stones were already resident when the seed was sown. The thorns were already resident in the ground when the seed was sown into thorny ground. The cares of this life, deceitfulness of riches, and lusts of other things sprang up and choked the Word, but the thorn seeds were already resident when the Word landed.

Then He talked about good ground:

These are they which are sown on good ground;

such as hear the word,

and receive it,

and bring forth fruit,

some thirtyfold,

some sixty,

and some an hundred.

Then He said:

Is a candle brought to be put under a bushel,

or under a bed?

and not to be set on a candlestick?

For there is nothing hid,

which shall not be manifested.

He was not typing the ground to say it had no chance. He was saying what made the ground good was that the Word was sown as a light in darkness, meant to illuminate everything hidden in darkness so you could purge it out of your life and produce thirty, sixty, and a hundredfold.

But not everything the Holy Spirit wants to purge is something we want to get rid of.

Critical Mass Comes When Prayer Confronts the Thorn Seeds

The first things He purges us from are in the progression Jesus gave: persecutions and afflictions, then cares of this life, then deceitfulness of riches, and finally lusts of other things.

The first things praying in the Holy Ghost does is edify you and make you strong enough that persecution and affliction do not take the Word from you anymore. The people on your job can treat you ugly, and it does not bother you like it used to. You have decided to put your feet on the rock, and hell or high water, that stuff is not going to make you backslide.

Then He works on the slow choking process of monotony, the cares of this world. You have heard a thousand messages, seen services, and gotten used to the work. But praying in the Holy Ghost will purge you past the cares of this world taking you. You remain excited because you light your own furnace.

Then comes the deceitfulness of riches. As long as money owns you, you are deceived by it.

Someone says, "I do not have to worry about that because I do not have any."

More men have been destroyed chasing money than by having it. If God called you to win Mexico, He gave you the faith to win it. If you stand before God and complain, "I could not do it because I did not have money," He will say, "You were deceived, because contained within your measure was all the money you ever needed to believe in."

As long as there is a thorn seed inside you that will rise up and choke the Word, as long as money can own you, you cannot get money from God rightly.

When the Holy Ghost performs transformation, He demands every area of your life.

After months or years of praying, depending on how deep-seated those thorn seeds are, the Holy Ghost begins to rise up inside you and exert influence on your mind:

"Who owns you? Are you Mine? Would you please give Me the key to that door you have never allowed Me in?"

You say, "No, I am not going to give You that key, but I do want to continue to pray."

Every day you continue to pray, He approaches your spirit and asks again:

"Would you please give Me the key to that inner sanctuary? Is everything you have Mine? Is your security in Me?"

You start getting annoyed. It does not come as a voice, but emotions and fear declare war against your mind. The more you pray, the more emotional you get, because you cannot continue in prayer without Him confronting you.

You go into prayer feeling up, but instead of feeling more anointed, you feel more down. You say, "I come to church and pray, and I leave feeling worse."

Why?

Because something in you is declaring war. It does not want to turn loose, and the Holy Spirit is demanding that you turn loose.

The Emotional War Is the Final Death Throe, Not a Reason to Quit

The lusts of other things are serious. Self-exaltation, complacency, indifference, they are serious.

It is easy to get purged from persecutions and afflictions to the place where you make up your mind to go to church and stay saved no matter what your relatives think. But those self-exaltation realms are another matter.

Our gift becomes a servant to the kingdom instead of a vocation. Otherwise we say, "I cannot preach at your church because it does not exalt my ministry. I can only minister where it benefits my call and builds my ministry."

When the Holy Ghost starts picking on self-exaltation, you say, "Holy Ghost, why can You not just pray through me and leave me alone?"

Uncontrolled anger starts rising. You have prayed six months and thought you were supposed to be more edified, but you feel less edified and more on edge because you do not want to turn loose.

Someone is sitting in your chair when you come in, and you say, "I could win the whole city through prayer except for one problem: you will not get out of my chair."

You may think, "I have been praying a year, and I am more of an emotional mess than before I began."

That is because you were more carnal about the things that were wrong with you before. When the purging process begins, it is something.

Some of the strongest things purged out of me in recent years were intimidation and fear of men. They blocked me from walking in a higher degree of peace than I had known before.

I have learned what the purging process is about. When I hit one of those things, it is not time to run. It is time to run into the closet.

The worse I feel about praying, the more I am accomplishing by doing it. When my emotions are fighting me hard, when something inside me does not want to die and I do not want to go to prayer, and I do not even want to want to go to prayer, that is when the Holy Spirit has pulled character flaws in front of me for personal examination.

When they are fighting you that hard emotionally, that is not the time to fall out of prayer. Even though you do not feel like you are producing anything, you are producing more than when you are free-flowing.

The free-flowing times build you spiritually and edify you to the place where He can pull what is wrong with you out and put it to death. The final death throe is that emotional war at the end of a season of prayer. That is not the time to quit.

That is the time to jump in and say, "I am not moved by what I see, hear, or feel. I am only moved by what I believe. If this is causing my death to transform me to the good, acceptable, and perfect will, then let us get on with it."

If you stay in, you will come out the other side, and He will bring you into a degree in God you have never walked in before, because something that had you bound has been put to death. The rest of your life will be different.

Charlie Stayed in Prayer When He Hit Meltdown Point

Charlie had known me for years and used to come to my revivals. When we started the center, he moved to Tulsa.

"God sent me to Tulsa."

"What for, Charlie?"

"To attend the prayer center."

When it started as a church, he was elated. "Now I understand why God sent me."

Charlie was indoctrinated to prayer. He started coming to regular prayer sessions two or three times a week in the other building and once a week here. Transformation was taking place. Little things were dying.

He always had a lot of zeal, but not quite enough zeal to put those final things to death that he knew in his heart were not right.

As Charlie prayed, the Holy Ghost began communicating with him by pulling his lifestyle out for examination. He started counting the cost. An emotional war started taking place.

Pretty soon he crossed over the line. He came storming into my office:

"God has called me to go over to this other church."

Changing your circumstances does not purge you from your own sin. You have to purge yourself from your problem. You can go somewhere for a year where they do not say anything that convicts you, and they accept your lifestyle like it is, but God is not really going to use you.

I threw my Bible down on the table and said, "Charlie, you have hit it. You are at critical mass. You are at meltdown point. For God's sake, do not stop now."

He turned back and forth, really straining and fighting. Then all of a sudden he said, "I am going to do it."

Right at critical mass, instead of going the other way and falling out of prayer, he ran into the church. He is still doing it. He works all night, so now he spends the whole morning in prayer.

He reached the place where the Holy Ghost declared war on it. Either you quit praying or you let Him purge it. You cannot do both.

At critical mass, many run somewhere that quits putting pressure on them. But why go from place to place and have a mediocre life? Why pass through your generation and make no difference?

God wants to purge the things that are keeping you from fulfilling His absolute perfect will. He will do it if you offer your body as a living sacrifice on the altar God prepared for it, and that altar is the Holy Spirit transforming you to the Word.

A Thorn Seed of Intimidation Tried to Pull Him Out of Prayer

For seven years, I had been in and out of praying in the Holy Ghost. I hit those critical mass times. Sometimes they took me out of prayer for a long time, then I would go back in. I would come up to that place again, get out again, then get back in.

Finally, after a time of fasting, I made it to phase two. God introduced me to phase two in an incredible way. I will never forget walking into the service and my banker falling down, unable to move for twenty minutes. The next night or two, I tried to reach the line and pray for people, and they started falling over. One time when I was generally praying over the crowd, people in the crowd started falling down.

I said, "God, You have done something. You have entered me into another phase."

I ministered for a year or two in this phase and started being asked to camp meetings. I got to speak with men I would have paid hundreds of dollars just to hold their sweaty coats in the back room. It was a wonderful phase, and I got caught up and had the time of my life.

Then, a couple of years into it, something entered my life and attached itself to me, and I did not know what it was. Evidently there was something in my life it could attach itself to: intimidation, fears, torments, and insecurity. I had not been able to reach it.

I used to get around well-known ministers in back rooms and fish until they complimented me because of my insecurity.

This pressure kept increasing year after year. The only relief I could get was to not even act like I was going to pray. If I gave up prayer, I was under less pressure.

The pressure wanted me to watch television all day long. It was not there if I buried myself in the television. It was not there if I did not pray. It was not there if I did not get into the Word and meditate.

But when I would shake myself, fire myself up, and begin to pray, that pressure would wear me back down, and I would quit again.

At the same time it increased, it pressured me to do more wrong in every area. Then a voice came and started preaching grace to me, ultimate grace:

"Go on. You can do that. After all, is God not still using you?"

Yes, He still used me. Every time I walked out in the camp meeting, the power of God would fall. That voice would preach, "It is okay. It is all by grace, not by works lest any man should boast. If you get into all that praying and stuff, that is just works."

Whatever this thing was, it attached itself to a thorn seed. Until I killed the thorn seed, I could not get rid of it. I would run it off for a while, then it would wear me down again until I would lie down in a motel and give up.

I watched many of my friends being destroyed, and this pressure was increasing on me. I knew the same thing had come to them because it was pressuring me to go in the same direction many of them went.

It was not until I was preaching at the Mile High Conference for Marilyn and Wally Hickey, looking at the deaf section, that I woke up and said, "I cannot take this anymore."

When many of my friends went the way of that pressure, it shocked me so badly that instead of going with the pressure anymore, I entered into more fasting and more prayer until the thorn seed of intimidation, fears, and worries began to disroot.

The more I continued, the more it was put to death. After one seeking time, I came out with the peace of God, and He said, "This is what you have been looking for, son. This will make you more unmanageable by the devil than anything that has ever come."

Revival Is Guarded Because the Enemy Cannot Afford the Harvest

Because of the deep-seated peace He embedded inside my spirit, I know there is a realm where revival lives, a tremendous outpouring of the Spirit. The devil is enraged with it. He hates anyone pressing for that realm.

He hates anyone who reports for constant prayer and keeps hammering at it until they hit those places, put them behind them, press on into God, get rid of complacency, crucify the flesh, and keep moving until God crucifies the stones and thorns and lifts them higher and higher.

Then He can bring us to a place where He can pour His Holy Ghost out on us the way He wants to.

This peace tells me there is a realm of revival, a realm for the hundred people in wheelchairs in church on Sunday and the thousand mothers who bring their deaf and non-speaking children in.

Why are they not lining up in the big camp meetings we have now? Because nothing happened the last year they had one.

Let me tell you what the devil is camping on. He loves to pick on your flesh, irritate you, keep you out of prayer, and get you mad at each other over little strife and second-rate, expendable things.

You pray awhile and then get mad because of where someone is sitting. It is not the person next to you who has the problem. It is you. You are at critical mass.

The devil likes to keep us at a low-level operation of fighting, strife, and being out of prayer.

Encamped on the perimeter of revival are principalities and powers. They know that if fifty or a hundred people jump out of wheelchairs, then they will have to deal with every relative of those people. They will have to deal with hundreds of thousands of people affected because a thousand deaf and non-speaking children walked out of the meeting talking.

It is not because they hate the people so much that they block the healing. They block the healing because they cannot afford the revival.

A Life of Prayer Can Still Make a Difference in This Generation

What do we do? We sit year after year in complacency. We have good worship, good songs, encourage each other, and go home. We face the devil, but we are not conquerors.

Many times we are so self-centered that we never get past the idea of our own life receiving from God. That is good, but how about a living sacrifice?

You do not have to pass through this generation without making a difference.

"My name is Mary Wallpaper. Nobody pays attention to me."

Nobody paid any attention to Anna either. She prayed and prayed, and the priest did not even know who she was. But she said, "My eyes have beheld the promise." What an honor God bestowed on that woman.

You say your life cannot make a difference? Oh yes, it can.
