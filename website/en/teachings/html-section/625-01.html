625-01 Fullness of Power, Joy, and Judgment in the Early Church

How many would know that we would be on Ananias and Sapphira?

In this teaching, there were two things in the early church that we have not really seen since, at least not to that magnitude.

One was the fullness of power they operated in. We will touch on that. How would you like to bring all who were demon possessed and all who were sick, and have some man of God, some apostle or something, walk out and every single one of them be healed and delivered?

When is the last time you saw that?

So there were two things in position. One was that something came that transformed them like that. Second was the absolute manifestation of the fullness of His power. And the third thing present was instantaneous judgment. Not after a long time, but instantaneous. They would speak it, and it would happen.

The reason it happened was not that God is a respecter of persons. It happened because He found what He needed to do it with when the mantles came to absolute fullness in the establishment of the early church.

The third part I had been trying to catch and was not quite catching, I finally caught. What caused all those Jews, three thousand the first day of Pentecost, and after the lame man over forty years old was healed, another five thousand added that day?

There had to be nine, ten, eleven, twelve thousand Jews in Solomon's porch just living, worshiping, considering nothing their own, sitting day and night at the feet of the twelve who had slept and eaten with Jesus and heard every parable He ever spoke.

Day and night, they considered nothing their own.

I thought, What is the fullness of that? I wanted to know. If the miracles were at their fullest, if all the demon possessed from the cities round about were healed at their fullest, what else was at its fullness?

What could cause a bunch of people like that to give everything they had with no fear?

The Fullness of Joy Removes Fear and Makes Total Surrender Possible

Finally, that anointing that swept in in Brazil and touched down, I have never seen anything quite like it. When it touched me, it did something on the inside of me.

What came to fullness was no fear, no insecurities. The only place I have been able to find that is where that extreme joy is. "The joy of the Lord is my strength." Perfect love casts out fear. "This is My peace."

When that touched down on me, I could very easily declare nothing my own with absolutely no fear.

So now I know that His joy was also to the fullness. If there is anything that ever gets God's people to do anything, this is it. We have tried to preach them into it and threaten them into it, but I am telling you, there is a place in His joy where total surrender is accomplished.

You cannot stand it in the middle of everything.

The joy of the Lord is what? My strength.

If it is my strength, what is trying to come and take it? How about a celebration when nothing is trying to be taken from you, much less when it is?

Good land.

We knew they had all things in common. We knew they saw miracles they had never seen before. We knew the mantle on them was at its fullness. We knew judgment was instantaneous.

That is why Peter was able to judge Ananias and Sapphira. He found what he needed to do it with.

Was it a special act of God? No, it was not. If God can reestablish that mantle, then you are going to see a whole lot of special acts of God in these last days as the church is getting ready to go home.

Let me ask you a question. Recently there was a ministry in town that believed a certain way and finally came forward with it. He had a big exodus from his church, went controversial, and was marked by several preachers in town because he began to preach an all-inclusive gospel, that everybody will be saved when it is all over.

So the only benefit of being saved is that you can pray, walk with God, and have answered prayer. For the sinner, he has to wait until he dies, and then all redemption is done, and God will resurrect him.

Do you know what would have happened to him if Peter or Paul was in town? There would be a lot of people who would not believe that right now.

How far would that doctrine have gotten? Nowhere.

How many people would have been spared because of that? How many people are going to hell because we are not reaching them?

Would God reach them if He could? Yes. Unfortunately, you are the only thing He has to work with.

If one of those guys was in town, everybody would walk on eggshells. They would get accountants and straighten their books up real quick.

What happened to Hymenaeus and Alexander? Paul said they had turned aside to vain jangling. They desired to be teachers of the law. They were destroying the foundation of the church. Paul said, in a manner of speaking, while having a cup of tea one day, "I turned them over to Satan so they would learn not to blaspheme."

I am thinking, You what?

I particularly like the one in 1 Corinthians who had his father's wife. I love the way Paul said, "A little leaven leaveneth the whole lump."

We thought God just got real mad because of the adultery. If it were not spreading through the church, He would have just dealt with that guy. But when Paul said little leaven leavens the whole lump, he meant the church was weak and in danger of receiving that lifestyle as okay.

So he says, "I am with you in my spirit." Paul did not kind of translate there. He said, "I am able to make a judgment from here because I am with you and I understand it. Turn this man over to Satan for the destruction of his flesh, that his spirit might be saved in the day of the Lord."

Paul had instantaneous judgment on Paphos. The deputy wanted to hear the gospel, so they sent for Paul and Barnabas. There was a Jewish sorcerer by the name of Bar-Jesus who withstood Paul. Paul said, "How about being blind for a season? Would you like that?"

It was a season, so he would come out of it. Someone had to lead him away by the hand. But guess what? The deputy was born again.

This sounds rough. I know. But one thing God is doing is this: when He can find what He needs, the fullness of many of those things is going to come back to the foundation of the church.

You can bet this about God: when it comes to judgment and those kinds of things, the feeble attempts preachers have made are usually for their own self-exaltation. That is not the kind of mercy that rejoices against judgment and qualifies.

Special Acts of God Do Not Explain What the Early Church Mantle Actually Did

Like most preachers, Tim, I have an excellent set of Greek books to access the original language. Unfortunately, sometimes the Greek version tells the story just as generically as the King James and gives no particular insight into why judgment would come on Ananias and Sapphira so suddenly over something most preachers would esteem trivial and insignificant, such as lying.

Most commentators console themselves with words like "special acts of God," not knowing how to reconcile what happened to Ananias and Sapphira with the morals of the modern-day church.

The general conclusion of most commentaries is that they were set forth as some kind of special example, a sort of one-time thing. But if that is the case, what have we learned?

The Fire Burns Works, but the Person Can Still Be Saved

Kick in around verse 9. Paul wanted to speak to them as spiritual, but he could not because they were not able to bear it. He gives an order here:

For we are labourers together with God: ye are God's husbandry, ye are God's building.

According to the grace of God which is given unto me, as a wise masterbuilder, I have laid the foundation,

and another buildeth thereon.

But let every man take heed how he buildeth thereupon.

For other foundation can no man lay than that is laid, which is Jesus Christ.

Each one of you is a lively stone in the building being laid on the foundation of what Paul preached, which is Jesus Christ crucified. Every one of your lives is making a contribution as a lively stone in that building. That is why he gives this warning:

Now if any man build upon this foundation gold, silver, precious stones, wood, hay, stubble;

Destroying the Foundation Is Different From Having Works Burned Away

Verse 15 again:

If any man's work shall be burned, he shall suffer loss:

but he himself shall be saved; yet so as by fire.

The final act of mercy is that all that stuff is burned away, and salvation holds because you believed He saved you and you were born again.

Verse 16 says:

Know ye not that ye are the temple of God, and that the Spirit of God dwelleth in you?

If any man defile the temple of God, him shall God destroy;

for the temple of God is holy, which temple ye are.

In holiness circles, they always preached that about smoking: "Know ye not that you are God's temple, and the Spirit dwells in you? If you destroy this temple, God will destroy you." So we preached smoking, drinking, chewing. That is how illuminated we were.

I used to make those big loggers mad in the break shack. I did not have much wisdom.

They said, "You mean smoking will send us to hell?"

I said, "Maybe not, but it makes you smell like you have been there."

He said, "You are God's temple, and if you defile it, him will God destroy." But he was not talking about this physical temple singularly, although it is included. He was talking first about Paul laying the foundation, which is none other than Jesus Christ crucified, and then you as lively stones making your contribution, whatever it is and whatever your calling is.

The one who destroys the temple is the person who destroys the foundation and takes Jesus crucified out of it. Him will God destroy because he removed salvation.

But you can have a lot of trash, a lot of wrong believing, a lot of wrong teaching, a lot of lying, and all that kind of stuff going on. It will all be burned. But if you love Jesus and are born again, He will burn that up, and you shall be saved, yet so as by fire.

So do not tell me Ananias and Sapphira went to hell.
