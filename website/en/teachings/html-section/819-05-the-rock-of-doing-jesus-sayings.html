819-05 The Rock of Doing Jesus' Sayings

Meditation Digs Into the Counsel That Lets a House Stand

We are beginning these messages on meditation with the theme from Psalm 1:1, which we probably will be doing in every service:

Blessed is the man that walketh not in the counsel of the ungodly, nor standeth in the way of sinners, nor sitteth in the seat of the scornful. But his delight is in the law of the Lord; and in His law doth he meditate day and night.

Once again, we found out what qualifies us to be that blessed man of God: if we do not take our counsel from the world, if we do not do things the way sinners do, and if we delight ourselves in His Word through meditation to extract the counsel we need to walk by, so we do not have to do things the way the world does.

Meditation is not study, memorization, or reading, although they all assist meditation. Meditation is when the Holy Spirit gets involved with your spirit in such a way as to extract that counsel from the Word, to take the same meaning that left the heart of God and reconstruct it in your heart in such a form that you can walk by it.

That is the true form of meditation.

How many understood more about that last night after the message?

We are going to get into the actual breaking of verses again. Once again, we have no trouble identifying Jesus as the Master Teacher, the greatest who ever lived. If He were teaching at this pulpit, using the mastery of words, by the time He finished He would have started painting a picture at the top of this pulpit. By the time He reached the bottom, He would have painted a perfect picture of this pulpit within your spirit.

Therefore, every verse is a thought within itself. It is a stroke of the brush, but invariably it is always building toward the completion of the entire picture.

It has been us who have been guilty of taking verses out of context. If I were to take a corn seed, inside of which is the diagramming and programming for a stalk, leaves, and two ears of corn on the average, and fragment it into four pieces, then plant the fragments hoping for a greater harvest, I would get nothing. The reason I would get nothing is because I destroyed the image on the inside of the seed.

Faith comes by hearing, not your pets, not your preconceived ideas, not your theology, not your doctrine. Faith comes by hearing, and hearing by the Word of God.

Glory to God.

If we refuse to take verses out of context, we will come up with what we came up with yesterday morning. Many times the meaning of each individual verse changes when you find out all they are is part of the whole.

We make meditation very difficult for the Holy Spirit by taking a verse out of the middle. We are asking for a miracle, and He can do it. God can take that singular verse and paint the whole picture around it, but that is the exception and not the rule. The rule is for you to meditate on the entire picture until the Holy Spirit can take that last verse and stroke it in.

So we try one verse when we should be meditating the whole image. The Holy Spirit always alerts me to the beginning of the subject and the end.

How many know Luke 6 is the conclusion of His tremendous message, the Sermon on the Mount? Tremendous message. He wound it up with this statement about doing His sayings. He said, "The man that does My sayings, I will show you to whom he is like."

We might as well begin right there, Luke 6:47. I am starting at the end of this, because how could I possibly preach all of the Beatitudes? We just have to demonstrate principles. We will take the last part, from about the twenty-seventh verse over to the forty-ninth verse, just to demonstrate meditation and principles. We would have to go back and take a week or two, as you saw yesterday morning.

Hallelujah.

The Rock Is Not Only Jesus but Doing What Jesus Said

Luke 6:47:

Whosoever cometh to Me, and heareth My sayings, and doeth them, I will shew you to whom he is like: He is like a man which built an house, and digged deep, and laid the foundation on a rock: and when the flood arose, the stream beat vehemently upon that house, and could not shake it: for it was founded upon a rock.

Notice this. Whosoever comes to Me, hears My sayings, and does them, I will show you what he is like. He is like a man who dug deep and laid a foundation on the rock.

You hear people preaching an awful lot about the rock being Jesus Christ. Well, the rock is Him. It is Him. But the foundational rock He is talking about here is doing Jesus' sayings. There are a lot of people on the Rock who have not laid a foundation on the rock. Therefore, anything that comes down that would take any sinner out takes them out: diseases, pain, anything else. When their storm arises, it takes them out because they are not founded on the rock of doing His sayings.

Jesus said, "Let Me show you to whom this man is like, the one who does them. He is like a man who dug deep."

If the rock is doing Jesus' sayings, then what would the deep digging be? The man would be digging deep through meditation into the revelation knowledge of His words so that he could do them. He would be digging deep into His sayings, into His words, through meditation, so he would have that counsel to walk by.

He says he dug deep and laid his foundation on a rock. When the storm came, Jesus did not tell you that you would not have a storm. As a matter of fact, He guaranteed that you would.

How many of you have lived your Christian life without one storm?

He said when the storm arose, it beat vehemently upon the house and could not shake it because it was founded on this rock. It could not shake it. It does not matter how bad the storm gets. What He said would come. He guaranteed you that if you did His sayings, your house would not go down the river.

This made me want to do His sayings probably more than any other time in my life when I first started discovering what He was talking about here.

Of course, coming from an ultra-holiness background, I had my favorite sayings of Jesus:

The works that I do shall ye do also; and greater works than these shall ye do; because I go unto My Father.

That was one of my favorite sayings. I would have liked to have done that one. Another favorite saying:

Whosoever shall say unto this mountain, Be thou removed, and be thou cast into the sea...

But when my house kept being shaken and shifted around, and I came out of the trials worse off than when I went in, I thought, "This is not right. I am supposed to come out at least equal."

I said, "But I am doing Your work, raising the dead, praying for the sick, trying to do the greater works. I am doing all Your sayings. How come I am having such trouble?"

In those days, He was teaching me how to meditate. He said, "Roberson, when are you going to quit taking that verse out of context?"

My favorite answer in those days was, "How about now? How about right now?"

He said, "Okay. I will show you what sayings I am talking about. There is no use going to another part of the Word of God and extracting any sayings you want. The sayings I am talking about pertain to My message here."

I did not have to back up very far to find out what sayings He was talking about. We will take it from around verse 27.

Loving Enemies Is One of the Sayings That Builds on the Rock

Luke 6:27:

But I say unto you which hear, Love your enemies, do good to them which hate you, bless them that curse you, and pray for them which despitefully use you. And unto him that smiteth thee on the one cheek offer also the other; and him that taketh away thy cloak forbid not to take thy coat also. Give to every man that asketh of thee; and of him that taketh away thy goods ask them not again.

Is this not one of His sayings that He is talking about? "Whosoever does My sayings"--is not this one of His sayings?

I said, "Now wait a minute. Let us go back to 'the works that I do shall you do also, and greater works than these.' What do You mean, love your enemies? That is one of My sayings? You think I am going to love my enemies, bless people that curse me, pray for people that despitefully use me, and he that asks me and sues me for my coat, I am going to give him my cloak also? Are You kidding? When I am done with him, he will have to move to Sioux City. You think I am going to do all that? Forget it. Nobody I know does that."

Bless them that curse you. Pray for those that despitefully use you.

Do you know why He says bless them that curse you? You can curse somebody from a distance, so bless them. But the reason you pray for somebody who despitefully uses you is because they are on your doorstep.

"You think I am going to do that? Forget it."

If you are not, would you please do me one favor? I used to run around protecting God's reputation, and I found out He did not need my help. He has His own lawyer, called the Holy Ghost. He has offered to be my lawyer, but He will not present my case unless I do it on the information He has gathered. If I do not want to present the case on the information He gathered, then I am on my own.

"I am not going to bless them that curse me."

Okay, then when your house goes down the river, please do not blame my Father. Please.

I certainly was not going to do those things.

Let me show you something. I am going to be jumping back and forth to show you how in harmony these verses are, because what we are studying is more the method of extracting revelation knowledge than even the revelation knowledge contained here itself.

Go back near the end, this time to verse 43:

For a good tree bringeth not forth corrupt fruit; neither doth a corrupt tree bring forth good fruit. For every tree is known by his own fruit. For of thorns men do not gather figs, nor of a bramble bush gather they grapes. A good man out of the good treasure of his heart bringeth forth that which is good; and an evil man out of the evil treasure of his heart bringeth forth that which is evil: for of the abundance of the heart his mouth speaketh. And why call ye Me, Lord, Lord, and do not the things which I say?

Is this not interesting? He says every tree is known by his own fruit. This is singular. It is talking about you. It is talking about me.

We can boil this down any way we want. He said the way you are going to be known is by the fruit you are bearing. The way you know what a seed is, is the manner that comes up after it. You are going to be known by your fruit. Whether we like that or not, that is a spiritual law. You simply will be known by your fruit.

You do not gather grapes off a bramble bush. If you want grapes, you have to plant grapes. If you get brambles, it is because you planted brambles, and you cannot blame anybody else.

This is what happens to us:

"I am a faith man."

"Oh, you are? What is your confession?"

"I am complete in Him. I walk in love. I am the righteousness of God in Christ. I am this, and I am that."

Then somebody does you wrong.

"Throw him!"

Later, your finances go crazy. Your body gets sick.

"I am a Word man. Watch me handle this."

You walk up to your bush, reach under, ready to grab a cluster of grapes, and stick your finger on a thorn.

"Who put that thorn on my grape bush? Who did it?"

You did, when you had the opportunity to do His sayings. He said, "Love your enemies. Do good to them that hate you. Pray for them that despitefully use you. Him that would smite you on one cheek, turn the other. Give to them that ask. If a man takes your cloak, give him your coat also."

For every action, there is a reaction. When that confrontation came to you, you sowed one manner of seed or you sowed the other.

Before we get too far into this, I want to thank God for Jesus Christ the Advocate. As soon as you realize what you are doing, the Advocate, Jesus Christ the righteous, goes between as our lawyer. When you confess it, He not only wipes the seed out, He wipes the harvest out and even destroys the ground it grew in. You would have to replant that thing.

This is what irritates the devil so badly. He can build a well-planned case against you for two years, plotting your failure, and in ten seconds you can take advantage of the Advocate and wipe out two years of work. He really has been defeated, if you can only realize that.

You have to make up your mind to walk in His principles and His truths.

When I first got onto this, I thought, "Wow. This is really something. It would not be worth the cost not to walk in it. I do not want my house going down the river."

If there is anything I want to be on God's green earth, it is a blessed man of God, a man who has and walks in His counsel. I cannot afford not to do these things. I love my family too much. I love you too much to afford not to walk in these principles.

Resisting the Devil Requires Corresponding Actions of Love

This started getting exciting. When I say exciting, I mean exciting.

"I say unto you which hear, love your enemies."

I made up my mind that anybody who ever did me wrong, I was going to love them. Of course, I certainly did not want to get involved with them. God forbid. If they sat on one side of the church, I would sit on the other, but He knew I was praying for them.

"Oh God, You know I am not going to be one of them in strife. I am confessing that I love so-and-so. I am not going to be like those who get in strife, because I resist the devil and he flees from me."

As time went on, it did not get better. It got worse. In fact, they got uglier. Everything got uglier.

I thought, "This is supposed to work, is it not? I am loving my enemy. I am praying for him. I am resisting the devil. I broke his hold over me."

The Lord said, "When are you going to quit taking My verses out of context?"

"How about now?"

He said, "Humble yourself under the mighty hand of God and resist the devil. He will flee from you."

Humble means totally teachable. Humble yourself by casting the whole of your care on Him, by casting it onto the Word. Then He said, "Resist the devil in the faith." Faith comes by hearing, and hearing by the Word.

I said, "God, how do You want me to resist the devil? I am doing it. Satan, get off him. Get off me."

When I went into my seeking time, I found out my nature. I did not want the devil to get off him for his sake. I wanted him off him for my sake. I did not want him doing me wrong. It was dirty, ugly, ugly. It was not him I was concerned about. It was me. I wanted the devil off him so he would get off me.

That is Old Testament kind of love: love your neighbor as yourself. The law said an eye for an eye, a tooth for a tooth. The only reason I did not pluck your eye out and kill your dog is because under the law you could do the same to me. It is not that I liked you. I liked me. So I would not do unto you, so you would not do unto me. That was the law that restrained the flesh. The punishment was simply worse than the sin. Adultery? Die. Homosexuality? Die. So you really had to count the cost.

At the end of this covenant, He says, "Love your brother as I have loved you," which is the supernatural kind of love. I found out it comes from within and is regardless of circumstances. It is a love that seeks not its own, no paychecks, no rewards rendered for services rendered. It is a debt I cannot pay. I do not care how many payments of love I make to you. No matter how you react to them, I cannot pay that payment in full because Jesus put me in debt to you to love.

This is getting interesting.

When it says resist the devil in the faith, I said, "I am."

He said, "No, faith without actions is dead."

"But I am."

"No, faith without corresponding actions is dead."

I said, "But I am resisting him. I am praying."

He said, "No. I said do good to them that hate you and pray for those that despitefully use you."

"You mean I am supposed to get involved with them?"

Do good.

Did you know that when there was some old preacher who just hated my guts, but nobody would say anything about it, when I took that corresponding action--not just saying I would break Satan's hold, but taking corresponding action and starting to send that preacher offerings--pretty soon he said, "Who did that?"

That corresponding action with my faith, with my confession, began to tear the wall down and eventually brought total healing.

One time I went to a church that had split. I am not talking about the many times, but this one particular time, because I have been to hundreds of them. The group that split off from the church was meeting in a house, and they wanted me to come over there in the mornings and go to the other ones at night.

I said, "No, I can fix this."

I started preaching on walking in love. When we were done with them, this side baked cakes and brought them to the other side. Restitution was made, and the church joined back together.

He says, "Do good to them that hate you. Pray for those that despitefully use you."

There are corresponding actions to my faith.

"Well, I am not going to do all that stuff, Roberson."

Of course you are not. But once again, if you do not equip the Holy Spirit to present your case, then He does not work on your exterior circumstances. He works on you. When He gets you to the place, then He works on the exterior. It does not work the reverse. If you are the one who needs changing, you are the one He is working on. Then He will take care of the storm.

That is some good stuff.

"What are you preaching on, Roberson?"

Faith.

How come you are so quiet? Are you having a good time?

I do not want to reach underneath my grape bush and stick my finger on a thorn. He said the curse causeless will not come.

Giving and Mercy Are Promised Great Reward in This Life

Let us go on. Verse 29:

And unto him that smiteth thee on the one cheek offer also the other; and him that taketh away thy cloak forbid not to take thy coat also. Give to every man that asketh of thee; and of him that taketh away thy goods ask them not again.

That is actually talking about suing there: ask not again.

He went on to say:

And as ye would that men should do to you, do ye also to them likewise. For if ye love them which love you, what thank have ye? for sinners also love those that love them. And if ye do good to them which do good to you, what thank have ye? for sinners also do even the same. And if ye lend to them of whom ye hope to receive, what thank have ye? for sinners also lend to sinners, to receive as much again.

He summed it up here:

But love ye your enemies, and do good, and lend, hoping for nothing again; and your reward shall be great, and ye shall be the children of the Highest: for He is kind unto the unthankful and to the evil. Be ye therefore merciful, as your Father also is merciful.

Notice this: "Your reward shall be great." The reward He was talking about here was not only the one you get when you get to heaven. First, I knew it by revelation, and second, I had been rewarded too many times by God.

He did not say, "I will give you just enough to get by on." He said, "You walk in these principles, and your reward will be great, for you shall be called the children of the Most High."

Who is going to call you that? Not God. You are already His child. People are going to call you that. That is how exceedingly great your blessings will be. It is almost like watching Solomon operate.

Your reward will be great. That reward He is talking about is here. You sow that kind of seed, and your reward will be great, for you shall be called the children of the Most High.

Notice the priorities God puts on these things. "Love your enemies. Do good. Lend, hoping for nothing again. Your reward shall be great. You shall be the children of the Highest, for He is kind unto the unthankful and the evil. Be merciful as your Father also is merciful."

It was not easy to walk in these precepts when I first began to discover them. I thought, "I am going to walk in them first, then I am going to start teaching them." But then I thought, "No. I know I got what I got by revelation. Why start cheating a bunch of people while I wait for it to manifest in my life?"

So I began to teach these principles.

A Stolen Car Became a Test of Whether Lack Had Been Eradicated

No sooner had I really gotten turned on to this and started understanding it than one morning at church, there were twenty-five born again and eighteen filled with the Holy Ghost. Maybe it was the other way around. Anyway, I remember being so high in the Spirit that I came whistling out the door of the church, really high.

I went out where my car was, and I looked at the ground. There was not even a shadow of where it had been parked. Not even a shadow.

My wife kept telling me, "Do not leave the keys in the car."

Well, a girl, I later found out, came by who was on dope and just took it. She just got in it and took it.

I stood there and looked at the ground and thought, "This cannot be." I had mixed emotions. I was still high from being in the Spirit, but my car was stolen, and that was trying to settle in.

What was happening in my spirit, I did not understand. God was eradicating the sense of lack, the actual sense of lack.

Do you know what it means to live with no sense of lack? He was beginning to eradicate this, and I did not understand it.

All of a sudden, the Holy Ghost came on me, and I busted out in hilarious laughter because I could not feel any lack. I laughed so hard that when the man who finished locking the church came out, he said, "What is the matter with you?"

I said, "Somebody has stolen my car."

He got this awkward grin and waited for me to get over it, then said, "Do you need a ride home?"

I said, "Yeah, I need a ride home."

I thought that was so funny. I did not have any sense of lack. None. What a funny feeling.

This is knowing that no matter what storm comes, I know my house is finally on the rock.

What rock?

"Oh, I am a confession man."

That is part of it. What rock? Doing His sayings.

A few days later, of course, I had to turn it in because of the insurance. Of course I abide by the law. They loaded my car up to take it to Reno, where they dismantled them and sold them for parts. This was in Oregon. It was a new car. They file the numbers off, get a phony title, and resell them.

But they were heading out of town and my car died on a side street and would not run until I got it.

The police said, "Are you going to press charges?"

I said, "Do I have the prerogative?"

He said, "Yes."

I said, "No."

"You are not?"

"They do not have it, do they?"

"You are not going to press charges?"

"No."

"Well, the state is."

"That is up to you."

The policeman got mad at me, and I do not blame him. As far as he was concerned, I was making life harder for everybody.

He said, "You are not going to press charges?"

I said, "No. I just want to talk to her."

"Talk to her?"

"Yeah, I want to talk to her."

They did not want to let me do that. They said, "You are out of this."

The reason I wanted to talk to her was not as a preacher. I wanted to tell her how much Jesus loved her. I wanted to tell her that I was more willing to give the car to her than put her in jail. I wanted the love of God in the form of the Word to get stuck in her, so that even if she ended up on death row, she would never be able to get it out. I know that it would have saved her, because she would know there are Christians out there who would not esteem a piece of damnable steel above her salvation.

Lenny's Motorcycle Showed What Love Is Willing to Give for Salvation

Then came Lenny. We started getting into these precepts, this love.

I said, "Lenny, what would you do to see your friend saved? What would you do?"

Words are really cheap if we know we do not have to pay for them. We can get down there and pray a prayer and know that we can renege any time.

"I am going to be an intercessor. I will be down here every night for a year."

Words are cheap when you know God is not going to punish you. But you are getting close to God when you come to a place in your spirit where you are not going to say something unless you can do it. You are not going to pray something in faith unless you are willing to take your stand, hell or high water, until it comes to pass.

You are not going to pledge anything. You are not going to say, "I am going to do anything," unless you come to that prayer confident that you will see it through. Then you are starting to think like God.

Of course words are cheap, especially in prayers. I remember that old holiness wailing, which is okay. The problem with it was that it was an instantaneous turn on and off.

"How are you doing, Roberson?"

"Okay."

"Well, I am praying for the lost. Oh God..."

It was not building compassion that resulted in groanings and intercessions of the Spirit. But you know how we are:

"Oh God, save my friend. I thank Thee for it."

Sure you do, as long as it does not cost you anything.

My friend Lenny was doing this: "Oh God, save my friend."

We were talking about it. I said, "What would you be willing to do to see your friend saved?"

He said, "Anything. Oh, anything."

So the next time in prayer, God took him up on it. Here he was on his knees: "God, save my friend."

The Lord said, "Lenny." You ought to hear him tell it. "Lenny, Lenny."

Pretty soon he said, "Yes."

"Do you want your friend saved?"

"Oh, Thou knowest that I do."

The Lord said, "All right. Let Me tell you what to do. That great big full-dress motorcycle out front, the one that thou lovest..."

You have to understand. First, there was Lenny's wife, of course, but brother, that motorcycle ran a close second.

He said, "The motorcycle that thou lovest?"

"Yes."

"You know your friend coveted that motorcycle? The one you want saved? The one that runs the store across the street? The one living with that woman? I want you to push this thing across the street and give it to him, and tell him it came from Me."

He said, "What? That is my motorcycle. The one that I love."

"Well, you said you would do anything."

He pushed it across the street. His friend came out, and he said, "You really like this motorcycle, do you not?"

"Oh boy, if I had the money, I would grab that thing up right now."

He said, "Here." He was kind of half mad about it too. "Here, it is yours."

"No, I cannot afford that."

"No, I am giving it to you."

"Why? You are giving this to me, Lenny?"

"Yes, take it. Do you want it or not?"

"Yeah, but why?"

Lenny said, "God told me to give it to you."

He said, "Wait a minute. Every preacher in town told me what a vile, filthy, low-down, no-good sinner I am. God hates me. I am living in adultery over here. I am messed up. And you mean to tell me this motorcycle came from God?"

Lenny said, "Yes," and marched back, leaving him standing there shaking his head.

Two or three days later, I came over to Lenny's house. I was in a meeting there and dropped in. Lenny met me at the door grinning from ear to ear, like a Cheshire cat.

I said, "Do you want to tell me the story?"

He said, "Yeah. He had that motorcycle about two days, and last night he came over to my house. He said, 'I could not sleep for two nights. I could not get over that God had given me that. I could not stand it anymore. What do I have to do to get saved?'"

That was not the end of the story.

He said, "Your reward shall be great. You will be the children of the Highest. Your reward will be great."

Two weeks later, a man pulled up into Lenny's driveway. He drove a truck eighty miles one way to get to Lenny's house. It was a four-wheel-drive truck that Lenny always wanted. It blue-booked at $4,000. To top that off, he had a brand new trials Montesa motorcycle tied in the back of it.

He jumped out, ran into the house, and gave the title to both of them to Lenny. This guy did not even know what Lenny had done.

Old Lenny said, "I just ached with the goodness and the glory of God, that there really is a walk like that."

It is hard to imagine what is in these chapters, is it not?

Jesus' Sayings Answer Every Attack With Judgment, Mercy, Forgiveness, and Giving

For every reaction from the cross, God has an action. For every action, there is a reaction. In other words, everything Satan does, God has an answer. In fact, He is way ahead of the devil. God had the reaction before the devil ever thought of it. That is what the cross was about.

"Demonstrate what you are talking about, Roberson."

Okay, we are going to go a little deeper in meditation now.

Take verse 27 and put your finger on it. Then put your finger on verse 37. Look at verse 27 and get your eyes ready to go to verse 37. Here is meditation:

But I say unto you which hear, Love your enemies, do good to them which hate you.

Verse 37 is His answer to this:

Judge not, and ye shall not be judged.

These are in correlation. These are interwoven thoughts. He did not change the subject. He said, "Love your enemies. Do good to them that hate you." In verse 37, He promises in this summary that if you will do that good and judge not, you will not be judged by Satan.

Verse 28:

Bless them that curse you, and pray for them which despitefully use you.

He is saying instead of condemning them, "Condemn not, and ye shall not be condemned." Instead of condemning them, if you bless them, then Satan cannot fulfill a condemning sentence in you.

Look at verse 29:

Unto him that smiteth thee on the one cheek offer also the other; and him that taketh away thy cloak forbid not to take thy coat also.

What is your reaction to this? This is God's answer:

Forgive, and ye shall be forgiven.

He smites you on the cheek and takes your cloak. Forgive. That is the reaction. Satan cannot bring a judgment against you.

How about the reaction to every man that asks you?

Give to every man that asketh of thee; and of him that taketh away thy goods ask them not again.

The answer to that is verse 38:

Give, and it shall be given unto you; good measure, pressed down, and shaken together, and running over, shall men give into your bosom.

He was not even actually talking about giving in a service. He was talking about meeting people's needs. "Give to every man that asks you. Him that takes away your cloak, ask him not again." He says, "Give, and it shall be given unto you."

To operate in a high level like that, your reward must be great, or you cannot keep on giving.

God has called us to meet the needs of the poor. We are doing that already. They are taking it away from us on purpose, though. They call it taxes. They get welfare departments and things like this. Your problem is that you gripe when you pay it.

Do not do it.

"I am making half my money."

Do not gripe. God will multiply the other half.

Oh, I like this.

The Blind Lead the Blind Until the Beam Is Removed by Doing His Sayings

Let us bring a summary to this. Verse 39:

And He spake a parable unto them, Can the blind lead the blind? shall they not both fall into the ditch? The disciple is not above his master: but every one that is perfect shall be as his master.

Is it not interesting that He sticks a parable in here? In the laws of meditation, what is a parable? A parable is something I can identify with my natural mind that illustrates a spiritual application. Whenever He gives a parable, He is always trying to cause you to understand some spiritual principle He is teaching on.

I kept looking at this parable: "Can the blind lead the blind? Shall they not both fall in the ditch?"

Then the fortieth verse says:

The disciple is not above his master: but every one that is perfect shall be as his master.

Do you know what this parable is saying? If I were blind and leading you, I could lead you off a canyon. He said the disciple is not above his master, but he who is mature, perfect, will be as his master. The disciple operates no higher than his master.

Therefore, if I am blind and leading you toward a cliff, you are blinded with the same blindness with which I blinded you. If you are my disciple, you cannot be above me because you are beneath me. You can be as mature or perfect as I am, but if you are my disciple, then you are being taught by me. If I am blind, my teachings will lead you over a cliff, and we will both die.

That is what is happening today in denominations. The blind are leading the blind, and all their disciples are not above their master.

I said, "Lord, how do I keep from falling in a ditch?"

He said, "Son, when all these truths, My sayings, come to light and you walk by them, you will be walking in My light. You will not be leading anybody blind."

Glory to God. Is this not good stuff?

"Brother Roberson, can I ever judge anybody? Can I say anything in judgment?"

As a matter of fact, you can. God has allowed you and me to make spiritual judgments if we do not have a beam in our eye that is blinding our insight. If you do this chapter, then you will not have anything in you that would judge a person wrong.

Look at verse 41:

And why beholdest thou the mote that is in thy brother's eye, but perceivest not the beam that is in thine own eye? Either how canst thou say to thy brother, Brother, let me pull out the mote that is in thine eye, when thou thyself beholdest not the beam that is in thine own eye? Thou hypocrite, cast out first the beam out of thine own eye, and then shalt thou see clearly to pull out the mote that is in thy brother's eye.

When can I judge? You can judge when you do not have a beam in your eye. If you see your brother walking in error, if you see him following something blind, you can say, "That is wrong."

People say, "You are judging me."

Of course I am, because I have extracted the beam out of my eye.

How do you get the beam out of your eye? You extract it through meditation of these principles we have just taught: love your enemies, do good, until you are walking in the light of what His Word says with no beam. Then when you see your brother in error, when you see him walking blind, you have a right to go over and help him extract the mote out of his own eye.

But if you are judging your brother for what you are guilty of, you have no right. In fact, you bring judgment back on yourself. We hate things in other people that are guilty in us. Have you ever noticed that?

He went on to say every good tree is known by its fruit. What are you planting? Good seeds or bad seeds? Every tree is known by its own fruit. Out of the abundance of the heart, the mouth speaks.

Therefore, whosoever hears My sayings and does them, I will show you what he is like. He is like this man who dug deep and laid his foundation on a rock. Hell or high water, he will not go down the river.

Refusing Judgment Releases What the Enemy Has Intercepted

I could tell you testimony after testimony, but I will not.

"You mean if I judge not, Satan cannot bring a judgment against my finances?"

What do you think it is saying? Judge not, and you will not be judged. Condemn not, and you will not be condemned. Forgive, and you will be forgiven. Give, and it will be given.

"If I refuse to make judgments, then when I give, it will be given?"

Yes, because Satan does not have the power to stop it. It will work every time. Better than every time.

I will tell you one vision I had. I was trying to teach this chapter. I got right down to "Give, and it shall be given" and "Judge not." At that time, I noticed in my spirit the correlation. I was prophesying to the people, and the power of God came on. He was talking about treasures that had been intercepted.

When I opened my eyes, I saw a vision. I saw a cloud. It looked like King Solomon's cave. God was illustrating the prophecy for me. It was heaped with precious jewels: strings of pearls and emeralds and diamonds. You have seen cartoons of Solomon's cave, chests of gold and treasures. This cloud's belly was sagging like a window of heaven.

Here all these believers were giving.

I asked God, "What was that?"

He said, "Son, that is the finances and the prosperity that has been intercepted by the evil one, that I sent out. I sent them, and he intercepted them. But you tell them now that if they will straighten their lives out, quit judging, start forgiving instead of condemning, and start walking after Me, then when they start giving, they will strip Satan of his power to bring these kinds of judgments. Everything that was laid up for them will be released. It will come. All the giving you ever did will be taken into account."

Is that not exciting?

Thank You. Thank You.

I think so.
