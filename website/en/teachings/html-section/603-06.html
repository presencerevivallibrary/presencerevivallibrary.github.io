603-06 Glimpses of Things to Be

Done Becomes More Real as Love Opens the View

Thank You, Jesus.

The fellowship is getting richer and richer with Him. Some of the things I used to esteem as richness and fellowship with Him, I am finding out were less than this, even though they seemed like more. The places He has brought me and the things I have done cannot compare.

Never have I been so content in the core, deep in my heart. Yet it is such an unusual place. I have never been here before. I have gone through these things before, but I am sure this is an increase of His love.

The problem with His love is that you start seeing things more and more through His eyes. When you do, it continues to disarm reasons, excuses, watered-down verses, and everything that weakens the Word.

Day by day, I am getting acquainted with this place we have called done: the work He has done for us through the cross and the reality of it. Every day, especially through the love of God, that place is getting more alive, more real, and more powerful.

At times, I can hardly handle it. I get glimpses of things to be. At those moments, those glimpses seem right there at my fingertips, yet they are the most impossible things: manifestations of Himself and His glory.

When I tried to coin it in something I could say to the crowd, He said something along this line: a revival of soundness of mind, sanity, purity of doctrine, and signs and wonders with no foolishness, signs that would cause Einstein to say, "This was impossible outside of God."

He is looking for some things like that.

As I get glimpses of these things, more and more of the Word opens up to confirm everything He is showing me in the Spirit. If I can get this over to you, I am embarrassed that I ever had fear, anxiety, or lack of peace.

At times, the confidence I see from these revelations is so powerful it scares me.

Grace Makes Earning Embarrassing

I became embarrassed when I thought I could earn any part of the standing, even if it was grace.

For ye know the grace of our Lord Jesus Christ, that, though he was rich, yet for your sakes he became poor...

That you, through the substitution of Himself in your poverty, might be rich.

Do you understand this grace? It would not matter how many billion years you worked for it; you still could not earn it because it is something unearnable and had to be provided.

I am finding out that my prosperity is more based on my love for Him and what I want to see done through Him than on my fear of not giving. I am embarrassed that I thought I could earn any part of that.

We understand this better when it comes to righteousness, because you knew there was nothing you could do to save yourself except believe. It is the same with disease, His blessings, and everything else. All of them require actions of faith. All of them require giving yourself over and believing.

I know I could not be born again outside of believing. Now I understand that the reservoirs are laid up, and I know what breaks them loose.

I have been catching glimpses of how He would see you healed, prosperous, and safe. The substitution and identification teachings keep pouring out, and they are all part of the trail He put me on.

Substitution Shows What Jesus Took So We Could Go Free

Jesus said, "Sheath your sword, Peter. I am going to die a violent death in your place so you will not have to. I am not going to call the angels so you can. I am going to carry your sicknesses and pains and suffer death under the penalty of them so you will not. I am going to die and suffer in the lower parts of the earth so you will not. I will close My mouth before Pilate. I will not give one word of wisdom in My defense, so when the devil frames you with something you are free from, you can open your mouth and give the wisdom."

He could have ended it. But I know substitution.

So I can look down this trail and know what He has done for me. I know what He has done with my peace, and I know what He has done with my joy.

Seeing all those things come together, more of the Word comes into focus, and I start understanding it. I think I got an overall glimpse of how He sees a person.

If we could walk where He sees you, if you could just see how He sees you, the Word paints it. If a person walked there, he would be a devastating war machine.

Let us try to catch a glimpse of this.

Hebrews Says Christ Offered Himself Once at the End of the World

Follow me closely. Begin with Hebrews 9:24, where we left off after Romans 10. Romans says:

Say not in thine heart, Who shall ascend into heaven?

In other words, do not say, "Who will bring Jesus down and crucify Him again, and raise Him from the dead over and over?" He is only going to provide everything for you one time. Then you have to stop and learn how to receive it.

Paul is explaining the difference between righteousness which is of Christ and righteousness which is of the law.

Hebrews 9:24 says:

For Christ is not entered into the holy places made with hands, which are the figures of the true; but into heaven itself, now to appear in the presence of God for us.

Then:

Nor yet that he should offer himself often, as the high priest entereth into the holy place every year with blood of others.

That is what Paul was describing in Romans 10: righteousness which is of the law versus righteousness which is by faith. That is why he said not to say in your heart, "Who will ascend into heaven and bring Him down year after year?" He is only going to offer all these things and provide them one time.

Hebrews continues:

For then must he often have suffered since the foundation of the world.

Then:

But now once in the end of the world hath he appeared to put away sin by the sacrifice of himself.

I never really taught this before because I could not understand this part. He called the crucifixion, the one-time offering He made, "the end of the world." He offered Himself once at the end of the world.

Since He offered Himself two thousand years ago, I have been asking, "Is this the once at the end of the world You are talking about? Did the world end two thousand years ago? What exactly are You talking about?"

When the rest of the substitution truths came together, I began to understand that He wanted me to see how finally, absolutely finally, He judged everything, including my diseases, poverty, and sin.

It was so thorough that Paul could say this Man offered Himself once at the end of the world. When I started seeing what he was talking about, sickness and disease came to an end in my thinking and heart with finality.

You judged this thing two thousand years ago, and for some reason You called it the end of the world. My disease has been so thoroughly judged and eliminated that if I dare to understand and believe it, not only will I walk in a free place in my own life, I will get a whole lot of diseases off a whole lot of people who do not know how.

Our Appointment to Die Was Fulfilled in Christ

Hebrews 9:25 says:

For then must he often have suffered since the foundation of the world.

Then:

But now once in the end of the world hath he appeared to put away sin by the sacrifice of himself.

Then the answer is in the next verses:

And as it is appointed unto men once to die, but after this the judgment:

So Christ was once offered to bear the sins of many...

He says this Man was offered once at the end of the world. Then he says it is appointed unto men once to die, but after that the judgment. So Christ was once offered to bear the sins of many, because not everyone would let Him.

Every Old Testament saint, in every dispensation before the flood, after the flood when God gave Noah commandments, through Abraham, Isaac, and the law given to Moses, was waiting for the cross.

Romans says:

Whom he did foreknow, he also did predestinate to be conformed to the image of his Son.

There was no image to conform to until Jesus came and rose from the dead. Those He foreknew had to be predestinated to that event. When Jesus rose from the dead, He quickened all those through every dispensation who were waiting for the Firstborn to be born, so they might be resurrected in their spirits and go home to be with Him.

When Hebrews says this Man died once at the end of the world, and that it is appointed unto man once to die and then the judgment, it finally dawned on me: when I accepted Jesus Christ, I died. The appointment I had to die once was fulfilled there.

I was baptized into Christ's death because I am on this side of the cross. I was not on the other side. I did not have to go to Abraham's bosom somewhere and wait for the Firstborn to be born. I live on this side of the cross; He has already been born.

When I die, my spirit goes straight to heaven. Old Testament saints went to a place of waiting. We reach back to receive what He has done. They reached forward for what He would do.

The amazing part is that it is appointed unto man once to die. I already died. My old man already died with Christ. I was baptized into His death and raised with Him in His resurrection. When I died with Him, my appointment for judgment was judged in Him, so I go free.

That is why Hebrews says "once at the end of the world." It goes both directions. I died in Him two thousand years ago, and they died in Him two thousand years ago by reaching forward. When He came and I died in Him, that fulfilled the appointment for man to die once. Since He took my judgment, I have already had my judgment.

Now I look for Him to return the second time with no sin and take me home.

The Believer Has Already Passed Judgment and Awaits Reward

What really excited me was how He sees me now.

I used to think He took notice of my little fears and discomforts: "I do not know if I could go do that. That is not my comfort zone." Of course He loves you and fellowships with you every moment, but He wants you to come to the place where you begin to see yourself like He does.

My appointment to die once has already taken place in Him. Since He was judged in my place, my judgment has already taken place in Him. So for me, I get to look for His second coming, and He will appear with no sin. I will not have that judgment, only reward.

Yes, we will have a judgment, but not the kind the sinner has. We get the kind where He looks and sees what we did and what kind of reward we will have. Then we go on into heaven. We do not get the judgment of dying outside Christ.

I have already died, and I am already living in Him. All I am waiting on is a change of bodies.

To understand how finally all these things were judged two thousand years ago when I died with Him: my disease was totally judged, my lack was totally judged, and I was resurrected with Him. My judgment of those things has already taken place, and I go free.

I have the privilege of building myself up in Him. Faith comes by hearing, and hearing by the Word. I can execute all those judgments by faith to the fullest degree because He has provided them and because they are mine now.

Impossible Cases Have Equal Rights in the Finished Judgment

I am really looking forward to that area of faith that comes from knowing disease has been so judged.

Give me the child with the scrambled legs and the white eyes. I know this has been judged, and I will not let it stand.

What makes the difference between you and that child? Were you lucky because you were born with your legs on frontwards and not shackled to a chair, never speaking a sentence, a prisoner in your body, alive but never able to communicate?

Who drew the straw and made them that way? Do not tell me they do not have a call and a reason. Do not tell me they do not have a fivefold ministry, helps, governments, diversities, or prayer call. Are you telling me they do not have the same chance you have because the cross judged everything two thousand years ago and ended that world? Are you telling me they do not have the same chance just because you were born healthy?

If I were born like that, I would want one of you to go get my miracle and give me a chance.

It seems impossible if you do nothing and stay where you are. Listen, I am not asking you to go out on the limb and saw it off to see if it works. I just want your prayers. I am not asking you to fight this for me.

God already told me that if I do not go get some grapes and bring them back, most are not going to believe it soon enough or fight the war hard enough to receive it themselves. I do not care about fighting this war. If I were in that condition, I would want you to bring me where somebody was fighting a fight for me.

Do you know any meeting anywhere in the world right now where you could bring a child like that, scrambled, never able to communicate, blind, deaf, just alive, and be guaranteed that child would be healed there?

When He first taught me about the Born Again Trail, He said:

It is expedient for you that I go away.

I asked, "How can You say it is expedient for You to go away? If You were stationed in Jerusalem and my son was born like that, I could make a pilgrimage to Jerusalem. It might take me two or three years to get close enough to You, but I know my son and I would come home whole. How can You say it is expedient that You go away?"

You know what He told me six years ago?

"Indeed, son, you will make that pilgrimage, but you will find your answers on the Born Again Trail."

And we have. Along the way, we have.

John 5 Shows the Greater Work of Passing From Death to Life

When I tell you I cannot stand it, yes, it is a form of intercession. I cannot stand it, knowing He was judged once and for all, and Hebrews calls that the end of the world for that disease. It has no right to stand in that child's way of equal rights to serve God and fulfill his calling.

Do not get excited or condemned. You are some of the best people I know. Stay in there and help me. Do not just say you will pray. I am engaged in a war.

I have caught glimpses of this all my life.

Go to John 5. The impotent man at the pool of Bethesda has just been healed. Verse 9 says:

And immediately the man was made whole, and took up his bed, and walked: and on the same day was the sabbath.

That infuriated the Jews. Verse 18 says:

Therefore the Jews sought the more to kill him, because he not only had broken the sabbath, but said also that God was his Father, making himself equal with God.

Then Jesus answered:

Verily, verily, I say unto you, The Son can do nothing of himself, but what he seeth the Father do.

Then:

For the Father loveth the Son, and sheweth him all things that himself doeth.

Notice:

And he will shew him greater works than these, that ye may marvel.

Greater works than what? Greater than the pool of Bethesda, greater than all the Old Testament saints' miracles.

Verse 21 tells you what those greater works are:

For as the Father raiseth up the dead, and quickeneth them; even so the Son quickeneth whom he will.

The word "them" is in italics. This is singular. The Father raises up the dead and quickens, and even so the Son turns around and quickens whom He will.

Quicken is different from resurrection. Quickening generally speaks of a spirit being made alive from death to life. Resurrection includes the body.

The Father was going to show the Son greater works than all Old Testament miracles, because as the Father raises up the Son and quickens His spirit from death to life, the Son can then turn around, the price having been paid, and quicken whom He will.

Verse 22 says:

For the Father judgeth no man, but hath committed all judgment unto the Son.

Why? Because every single human being was judged in the Son. The atrocities that came out of Hitler's spiritual death were judged in the Son. Every human being who would accept it was judged in Him and goes free.

The Father was saying, "I will show You greater works than all the Old Testament saints. As I, Your Father, quicken Your spirit from the dead, all judgment will have been laid on You. Then You can quicken to life everyone who believes in Me through You."

All judgment is committed to the Son because every human being was judged in Him.

Those Who Believe Do Not Enter the Condemnation of Dying Outside Christ

John 5:24 says:

Verily, verily, I say unto you, He that heareth my word, and believeth on him that sent me, hath everlasting life.

Then:

And shall not come into condemnation; but is passed from death unto life.

If that were not enough, verse 25 says:

Verily, verily, I say unto you, The hour is coming, and now is...

It has come. It is here.

...when the dead shall hear the voice of the Son of God: and they that hear shall live.

In verse 24, He is talking about people standing on top of the planet alive. Whoever hears His Word and believes on Him who sent Him has everlasting life, shall not come into condemnation, and has passed from death to life, having been born again.

The condemnation you will not come into is dying outside Christ and having your final judgment be hell. You will not enter that condemnation when you accept Him now.

Then He says the hour is here when the dead will hear the voice of the Son of God, and those who hear will live. When He died, during the three days and nights, He went to the center of the earth. He went to the flood generation. He took the harvest out of that. He took the harvest out of every dispensation.

Those who are alive and hear Him go from death to life on top of the planet. They will not enter the condemnation of dying outside Christ and going to hell.

We already died. When we died with Him and were baptized into His death, He was judged in our place, and we were raised up with Him. We have already been judged. We do not enter condemnation; we enter life.

If I stepped out of my body, it would just fall off. I would go be with Him. Before the cross, they went to a place of waiting because they had not yet been quickened from death to life. But before Jesus died, He said the hour was here when the dead would hear His voice, and every dispensation that believed in the salvation offered in that dispensation would be quickened from death to life.

When I step out of my body, I go straight there. Why? Because two thousand years ago, at the end of the world, He was judged once for all who came from Adam to the cross and everyone after. Anyone who accepted Jesus before, and anyone who accepts Him now, dies that one death and escapes the judgment because the judgment has been laid on Him.

Never Die Means Eternal Life Has Already Begun

This is where some no-die doctrines come from. People preach, "I am never going to die because Jesus said I would not."

Yes, you are. Your body is going to fall off, and you are going to get a glorified one. But wait until you see how God looks at that.

Go to John 11, where Jesus raises Lazarus. Begin at verse 21:

Then said Martha unto Jesus, Lord, if thou hadst been here, my brother had not died.

Then:

But I know, that even now, whatsoever thou wilt ask of God, God will give it thee.

Jesus said:

Thy brother shall rise again.

Martha said:

I know that he shall rise again in the resurrection at the last day.

Jesus answered:

I am the resurrection, and the life: he that believeth in me, though he were dead, yet shall he live.

Then:

And whosoever liveth and believeth in me shall never die. Believest thou this?

Whoever lives and believes in Me shall never die. That is us.

I know that because it is appointed unto man once to die and then the judgment. I have already died, and He has already suffered under the judgment for me.

There are doctrines built around the idea that people are not going to perish in these bodies. The founders of those movements eventually died, and the movements had to explain it. A friend of mine I knew for thirty-four years got into that. He died about six months ago. He once sat at a table with me and said, "You could shoot me right in the heart, and I would not die. Jesus said, 'He that liveth and believeth in Me shall never die.'"

He is dead now, and he died of disease.

So what does Jesus mean? This is where I got my overall look at how God sees us.

When you were born again, you entered His life. You entered eternity, and you are never going to die again. There are steps we are going to take. We will put this planet off. We will put these bodies off. There will be a new heaven and a new earth, and we will get glorified bodies. But to Him, those are just steps.

You have already gone from death to life. To Him, those other things are trivial compared to that.

That is why I am embarrassed to think I ever had fear when He looks at us changing our bodies as a blip in the line of you and me entering everlasting life.

What is there to be afraid of?

If God be for us, who can be against us? If God spared not His own Son, but gave Him up for us all, how shall He not with Him freely give us all things?

I have already gone from death to life. I know there is a process until we get our new universe, new heaven and earth, and new body. But it does not change the fact that I have already been born again, gone from death to life, and am walking with Him.

Once, at the end of the world, He so thoroughly judged all those things that used to stop me. I am not going to let them walk with me.

Hallelujah, Jesus.

Revival Will Carry Healing and Judgment Back Into Full Manifestation

The more ready you are to go, the more likely you are to stay longer. I have never been so ready. I am really ready, but I am likely to stay.

I am starting to see the life I have entered into. So what if it is full of trials and tribulations? I am starting to see how thoroughly He has finished it and how to execute it like never before. I am excited about wreaking havoc upon this city.

The way He talks, you have already entered life, and you will never die. But while I am here, I have jurisdiction. Where I plant my feet, I have authority. I plan on using the daylights out of it.

How many want to go to revival?

I have good news. Once grace is received in some of those realms, it will also be much easier imparted, because there is no way you can earn any of it. The grace to live in it, war in it, and hold up under tribulation in it will be imparted with it.

The reason the judgment gift is not in full manifestation is because there is nobody He can render judgment through. But before He comes back, there will be.

How can you compare that to the Dark Ages, when nobody was being healed? Why were people getting sick and dying all the time? Because nobody was being used in healing. It is the same with instantaneous judgment. It will be back in the last days, but you do not see it now because nobody is operating in it.

In the end, you will see that even that gifting is because of love.

Would you rather have been the sorcerer Paul called instantaneous judgment on, who was blind for a season, or Sergius Paulus, the deputy of Cyprus? Sergius Paulus was born again, and I imagine much of the island was. The sorcerer was only blind for a season because he was lost. If he had dropped straight into hell, that would have been worse.

Would you rather have been that sorcerer, who undoubtedly repented during that season and is now in heaven, instead of being in hell for the last two thousand years? Would you rather be the crowd that got born again because judgment was rendered, or a crowd that did not?

It would have been good if somebody had shown up and said, "You are not going to leaven the foundation of the body of Christ by preaching that everybody is included in salvation." It would have been good if that could have been judged in a way apparent to all, so the person learned not to blaspheme.

Someone says, "Let us just love him through it."

He already thwarted the crowd that loved him through it and the crowd that tried to reason him through it. He has already sat under the best.

If Paul had been here, what do you think he would have done? "What are you doing leavening the foundation of the body of Christ? If you do not stop, I am turning you over to Satan so you learn not to blaspheme."

Do you believe that is coming back? You are right. It is.

And it belongs to the kind of revival they had in the book of Acts, where they brought the sick and tormented from the cities around them, and they were healed, every single one.
