1071-02 Worship in Spirit and in Truth

John 1:1. We will work our way through in passing, review, and highlights so you will understand once again the depths we are going to enter into.

Hallelujah.

John 1:1 begins:

In the beginning was the Word, and the Word was with God, and the Word was God.

In the beginning, before anything you and I understand to exist had a beginning, was the Word, the Logos. Before the natural universe, before all substance, was the mighty Logos, the second Person in the Godhead. The Logos was with God, and the Logos was God.

Genesis says, "In the beginning God created the heavens and the earth." John is talking about the respective roles the Godhead took in creation. He is able to divide them by understanding who took what role.

When you say, "I love You, God," you are talking to the three members of the Godhead that make up the One. When you say, "I love You, Father," you are talking to Him whom the Jews referred to as the mighty Jehovah. When you say, "I love You, Jesus," you are talking to the glorified humanity of Him who is both deity and humanity, the second Person in the Godhead, the mighty Logos.

It is all right to say, "I love You, Holy Spirit." If you try to worship Him, His ministry is to divert it to Jesus. He will glorify Him. He does not talk about Himself. He does not speak of Himself. All He teaches, speaks of, and glorifies is Jesus.

When He first taught me to worship, He is the One I went to. I went to the Holy Spirit to be taught how to worship. I did not know if anybody knew how.

Hallelujah.

When you say, "I love You, God," Genesis 1:1 shows the three members of the Godhead taking their respective roles and creating everything that was. The Father was the planner. Jesus was the One by whom He made the worlds.

John 1:3 says:

All things were made by him; and without him was not any thing made that was made.

How do you know John is talking about Jesus? Because verse 10 says:

He was in the world, and the world was made by him, and the world knew him not.

As Colossians says, by Him, for Him, and through Him all things consist: thrones, dominions, principalities, powers, things present, things to come, angels, men, all things. The substance they consist of came from the One we have come to know and love as our Redeemer, Jesus. The atoms your chair is made of came from the consistency of Him.

By Him, for Him, and through Him all things consist.

Glory be to God forever. Hallelujah.

He is the Creator. The mighty Jehovah was the planner. The Holy Spirit is the Regenerator. In Genesis, after God created the heavens and the earth, the Holy Spirit moved to make it habitable for man. The Holy Ghost has always been the Regenerator. Even when it came time for your salvation, He was the One who regenerated you.

An anthropomorphism is a characteristic God assigns to Himself so you can understand Him by it. You are the created; He is the Creator. The capacity of your mind was created by Him. Do you think you can understand Him without help?

The Father is an anthropomorphism. We can understand fatherhood. The Son is an anthropomorphism. The Son of God, the humanity of Jesus, had a beginning, but not His deity. You can be equal to the glorified humanity of Jesus as His brother or sister, but you will never touch His deity. Never.

The Godhead is one in essence. There are three Persons, but they are one in essence. This essence of God that each Person of the Godhead possesses consists of nine attributes. The Father has it. The mighty Logos consists of these nine attributes in one essence, and so does the mighty Holy Spirit. They are all one in essence, but three in Persons.

Those attributes are absolute sovereignty, righteousness, judgment, immutability, eternal life, love, omnipotence, omniscience, and omnipresence. He is all-knowing, without time, and there is nowhere He is not.

When I understood the essence of God, I knew what made Him God was the fact that He could not vary from that one essence. As we touched on yesterday, He is sovereignly love, meaning He cannot operate outside of it.

Judgment derives from the word just and justification, which means He is just and justified in every decision or judgment He is forced to render or hand down.

At the great white throne judgment, there will be a time when you will be quiet. It will be God's turn. But now you can swell your chest and choose any words you want to say. You can give great swelling expositions of theology as though that makes you right.

Jesus said, "I came not to judge the world." There is One who judges you already: the words He has spoken. You will be judged by them in the last day. But right now you are a free-will moral agency. You can say anything you want, act any way you want, and choose anything you want.

Then that day, when you stand before Him, it will be His turn, and you will take what He hands down. Your turn is now.

Every person hell gives up will stand before Him for their works, and their name will not be in the Book of Life. He will tell them the hour of their visitation and why He is sending them to the lake of fire.

He is absolutely sovereign. He is sovereignly immutable. Immutability means He cannot change, nor can you change Him. The devil, you, the flesh, the world, nothing has the power to alter Him even slightly. There is not even a shadow of turning in His whole being. He cannot change, and you cannot change Him. He is sovereignly immutable.

That means He is the same yesterday, today, and forever. What He said a thousand years ago is as good now as it was when He said it. That is why you can have faith. The moment you believe it, He is sovereignly under obligation to come and do it, even if He wanted a way out.

Someone says, "My Aunt Susie believed God, and God let her die. I know He had a purpose for letting her die."

Sure He did. He could not heal her in that situation. If a friend of mine was going to go to hell, and he was only saved through a window of time because of difficulties, I would rather God take him then than for him to recover, fall back into sin, and go to hell. In my estimation, God could not heal him and still bring him to heaven.

When I found out God is sovereignly love, I began to find out about His character. That was when He started opening up salvation under every dispensation. I saw the lengths God went to so people would be full of the knowledge of Him. I was astounded.

He would not even let me into the Old Testament at first. I had to assimilate our covenant first. When I found out our covenant turned to sonship and inheritance, and after I came to that point of understanding, then He started opening the Old Testament.

I said, "Why did You wait so long, Father?"

I could read it, but He would not open it up.

He said to me, "Because you would have used all those spiritually dead examples wrong, like so many have."

I said, "What protected me?"

He said, "Your praying in tongues, son."

I started talking about how praying in tongues boosts your immune system by thirty-five to forty percent. Are those my findings? No, sir. Dr. Peterson's findings. Praying in tongues activates a part of your brain that is not used for anything else. It is dormant until you speak with tongues, and then it starts producing chemicals that can increase the immune system thirty-five to forty percent.

I have never missed a preaching engagement in twenty-seven years because of sickness. I can testify to the glory of God that what He said is true.

Nevertheless, for two and a half to three years my mind has been absolutely out from underneath pressure. I did not understand the effect pressure had on the mind, except that it breaks the thing down and you have senior moments.

Have you ever had a policeman stop you and say, "What is your name?" and you forgot it? Under temporary pressure you cannot find your license, you cannot even find your purse. If a temporary pressure system does that to your mind, what is the long-term effect? What are you doing to yourself?

On the other hand, if your mind is absolutely bathed in peace, what will happen to your body?

It took two and a half to three years of getting out from under pressure before my mind was no longer subject to it. Then it started healing itself.

When God began to teach me the Old Testament, I had never seen anything like the genealogies. He made a simple statement in my spirit: "I want you to go back and find out how long from Noah backward to Adam. I want you to find out how long their sons lived and preached this gospel."

So I went back, and to my amazement, it was all in there. They preached this gospel longer than their fathers. I figured it out with pencil and paper and thought, "I am going to introduce this on the Born-Again Trail."

I brought those notes out. My notes are usually little dots by Scriptures, because the Scriptures are my notes. I assimilate chapters, so the Scripture tells me where I am.

We started with Adam. Cain killed Abel and was banished to the land of Nod. He built a city after his son Enoch. Then came Irad, Mehujael, Methusael, and Lamech. Lamech married Adah and Zillah, and they had Jabal, Jubal, Tubal-cain, and a sister named Naamah.

Of course, you already knew that.

Then we went back to the bloodline. With Seth, men began to call upon God again. Seth lived 912 years and preached the gospel 112 years longer than his father Adam, who lived 930 years.

Seth had Enos, who lived 905 years and preached the gospel 105 years longer than his father.

Enos had Cainan, who lived 910 years and preached the gospel ninety years longer than his father.

Cainan had Mahalaleel, who lived 895 years and preached the gospel sixty years longer than his father.

Mahalaleel had Jared, who lived 962 years and preached the gospel 162 years longer than his father.

Jared had Enoch. Enoch lived 365 years, and his father preached the gospel 435 years longer than his son.

Enoch had Methuselah. Methuselah lived 969 years and preached the gospel 604 years longer than his father. He was Noah's grandfather and lived to the year of the flood.

Methuselah's son, Noah's father, was Lamech. Lamech lived 777 years, and Methuselah preached this gospel five years longer than his son Lamech.

Noah lived 500 years and had Shem, Ham, and Japheth. They rode the flood out. Noah lived 950 years and preached this gospel 955 years longer than his father Lamech.

Shem lived 600 years. He had Arphaxad. Arphaxad lived 438 years and had Shelah. Shelah lived 433 years and had Eber. Eber lived 464 years. Then came Peleg, who dropped the lifespan by about two hundred years. He lived 239 years. Reu lived 239. Serug lived 230. Nahor, Abraham's grandfather, lived 148 years. He had Terah, who lived 205 years and had Abraham, who lived 175. Abraham had Isaac, who lived 180, and Isaac had Jacob, who lived 147.

Noah lived fifty-eight years into Abraham's life. The thing Noah taught was sacrifice from the clean beasts, which were on the ark by sevens. He taught Shem, Ham, and Japheth God's ways, and he stayed with them until Abraham came. They could take a pilgrimage any time and sit at Noah's feet and hear the gospel.

Shem lived 108 years into Isaac's life. He rode the flood. Job knew the Redeemer was coming and would stand on the earth in the latter days. Job lived around the time of Abraham. He came from Shem's bloodline, as did Abraham. Abraham came through Shem's son Arphaxad. Job's people came through Aram. They lived about the same time.

Even though Abraham carried the bloodline and had the promise, Job knew all about God.

You talk about the tender loving mercies and kindness of God. What made me discover these things? Finding out His attributes, finding out what He is self-governed by, made me know how much He loves me and what He went through for the salvation of the world.

I cannot tell you what finding out about His character and attributes has done to my faith and trust in God. There is nowhere you can put me that I cannot believe Him.

As I began to understand His attributes, I began to question my Father, and He began to talk to me about things that had been hidden from me since the foundation of the world.

Now pick this up where we left off. Go to John 4. All creation was waiting for this hour. We left off at verses 23 and 24. Jesus sat down at Jacob's well and talked to the Samaritan woman, and this conversation changed my life forever.

He said to her:

But the hour cometh, and now is, when the true worshippers shall worship the Father in spirit and in truth:

for the Father seeketh such to worship him.

God is a Spirit: and they that worship him must worship him in spirit and in truth.

When Jesus said, "The hour cometh, and now is," there was no hour before that one, not since the spiritual death of the human race at the hands of Adam. The hour had finally come when the true worshipers would worship the Father in spirit and in truth.

Notice that "spirit" has a lowercase s, which is correct, because it refers to your human spirit. The true worshipers worship the Father in spirit and in truth, for the Father seeketh such to worship Him.

God is a Spirit. What you can give Him that He wants from you, you can only give Him through your spirit. He is a Spirit, and they that worship Him must worship Him in spirit and in truth.

When He said God seeketh such to worship Him, remember that there is not much we can give Him. Most of His attributes flow to us and through us. Praying in tongues is Him ministering to our spirit. Fasting is us ministering to our own flesh, shutting it down so it will not steal the operation of our spirit. Speaking the Word is changing trouble spots by bringing God's Word and what He said about the problem onto the scene.

Even angels have come to minister to you in presence, anointings, and atmospheres. All these things are to you and through you for your benefit and the benefit of others.

But there is not much you can give Him that ministers to Him. Not even your works.

He said God is a Spirit, making reference to the nature He has and the kind of Person He is. The people He is looking for to worship Him in spirit and in truth have something about them that is like Him. If you can give Him that, but will not, then you are not giving Him something He wants from you.

First He sought you out of spiritual death. Somebody must have prayed, because He found you. He sought us, saved us, and found us. But the next thing He seeks is what you have that He wants. That is the kind of fellowship and rapport that can only come from a nature like His own.

When He said He is seeking such to worship Him, it did not mean He was running from church to church and camp meeting to camp meeting, looking for people who worship better than others. Seeking such to worship Him meant He waited out the dispensations of four thousand years of spiritually dead men who could not give Him the fellowship He longed for.

They could only give Him in part, through their soul and in the measure He talked about at the end of John 3. The Holy Spirit would rest upon them for the purposes, pursuits, and plans of God in their lives as the clock ticked down to D-Day, when the first spiritually alive Man would stand on this planet in four thousand years.

The Father waited out the dispensations, seeking such to worship Him.

It is impossible, Hebrews says, for the blood of bulls and goats to take away sin. If it could have, those sacrifices would have ceased to be offered. The worshipers were not purged from the consciousness of sin, which is part of the death nature.

They could not give God what He wanted.

When I was in Israel, we visited a city gate on Abraham's journey. They uncovered the gate and knew from the record that Abraham, having gone into that city, would have passed through that gate. It was crumbling and falling down.

I thought, "My God, these stones are old."

All of a sudden the Holy Spirit spoke to me. He said, "Your born-again experience, in accounting, walked through that gate."

I almost melted right there. I have been born again.

They could only give Him so much, and that was through the veil of animal blood. They could not take that last step.

The disciples came back saying, "Jesus, the devils are subject to us in Thy name."

To be honest with you, the disciples were more shocked than the devil was that he had to leave when they told him to go. They said, "They are subject to us in Thy name."

Jesus said, in effect, "That is right. But shift your rejoicing to the fact that your names are written in heaven because I have come to pay the price."

Have you figured it out yet? He created and saved you for fellowship. If God had a weakness, it would be you.

Now that you have a nature that can fellowship with Him, why will you not give Him what He wants?

Do you think you are fulfilling His desire by sitting in church listening to some man's opinion from the pulpit? Do you think you are fulfilling His desire to know you individually and fellowship with you by gracing Him with your britches on a chair a few hours a week?

Hardly.

The teaching on the prodigal son was not really about the younger brother. It was about the elder.

The music and celebration from the younger son coming home sent servants out to get the elder brother in the field. He said, "What is all the music?"

They said, "Your brother who was lost has come home."

He got mad and would not come in until the father finally went out and entreated him.

He said, "I have never once transgressed any of your commandments."

That is right. He based his relationship with his father on his works.

"I have never one time transgressed your commandments. I have always done everything you told me to do. This younger son of yours wasted your money on harlots and riotous living, and you killed the fatted calf for him."

You have to understand that the younger son broke relationship with his father and also broke fellowship. Five minutes after he restored relationship, he restored fellowship. The elder brother never broke relationship, but he never had fellowship.

The father said, "Yes, son, but all I have is yours. Your problem is that you never knew Me well enough to know that. I would have killed the fatted calf every day for you if need be. You did not know Me, son. You did not know Me."

Religion is mad because the person down the street is getting more saved than you are, because he wants to know Him.

There are a lot of things I want to know about God. Learning His attributes and getting to know Him opens mysteries that my spirit holds before Him, and He comes.

There are certain things I wanted to understand. One of them is this: if God was going to break your back, He would have put Adam down on his way to the tree of the knowledge of good and evil.

I have heard testimonies, especially in ultra-holiness circles, where people could not figure out why they were all broken up in car wrecks and their kids were in sin. They had to equate it with some logical reasoning on God's behalf.

"He put me down on my back because I was living in sin. He broke my back. Glory be to God, He knew what it would take to get me to serve Him."

You think that is a testimony? "He broke my back. The only way I can look is up."

If God was going to break anybody's back, He would have put Adam down on his way to the tree of knowledge of good and evil. There would be a whole lot of people left out of hell because He would have stopped him.

If He would not put Adam down, do you think He is putting you down? What do you think you are worth anyway?

Sure, you got saved after breaking your back. If I were you, I would give Him the glory for being there the whole time while you were too dumb to see it, and start calling your healing down.

No, sir. If He was going to put anybody down, it would have been Adam, because there are a lot of people in hell because He did not.

Why did He not, Brother Roberson? Because He could not.

God does not even delight in the sacrifice of animals, much less the sacrifice of His Son. Ten million equations later, when Jesus cried, "If it be possible, let this cup pass from Me; nevertheless not My will, but Thine, be done," that meant, "If there is any other way to save mankind, I will not drink this cup."

But there was not. After ten million equations, there was only one way God could salvage humanity, and Jesus had to go to the cross.

God could not stop Adam's journey to the tree. If He could have, He would have. There would not be a lot of people screaming eternity out now, would there? He did not stop him because He could not.

God is sovereignly love. That means He cannot act outside of love. It means God cannot exist as a dictator over you and me forever in heaven. He is self-governed by the nine attributes, so ten billion eons from now He will not get mad and wipe you out because you cannot change. You are saved. He will not change His mind. He will never hurt you. He will not destroy you because He gets irritated about something.

He is the same yesterday, today, and forever. Your future is secure. And when you know Him, you can have everything He has here.

In His love, He could not be a dictator. There is something about your will and individuality that God holds higher than anything else in creation. He protected it so severely with His attributes that He could not stop Adam.

My ability to choose is worth more to Him than we can imagine. Hell is full so you can sit in the middle of testings and trials, inhale, choose God, lift your hands, and worship Him.

A year before I broke into worship, God tried to get me in. Some people kept wanting to go see a passion play, and I finally went with them. It was a super church passion play. Everything was perfect. Angels flew. Hell was hot. Every singer was exactly on key.

There was just one problem: no anointing.

He showed up in the altar call, but on the way back, I was musing in my heart: "What would it take, God, to get an anointing in a passion play like that?"

To my surprise, because I was musing in my spirit, He immediately answered and said, "If you want to find out, go home and worship Me all night."

At the same time He said that, I saw a rock landing in a pond. The ripples from that single rock started with one ripple, then another came from it and pushed the other one, and another, and another, until every nook and cranny in the pond was filled. Wave after wave.

I said, "What was that?"

He said, "When you learn to worship Me, first My presence will permeate your spirit. Then it will permeate your soul and your senses. Then it will permeate your geographical location. Wherever you are, I will come."

I can remember when I finally broke into private worship. I worshiped Him for about three months, a set amount of time a day.

About a year or a year and a half later, I was in my church standing at the front. I had prayed in the Holy Ghost on the Born-Again Trail until it was time to put the worship limb on. There was no going past that date. Even though a year and a half before He had tried to get me voluntarily to go into it, praying in tongues brought me to the point where it was time to put it on. I could not pass that point, so it released God to do what He did in my life.

I was standing in front of the church, and a breeze came up, blew past me, and blew on my spirit. My spirit got so light I did not know whether I was going up or down. I ended up on the floor, totally out of it. As soon as I hit the floor, He started talking to me.

I will be kind to myself and say He said, "You do not know anything about worship."

My spirit began to protest. I was on a certain day of a fast, so I said, "How about my fasting?"

He said, "I did not say not to fast. But fasting will not give you what private worship does, and private worship cannot give you what fasting does."

He said, "Son, when people put all their eggs in one basket, such as fasting, there are many things I can do in their lives. But there are other areas I cannot reach because they do not employ the other keys."

Immediately I felt guilty. I said, "If I have put all my eggs in one basket, it would be praying in tongues."

He immediately responded, "Yes, son, but fortunately you chose the key that unlocked the door to revelation knowledge. That is why you are now enjoying these other limbs you are beginning to walk in."

I felt quite good for a few minutes.

He began to lay out in detail how long He wanted me to worship Him in private worship. Remember, I did not know how to do it and did not know much about it. My private worship consisted of an hour now and then. I did not know what was beyond an hour.

He said, "I want you to learn all about it. Spend this amount of time."

He knew I would do it when He told me to do it.

So I went into my soundproof radio room. I turned the lights out and closed the door. At that time I had a half couch in there and the chair for making tapes. Since then, Tim replaced the half couch with a small bed that fits in there. Back then, I was lying on that couch with one leg hanging over the end and one on the floor.

I had no music. I cannot hire a worship team to follow me around to create an atmosphere conducive to worship. It was me, myself, and I.

I knew enough to pour everything in me into every statement of adoration. Before, when I tried to worship, I thought it would be about the nice things I said. But it narrowed down to three or four short statements that I poured my whole self into.

I knew it was my soul I was offering up, and my soul I had to get past. I knew the soul is the part of me that communicates with my neighbor through accumulated knowledge: "Hello. How are you doing? Fine."

I knew I would worship in the language I knew. I would not let myself pray in tongues. I wanted to find out how to worship Him in straight English.

I would worship Him five minutes, and my mind would wander down the street. I would say, "No you do not. No you do not." I would stop, go get my mind, and bring it back. I would say, "I am in the presence of a King. You are going to show respect and protocol. As long as I am worshiping Him, all of me is going to worship Him."

So I poured all of me into every short statement:

"I love You. I worship You. I glorify You."

Now it is no problem. I am in as soon as I raise my hands. But back then my mind must have wandered off forty times in that first hour.

I stayed. Hour after hour dragged by. I would have highs and lows, but I stayed in there anyway.

When He said "in spirit and in truth," it was a positional truth. He was telling the Samaritan woman, "You can do it. Old Testament saints could not give Him what He wanted. You can. They could not. You can."

So I knew to stay in there whether I felt like worshiping Him or not, because God had given me a certain amount of time. I dried up. After I got past the first hour I was familiar with, I started drying up. I did not know what was beyond an hour.

Some days I would lie there and say, "I love You. I love You," as dry as could be. But I stayed because He told me to stay.

Little did I know He was talking positional truth. "In spirit" means out of that new nature He has given you, out of the new creation. The Old Testament saints could not do it. You can.

All the books I read on worship told me that "in spirit and in truth" meant you start worshiping Him in the flesh and finally end up in the Spirit as an experience. That is not what Jesus was talking about, although that does happen.

They lead you to believe that when you say, "I love You, I love You," and cannot feel anything, you are not getting through. Well, if I was not getting through, I had a lot of hours ahead of me. Drinking water did not help. I would drink a little water and get dry again.

"I love You. Hallelujah. Hallelujah. I really love You. Glory, glory. I mean, I really love You."

Little did I know there were ceilings on my life. My spirit had never fellowshipped with Him beyond those ceilings except under gifts of the Spirit that flowed through Him, but not through my own spirit. Not through me. I had a ceiling over me. I had never fellowshipped beyond that before, and now I was up against it.

Hallelujah.

I was up against it. I said, "Hallelujah. I love You. I mean, I really love You. I praise You. I glorify You."

That ceiling of flesh was there, and I could not feel Him. I could not feel anything. But I was getting through even more than when I was feeling feelings, because He had let me feel those feelings where I had already walked. Now I was getting to worship Him beyond anything I had ever walked in, and I had run up against the flesh.

Did I stay in? Yes, sir. He put me through this for teaching's sake. I can save you time now. I did not know what I was up against, but I stayed in to learn.

Each day I wore that flesh down. Each day I would have a good hour or two, then some bad ones. Pretty soon, I had the whole time good.

Then I can still remember the day He came and followed me around all day. I thought, "All I have to do is think about Him and He surrounds me? What is this? This is glorious. Nothing bad likes to live here."

He followed me around all day. He was never more than a microfiber away. I would raise my hands and say, "Glory."

One time I was standing in a friend's church, and I lifted my hands and started worshiping. The lady in front of me fell down on the floor. The pastor looked at me, and I just looked back.

I am going to tell you now, some kind of baptism of the senses came. I will only say this much at this point because we are near capacity.

Some of you have analytical, clinical faith. You look into the law of His Word, know it works, and know He will work it for you. But you do not know Him very well. You know He said these things.

Let me save you some time. The missing ingredient you are looking for, the one that causes your faith to come alive, is private worship.

Why? Because a baptism of the senses comes. He will so baptize your senses in His presence that the part of you resisting what faith says without seeing it begins to come under ascendancy. Things just start happening.

I did not know there was healing in that anointing.

In the last March camp meeting we had, every person who came in with braces left without them. One woman said it looked like the anointing was on demand. Every person who came with braces or crutches left without them. It was the first time ever.

I did not understand that in the presence of that anointing that comes when you worship, there is healing. I did not know that.

It was after that March, when all the people were healed, that I went to my next camp meeting and discovered healing was in that presence, in that geographical wave of presence that comes through.

I was ministering at the camp meeting as usual. There was a man whose arms had been burned in a chemical accident several years before. He had been chemically poisoned, and the wounds would never heal. They would wrap his arms with bandages in the morning, and by night the blood would be through the bandages.

Compassion and that presence came on me. I hugged him and prayed. The next day it was all dried up. The next day he had pink skin. He was their cameraman, so they had known him for years in that condition. Now he was healed.

I was recovering in the back room, drinking a cup of tea, tired, with the anointing lifted. The pastor's wife came back. She had a thumb that had been jammed for four or five years and would not bend. She sat by me, reached over with her thumb, and said, "Brother Roberson, would you pray for my thumb?"

I said, "Sure."

Out of sheer habit, I lifted my hands and began to worship. Then I reached over, took her thumb, and started to pray. It just bent with my fingers.

I thought, "Is this a presence that is tangibly and generally present in a hospital room when everybody is crying? Is this what Smith Wigglesworth walked in? Is this what he had?"

They asked Smith, "How long do you pray?"

He said, "I never go over thirty minutes, and I never go thirty minutes without."

Then a sixteen-year-old boy, the son of the elders there, who had been born blind in one eye, saw her thumb. He came running over and threw himself down on his knees in front of me because I was still sitting there recovering. I put my hand over his eye and worshiped Him. I took my hand off his eye, and he was looking back at me.

He went out and told his mother and father, the elders. You could hear the screaming for twenty or thirty minutes.

I said, "Father, explain this to me. I have operated in the gifts for years. I come out, worship God, finally put my carnality aside enough for the flow through my spirit for the needs of others. But this seems to be a microfiber away. What has happened?"

He said, "You have touched Me with your spirit, son. When you touch Me with your spirit, all that I am can flow through you. You will not suspend your spirit in My presence for hours without touching Me, and I will flow through you."

I said, "Who can have this?"

He said, "This is not the giftings I am talking about. This is something available to those who believe: 'These signs shall follow them that believe.'"

Second Corinthians 3 says that, from glory to glory, as in a mirror, He changes us into His image. Not like Moses, who had a veil over his face. That veil was spiritual death, and they could not see. But not us. With open face, we no longer have that veil of spiritual death over us. We get to look into the face of God through Jesus with no veil of spiritual death like Moses had. Therefore, we can be transformed from glory to glory into His image.

From glory to glory is where He is taking your spirit.

What we have tried to do is operate where we are in the spirit, read the Word that says these signs will follow those who believe, and then try to get God to go out and do those miracles for us. According to John 16, He keeps coming back and getting us, trying to guide us into all truth. He is trying to get our spirit to go where those miracles live.

We keep trying to get Him to leave us the way we are and go out there and do those miracles.

Anybody can get there. The fisherman on the Amazon River can hardly read, but they are pouring in by the thousands because he broke into worship. Others have broken into worship. They said, "Brother Roberson, that is the hardest thing I ever broke into. My God, I hated myself. I thought I was going to die."

Thank You, God.

There will never be a time in the millennia to come when you can minister to your Father like you can now. Your spontaneous worship in heaven will be no problem. It will be waves of glory. Never will you be able to give Him what you can give Him now, at the cost of untold millions in hell for you to have the choice.

Hell has built its bulwarks in front of me, armies on my left and right, with nowhere to retreat. When it could not get any worse, I choose to lift my hands and worship Him, love Him, and glorify Him. I choose.

At the cost of hundreds of thousands in hell to make that choice, to sit there and make the decision, yes, it moves Him.

There is no way out. Hell has indicted God for unfaithfulness. Things could not look worse. Finances are in shambles. Nothing is going right. I lift my hands and worship Him.

Things will not stay the same.

I choose to lift my hands and tell Him how much I love Him, as long as I want, as often as I want. There is nothing hell can do about it. There is nothing it can conjure, effect, or bring against me that has anything to do with my personal fellowship with Him who saved me and made it possible.

I choose. I choose. I am at the helm of this ship. I am the bit in this horse's mouth.

I choose.
