1072-03 If He Provided It, You Have It

I would like to tell You again and again how grateful and thankful I am for You, for Your Word, and for the power of the Holy Spirit. You gave us all things.

The question is not whether we have it or not. The question is whether You provided it. If it is settled in our heart that You provided it, then we have it. So I want to worship You for providing it, because if You provided it, I know I have it.

Therefore I want to glorify and magnify how good You are. You loved me so much that when I was still lost, You went after me. How could You love me so much as to set all this provision in order and then give it to me? Hallelujah.

The question is not whether I have it or not. The question is whether He provided it. If He provided it, I have it. It is forever settled in heaven that for this dispensation of grace, I have it. I live in this dispensation, so therefore I have it.

But what if you do not have it? Then you need to go into the place where it is established that you have it. It is already established from the foundations of the world that you have it. Therefore, if you do not have it, it is not Him. You need to take another step into Him, where it is done.

It is already done for Him. If it is not done for me, it is because I am outside the done part. There is no use lying around crying about it. You are the one changing, not Him.

If I do not have something, I think I will go from where I do not have it to where I do have it, since the foundations of the world. Since He cannot change, it must be me changing. If He cannot change, that means I already have it. If I do not, then I am the one who has to change into someone who does.

Do not try to get Him to give it to you. You cannot get somebody to do something He has already done.

Paul's great discourse to Rome, especially to the Jewish community, is his great argument dividing the two covenants. Like a physician, he divided them, and then proved to the Jew in no uncertain terms why the Gentile could be saved.

When we get to Romans 10, it is interesting because he talks about the righteousness under the law and the righteousness by faith. Righteousness under the law did not change your nature. You had right standing with God, but that was through the sacrifice of animal blood. There was a right standing with God, but it was not righteousness by faith.

Romans says:

"The righteousness which is of faith speaketh on this wise,

Say not in thine heart,

Who shall ascend into heaven?

(that is, to bring Christ down from above:)

Or, Who shall descend into the deep?

(that is, to bring up Christ again from the dead.)"

In other words, who is going to bring Him down and crucify Him again for mankind? Who is going to descend into death and raise Him again?

Righteousness by faith says, "Do not say that in your heart." It says:

"The word is nigh thee,

even in thy mouth,

and in thy heart."

How can I do that? Because He is not going to come every year like the sacrificial lamb and provide it over and over again. He came one time, and it is forever settled in heaven and earth that He is not going to provide anything again.

Someone says, "It would be nice if He would heal me."

He already descended and ascended. He already came and provided the healing. He is not going to come down and provide it all over again for you. Faith, which is righteousness, says, "Do not say in your heart, 'Come and provide it again this year.'" What does it say? "It is already done. The rest is up to you. Put it in your heart. Put it in your mouth."

He is not going to provide it again. Somewhere along the line, you have to stop and accept what He has already done.

Paul said, "How beautiful are the feet" of the one who carries this message, this gospel of good news, of glad tidings, of good things, plural. Beautiful are the feet of the person who carries the message that Jesus has already come two thousand years ago and provided not only salvation, but good things.

I appreciate the fact that He destroyed the hold the devil had over me by destroying the nature I used to have, the nature that gave the devil control. When He destroyed that, He ultimately destroyed the hold that any devil had, especially the little diseases they create in their workshops.

The gospel is glad tidings of good things. Sinner, you do not have to be a sinner anymore. I do not have to be controlled by devils anymore. My reasoning does not have to be a victim of Satan's reasonings and pressures. I do not have to be a victim anymore. In fact, I do not have to be anything anymore except what He said I am.

This is why ministering to your soul is so important. In order for you to go from a place where you cannot to a place where you can, there is only one thing that will get you there: the Word of God.

Jesus said the servant, the one being taught, is not above his Master or Teacher. When He said, "Call no man on this earth teacher," He was saying, "If you are not teaching in agreement with what He says, I have no other teacher." I may accept what you say, but you had better be saying what He said, because as far as religion is concerned, I have no other Teacher.

Ministering to your own soul is important. I do not know if you noticed this or not, but if you get a mirror and take a close look, you will find out you do not have a glorified body.

We who have the firstfruits are born again. If there is anything else here filled with the Holy Ghost, he says we groan within that new nature, waiting for this whole adoption process to be made complete. He told you when it would be: at the redemption of your body.

That is why he said we have the firstfruits. If there are firstfruits, there has to be a second. The first member of the choir means another one is coming. We who have the firstfruits groan in the new nature, a nature that opposes anything against the Ten Commandments. We groan within ourselves, waiting for the adoption process to be made complete at the redemption of our bodies.

Are you telling me my body is not redeemed? Positionally, Jesus has redeemed it. Rights-wise, diseases do not have rights over you. But your body is quite natural, and you know it.

The adoption process will be made complete at the redemption. You ask, "Do you mean it is not redeemed?" It is redeemed from the effects of the curse. The devil has no right to kill you or put disease on you. But you have to honor the things your body demands from you because it is a natural creation. You have to have rest and all these kinds of things.

I know people who flipped out and said, "God has delivered me from rest." That is not your best day. You have to take care of your body.

When he talks about your body not being redeemed, he does not mean it is not redeemed from all the things He set you free from. But when this dispensation is over, what He has provided for you as a joint heir will be a glorified body. Meanwhile, we groan within ourselves waiting for this adoption process to be made complete.

As long as you have this body, and it needs to be glorified, this body served you well when you were spiritually dead, and it will serve you as well as it can when you are spiritually alive. But make no mistake: you have the same body. If you did not, it would not be dying now.

Your body can be resown. It has capacity to sin, and it has capacity to serve your righteous spirit. Your righteous spirit has more power than your body does. It has more power than the devil does. It has more power than anything the devil can do to excite the things that still have capacity to operate in your flesh.

The glorious part is that there is a way you can offer your soul up. There is a way you can offer your spirit up. There is a way you can offer your body up in the presence of God, ministering to yourself and ministering to yourself. Any part of you that is out of line and unable to believe what He said, you yourself can make a quality decision and pull it back into line.

You yourself can defeat it and overturn it. It is a glorious thing, and I love God for doing that.

There is one thing He enjoys so much from me: when somehow the new nature He gave me starts flowing with Him and His nature. When that begins to happen, He seems to derive some kind of fellowship from it. The Holy Spirit is the linkup.

It is the most amazing thing. God wants something from me. When I give it to Him for any amount of time, He seems to come. Like a good friend, He comes and does more, and more, and more for me.

It is almost like I welcome Him into the room and say, "I want that fellowship promised in heaven, but I want it now." When He starts coming because of this, He ministers to your soul and ends up ministering to your spirit. Ministering to your soul and spirit seems to suspend your spirit in His presence for some reason. He derives so much fellowship from that.

Here is one of the things He is doing for me. I should say He is doing it in spite of me. He wanted to do it for me before. We keep thinking we are the ones leading the charge and wanting to. He is the One leading the charge and wanting to. We are just finding that out.

This private worship thing: the more I think about it, the more I ask, "What do I have to give Him?" I am trying to figure out what the big deal is about us. We have been nothing but trouble since the garden. I am trying to figure out the order of our creation. I am trying to find out why He loves us so much.

That is something people say we are supposed to take by faith. But I have derived the greatest places in Him of peace, joy, and fellowship on this limb, discovering my Father and His love for me.

At times I have periods of enjoyment that get so strong it is hard for me to find anything to compare it with. When I kick back and the waves of it are on me, and I am in His presence like that, I think, "What can I compare this to?" That is a place where things suspend. You can stay there a long time because you do not want to leave.

He comes and blesses us like that because of private worship. I think, "What is He getting out of it?" He went through a lot of trouble to bring us to the place where He can have that fellowship. What is He getting out of it? Is He deriving joy and pleasure Himself from this kind of fellowship, even though there is a barrier of flesh between us, unredeemed in the respect of glorification?

When I am glorified, I will be popping in and out of His physical presence, and so will you. You will see the great compounds of worship where people gather before the throne. The trouble they have is stopping. Do not measure worship by what you have seen in the local first church. If you do, you will miss it.

If, through this veil of flesh, times of joy can become so ecstatic that I cannot find anything to compare them to, do you think standing there ten years worshiping Him will tax you? Someone will have to come and pull you away from the throne to get you to leave.

What I like about Him is that He wants something from me here. This is godly exercise. It profits both now and for eternity. Doing the things that please Him, the things that keep me in His presence, pleases Him and profits much both here and in eternity.

I am profiting much here. There are places of fellowship you can have even with this unredeemed flesh. These places will make you know there are greater places. It seems like such a shallow limit in fellowship, yet He seems to derive something from it.

When you go into His presence with flesh separating you from face-to-face encounter, even through the flesh you seem to be able to give Him enough that He keeps wanting to come back. He wants you to keep it up. You will begin to see the profit both now and in eternal life. I am talking about profiting in godliness, the kind of godliness that speaks of the eternity to come.

I have something He wants. He has something I want. He wants me, but I want Him. I would say we are even, but He wants me more than I want Him, so it is uneven.

Think about this: everything you have received from God has basically been Him to you. All the callings are Him through us to the world. The gifts of the Spirit are generally Him through us. Miracles, healings, wisdom, all flow through us for the benefit of others.

Even diversities of tongues, in the last three operations of it--tongues as a sign to the unbeliever, deep intercessional groanings, and tongues for interpretation--are all as He wills, and generally flow through us for others. Deep intercessional groanings are usually for others, though there is a kind that comes on you for yourself connected with edification.

But edification is the gift that goes to us, for us. It is one of the only gifts that flows to us for us. All the rest flow through us.

All God has is always flowing to us, through us, or for us. We really do not have much to give back. We are pleasing when we do exploits. We are pleasing when we get people saved. We are pleasing when we fulfill His will, because through redemption we are giving Him more people who will serve Him and give Him what He wants from them.

There is only one thing I can give Him. Everything else has come from Him, to me, for me, or through me. Just one thing. That is me. Even all the works we do for Him give Him more people, and then He ends up wanting the people. He wants me.

The great mystery to me is this: what is it about me that He wants? If that is not amazing enough, ask yourself, what is it about you that He wants?

There is something about you He wants. The only thing I can give Him is myself. One aspect of giving myself is the fellowship I can have with Him.

My flesh cannot offer Him too much fellowship. It is too crazy. I have to bring it under ascendancy and force it to spend time in His presence. But my spirit can give Him me.

That is my worship, my adoration, approaching Him as my Father and adoring Him for everything He is, fellowshipping with Him, thanking Him, especially through this dispensation of my redemption.

It will not be hard when I get there. You will have to pull me away from the throne. But it is difficult now. The doorway for the devil back into you is the flesh connected to your soul. It is hard sometimes to get this flesh under ascendancy. It wants pleasure, security, and many things it is willing to find outside God. It does not like to stand there and be left out.

The reason God gave you the mystery of praying in tongues is that as it flows out of you, your mind can be wandering down the street doing what it wants, and God still gets the last say because He has more than one part of you. He has the new nature and the authority in it, creating His own prayer for you according to your need, desires, and calling, praying it out and borrowing your authority.

Your old mind may be off somewhere figuring out how to take something away from somebody.

Somebody says, "I do not feel like I am really praying in tongues unless I try to pay attention."

If you are going to get enough prayer in to change you, you will have to forget that. I make my mind read the Word. I have trained myself. In fact, when I pick up my Bible, I notice my mouth is moving.

The flesh is crazy. In the holiest moment of your life, when you are receiving something, a thought can walk by, and under the anointing you go, "Oh man, I should not be thinking like that." Flesh is crazy. That does not mean you are walking in it when it throws a surprise on you. Birds can fly over your head, but you should keep them from building a nest in your hair.

The flesh usually does not like what I like to do in my spirit. It was quite a fight to whip it enough. In praying in tongues, the mind sometimes wanders, but I still get the same results because I am not the one creating the prayer.

"Thank You, Holy Spirit, for helping me. You are praying for me when I am being wacky and walking down the street. Holy Spirit, can You hear some of these thoughts that are trying to come into my mind while You are praying for me? You must not be able to, to keep creating that prayer."

After a while, your mind wears down and finally accepts its inevitable fate. When God begins to amplify Himself inside, those waves of anointing begin to take care of the flesh.

You begin ministering to yourself, your own soul, in the Word, hour after hour, in private worship. You can say, "I am going to give Him a couple of hours here."

If you listen to some popular teachings today, they will say things like, "If you think that scheduled hour praying in tongues is going to get God to do anything for you, think again. It is all by grace."

We are talking about things that amplify and enforce that standing of grace beyond what I see most people walking in. Do you think we are trying to earn anything by spending time with Him? No. I want to spend time with Him. I want to get to know Him.

When I enter the closet, the one thing I have to give Him is me. And He wants me. He does not just want me as one ant in an ant farm that has to stop Him on the street and say, "Do You remember me? I was the one that always carried the red leaf."

He knows you. He is God and the Father. He has the attributes of God, and He stepped forward as the Father to you. All your rights are in sonship. God has stepped forward as the Father. He is the One you are supposed to get to know.

To you, He will be your Father, yet He has the attributes of God because He is God. When He looks at you, He does not see a crowd. For you, you are all He sees. Concerning you, His whole world is just seeing you. When you think you have spent a lot of time with Him, He has another million years to totally pay attention just to you.

That is the kind of Father He is. He falls short in none of the ways any spiritually dead earthly father has fallen short. He has all the time of forever to listen to you and talk about things. The only thing He is impatient about is how long it takes you to start the relationship, because you have a wall of flesh between you and Him.

God cannot change. He can alter His form. God the Word did that when He stepped forward in the man Jesus. But He did not change. He is sovereignly immutable. He cannot change, nor can He be changed. The essence of what makes God is still standing there in that Man, absolutely unchanged.

God, Jehovah, has stepped forward as the Father. He did the same thing Jesus did, only He did not alter His outer form. He became God, your Father. That is who you are in sonship and in the creation of the universe. Of all the worlds and creatures, you can step forward and say, "I have come from a life born of You, and I am Your son."

Sorry, angel, you were created. Of course, you will not say that to an angel, because you will not be that mean. But my Father wants me. He wants me.

That is the miracle of all miracles. Only God is smart enough to do this. Who was Adam? Of the two times sons of God are mentioned, Adam and Jesus are both called sons of God. Everyone in between had been born spiritually dead since Adam's fall.

God lost His son. He lost Adam. Unless God makes it known, there is no way we can understand how Adam, with full rights in paradise, gave himself to something so thoroughly that if you belong to the family that was lost, God has to literally adopt you out of it and back into sonship.

Before the fall, Adam had sonship. God cut covenant with man after the fall. Before the fall, Adam was His son. To see the degree to which Adam was His son, you have to look at the second Adam.

When God gets Adam back, how does He want him back? Does He bring him back mutilated or shackled so he cannot hurt Him? If you had a child you lost to someone who taught him to hate you and want to kill you, how would you want him back? In a cage? Or with all the rights, freedom, and love of a child?

Only God was smart enough to have you born back into His family with all the rights and more than you had before you left.

Now that He has you back, what does He want you for? What are you and He going to do forever? He wants you to break the flesh down, come over with Him, and start spending time in fellowship so you and He can shake this planet.

The devil thought he was smart. He said, "I have a spiritually dead gene pool. I have You, God. You cannot illegally inject a man into the earth. That would be a whole other creation and not part of this one."

God said, in effect, "You think I cannot get a man into the earth? I will not start something new. I will become that Man in the human spirit. I will come and get him from the top."

He walked in and came after us.

How much does He love me? "My glory I give unto you, that the world may know You have loved them as You have loved Me." Since starting this fellowship, I found out He loves me enough that He is totally willing to start that throne-room kind of fellowship now, even though I can only take it up to the overload point of this flesh.

But believe me, that much fellowship with the Father is enough to turn this world upside down.

There was a woman in Brazil who said, "Dave. Dave. I don't know nothing. I don't know nothing."

She had gotten the worship series and started listening to it. Some area of her life that was really messed up, that needed forgiveness, just came gushing out of her one day and washed and cleansed her.

Why? Because you cannot invade that flesh, stay in His presence, and give fellowship to the Father in adoration without Him building Himself in fellowship with you, and building you, until all ugliness is going to suffer severely. You will not look for ways to keep hold of the flesh. You will look for ways to get out of it.

He wants me. He went through all of that to get me back with all my rights. The most amazing thing is that He wants to fellowship with me now.

When He said, "God so loved the world that He gave," that "so loved" goes past any confines we can understand. It belongs up there in deity. "So loved" can only be measured by deity. The world there is not cosmos in the sense of dirt and stars; it is the people on it. That is how much He loved me.

The love reached into deity and began to manifest itself for me. He so loved me that the Godhead got involved, altered itself, redeemed, and became the Teacher. He came to get me. He loved me that much before I was saved. He did this before any of us were saved.

He said, "I came not into the world to condemn the world; but that the world through Me might be saved." He did not come into the world as an alternative. He came into it as the answer, the only answer and the only door out of darkness.

If He loved me that much before He received me back into full rights and sonship, how much does He love the person next to me who has not received it? What will He do for me, His son, when my heart fills with compassion so much that I will do anything to see that person come to Christ? How much of Himself do you think He will give to me?

Will it be almost the walk Jesus has? What would He do in the hospital? Would He so disrupt the city that all these children come running out with little bags of candy, jumping on His lap, hugging His neck, and all the doctors wanting to know what happened, while the Pharisees try to put their heads together to figure it out?

He loves these people so much before they are even saved. What would He do with me if I fellowshiped with Him so much that I opened myself up to what He is, and that love began to pour through me on behalf of other people?

What keeps Him from doing it? Big, thick jackets of flesh.

In my spiritually dead spirit, I had some capacity to love, but most of it was based on whether the love would somehow benefit me. Natural love can reach pretty far. Men will die for causes. A man may love his wife enough to step in the way and die to keep her from dying. But all of that pales compared to what happened to you now.

When you traded your nature for His, that little balloon you had inside turned into an entire reservoir that can hold the capacity and compassion of the love that comes from God, not only for you as your Father, but for you and through you, mostly for other people whom He loves as much as you but does not have yet.

He wants something from you now. You do not have to wait. The more you minister to your own soul in psalms, hymns, and spiritual songs, making melody in your heart and giving thanks for all things to God and the Father in the name of the Lord Jesus Christ, the more your senses are baptized in the presence of God until it starts altering your thinking, receiving level, motivations, and directions.

When you go into impossible situations, you can feel His presence rise up. You can feel the anointing flood into impossible situations. That is from fellowship.

Who are you trying to benefit from all this? First, you. The rest comes later.

When the first miracle took place because of worship, it was a blind boy and a thumb that was jammed. I had seen quite a few miracles like that, but this one was different.

A schoolteacher, I think she was Baptist or something, was in the meeting. She wanted to leave. I said to bring her back to my office. She said, "I want a ride home."

I knew who brought her. I said, "She wanted prayer. Could you wait until she gets in the line and receives prayer?"

She said, "Oh no. I want to go home right now."

She was white and shaking. I said, "Ma'am, what happened to your arm?" She had a brace or cast on it.

She said, "It split."

I said, "Does it hurt?"

She said, "Oh yes, especially at night. Real bad."

I said, "Listen, I am not going to yell at you. You are not going to fall down or anything. All I am going to do is take you by the hand. Is that okay? You will not fall. Nothing will happen. I promise you."

She said, "Okay."

I cannot tell you the pleasure I felt to feel God, not with the anointing on the gift He gave me in my call, but from Him being in the room in presence. Every thought running through my mind was, "Can You do this all the time?"

I said, "In Jesus' name. Thank You, Father."

Being a schoolteacher, she woke up the next morning with no pain, totally healed. She went around the school telling everyone, "They are funny people, but when they pray, it works."

Finally, at noon, when her friend picked her up, she could not take it anymore. She called and said, "I want to go back to that church. I have insomnia."

I hardly noticed her because I was totally taken by the fact that He came so readily. If you remember, that is when He began to teach me, "You can have that. These signs shall follow them that believe. If you are the believer, these signs will follow them that believe. They shall lay hands on the sick, and they shall recover. If you are the believer, who are you laying hands on?"

I was beside myself. I had just injured the flesh a little bit, as far as fellowship was concerned, and He seemed to get so much out of it.

So what are you? Can anybody tell me? What are you? Worshipers? Sons of God? That is right.

I think God is going to keep doing this until you realize there is no other step at this point. It is the next important thing. It is the next qualifier if you want to go on the trip.

Remember last week when I had to lead you into it a little bit? Some of you were crying in a little while, and it was getting sweet in here. Do the same thing, only stay in for about an hour. Once in a while, call something out:

"I love You. I worship You. I glorify You. I magnify You. I exalt You."

Demand nothing but to love Him. Bring no reproach on this gospel through past failures. Tell Him, "Have I told You lately that I love You? I worship You, glorify You, and praise You."

Do not mind if your mind wanders off. Bring it back once in a while and make it pay attention. Watch the anointing come up.

Why don't you stand up? Then you can sit down any time after we start.
