1072-06 The Love He Cannot Develop for You

Very gently, you can be seated. It has taken me a minute to catch this one.

At this point, every time I get caught in one of these, He likes me to give the explanation. I can do so while I am waiting.

In Romans 12, he says every man should prophesy in proportion to the faith that is given him. All prophecy is given for edification, exhortation, and comfort. We understand edification and comfort. But to prophesy exhortation, there has to be the foundation of something that already exists.

He said to prophesy in proportion to the faith given to him, meaning he cannot prophesy beyond the level of the Word he is walking in and the ability to expound on it. This is especially true around holiness and most prophecy. Many prophecies sound like, "Thus saith the Lord, you will go, you will come, you will do this and that," more like direction or a word of knowledge.

But exhortation must be in proportion to the faith given me. I have to exhort from some foundation that already exists. If I am exhorting on building a building, it has to be on a blueprint. At least it has to be revelation to me.

All of a sudden, He starts saying things just on the edge of everything I am walking in. I have to wait for it to come together to even begin to prophesy it when it takes the form of interpretation. Many times He will use tongues to get something from the order of another direction and call attention to it, and then interpretation will follow. Sometimes He has to do that to me when I am strong in a direction, and He creates tongues in my spirit to pull me back into the mind of God and what He has.

The last time this happened was Wednesday night. God set us as a church into different kinds of worship, particularly private worship, because He wants your personal life to be affected as much as your corporate worship. He wants your personal life to be affected by revival as much as we want this city to be affected.

He was exhorting heavily on private worship, where it will bring you, and what it will do to you. Even though I had already broken into some private worship, what He prophesied stretched me just to the place where I could grab and understand what He was saying.

I kept asking Gary what the statement was because He had taken me so far. The prophecy was about the love of God. I had heard bits here and there, but suddenly they came together and I understood a picture. That truth was what I was able to prophesy from for a few moments, and it did something to my edification. It did something to my faith that is still shooting through me like everything is possible.

The heart of the prophecy was this: He gave you grace, and when His grace is enacted in you, He trades your weaknesses for His strength, your sickness for His health, and on and on. He even gave us a supernatural language of tongues, so when we consent to it, He can change us.

But when it came to loving Him, He said something that pulled out everything I have ever known to get the statement out. I had three tries at it, and the third time it came out. I thought, "Do not take credit for this, boy. You will get in trouble."

He said, "I cannot love Myself in you for you."

He cannot love Himself in you, in your place, or for you.

He wants fellowship with you. As your Father, He desires to develop a relationship with you where you really love Him with your heart. It is in that place that all things are possible.

He gave you a new nature that made you a partaker of His divine nature. Like any baby born into this world, Jesus said, "Except ye be converted, and become as little children, ye shall not enter into the kingdom of heaven." The newborn baby under the shadow was as close as you could get to typing the rebirth.

Out of the mouth of babes and sucklings, His newborn people in the kingdom, He perfected praise. Out of the mouth of babes and sucklings, He ordained strength to still the enemy. That ordained strength is the new nature.

When you came into the kingdom, you came in as a newborn. But what you received was a nature like your Father's, so much so that you are able to partake with Him in that nature. It is in that nature that you have the capacity to reservoir and develop the love of God that you love Him with.

He could give you the nature, but He could not come in and love Himself for you. He gave you the goods; you have to develop it.

There is unlimited capacity in me through that new nature. All limits are off, and I can give God what He wants: fellowship where I love Him with all my heart and have it come from me to Him.

This is the next step into receiving what God wants for you, for me, and for Tulsa. Do not think the mind of Christ is going to get far from it in any service. God is single in purpose. In His mind, He has one path and one goal for your life and for Tulsa: to come to the fullness in the Father that you can possibly come to before you get your glorified body and go home to check on your reward.

In Galatians, when Paul confronted Peter, his conclusion was:

"I am crucified with Christ:

nevertheless I live;

yet not I,

but Christ liveth in me."

He said, "I live," then clarified, "not I." If I live, yet it is not I, then Christ lives in me.

He also said I have the Spirit of Christ. Before I had the Spirit of Christ, I had the spirit of Adam. Did I have Adam's own personal human spirit? No, but I had the death I inherited from him when he fell.

Now I do not have the death I inherited from the first man Adam anymore. I do not have his spirit. I have the Spirit of Christ. Do I have Jesus Christ's personal human spirit? No, but I have the life I inherited from Him.

Therefore I no longer have the spirit Adam gave me. I have the Spirit Christ gave me, for I am a new creation. I have been crucified with Him. Nevertheless I live, but not I. The old man has passed away, and a new creation has come into existence. I have the life in me that was in Christ.

If you are a new man and a new creation, you also received the mind of that new creation. I have the Spirit of Christ because I inherited the nature that set me free from sin. Along with that new creation, I received a mind like Christ.

Do I have His personal mind? No. I have my own mind that belongs to the new creation. When I received a new nature, I also received a mind that can discern and understand spiritual things. You have the mind of Christ, but not His personal mind. You have the mind that comes with the new creation.

Who carries what is in Christ's mind to you? The Holy Spirit. Especially when you pray in tongues. If you are reading your Bible, especially the epistles Paul taught, you are reading the mind of Christ through Paul, put down on paper. But when you pray in the Holy Spirit, the Holy Spirit takes the mind of Christ and prays what is in it for your personal life, plan, pursuits, calling, equipping, power, edification, and the faith you are called to walk in.

Whatever is in the mind of Christ, which is in the Holy Spirit, that is what He is praying for you. The good thing is that you have the mind of the new creation that can understand what the Holy Spirit is praying when He reveals it to you, and you can become those things.

The natural man does not understand the things of the Spirit of God, because they are spiritually discerned. But I have the mind of the new creation. I have a spiritual mind that can discern and understand spiritual things. The Holy Spirit's calling in you is to guide you into all truth, and Jesus said, "Thy word is truth."

This mind I have for discerning and understanding spiritual things is different from the nature I have. When I was a sinner and had the sin nature, that nature enslaved my mind and made me serve it. That is the scenario Paul was talking about with the Jew who was delivered to the law and could not keep it.

Paul said, "I had not known lust, except the law had said, Thou shalt not covet." He did not know lusting was wrong because it was part of the nature he was born with. Then God came and said, "Stop." The good he wanted to do, he quit doing; the evil he refused, he ended up doing. Why? Because he had a sin nature that enslaved his mind and made him a servant to it.

Now that I have been born again, I have a new nature. My new nature bathes my mind in life and causes my mind to become a willing servant to the new nature that freed me from sin. Now the devil cannot make me sin because I do not want to.

There are two parts of my new creation. One is my spiritual mind that does not like to sin and does not want to. The nature that bathes my mind does not like to sin. Thank God I have the mind of Christ that can discern spiritual things, and I also have His nature.

With this mind I understand revelation knowledge. I crave to understand things about God. I want to know where He came from, what happened to the first man, why we needed to be saved, what happened when I was saved, how much power I can really walk in, whether I am really God's son, and whether all things really are possible to him that believes.

Do you crave to know things like this? Do you want to know that Noah walked on this planet fifty years into Abraham's life, and Abraham could have sat at the feet of the old one and heard about the early world? Do you want to know that Shem, Noah's son, outlived Abraham by twenty-five years? Shem was a hundred years old before the flood. He watched the destruction of the early world and knew why. The mercy of God had him on the earth as a witness between Abraham's ninety-ninth and hundredth year when God destroyed Sodom and Gomorrah.

What do you crave? Do you want to know where faith comes from? It sure is not from guessing about God or being sold a religious bill of goods that says sometimes He will, sometimes He will not, because He is sovereign and does what He wants. It does no good to have faith if He will not keep His Word. How can you have faith if He has not bound Himself to His Word? But if He has, and it is up to me to believe it, then look out, because everything really is possible to him that believes.

I am hungry to know Him, and I get a good deal of that hunger quenched from praying in tongues. Every piece of the mind of Christ I pray out speaks in mysteries.

First Corinthians 14 says that he who speaks in an unknown tongue speaks not unto men but unto God, and in the Spirit he speaks mysteries. The moment a man speaks the first tongue out of his mouth, he has put himself in the Spirit. What is transpiring in the Spirit? He is speaking mysteries.

What mysteries? Are you going to sneak up on God's blind side and whisper some deep, dark spiritual secret in His ear that He did not know two millennia before you graced this planet? These mysteries are not for God's benefit. A mystery is that which is out of the range of unassisted natural apprehension and can be made known only by divine intervention.

Praying in tongues, like any other form of prayer, is for your benefit and is designed by God to be answered. You cannot speak one hour in tongues through your own edification without praying out some aspect of the mystery of everything Christ is in you, to you, and through you, the hope of glory.

There was no book in the Bible with your name on it when you were born again. But you did receive a book, so to speak, when you were baptized in the Holy Spirit, because God's personal plan for your life is in His mind. You will not pray one hour without Him laying out part of that plan.

The mysteries you are speaking are the same ones hidden since the foundation of the world and now made manifest to the church: Christ in you, the hope of glory.

That makes a person want to run and pray in tongues.

I have the mind of Christ, the mind of the new creation. So do you. I have capacity to discern spiritual things, so I am hungry. I found out that most men do not know much, but the Holy Spirit knows everything. I will listen to anybody who knows something, but once I find out they have not been taught by the Holy Spirit, I lose interest.

There is another part of my new creation. Second Peter 1 says that by the exceeding great and precious promises you have been made a partaker of His divine nature. By these promises, I reached out, laid hold of God, and was born again. He made me a partaker of His divine nature.

Then He said, since you have been made a partaker of the divine nature and you have My mind, add to your faith. Faith comes by hearing, and hearing by the Word of God. Then he lists knowledge, temperance, virtue, patience, brotherly kindness, and love.

I know how to add more Word and revelation knowledge. I have been doing that for a long time. But I did not really know how to add more presence. I knew to believe what He was showing me, and that brought much equipping to my gifting and calling. But to add God's plain presence, I thought praying in tongues mysteriously charged me like plugging into 110 volts.

Little did I know that the way He edifies you from praying in tongues is by revealing things you need to do. So the revelation knowledge of worship came. I could have said, "I will just develop my love for You without worship," or, "I will just pray in tongues; that will be enough." But He kept delivering me to the worship limb until I started ministering to Him out of that new nature, where love develops.

One part of me, the things of the nature, develop. The other part, the mind of the new creation, discerns spiritual things. That is the mind of Christ.

He said, add to your faith these things, and you will not be barren or unfruitful in Christ, nor will you fall. No so-called generational curse or stronghold will survive an onslaught of praying in tongues and worship. He will smite the root, and the ugly fruit will rot and fall off.

I hated quitting smoking. I never inhaled. I would party all weekend and then have an all-night watch on Monday at sea on a destroyer. I could not fall asleep on duty. So I would get a cigarette and chew it. It woke me up, and that was better than a court-martial.

Grandpa got me with chewing tobacco. He had a plug of Day's Work, and I got to chewing his tobacco. Then I graduated to cigars and a pipe, and I never inhaled. But it was addictive. Whether nicotine comes through the saliva glands or the lungs, it still gets there.

After I got born again in a holiness church, they preached hellfire and brimstone. They said if you were not sanctified, that cigarette would send you to hell. I would run up, throw the stuff out of my pocket, go home saying, "I am free," and then on Monday buy another pipe and pouch of tobacco. Wednesday would come, and I would avoid church. Then Sister Pryor, a retired holiness schoolteacher, would be on my porch saying, "I know you are in there."

On the way home from church, I would throw my pipe away. Monday I would get another one. I was using will worship to try to put it down, because I did not know the difference between mortification and will worship.

Will worship depends completely on your own strength. Mortification happens when you learn how to loose the Holy Spirit inside you, and He rises up and destroys the hold feeding the problem. Eventually it dries up, and you get rid of it one way or another.

When I turned the Holy Spirit loose inside me, He did not come looking for somebody perfect. What I could give Him was that nature to work with. When He started praying for me out of that new nature, that was a prayer God could answer, and the mortification process began. I am so free now I can hardly stand myself.

He said I can add to my faith virtue, temperance, patience, brotherly kindness, and love. Since private worship, those things have empowered the revelations I have. When the Holy Spirit has your freedom in mind, He usually delivers you to the revelation that sets you free.

When He delivered me to fasting, I had major portions of the flesh to deal with. He kept delivering me to the things that would set me free in the areas I was believing for.

There is a way you can magnify the part of you that has the capacity to love God. It is you loving Him, because you are making the choice to develop that part of you that came into the kingdom as a newborn babe with the nature of God.

He said, "I cannot love Myself in you for you."

Let's talk about developing the part that loves: the part that has to do with emotion, joy, virtue, temperance, patience, brotherly kindness, and love. I can read in the Word that I am supposed to be that way, but how? What part of me can be developed?

Before you were born again, you had the same two kinds of parts. You had an emotional side that could love, enjoy emotion, be happy, and delight in life. You also had a part that craved knowledge.

Knowledge is not enough. The day I met Rosalie, knowledge was not enough. Something in me could not be satisfied except in her presence. There is a capacity to love. I love her more today than I did the day I married her.

Think of a computer expert who loves knowledge, codes, programs, and systems, and wants nothing else. Then one day someone walks down the hall and that person discovers love. From then on, knowledge is not enough. What he needs can only be found in the presence of the one he loves.

There comes a time when knowledge is not enough. Some things you can only get by being in His presence. Your born-again spirit has been made a partaker of His divine nature and has unlimited capacity to reservoir and hold the love of God, His temperance, virtue, kindness, and nature.

That is why we will spend these hours advancing further into worship, particularly private worship, even the kind that makes your confession stand up, salute, and go get what you are believing for. We are talking about going into the place where the part of you lives that believes what He says.

Go to John 16. Jesus is talking about the arriving of the Holy Spirit and what He will do.

John 16:12 says:

"I have yet many things to say unto you,

but ye cannot bear them now."

In other words, you cannot bear them because you cannot understand them. But you will when you receive a new nature and the Teacher, the Holy Spirit, comes to reveal them to you.

Verse 13 says:

"Howbeit when he, the Spirit of truth, is come,

he will guide you into all truth:

for he shall not speak of himself;

but whatsoever he shall hear, that shall he speak:

and he will shew you things to come."

Notice He did not say the Holy Spirit would push you. He could not push Adam, and He will not push you. He has come to guide you into all truth.

Verse 14 says:

"He shall glorify me:

for he shall receive of mine,

and shall shew it unto you."

Verse 15 says:

"All things that the Father hath are mine:

therefore said I,

that he shall take of mine,

and shall shew it unto you."

Jesus was speaking out of His humanity as the Firstborn Son of God. He said all things the Father has are His. The Holy Spirit will take what is His and show it to you. What does Jesus have that we want, and that glorifies Him? His standing with the Father as the Firstborn.

The Holy Spirit has been assigned to show me that standing, because I have been born again, and the place Jesus holds with the Father I hold too. He is the Firstborn. I do not know how many thousands down the line I am in the lineage of being born again, and I do not care. All I know is that the standing He has with the Father as the Firstborn is what the Holy Spirit has been assigned to show me.

I can identify with His humanity, but I will never approach His deity. But His humanity, and the place He holds with the Father as the Firstborn, the Holy Spirit can receive and show to me.

Jesus said all things are possible to him that believes. Revelation knowledge is not automatic by merit of the baptism of the Holy Spirit alone, or everybody would be saying the same thing. There must be a way to activate the Teacher inside me as often as I want, as long as I want.

I can pray out of the mind of Christ any time I pull myself in and begin to pray in tongues. I begin praying out of the mind of Christ, everything He is in me and through me, the hope of glory. He comes to guide me, but He will not push me. He does not make me pray, walk with Him, read my Bible, or go to church. He will not make me do what I choose not to do.

We have been calling revival and the legions of angels into the atmosphere: "Tulsa, Jesus is Lord over Tulsa, because Tulsa is in revival." That has been on people's lips for six years. How many can feel the harvest? It is close.

Guess who is more scared than we are? I am not scared at all. But if you have a problem with fear, I can tell you the devil is more scared than you are.
