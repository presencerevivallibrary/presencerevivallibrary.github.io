819-07 Jesus Was Married to Israel by the Law

Thank You, Jesus. We are ready. Amen. Hallelujah. This is going to be one of those nights, is it not?

Are you ready to continue our study on the laws that govern meditation of His Word? As you are seated, open to Luke 16, where we began this study on the laws of meditation Monday night.

We brought it up to this concept: Jesus was the greatest teacher who ever existed. He was the Master Teacher. Nobody who ever lived taught any better than Jesus. We have no trouble agreeing on that, do we?

Being the Master Teacher that He is, we found out in studying His teachings that Jesus never took a verse out of context. Never. In fact, He never went on to the next subject until He totally finished the one He was on.

Again, in passing review, if Jesus were teaching on this pulpit, using the mastery and imagery of words, He would begin at the top of the pulpit. By the time He reached the bottom, He would have painted a perfect picture in your spirit, the perfect image.

Therefore, a scripture is a thought within itself. It is a stroke of the brush, but invariably it is always building toward the completion of the entire picture.

It is us preachers who have taken verses out of context and destroyed faith. We usually have some preconceived, programmed idea of what God and religion is, so we search the Word of God to confirm what we already know to be right. We do not conform to the Word. We try to conform it to us. We want it to say what we believe, so we search the Word of God and pick out isolated scriptures that back up what our theology already is.

Then when faith does not work, everything falls off the Church: healing, the Holy Ghost baptism, speaking with tongues. Then the devil is after the born-again experience. He likes to turn it into some kind of ritual.

How did that all begin? It began with people losing the ability to extract counsel, revelation, and knowledge from the Word of God in the hands of their Teacher, the Holy Spirit.

If Jesus always taught in total and entire subjects, then what business do I have taking any of His thoughts out of context and using them to back up some foolish precept that I happen to think is God? You had better know.

Once again, for example, if I took a corn seed and smashed it into four fragments because some idiot told me I could plant the fragments and get a greater harvest, I would get nothing. The reason I would get nothing is because I would have destroyed the image that was in the seed.

Jesus' messages carry a message. They carry an image. Faith comes by hearing, and hearing, and hearing, and hearing by the Word. When I found this out, Psalm 1:1 really began to make sense, which has been our theme:

Blessed is the man that walketh not in the counsel of the ungodly, nor standeth in the place of the sinner, nor sitteth in the seat of the scornful.

Thank You, Heavenly Father. My spirit has already run one mile ahead in this message. You ought to see what I am doing. A whole other set is coming up while I am saying where I was a while ago.

Blessed is the man who does not walk in the counsel of the ungodly, nor stand in the way of sinners, nor sit in the seat of the scornful. What qualified me to be that blessed man? I do not take my counsel from the world, and I do not do things the way sinners do.

This man delights himself in the Word of God, and in it he meditates day and night. But the meditation is for the sole purpose of extracting the counsel, so I do not have to do things the way the world does.

We have been demonstrating what happens to the Word of God when we do not take verses out of context. Have you been having a good time?

Let us take this one step further in this chapter. Look at the first verse again:

And he said also unto his disciples, There was a certain rich man...

Now go to the nineteenth verse. What does the nineteenth verse say? That is real good. You people surprise me sometimes.

You mean verse 1 said there was a certain rich man, and verse 19 said there was a certain rich man? Is that right? Then tell me, did Jesus change the subject?

No, He did not.

If He did not change the subject, then maybe we had better back up to the eighteenth verse and see what He said there:

Whosoever putteth away his wife, and marrieth another, committeth adultery: and whosoever marrieth her that is put away from her husband committeth adultery.

I thought, "My God, poor Jesus. What all those preachers said about Him is right. He cannot stay on the same subject for more than twenty verses. He was doing real good, but come to find out, He preaches like most preachers I know. One moment they are shotgunning over here, and the next moment they are shotgunning over there. You go home and ask, 'What did they say?' 'Why, I do not know.'"

Of course you do not.

It looked like poor Jesus was teaching the same way. He was doing real well teaching on the stewardship of money. In fact, in the ninth verse He even gave us the proper use of money. He said, "I say unto you, use the power that is in money to get people born again with. In doing so, you will cancel their debts against God, which is the sin question, and you will lay them up into an eternal habitation, and they will receive you in when you get your reward."

Then He went on to say you cannot serve God and money. He kept teaching very well until He got to verse 18, and all of a sudden, for no apparent reason, poor Jesus just blurts out into the air, "Oh, by the way, boys, whosoever puts away his wife and marries another commits adultery. Do you have that?"

And they go, "Yeah, yeah, yeah."

Then He goes, "Now there was a certain rich man that fared sumptuously every day."

We have a problem here. He was still teaching on the same subject, and the law of meditation says that each verse correlates with the others. Every verse is a thought within itself. It is a stroke of the brush, but invariably it is always building toward the completion of the entire picture. That is where the faith comes from.

So I said, "Jesus, why in the world did You stick a verse in like that? It messed Your whole thrust up. I know people do that, but Jesus, it seems like You could teach better than that."

He can. We cannot understand better than that.

When God taught me to meditate, I passed over that chapter and said, "Lord, You did not change the subject. If there is anything in my spirit, I know that. Now, where did that verse come from? Why did the Author suddenly stick it in there? Did You really say it? Where did it come from? Is there a defect here?"

I kept meditating and meditating, passing over the whole teaching a hundred times, waiting for the Spirit to lift that verse up and stroke it into place.

Finally, one morning at two o'clock in my office at the house, everybody had gone to bed. I was meditating and passing over that thing probably God only knows how many times. Suddenly the Holy Spirit picked it up, stroked the brush, and my Master Teacher, whose commission is to guide me into all truth, took my hand and guided the stroke. The whole thing fell into place.

I screamed, threw the Bible up in the air, ran down the hall, and woke my wife up.

Long-suffering. I explained the revelation to her while she sat there going, "That is real good," patting me on the back while I was explaining one of our greatest feats up to that time.

I used to think believing for a Cadillac was the greatest achievement. Then an airplane. My God, when I believed in my first airplane, I joined my ranks with "who's who." I used to think that was the greatest feat.

I do not think so anymore. You say, "What is the greatest feat?"

The greatest feat is when I am seeking the kingdom of God and His righteousness first, and the Holy Spirit takes my hand, guides the stroke of the brush, and speaks eternity in my spirit that brings forth one more thing from the realm of the Spirit that spells F-R-E-E-D-O-M: freedom.

That is what turns me on. That turns me on.

Immediately I walked into the class, the advanced evangelism class that I had. My chest was sticking out, and I said, "I have a verse for you, class, to break. I will give you two weeks. I will give you until Sunday after next."

Then I said, "I am going to assign each of you about three minutes to stand up and explain what this verse means."

Little did I tell them that it took me at least the latter part of two years to break it. But I did not want to tell them. Going to the Greek did not do any good. The Greek said the same thing: whoever puts away her and whoever marries her commits adultery. It said exactly the same thing. So I had to get it from God in context.

You ought to have heard them. They would get up and give their little speeches. It was great. Some of them would stand up and say, "There is no way, shape, or form that this is talking about anything other than a man putting away his wife and committing adultery. It is talking about marriage. There is no other way."

Some of them took their stand on that. I said, "Are you sure?"

"Yes, we are sure. You have given us an impossible assignment."

Then some of them would stand up and say, "Well, Israel married money. That is what happened. They got married to money, and when they did, they forsook God."

I said, "Then if they married money, who was married to God that they put away? Who was put away? If they forsook God to run off with another woman, and that other woman was money, then who married her that was put away after he married the other one?"

They would say, "Oh."

But I said, "Go on." As the line went down, they got more and more scared to stand up and say anything. One of them got very close.

If you and I are going to understand this in context as we study these laws of meditation, then we have to at least back up to the tenth verse where we left off. Are you ready?

Now, you know me well enough to know we are going to do it.

We actually left off at verse 9, so let us take it to verse 10:

He that is faithful in that which is least is faithful also in much: and he that is unjust in the least is unjust also in much.

If therefore ye have not been faithful in the unrighteous mammon, who will commit to your trust the true riches?

And if ye have not been faithful in that which is another man's, who shall give you that which is your own?

If you have not been faithful in handling the power that is in money, in unrighteous mammon, money and mankind, if you have not been faithful as a steward on this earth in handling that, who will commit to you the spiritual things, or the true riches?

What are true riches? It is not hard to figure out, even though we are not going to stay on that. It is God entrusting you with eternal things, like miracles and the gifts of healing. It is tremendous. But if you cannot be faithful in handling something temporary that He gives you, how in the world is He going to give you eternal things, true riches? How is He going to do it?

He went on further to say, "If you have not been faithful in that which is another man's, who shall give to you that which is your own?"

All I am here is a steward. Nothing I have did not come from Him. The question is, what am I going to do with it?

Then He made quite a statement:

No servant can serve two masters: for either he will hate the one, and love the other; or else he will hold to the one, and despise the other. Ye cannot serve God and mammon.

Notice this: the two masters He listed here were God on one hand and money on the other. He said you cannot serve both of them. You have to serve one or the other.

This is most interesting, because that statement really made the Pharisees mad. It made them so mad that in verse 14 it says they began to deride Him. That means they challenged Him, screaming from the floor, challenging His doctrine, contending with everything He was saying. That is what deriding is.

I have never made a bunch of people so mad that they challenged me from the floor. So I thought, "Jesus, what in the world did You say that caused these Pharisees to get so mad?"

In my meditations, the Holy Spirit kept painting these pictures. He kept painting this entire outline until I could actually see in my spirit these Pharisees sitting there listening to Him. The veins in their necks were sticking out. Their faces were puffed out. There was a violent, vehement, hateful look in their eyes.

Jesus was saying as they sat there listening to Him, "You cannot serve two masters. You will hate the one and hold to the other, love one and cleave to the other, despise the other. You cannot serve God and money."

I said, "God, what was in that statement that made them so mad? What do You mean, you cannot serve God and money?"

When God first liberated me from poverty, it was not poverty that He set me free from first. It was deception. He delivered me from deception, and then came the faith that delivered me from poverty.

I do not mind telling you, I drove an old car. My God, it had 160,000 miles on it when the speedometer broke, and the police stopped me six times in one 150-mile trip to see what was in the smoke screen. You know why I hated red lights? Because I got asphyxiated at them.

That car complemented my house. I used to buy my tires at the junkyard for four dollars apiece as long as the cords were showing. I would put four of them in the car, head off on a long trip, have a popping good time with blowouts, and just put another one on.

The house? It did no good to pull the curtains. If you wanted to know whether I was home, you could just look through the wall of that house.

Both of them complemented my suit. When I first started preaching, I was trying to preach in anything I had, and people felt sorry for me.

"Did you have any money?"

No, I was way behind on everything.

"Did you have any money?"

No, it went as fast as we got it.

My preaching suit was something else. One man gave me the pants. Could I help it if they were bright red? Another man gave me a bright blue plaid sports coat. Another man gave me a green shirt. Another one gave me a gold tie.

I parked that car behind the hall, walked out, and preached to all five people who turned out on a little bitty paper ad to hear me. I came out and preached up a storm. Then I said, "Do you all want what I have?"

They all just smiled so patiently at me.

I want you to know that because I loved Him so much, and because I was praying so much in tongues and working the plan of God in my life, God got to me a Hagin tape and a Copeland tape. I started chewing Copeland and Hagin, and they said, "Boy, you do not have to be poor anymore."

Deception started giving way. Pretty soon, one year, twelve months from the day I got my first Hagin tape and began to pray in tongues, parked out in front of our two-story house with the down payment made, there was a 27-foot motor home paid for, a brand-new Volkswagen van paid for, and a brand-new Volkswagen Bug for my wife paid for. All in twelve months.

I went all over Oregon with the good news: "Poor, you do not have to be poor anymore, for Jesus has borne your poverty."

God, I thought they would be happy. They were not happy. In fact, they puffed up like toads and got their prophet rocks from under their chairs.

I said, "For which miracle are you going to stone me?"

"Not because of the miracles, but because you think God is a great big Visa card in the sky."

I said, "Look, I do not know all there is to know about this, but I do not think He is that."

They hurt my feelings.

One morning, while I was staying in a pastor's house, somebody had actually invited me to preach in their church. The night before, a man had hitchhiked into the meeting. He was staying at the pastor's house too, because he did not have a place to stay.

The next morning we came in for breakfast, and there was the hitchhiker. All the time I was trying to eat my oatmeal, and I can remember it was oatmeal because his eyes were boring a hole in my face. Every time I tried to take a bite, I became self-conscious because he kept staring at me.

I knew it was coming. I knew that look.

"Tell me something, Brother Roberson."

I said, "Yes."

"You and Kenneth Copeland, Kenneth Hagin, and Fred Price, with your get-rich-quick schemes, this faith fallacy, fleecing the sheep..."

In those days, I was not nearly sanctified. My blood boiled. I got so mad that his mouth was fading off into the distance, and I could see it moving. My exterior was trying to remain calm, but my blood was boiling.

The Spirit of the Lord rose up and said, "Calm down, Roberson. Calm down. If you will calm down, I will give you the answer to give this man."

I said, "Yes, sir. Yes, sir." I breathed deeply and calmed down.

He wound down with these words: "I have my pack sack. I hitchhike up and down Interstate 5. God supplies my need. I have everything I could possibly want. I witness to people who pick me up. What do you say to that?"

I said, "Okay, Lord. You said You would give me an answer. What do You want me to say?"

Before I could even think about it, it popped out of my mouth:

"Sir, you are selfish."

"Selfish?"

I cannot remember his exact wording, but it was something like, "I beg your pardon."

I said, "You are selfish."

"Me?"

"Yes, you."

"Why do you call me selfish?"

I thought, "Uh-oh. Okay, Lord. Here we go again."

Immediately this verse popped in, and it was that one:

No man can serve two masters... Ye cannot serve God and mammon.

I said, "Sir, I think you think a man who serves money is the man who forsakes everything, forsakes his family, uses Christians, and walks on Christians in his trek for the almighty lust that is in that dollar, until he will use anybody who can be used, Christian or not. You think that is what serving money is, do you not?"

He said, "As a matter of fact, I do."

I said, "Let me tell you another way you can serve money. In the life God has dealt to every man, one talent, two talents, three talents, or several, according to each one's ability. He told them all to invest the talent. The one buried his, and at the coming of the Lord, he was held accountable for not investing that one talent into the kingdom."

"In light of that, Jesus has borne our poverty, and He has invested in us for the kingdom of God's sake. Sir, you have buried your talent. Therefore, poverty is dictating to you what you will or will not do for the kingdom of God. Therefore, sir, you are serving money because it has become the lord that told you what you would not do for the gospel."

There are two ways you can serve money. It can force you to bow your knees to it in the trek of the lust for money. When you find yourself using people to do that, then you are serving the junk.

The second way is when Satan deceives you into thinking that poverty is the will of God when it is not. Therefore, poverty becomes your lord and master because it dictates to you what you will or will not do for the kingdom of God.

In either case, you have served the filthy stuff because it has become your dictating master.

The first thing the Lord showed me in that verse was this. He said, "Son, if you become an ultimate servant to Me and Me alone, then in proportion to the degree that you serve Me, I will cause money to become a servant to you."

You do not think His preaching there made the Pharisees mad? You do not think they were mad by then? Their faces were red. Their veins were sticking out. Hatred was pouring out of their eyes. They were violent. They were vehement. They began to deride Him. They began to challenge Him from the floor:

"Who do You think You are calling us covetous? We know whose seed we are. We are the seed of Abraham. We do not even know what kind of stuff You are preaching."

They were so mad.

How do you know? Look at verse 14:

And the Pharisees also, who were covetous, heard all these things: and they derided him.

But thank God, when you went up against the wisdom of Jesus Christ, you were outmatched. You do not think He had an answer for them? By the time He shut their mouths, it took them a week to get them back open again.

Look at His answer:

And he said unto them, Ye are they which justify yourselves before men; but God knoweth your hearts: for that which is highly esteemed among men is abomination in the sight of God.

In other words, "As long as you appear to be righteous before men, in your hearts you really do not care. But God knows your hearts. That which is highly esteemed among men is an abomination in the sight of God."

He said, "You Pharisees will devour widows' houses. You will take everything she has, and then you will make long religious prayers of pretense to cover up for it. Let Me tell you something, boys. On the outside you are as whited sepulchers, but on the inside you are full of putrid, rotting, decaying bones. You are an abomination in the sight of God."

Then, all of a sudden, when these Pharisees were so mad, Jesus made a statement that took me two years to understand. The reason I could not understand it was because I took it out of context.

Look at verse 16. All of a sudden, for no seemingly apparent reason, Jesus cries out:

The law and the prophets were until John: since that time the kingdom of God is preached, and every man presseth into it.

In the laws of meditation, I kept saying, "Jesus, why did You say that?"

It finally dawned on me in meditation, over and over, when I saw the whole thing take place. They were deriding Him, challenging the very root of His doctrine.

"We do not know what You are preaching. We are the seed of Abraham. We know we are of God. We do not know where You came from."

They were challenging and deriding Him. In answer to their challenge, He said, "Boys, let Me tell you something. The law and the prophets were until John the Baptist. Since that time I have come on the scene, and you are right: I am preaching the kingdom of God. Before this is over, in answer to your derision, every one of you is going to have to press into it."

If that were not enough, in verse 17 He said:

And it is easier for heaven and earth to pass, than one tittle of the law to fail.

They were deriding Him, challenging the root of His doctrine, and He said, "You are right. I am preaching the kingdom of God. You are going to have to press into it. But in answer to your derision that I am preaching something other than the law, let Me tell you something: it is easier for all of the heavens and all the earth to pass than the tiniest crossing of the law to fail to come to pass."

What we have done is separate verse 18 from verse 17. In doing so, we destroyed the spiritual thrust of the meaning in verse 18. As a matter of fact, we have destroyed marriages on the eighteenth verse.

How many know God was married to Israel through the law? God was married to Israel through the law. Verses 17 and 18 are one thought. One thought.

They were challenging Him for preaching something other than the law. They challenged Him for forsaking the law. His answer to them was in verse 17:

It is easier for heaven and earth to pass, than one tittle of the law to fail.

Why?

Whosoever putteth away his wife, and marrieth another, committeth adultery.

In other words, "I cannot put the law away. It is easier for heaven and earth to pass than the law to fail. I cannot put it away, because if I put the law away, by which I am married to Israel, I will be in adultery."

He was not talking about marriage in the Church of God today. When He talked about marriage in the Church of God in two other places, He included the clause of fornication. He was not talking about marriage in the Church of God today. He was talking about a change of covenants.

He said, "It is easier for heaven and earth to pass than one tittle of the law to fail, for whosoever puts away his wife and marries another commits adultery."

Jesus was married to Israel by the law. He was saying, "I cannot put the law away. I would be in adultery."

Now briefly, let us go over to Romans chapter 7. Are you learning anything?

Romans 7:1 says:

Know ye not, brethren, (for I speak to them that know the law,) how that the law hath dominion over a man as long as he liveth?

Did the Pharisees know the law? You bet your boots they did. If they were going to be a minister, a Pharisee, they had to study the law and memorization for seven hours a day from the age of three.

He said, "I speak to them that know the law, that the law hath dominion over a man as long as he liveth."

For the woman which hath an husband is bound by the law to her husband so long as he liveth; but if the husband be dead, she is loosed from the law of her husband.

So then if, while her husband liveth, she be married to another man, she shall be called an adulteress: but if her husband be dead, she is free from that law; so that she is no adulteress, though she be married to another man.

Then he sums it up in verse 4:

Wherefore, my brethren, ye also are become dead to the law by the body of Christ; that ye should be married to another, even to him who is raised from the dead, that we should bring forth fruit unto God.

What caused Israel to become dead to the law? The body of Christ, so that she should be married to another, even to Him who was raised from the dead, and bring forth fruit unto God.

Jesus was married to Israel through the law. When they challenged Him for preaching something else, He said, "How can I? It is easier for heaven and earth to pass than one tittle of the law to fail. For whosoever puts away his wife and marries another commits adultery, and whosoever marries her that is put away from her husband commits adultery."

What did He know? Jesus knew He was going to die. When He died, it would widow Israel and free her to marry another, even Him who was raised from the dead, Christ Jesus.

If Israel stayed married to her whom Jesus had widowed through His death, then her offspring would be bastards. But the ones who married Jesus through His resurrection, their offspring would be fruit unto life.

Therefore, whosoever marries her that was put away commits adultery. He was talking about a change of covenant.

What about these laws of meditation? How about these laws of meditation?

Then in verse 19, after He shut their mouths, He went right back and said:

There was a certain rich man...

If you do not think their mouths were shut, they were shut.

You might as well go back over to Luke 16. I am going to give you this one example because I know how close He is to total change here.

For a little while I belonged to a holiness church. The way I got into it was this: I was born again in a real holiness church, but it was not as holy as the one I joined later.

I moved to Oregon, to a mill town of about six or seven hundred people. There was only one church in the whole town that spoke with tongues. As far as I was concerned, that was the only difference. If they spoke with tongues, they were okay. So I cleaved to them. I ran over and joined up.

They made me take a covenant. "Raise your hand. I swear an allegiance to the church. She is the only church under God, indivisible." That was the one we had to take. We rewrote it. We pledged allegiance to that church.

They made us take our rings off. We could wear no rings, no jewelry. I remember the poor ladies were groping for femininity. They would take little watches, because they could not wear jewelry, and ask, "Is it okay if we hang a watch on a chain?" They were groping to be feminine. It was so pathetic.

They had absolutely no makeup. They wore little buns on the back of their heads that were so tight that when they blinked, they jumped up and down. That is okay if you like that style. But if they say something is wrong with your godliness if you do not wear it, then there is something wrong with them. That is all I am saying.

Our women washed out barns, threw dresses over them, and looked like they had shaved their eyebrows. Women sometimes look like their eyebrows are shaved if they do not wear makeup. The same men would go paint their barns, but they would not paint their wives. It is hard to understand, is it not? They wanted their barns to look better than their wives.

That was part of the code. No rings, no nothing.

They also took that verse out of context and had that one marriage thing. Now, do not get me wrong: I am not advocating divorce. God hates divorce, but He loves the divorcee. He is not through with you. Somebody else cannot mess your life up for you. Only you can mess your life up. You are the only one. Somebody else cannot go to heaven or hell for you. You are the only one.

They took that verse out of context. I do not care if you were married when you were sixteen years old, had a child, and he ran off with a bunch of women. If you went and got married again, they said you had to divorce that husband and go back to the one you married when you were sixteen, or else you would go to hell.

I said, "But it looks like they got saved and filled with the Holy Ghost."

They said, "Yes, they do. They are that way until they get home."

It is pathetic, but it is true.

I backslid for a while because of the teaching in holiness. Then the way I got involved with that church in the first place, I was ashamed to go back to the one I had backslid out of.

My brother came to visit, and I wanted to get him saved even though I did not think I was. So I took him down to the closest church to get him saved because I did not even know I could get him saved. God, we were equipped.

Then he got my sister saved in this church. My sister had been married when she was sixteen and had a child. Her husband ran off with a woman. Well, she was not much better because she ran off with a man. They were about equal. They were both aliens.

I interceded for her for years. When she had a conversion, it was a powerful one. She loved the husband she was married to then.

When they got born again, the elders of the church finally got brave enough to come and say, "You will have to divorce your husband or you will not be saved. You must go back to your first one. If he is married to somebody else, you will just have to wait."

You do not think things like that are going on? They are. They really are.

So my sister was in a dilemma. She wanted to stay saved. She had such a conversion that heaven was a reality. They set their divorce date so they both could stay saved.

Do you know what God did? He got hold of me in Oregon, and just before their divorce date I came walking up on the porch. My little hellion sister threw her arms around me and began to cry. She told me about her conversion and about seeing me in the Spirit coming up on the porch in a nighttime dream. She saw me and knew that whatever I said, she was supposed to listen to.

Brother, I started taking her through the Word of God. I started showing her. The thing I said that finally convinced her was this:

"Donna, I have a .357 stainless steel magnum. It is a little bit smaller than Clint Eastwood's, but it will do the job. I will load it for you, and we will track your first husband down. Then you and I will sneak in when that first one is asleep, and we will put a .357 magnum slug right between his eyes."

I said, "Because the Church will forgive you for murder. In some circles, they will promote you: 'Ex-murderer gives testimony at breakfast.' How many of you have ever heard, 'Ex-adulteress survives condemnation from second marriage and gives testimony at breakfast'?"

The thing that finally got me out of that church was a woman who had seven children and was born again while I was in that church. She had been married to a man before this one for three months when she was a teenager, and then he ran off. She did not even know where he went. Later she remarried a man who dearly loved her and had seven children by him. Then she got born again in this church.

Since I had taken their covenant, they let me in on their elderly counseling sessions. In other words, I was esteemed as one of their elders.

So I sat there and listened to the preachers from the district. I listened to them cast lots back and forth over who was going to go tell this woman. It made me so sick. When one of them finally told her, she did not choose the church. She did not choose Jesus. She chose the husband and the children she loved so much.

To this day, I do not know. As far as I know, she is living under the condemnation that she still thinks she is going to hell.

Well, I think a lot of people are going to be surprised who wakes up there.

Thank God for the laws of meditation. Thank God for our Teacher, our Teacher in the person of the Holy Spirit, who, if we will take time, will take these divine secrets and mysteries and make them a devil-stomping, I-am-free reality.

Glory to God. Hallelujah.

My sister did not fall under the condemnation of that. Man, not only did we get her, we got my brother. Not only did we get my brother, we got the district overseer. Not only the district overseer, we got the pastor of that church. We got them all into the Word of God.

I know where I am going, and I know how I am getting there, because I know the Man who is bringing me there. His name is Jesus, and He has taught me how to meditate.

Give Him a hand. He is worthy.
