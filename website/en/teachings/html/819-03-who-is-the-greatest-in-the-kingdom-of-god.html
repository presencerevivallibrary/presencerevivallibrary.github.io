819-03 Who Is the Greatest in the Kingdom of God

I want you to remember this: we are studying more the method of extracting revelation knowledge than we are the revelation knowledge itself. The method of extracting it. Thank You, Jesus.

Matthew 18. We will begin with the first verse.

In these particular kinds of messages, you will have to hang in very close with me, because as we study these laws that govern the meditation of His Word, one verse will breed the meaning of the next, and the next, and the next, and so on. In other words, one set of verses will not make any sense to you, and they probably never have. But the reason they are not making any sense is because you did not understand the set of verses before it.

We demonstrated some of that last night, just briefly. But today we get to study the actual method.

In Psalm 1, which you do not have to go to, and which we will be quoting frequently in these teaching sessions, it says:

Blessed is the man that walketh not in the counsel of the ungodly, nor standeth in the way of sinners.

Analyzing that briefly, if there is anything on God's green earth that Dave Roberson wants to be, it is a blessed man of God. I do not care if denominations call me blessed or people call me blessed. What I am concerned with is if I am a blessed man of God, if His evidence is in my life.

What qualifies me to be that blessed man? Number one, he walks not in the counsel of the ungodly. He does not receive his counsel from the world. Number two, he stands not in the way of sinners, or he does not do things the way sinners do. Nor does he sit in the seat of the scornful.

If I am not going to take my counsel from the world, if I am not going to do things the way sinners do, then I had better take this one step further, which is Psalm 1:2. His delight is in the law, in the Word of God, and in that Word he meditates day and night.

What is the purpose of this meditation? It is not so I can out-quote more verses than Gordon, and not so I can appear to be so intellectual. The purpose of meditation is to extract the counsel from the Word of God so I do not have to take my counsel from the world or stand in the way of sinners, but have counsel from the Word to walk by.

Therefore, in a quick analysis this round, what is meditation? It is not memorization, although memorization will assist your meditation. It is not study, although study will assist your meditation. Meditation is not reading, although reading will assist your meditation.

Meditation is when you allow the Holy Spirit to get involved with your spirit in such a way that He actually takes the counsel that left the heart of God in the form of the Word and reconstructs it in your heart, so that it becomes the understanding counsel you and I need to walk by, counsel that liberates us from the counsel of the world.

Hallelujah.

Meditation is when you allow the Spirit to get involved with you in such a way that you can actually extract the counsel that left the heart of God in the form of the Word. Jesus said:

I am going to give you the number one rule of Jesus Christ, for I have patterned my teachings and my studies after the Master Teacher Himself. I have found no better Teacher in all the writings of the world than Jesus Christ.

Part of what He saw His Father do was through the imagery of the Old Testament. Part of what we see our Father do is through the imagery of the New Testament. That is part of it. The other part is the gifts of the Spirit.

We have no trouble believing that Jesus Christ was the Master Teacher, the greatest Teacher who ever lived, totally yielded to the Spirit.

Then I found out this number one rule about Jesus Christ: He always taught in total and entire subjects. To write that down, you would say He never took a verse out of context. Ever.

Jesus always taught in total and entire subjects. He never went on to the next subject until He completely finished the one He was on.

We are the ones guilty of that. Us preachers will conceive some doctrine in our thinking, and then we search the Word of God to get scattered verses to prove our theology. In doing so, we create doctrines of flesh, and they destroy us. It is as simple as that. If Jesus was teaching at this pulpit, He would begin at the top, using the energy and mastery of words. "They did me wrong. Anything else you can think about them?"

"Yeah, yeah."

When two or more enter into that kind of agreement, the devil is in your midst to carry your agreement out. He says, "Good. You just gave me authority to dominate that little one and you too."

If he hears you, that is good. But what if he does not? Thank God for the Word of God, because it gives you the next step.

Step number two:

But if he will not hear thee, then take with thee one or two more, that in the mouth of two or three witnesses every word may be established.

Now what you do is go get two or three witnesses who are on the same mature level as you are. Then you take them with you to talk to this person. They are supposed to stand and judge what one did to the other, deem one or the other wrong, and counsel them, so that more than one is telling him what he did was wrong.

If He is truly after this little one who went astray, which I guarantee He is, then the Lord checked me. He told me in my spirit, and I could hear Him:

"Look. I said, go to him. If he will not hear you, take three witnesses. If he will not hear them, then get the counsel of the church. If he will not hear the church, I said, let him be unto thee as a heathen man and a publican. I did not say you be one unto him. He is determined to be one to you, so let him be one to you, because you have already taken all the steps in the natural for his return."

So He says, "He is going to treat you like one, so let him treat you like one."

"All right, but how about my $50,000?"

I still had myself on my hands, and I did not realize what God was saying until the next verse. I said, "How about my $50,000?"

He said, "Thank God for step four."

Step four reads:

Verily I say unto you, Whatsoever ye shall bind on earth shall be bound in heaven: and whatsoever ye shall loose on earth shall be loosed in heaven.

If that was not enough, He says:

Again I say unto you, That if two of you shall agree on earth as touching any thing that they shall ask, it shall be done for them of My Father which is in heaven. For where two or three are gathered together in My name, there am I in the midst of them.

Where did the two or three people come in? I will tell you where they came in. These two or three people were in it almost from the beginning. Who in the world do you think He was talking about when He said, "Go get mature witnesses and call them in to judge and weigh every word"?

They are in it from the beginning. They know the man's problem. You go get them in legal agreement with you. Jesus says, "I will get in your midst on the legal agreement. When you draw this thing up and you cancel the man's debt, I am there in the midst of all of you and these witnesses in this legal agreement. These witnesses signed this legal agreement in the Spirit. I am in your midst. He will be loosed, and I will bind the devil out of his life, and I will return him to the fold. For I, Jesus, personally will go after him and bring him back."

Oh, hallelujah.
