603-03 A Place Called Done Brings Exceeding Joy

As you know, my family has been through a couple of really rough months. But the joy that is resident is beginning to make its move on me again, the really deep-seated joy, and it makes me want to know more about it.

This is the way I have been explaining it, and I have not been able to do better.

I cannot tell you how many times I have been promised certain amounts of money. At first you really plan for it, then it does not come through. After a while you even quit planning. You just wait.

You may be happy, excited, full of joy, and emotional for a period of time, but you survive all of it because of the deep-seated peace you have. Peace can sustain you when all hell is turning loose around you.

But this is the difference between peace and joy.

Joy is what it feels like when somebody comes up and says, "I am not going to put one million dollars in your bank account. I am going to put five million in, and you are going with me because I am going to do it right now. Just to prove I am going to do it, I will cash out one million for you and put it in your hand as cash, and then you can put it back in the bank."

Then you are walking around with the power of that five million dollars in the bank. Tremendous joy.

"See that car? I could have that if I wanted. See that house? I could have that house if I wanted it."

What could you not have? Not much.

What joy feels like is when you do not have the five million, but you feel like you do. You feel like you do.

"But I do not have a thing."

Why are you happy?

"Because of my exceeding joy."

"But you should not be full of joy. Look at the mess you are in."

Do you not get it? Everything here is temporal. It is all temporal. I am not going to be in this long. So why should I waste one of my breaths not full of joy?

I do not think so.

The more you shout out the victory, the more it is going to apprehend you and take you on a journey that will manifest itself.

The joy I experienced came after the defeat of something Satan really tried to destroy my whole life and ministry with. When I won, that was the first time I experienced this kind of joy. It had sustaining power like nothing I had ever walked in.

Yet the walk to that kind of joy is just as systematic as any other blessing He has for us. It seems to add a dimension to a person's walk and life that fills, not just feels. It fills your life with the life you are looking for.

What pulled me within range of that kind of joy? It was when I first started breaking into the room. Do you remember when I first started using the terminology "a place called done"? It was when I began to touch that place.

This place called done is the place of faith admired by every preacher trying to get anything done, every preacher who has ever gone overseas, every preacher who has broken through to the phenomenal supernatural.

This place called done is the most connected thing with grace you will ever get hold of.

When we think about grace, we usually break it down into our religious terms. We say, "It is the unmerited, unearnable standing of God," then look at each other like we know.

Indeed, it is the unmerited, unearnable standing you and I receive from Him. We know that mostly concerning righteousness, because we understand we could not be saved without Him. But we know very little about grace, the place called done, and everything else He has provided for us. If we knew, worry would be something we knew nothing about.

With righteousness, it is not hard to explain. Even the difference between mercy and grace is vast. Mercy is mostly what He has toward the sinner. Grace is mostly what He has toward the believer.

As hard as I have tried, I cannot come up with a better definition than the one He gave me in the Spirit one day after praying the mystery of mercy and grace before the Father.

You and I were thrown to the other side of a void of spiritual death. We could not go over to God, and really He could not come over to us in fellowship. But in a great act of mercy, Jesus, the mighty Logos, became a man. The only way He could get across the void of spiritual death was to walk through it. He had to come in where we were to take us out where He is.

Before He crossed that void of death, you could stand on the edge of it and beg God to save you, and He had nothing to save you with. But in an extreme act of mercy, Jesus crossed that void, died in our place, and shed His blood, so you could pick His blood up, walk to the edge of that void, and say, "Can You save me now?"

God said, "Yes, I can, because of the mercy Jesus Christ had on you."

Through that mercy you are born again, and His grace comes into manifestation because He gives you something you could not earn.

The difference between mercy and grace is this. You come home and a man is robbing your house. You come out with a gun. He says, "I am doing this because my family is hungry. We have not eaten in days." Finally, he convinces you it is true. So you have mercy on him. "Put the stuff back and you can leave."

Mercy lets him go. Grace steps in, adopts him into the family, gives him your last name, and gives him an inheritance in your household.

The closer I get to that place called done, the more what I know He did for me becomes a finality. I want to know how final this thing is that He did for me. When I leave something there and walk away, I want to know it has been taken care of. I want to know it has been left totally in His hands because of something Jesus provided by the grace of God that makes it entirely possible.

When I started believing that with more and more of what was in me, the joy started getting stronger and stronger. Inside, with my heart, I started really believing there is nothing He cannot do. Nothing.

Did it ever bother you that He uses terminology like this?

Nothing shall be impossible unto you.

In that passage He was talking about dealing with a certain kind of unbelief, which included fasting, and He ended it by saying nothing shall be impossible unto you.

How about this one?

All things are possible to him that believeth.

How about this?

Whosoever shall say unto this mountain, Be thou removed, and be thou cast into the sea...

How do we break those things down into normal living? When I first discovered what He was really saying, I told Him, "The journey to those things is longer than most people's lives that I know of." Yet if anyone could say something like that and it be true, it would be Him.

Then He said:

The works that I do shall he do also; and greater works than these shall he do; because I go unto my Father.

There is no plural on "works" in the greater. The works I do, you will do also, and greater than these you will do because I go to My Father.

Every miracle He did, every Old Testament miracle, He said you can do also. But there was one greater work that even He could not do at that point. He went to the Father, finished redemption, and made you a minister not of the law but of the Spirit. The one thing He could not do before that was stand up and administer the new birth by talking somebody into believing.

We are talking about a place called done.

This month I know better how to leave people and circumstances there.

The other day I needed quite a bit of money in just a few days. It was Tuesday. By Thursday I had it.

"Did you go get the money?"

No.

"Did you go ask for it?"

No.

"What did you do?"

I took Gary's advice and asked the Father.

This place is so done that I might as well have the five million in the bank, because I am already experiencing the life that is life through joy. I know that I know, that I know, that I am knowing, that I am getting to know more that I know, that I can drop people off there and things off there, and I can go there and get things.

It is just beginning.

Tulsa is going to suffer greatly at the hands of righteousness if it wants to continue sinning. The cities around us are going to suffer greatly because of the light that is going to shine here.

We have taken a long time to build a foundation underneath this, but I expect you to stand up like a soldier and fight all hell for the only thing God really cares about: people.

It is the only thing I know of where a ninety-eight-pound grandmother does not have to lift weights to whip the devil. She does not have to lift weights to move the mountain. She does not have to lift weights to believe a million souls in when she goes home. Why? Because she has found a place called done.

It has become one of my favorite things to leave people there. I particularly love leaving people in that place called done, then hearing testimonies come back later.

Sometimes we give a testimony over again because it agrees with most people's problems.

In my first discoveries about this place called done, I was not walking heavily in it, but it was enough to give God what He wanted. When God wants to do something for you, He generally has to get what He needs from you to do it with. We call that faith.

I had enough sense from praying in the Holy Ghost to know that what He loved more than anything was people. I thought He loved the new car I wanted. He just loved people.

So I started taking my relatives and dropping them off in the place called done. I took my oldest living aunt, dropped her off there, and went my way.

A couple of years later, I got a call from the office. They said, "We have this letter. You might want to return the call."

The letter was from a woman who, when I knew her, was eleven years old and I was a freshman in high school. Her brothers, Phil and Henry, and I ran around together. We were pretty ornery. I would sneak Grandpa's cigars, and we would hide behind the car. I got that good Baptist family smoking cigars. I would grab Grandpa's chew too, though they did not like the chew as much as the cigars.

One time Phil and Henry stuck a knife through a board into a shirt, put the shirt on, lay by the freeway, and put ketchup on it. That time they got in trouble. I am glad I was not with them. The police came and chased them down.

I joined the Navy and left. Carol used to come out when we were smoking those cigars, catch us, and tell Jewel, that precious Baptist woman who really loved God. We got it. I could still hear them when I was running.

Then, years later in ministry, I got this letter from Carol. It said, "Are you the same Dave Roberson who lived on White Road and knew my brothers Henry and Phil?"

She said, "My mama Jewel said, 'Do not waste your time, daughter. It is not him.' Are you him?"

"Yes, ma'am, I am."

"Really?"

"Yes."

"What happened?"

I said, "It is a long story."

She asked, "Are you going to be anywhere near New Mexico?"

I said, "I will be in Tucson, Arizona before long."

It turned out to be a four- or five-week meeting. Carol came with her precious mother Jewel. I think about two hundred people were being filled with the Holy Ghost. If you asked me, I would have said I would be doing more out there. But I have it from a good source that five years into this revival, more will already have been born again than I saw in my whole lifetime at the other level.

I have come home. It may not look like much yet, but you had better hang on. He is requiring a lot of dying and some different things you would not understand, but you had better hang on.

Carol and Jewel were filled with the Holy Spirit. Carol asked, "Do you have any relatives around New Mexico?"

I said, "Yes, I think I had some aunts and uncles in New Mexico."

She said, "That is where we are from."

Unknown to me, Carol took it on herself to track down the aunt whose name I gave her. She found her in a rest home, dying, and got her born again.

What are the odds?

Why do you think He said to pray the Lord of the harvest to send forth laborers? He paid the price. You did not. What does He want from you? Do you think of paying the price? He just goes after them. He is the Head. Where the body moves is where He moves.

When you pray for somebody, that is where He sends people. You call somebody who is lost saved, and you have just incorporated heaven to go after them.

How do I go from where I am to where He is?

I can tell you this much about Him. One of the nine attributes is immutable. Immutability means He cannot change, nor can He be changed. The devil does not have enough power to change Him, and neither do you. He is constant. He has always been the same.

He said that I am healed. If I am not healed, He cannot come where I am. But I can stand up and go where He is, because where He is, that disease is not going to go.

Where is He? In a place called done.

Where did He erect the place called done on my behalf? At the cross, when He passed a permanent judgment on everything He was judged in: disease, sickness, lack of peace, and sin. He passed a permanent judgment. This Man, at the end of the world, sustained one judgment for you.

That means if I can attain to it, I am as free as I can be, and there is nothing anybody can do about it.

He is not only immutable. The nine attributes are sovereign in each other. He loves immutably, and He immutably loves. He is sovereign in immutability and immutably sovereign. So when He says something is done, nobody can change the conditions. All I have to do is believe it, and hell and everything else has to get out of the way because He cannot change.

He is sovereignly love, and He loves sovereignly. He is sovereignly judgment, derived from the word just and justification. He is sovereignly just, and He loves justly.

He is eternal life, not merely everlasting life, because everlasting would mean He had a beginning. He is eternal life. There is nowhere He began. He is immutably eternal, and He loves eternally.

He loves you.

"It does not feel like it."

But He does.

"I wish He would love me more."

He cannot. He is love. If He could love you more, He would. But He is God, and He has maxed it. So why do you not stop and find out how to receive more of what He already loves you with?

"Maybe He will change."

It is impossible for Him to change.

"But all the things happening to me..."

You are the one who can change them because He already has.

I know what He provided for how it is with you. So what am I going to do? I am going to find out how to go where He said it is done.

He did not lightly heal you. He did not lightly destroy the curse. In fact, He cannot do anything lightly because He is.

Moses started questioning, "Who shall I say sent me?"

God said, "You tell Pharaoh that I AM has sent you."

Do you know what I AM means? It means He is simply the sum total of everything that is, was, or ever will be.

What assurance do I have? I AM sent him.

He sovereignly provided righteousness for anyone who wants what He has provided. What else did He give me? He destroyed the curse of disease and sovereignly provided healing for anyone who wants it.

"Who shall I say is healing me?"

"I AM He who is healing you."

"Do you need any help?"

"I AM."

Okay, I am healed.

How many have more faith than when you came?

The fight of faith begins when nothing can stop you from acting on the laws He set into motion that change you and everything around you. The devil, your flesh, and dark angels may have an encounter with you, but nothing can stop you from acting on those laws.

"But it does not look good."

Nothing can stop you.

"But I do not see how."

That is where you fight.

We have to find out about this place. You can go there on purpose, absolutely deliberately. Why would He pay such a horrendous price at the cross and then make the path to the cross haphazard, with tricks that throw you off the road? The path to the miracle is as sovereign and ordained as the miracle itself.

My God, you can put one foot in front of the other until you get where He said you are going.

No one has ever had revival and sustained revival who did not finish their journey to understand the miracle itself and who they are in Me. Those who understand that fullness will not only sustain what I am doing, but will also be sustained themselves.

So be attentive and spend much time in the presence of your Teacher, the Holy Spirit. I am lining you up to understand things you have not understood before, and they are major keys in your faith. Indeed, a great work is on its way.

Many of you will remember the things you said, and many of you will even have testimonies deep inside that you doubted. But in that day, you will testify and believe, saith the Lord.

Those who will not be moved learn more and more about walking out of the flesh and into My presence. Each time, they are able to teach the new groups more accurately and effectively, so you lose less. Know this: the wisdom is already on the way. The understandings have already been released. Indeed, you will enjoy the move. Many who have forsaken such a path will be back, and many will humble themselves and rejoice that they got in at such a late hour, saith the Lord.

This is not a walk of the flesh, but a walk of power, saith the Lord. Many said, "This is too hard." Many pulled back and went into a comfort place, a place where they knew they could have Me, but not much tribulation, not understanding this path.

But for you who hold fast and get past these strongholds, it is the joy I have been talking about. There are places in Me that will sustain you. You will be full of joy and afraid of nothing. I will use you to harvest many of the dark places nobody wanted to go. Great will be your reward, saith the Lord.

Hallelujah.

How many know that all the apostles did not die by martyrdom? Some of them did not resist death, wanting to go home that way and attain a better resurrection.

Do you remember when Jesus told Peter, "Enough," about the sword? He needed it for the garden. Peter thought, "This is where I am going to use it." When the high priest's servant came with soldiers and Judas to arrest Jesus, Peter unsheathed his sword and cut off the servant's ear.

In the middle of chaos, with people running and hiding, soldiers arresting, falling down and getting back up, I would have left Jesus alone right then if I were them. You can be sure they knew it was out of their hands, but they were under orders.

Peter cut the servant's ear off. Jesus said, "Peter, sheath your sword. If My kingdom were of this world, I would fight." Then, in the middle of all that chaos, He reached up and put the ear back on.

I do not think He hunted for the other one. I think when He took His hand away, there was an ear.

If I had been a soldier right there, I would have said, "Master, may I go die with You?"

Jesus said, "Sheath your sword, Peter. If My kingdom were of this world, I would fight. But it is not of this world."

So Peter sheathed his sword, and they did not resist.

Then, in Peter's place, Jesus substituted Himself. He washed the filth of betrayal off Judas' feet before Judas went and betrayed Him. Jesus went away in Peter's place. He was pierced in the side. He was lashed until His back was a mass of flesh. They crowned Him with Jerusalem thorns, hit Him with the palms of their hands, and said, "Prophesy. Who hit You?" Then they hung Him up, nailed Him, and waited for Him to die.

He died the worst kind of violent death.

In whose place? Peter's? Yours.

But notice what He said: "Sheath the sword. If My kingdom were of this world, I would fight."

Do you know what I know now? I can walk into the darkest part of any nation, no matter how severe, how full of witchcraft, how full of violence, and preach Jesus because I know He died that death for me.

Some apostles died of old age. John did. Mary, the mother of Jesus, died as the adopted mother of John in his house in Ephesus, Asia Minor. After her death, he was banished to the Isle of Patmos. There he received the book of Revelation, came back, and died at a good old age.

Paul said, "I am ready to be offered. I have fought a good fight. I have kept the faith. I have run a good race."

I am talking about a place called done.
