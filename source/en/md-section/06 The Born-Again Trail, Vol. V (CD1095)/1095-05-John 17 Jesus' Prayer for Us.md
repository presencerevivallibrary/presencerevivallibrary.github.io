# John 17: Jesus' Prayer for Us

## John 17 Cannot Be Understood From a Neutral Place

All right, let's go back to John 17. We have been talking about His glory, and we just caught a glimpse of it. Already we have talked about it for four or five hours, just from one tiny little glimpse.

As you know, this is basically the last prayer Jesus prayed. He prayed for His disciples, the apostles. He had kept them Himself from Satan and evil, and now He was asking the Father to keep them. He said that as the Father had sent Him into the world, He was sending us. He prayed for all of us throughout time who would believe on Him through the word of the apostles.

It is a tremendous prayer, and it is hardly ever preached on. We will take verses out of it once in a while, such as when Jesus said, "For their sakes I sanctify Myself, that they also might be sanctified through the truth," or "Thy word is truth." We will occasionally take a verse out of there and preach on it.

But to leave it in context and then stay neutral, not doing anything for God, would be impossible. You could not understand the content of this prayer without basically being sold out. It would be hard to believe what He is actually saying in this prayer.

We would want to relate it more to heaven. We hear all the glory: "My glory I give to them." Then we think, "Oh, this is really good. This must be what heaven is like, because here our life is short and full of troubles."

So a prayer like this is hard to understand if it is approached with any part of the natural mind. It is very, very hard.

He did not even attempt to give me a shot at it until almost three years into a six-month course on the born-again trail. Then, after we began to understand that life in us, that new nature, and how He translated us out of darkness, we began to see it. That life is the wellspring. It is the only part of us the Holy Spirit has to use. It is what God uses even to quicken, or supply life to, our mortal bodies so that we do not have to walk in sin.

He says He quickens our mortal bodies. We are no longer debtors to live after the flesh.

> For if ye live after the flesh, ye shall die: but if ye through the Spirit do mortify the deeds of the body, ye shall live.

Then the Holy Ghost is introduced: "Likewise the Spirit also helpeth our infirmities." The new nature is already setting us free from sin. Then He comes with all His power and glory, and He also comes to help us.

The more we get into this born-again trail and what He has made us and who we are, the harder it is to justify failure. It is harder to justify not doing anything for God. It is harder to justify walking in the flesh and not walking in His glory and power. It is harder to justify just being in a neutral state and letting your life pass you by.

This is a very uncomfortable church to go to if you are just biding your time as a believer. Very, very hard.

We are not sold out to limbs of the gospel. We are sold out to the whole gospel. If we pick out one limb and sell out to it, it is highly favorable to human beings. It is just as hard to get people to follow the holiness of a message like this many times as it was in the early day, or as it was when the apostles preached. But it seems to grab hold of hungry people and never turn loose of them. The person who is hungry says, "This is what I have been looking for."

## The Promise That Everything He Sought Was on This Trail

There will be a time on this trail, and it has already begun, when we will start to see things that will cause even carnal believers to take a second look.

When we entered into this, what really turned my whole life around was the visitation and the promise I got from God: that all I had ever looked for, I would find on this trail. Every personal promise He made to me, I would find it there.

And He is right.

I got my first glimpse at His glory, and it was not like what most of us preach His glory to be. It was not just some manifestation or some presence that comes and goes. I got a first glimpse at His glory, and I know I could never have gone there, or be going there, had He not first prepared me to understand who I am in the born-again trail.

Hallelujah.

This is a powerful prayer. A powerful prayer.

When I first told Gary that God was opening this prayer up to me, he said that this prayer, and several truths that preceded it, were part of his daily confession. Every day, every day, every day, he had been reading it out loud as his confession for several years. He had been confessing it and praying it.

He went to the end of the story and did not even know what he was praying for until all of us started getting into who we have become in sonship and inheritance.

But I am absolutely confident that one reason God is bringing us there is because of people like Gary, who faithfully confessed for years:

> Where I am, there shall also My servant be; that they may see My glory where I am.

Every day. Every day. Confessing it, confessing it, confessing it.

Thank God that it is coming here. The answer to that prayer is coming here.

## Glorifying Him Until Manifestations Come to Fullness

One thing I do know for sure: when we are able to glorify Him like that, manifestations will come to fullness also.

Jesus said, "The glory which Thou gavest Me I have given them." He has given it to us. He also said, "Father, I will that they also, whom Thou hast given Me, be with Me where I am, that they may behold My glory, for Thou lovedst Me before the foundation of the world."

Not only did He give us His glory, He wants us to see Him in that glory.

I noticed that when I had that little visitation, the thing that stayed with me predominantly for several days was this: when you are able to glorify Him, when He is fully glorified in your midst, then manifestations will come to fullness also.

In the greatest sense, that includes all those twisted children we have been believing for over the years. If we can glorify Him, if we can glorify Him, then those kinds of manifestations will come into existence.

I do not know anywhere in the United States or in the world where you can bring a busload of children like that, children who are not accountable for themselves, and bring them back home whole. If there were meetings like that anywhere in the world, you would be hearing about them. Some of you would be getting on the plane yourselves with your children.

But that does not change the fact that if a group manages to glorify Him, and He is glorified in our midst, all that He is and all the work He finished for His Father when He glorified the Father becomes present. He says, "As the Father sent Me, even so send I you." He says the same love the Father loved Him with, He loves you. That is what Jesus said.

Jesus fully glorified the Father. The dead were raised. The maimed were healed. The lepers were cleansed, even made whole. They were not just dried up of their disease; He put everything back on them because they turned to worship Him. He put everything back like it was.

At times we have had flashes of His glory. I believe one time in one of T. L. Osborn's massive crusades, a man was sitting on the edge of the platform with a stub for a leg sticking out in front of all the witnesses. His leg just grew out. It grew out right there.

Well, that is not any harder than raising the dead. People seem to have more faith for raising the dead than they do for somebody who has been all twisted up and born that way. Everybody has been programmed, and the whole program has taken over that person being that way. It is just about as hard to break that as breaking out of jail.

But does it change anything? Does it change the fact that it is true?

I have a sweet assurance from heaven that when Jesus glorified the Father totally, all those things were done on the earth. If we glorify Him and finish the work He sent us to do, then in the presence of that fullness, those kinds of miracles will exist.

Hallelujah.

In that glory I saw just for a moment, the emphasis He kept putting on me in the Spirit was glorifying Him as in the born-again trail. I knew the born-again trail had everything to do with even coming to the acknowledgment that He could be glorified to the fullness in those realms.

So I know it has everything to do with that life He has given us, that life in us, and the capacity to believe Him and go where He is.

Hallelujah. It is an exciting thing to think about. That would devastate a city.

Glory be to God.

## Jesus Overcame the World and Broke Satan's Hold Over Death

We are almost three years into this journey to come to this place. There is no telling how long we are going to be camped here. So in John 17, it would do us good to read it again.

Let us back up to the end of the sixteenth chapter, verse 33. He was talking about going to the Father and coming again:

> Again, I leave the world, and go to the Father.

Then in verse 33 He said:

> These things I have spoken unto you, that in Me ye might have peace. In the world ye shall have tribulation: but be of good cheer; I have overcome the world.

He destroyed him who had the power of death, even the devil. That power of death, that hold the devil had, was spiritual death. But to those who are still spiritually dead on this planet, the devil still has that power over them. He brought them under bondage because their whole lifetime they were subject to the fear of death.

As you know, Satan used the fear of death to manipulate the human race. He manipulated them to the degree that they threw their children into volcanoes to have better crops. He would always promise them some form of religion or some form of afterlife, and then, using the fear of death, he would sell them a false religion. Whole nations reached the age of accountability and went into hell generation after generation because the devil had power over death and used the fear of it to manipulate the human race through that old nature.

But Jesus said, "Now is the prince of this world judged: now shall he be cast out."

He may have reign over spiritually dead people, but he does not have reign over you. This planet is shared with people who have authority and serve the devil, and it is also shared with people who have authority here and serve God.

That is why God needs your authority. This planet is shared. The prince of this world has been cast out, but he still reigns over those who are spiritually dead.

Thank God, in the fullness of it, all this belongs to God by reason of creation. But the social systems and ownership of it have been taken over. Now that his hold has been broken, thank God.

In the Old Testament, they operated on accounted righteousness. God almost had to have permission from spiritually dead men to operate in this earth. They had to sacrifice animals and everything else just to give Him permission to work here, basically.

Now you and I are born again. The reason He needs your authority is that you call people saved. This planet is ours too. It is ours too, just like it belongs to the spiritually dead ones over whom Satan reigns.

This planet belongs to us too. Therefore, our authority becomes His authority. I say, "I call them saved." Thank God, the corporate structure of heaven gets to go into this earth because we say it is saved.

At the end of it, we win. We win. He is even going to remodel everything for us.

Hallelujah. Can you imagine that?

To tell you the truth, what mansion would I rather have? The one made out of gold and two-by-fours, as they all preach about? Or would I rather that "mansion" be in my Father's house, where there are many rooms? I would rather occupy the room He has for me here than a mansion later in glory that I do not care much about.

Amen.

I like that in the midst of all this tribulation He said I can be of good cheer. "Be of good cheer; I have overcome the world."

>

>

Then He said:

> I have glorified Thee on the earth: I have finished the work which Thou gavest Me to do.

It was in that finished work that He glorified His Father. Well, you can glorify Jesus by finishing the work He has sent you to do.

In verse 5 He said:

> And now, O Father, glorify Thou Me with Thine own self with the glory which I had with Thee before the world was.
>
> I have manifested Thy name unto the men which Thou gavest Me out of the world: Thine they were, and Thou gavest them Me; and they have kept Thy word.
>
> Now they have known that all things whatsoever Thou hast given Me are of Thee.
>
> For I have given unto them the words which Thou gavest Me; and they have received them, and have known surely that I came out from Thee, and they have believed that Thou didst send Me.

>
> And all Mine are Thine, and Thine are Mine; and I am glorified in them.
>
> And now I am no more in the world, but these are in the world, and I come to Thee. Holy Father, keep through Thine own name those whom Thou hast given Me, that they may be one, as We are.

Then He said:

> Neither pray I for these alone, but for them also which shall believe on Me through their word.

That is why Gary prayed that every day. It is the same prayer He prayed for them. He prayed it for you and me, who believed on Him through their word. Remember that. This prayer Jesus prayed for you, that He would be glorified in you.

Again, in verse 21:

> That they all may be one; as Thou, Father, art in Me, and I in Thee, that they also may be one in Us: that the world may believe that Thou hast sent Me.
>
> And the glory which Thou gavest Me I have given them; that they may be one, even as We are one.

Now notice that:

> And the glory which Thou gavest Me I have given them; that they may be one, even as We are one.

## The Same Life That Made Jesus One With the Father Makes Us One With Him

As you know, the Father had life within Himself. He gave to the Son to have life within Himself, and the Son gave to you to have life within yourself. He said that we might be one in Him as He is one in the Father.

That is talking about how, by the transgression of one man, sin entered into the world. In that man's transgression, all sinned. In the same way that we all drank of the same spiritually dead cup with Adam and became one with him because of his transgression, by one Man and the abundance of grace, we reign in righteousness through the life of one Man into whom we have been baptized.

That One got His life from the Father and is one with Him. So Jesus says we are one with Him the same way we were one with Adam in spiritual death, and He is one with the Father. We are one with Him in His life, and He is one with the Father. We are one in Him, and we are all made one in Him, in the Father, and Him in us, because of that life He gave us.

Hallelujah, I tell you.

Verse 22 says:

> And the glory which Thou gavest Me I have given them; that they may be one, even as We are one:
>
> I in them, and Thou in Me, that they may be made perfect in one; and that the world may know that Thou hast sent Me, and hast loved them, as Thou hast loved Me.

It is hard to conceive that the same love He loved Jesus with is the same love He loves you with. You cannot think, "Well, maybe He loves the humanity and glorified Jesus a little more than me."

No, He does not.

The same love He loved Him with, He loved you with. So in His humanity, the same things He did with Him, for Him, and to Him, He is willing to do with you, for you, and to you.

This is so far from the carnal mind.

In verse 24 He says:

> Father, I will that they also, whom Thou hast given Me, be with Me where I am; that they may behold My glory, which Thou hast given Me: for Thou lovedst Me before the foundation of the world.

Oh, righteous Father, the world has not known Thee, but I have known Thee, and these have known that Thou hast sent Me. The last thing He said was:

> And I have declared unto them Thy name, and will declare it: that the love wherewith Thou hast loved Me may be in them, and I in them.

The same way that, by the transgression of one man, the whole human race tasted spiritual death and literally inherited it, now we are being made one by the life of one Man. We are all tasting life. This life that He has given us in His glory is not only so we might be one in Him, but so He might be in us, and we might be one with the Father.

Thank God.

In tracing that life, this oneness He is talking about helps you understand it. You understood how you were one with Adam, because the whole world was baptized into his nature. You see the acts of the world. Well now, hallelujah, we have been baptized into One who is the new nature of the last Adam, so that the whole world can know we are not one with spiritual death, but one with Him, one with the Father, and doing His works.

Hallelujah.

## John Carried the Original Born-Again Trail

For the last two Sundays I have been trying to trace that life, the life that came from the Father and made me one with Jesus and one with the Father.

Matthew, Mark, and Luke are tremendous. They gave tremendous parables with the mind of Christ in the areas He wanted us to understand. But John was the one who understood the new nature. John was the original author of the born-again trail.

Here I thought I had something original.

Hallelujah.

In fact, I have been in this now for these last seven years, walking further and further down the born-again trail, as He first began to turn me and purge me from some things of the world that a man ought not have. One of them was the mistreating of money.

I went on and on, and finally I said, "Oh, this thing was just the love of money." You psych yourself up into believing that God owed you; He owed you that.

Anyway, when I was talking to Don, he said Lester Sumrall sat him down years and years ago, when he dropped in at Washington where Don had the church. Lester sat him down and said, "Let me tell you something." He told him, "You need to establish very early in your ministry how much money you need to live on. Then all the rest belongs to the gospel. Do not change it."

He told him that years and years ago, when it was totally unpopular. Not that it is any more popular now.

That is what Lester did. When Rod Parsley buried him, he was weeping like a baby, and he said, "Even the suit they are burying him in, I bought it." He said, "I bought it."

You see, Lester established provision versus pound. He did it because God moved on him to do it, without him establishing verses or looking for anything. He just knew it was right because of the witness in his spirit. He established what he would take personally and live on, and all the rest, the millions and millions, went into the gospel.

When Don told me that, I just about broke down, because that was the first I had really heard it on the top level outside of our group.

But I had been walking in it. I did not know what I was doing. I was establishing, or attempting to establish, pound versus provision seven or eight years ago, when He started explaining greed and what was wrong with some of the paths we were walking on.

I said, "That is it. That is it."

I was making a thousand-dollar payment on the cars I owned, and I had just gotten rid of an airplane. I went down the line saying, "I do not know where this is. I do not know." But I quit making car payments, cut it by a thousand, quit doing this, and quit doing that, because I knew something was out of balance.

So I quit all of it, and I have not made a car payment since or anything. Everything I got after that, I was waiting for the dust to settle. I wanted to know what He wanted to teach me.

Now we are understanding it. But I started to act on it way back then. People give me new cars and things like that. I am not worried about any of that. I do not care. If they designate things and say, "You are going to take this," then I am going to take it.

"Do you hear me?"

I said, "Okay."

If they do not designate it, it will go into the pound.

Now that I am out running into this one and that one and holding meetings, I will tell you the truth: I will never go back. I do not want to live the way they do anymore. I have too much peace, too much fellowship with my Father, too much of the love of God. I do not want any of it anymore.

Hallelujah.

I want to be where He is. That is all I want. I do not care about the rest.

Hallelujah, Jesus. Hallelujah.

The only thing that bothers me is any part of anything I have, or anything in my life, that is not a testimony. That I want to change. That is what bothers me. But there is not much else.

Hallelujah.

So do what Lester Sumrall said: establish what you need and give the rest to the gospel.

Hallelujah.

## John 1 Reveals the Life That Became the Light of Men

Now I want to go back and trace this life so you can feel it and start learning how to operate out of it and lean to it.

Let us go back and start tracing that life as we head toward this final prayer. Let us go back to the first chapter on the born-again trail, when He first opened it up, and to the first life we found: John 1:1. Let us trace it through here and take just the highlights out of several of these chapters leading up to it.

The verse that actually began to walk for me in John was verse 4. That was after the big "S" and the little "s" had been established somewhere along there in Romans 8. When I saw this verse and God told me what it meant, I literally shook with the fear and the glory and the power of God because of what it opened up to me.

Several people I was able to explain it to had the same thing happen to them. So far I have been able to explain the glory of God to Tim to where Tim saw it, and it shook him. He said, "I see it. I see it. I see it." He is the only one I have been able to get it over to in such a degree that it affected him almost like it affected me when I first saw it.

But that life we are tracing is His glory that He gave to you. It was the life that was in Him from the Father, and all that goes with it is what He gave to you.

The world has it so messed up, even the born-again church world. Ask them what born again is, and they hardly know what it means.

"Well, I have been born. I am a reborn believer."

"Can you explain a little bit about what you received?"

They basically have no idea, and the whole gospel and the glory of God are based on it.

Hallelujah.

I first discovered this life in John's teachings when he switched from Jesus' deity to His humanity. I realized that the human spirit Jesus was born with was the life John was talking about, the life we would be born with when we were born again.

So let us follow this life.

> In the beginning was the Word.

That was the Logos, Jesus. In the beginning was Jesus, and Jesus was with God, and He was God. The same was in the beginning, face to face, eyes to eyes, nose to nose, tri-equal with the other members of the Godhead.

But John isolates Him as Creator in verse 3:

> All things were made by Him; and without Him was not any thing made that was made.

Then:

> In Him was life; and the life was the light of men.
>
> And the light shineth in darkness; and the darkness comprehended it not.

This sets the precedent for the whole born-again trail. He was the first Man born spiritually alive to stand on the face of this earth. That one solitary life, that one candle burning, shined in the darkness of the spiritually dead human race, and the darkness comprehended it not.

So there is the establishment of that life. That life was that first spirit to be born spiritually alive. The same spirit He had when He was born, you now have because you were born again.

Hallelujah.

## Nicodemus Heard That the Breath of God Must Birth a Man From Above

Going on over to the third chapter, Nicodemus is where we first get that term: "You must be born from above." "You must be born again." That is where we coined the phrase from, His conversation with Nicodemus.

In John 3:3, Jesus answered and said:

> Verily, verily, I say unto thee, Except a man be born again, he cannot see the kingdom of God.

Then He went on to explain in verse 8:

> The wind bloweth where it listeth, and thou hearest the sound thereof, but canst not tell whence it cometh, and whither it goeth: so is every one that is born of the Spirit.

The word for wind is the same as breath. It is that breath God breathed into Adam, and Adam suddenly became a living soul. He found himself to be a spiritual being in a physical body, living through a soul.

God breathed into him, meaning God took life from inside Himself. God does not have a breath. If He had a breath, He would have to live here on the earth, because there is no air in outer space. I hope you do not think He goes, "[inhales]," and replenishes His body with oxygen.

No. He took life from the inside of Himself and transferred that life to the first man, and that man became a son of God.

When you were born again, the breath of God came. "The wind bloweth where it listeth. You hear the sound. You do not know where it comes from or where it goes. So is everyone born of the Spirit." He was there, and He breathed that same life into you that He breathed into Adam, the same life Jesus was born with.

Hallelujah. Glory be to God.

## The Spirit Without Measure Belongs to the Life That Believes on the Son

That life emerges again at the end of the third chapter. In John 3:34-36, He said:

> For He whom God hath sent speaketh the words of God: for God giveth not the Spirit by measure unto Him.
>
> The Father loveth the Son, and hath given all things into His hand.
>
> He that believeth on the Son hath everlasting life: and he that believeth not the Son shall not see life; but the wrath of God abideth on him.

He said if you believe on the Son, you have this everlasting life. Jesus, whom God sent, speaks the words of God, and God gave Him the Spirit without measure. If you believe on Him, you will have the everlasting life that was in Him, the life that caused Him to be able to have the Spirit without measure.

I know what they say about "without measure." They say, "See, Jesus had the Holy Spirit without measure because He was God. He could do any kind of miracles He wanted, but you cannot, because you have Him by measure."

That is not what He was saying.

He was saying Jesus was the only Man on the planet who had the life of God in His spirit. Therefore, He could receive the baptism. The Holy Spirit did not abide on the outside of Him. He stepped into Him without measure.

Hallelujah.

So Jesus was the only one at that time who had Him without measure. But if you go on and let the everlasting life abide in you, you too can receive Him without measure.

Hallelujah.

Just like Moses could not take it anymore. He said, "I will see God." So God cleft a rock.

What was the rock a type of? Jesus.

God cleft it, made a place in it for Moses, and put him in it. "I will see You, God." So God put His hand over him and passed by him so he could not see His glory.

Well, what did he get to see? God put His hand over him and passed by.

Then you came along. Somebody preached to you, and you said, "I will see Your glory, God." So the cleft He had cleft in Jesus the Rock, He picked you up and put you into Jesus by giving you a new nature. Then God went to pass by you. Instead of passing by, He stopped, and you received Him without measure.

He stepped in. He did not pass by. You are not the type. You are not the shadow. You are in the Rock, and He is in you. God stepped in.

You received Him without measure. That had not happened before since Adam. But I have been born again. "I will see God." Since you got there, right?

Hallelujah.

## The Samaritan Woman Heard That the New Nature Is a Wellspring

The next time this life shows up is with the Samaritan woman in John 4. Of course, He talked about natural water, but then He tells you what it is in verse 13:

> Whosoever drinketh of this water shall thirst again:
>
> But whosoever drinketh of the water that I shall give him shall never thirst; but the water that I shall give him shall be in him a well of water springing up into everlasting life.

Is He talking about the Holy Ghost? No, sir. The Holy Ghost is a river.

Hallelujah.

This is a wellspring springing up into everlasting life. What does the Holy Spirit have to use? He does not have two things. He only has one thing: your new nature, the wellspring springing up into everlasting life. You are a well. The Holy Ghost is a river. You are a well.

Then He starts telling you what your Father wants. Now that this well is opening up, He tells you what the Father wants while talking to this Samaritan woman.

In John 4:23, He said:

> But the hour cometh, and now is, when the true worshippers shall worship the Father in spirit and in truth: for the Father seeketh such to worship Him.

In the millennial time clock, this generation is the hour that comes and now is. The true worshipers shall worship the Father in spirit. There is that life again, spirit with a little "s." The Old Testament saints did not have a new nature. You do.

You can give this fellowship to Him. They could not. You have this new life, this intimacy that has put you in that kind of relationship with Him. You have His nature. You are His son. You are His daughter. You can worship Him out of that new nature. They could not.

The rock was cleft, and He passed by. Not you. He steps in for a cup of tea.

What did He say?

> The hour cometh, and now is, when the true worshippers shall worship the Father in spirit and in truth.

He does not seek much from you. He does not seek much from you. But this thing He is seeking, the Father seeketh such to worship Him.

He is looking for this. He is looking for you.

Why? Because God is a Spirit. Verse 24 says:

> God is a Spirit: and they that worship Him must worship Him in spirit and in truth.

He is looking for this because He is a Spirit. There is no fellowship with Him outside of what is in your spirit, that new nature. God is a Spirit, and they that worship Him must worship Him in spirit and in truth. There is no other way.

He derives no fellowship from these people running around spiritually dead. He had a relationship, but no fellowship.

Hallelujah.

But not you. You have a nature like His. He is Spirit. You are a spirit. At last, you are back in fellowship with Him. So He is looking for you. He would like to stop and fellowship. He seeketh such to worship Him, so He can enter into that kind of relationship and fellowship with you.

Hallelujah.

## John 5 Shows That the Life Began Its Journey in the Father

The next time it shows up as strong is in the fifth chapter. There He reveals where it is all coming from. He reveals where the life is coming from.

In John 5:26, He finally tells you where this life began its journey with the breath of God. Of course, we cannot just leave it with that, because He said more about the life in this chapter than since Nicodemus and being born again. But notice verse 26:

> For as the Father hath life in Himself; so hath He given to the Son to have life in Himself.

No wonder when you were made one with the Son, you were made one with the Father, because that is where your life began its journey: with the Father.

Oh, that I may see His glory the way Jesus saw it.

Hallelujah.

I have been born again. He is telling where the life began its journey. It began its journey as the Father had that life in Himself. Now I have it. I have it.

## John 10 Presents Jesus as the Door Who Leads the Sheep Out of Spiritual Death

In the tenth chapter, He tells you how He had to come through the doorway of humanity as a human spirit to open the door for the sheep who were bound in spiritual death, so they could walk out of it.

Had He not come in His human spirit and become the door, we would still be locked up.

He said:

> I am the door: by Me if any man enter in, he shall be saved, and shall go in and out, and find pasture.
