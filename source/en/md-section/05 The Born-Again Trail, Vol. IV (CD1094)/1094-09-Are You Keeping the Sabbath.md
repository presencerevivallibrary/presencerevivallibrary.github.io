# Are You Keeping the Sabbath

## Paul's Foundation Keeps the Church Out of Legalism

Hallelujah.

When the mind of Christ dropped on me and the gift of prophecy kicked in for the last three services, up until the last one, I was in the middle of teaching from glory to glory. I was even trying to finish breaking down 2 Corinthians 3. I opened up to it probably three times without ever going back.

Finally I just surrendered and rode that wave out until He finished saying what He wanted to say in our first encounter with the judgment line. I think He gave us about as much as we could understand.

Now I have been trying to go back.

It is interesting that when Paul gave us our foundation as an apostle born out of due season, he even steered some of the other apostles, in particular Peter, back into the foundation of the Church, which is justification by faith.

No matter who Paul was writing to, whether the Gentile born-again Church, the Jewish sector, a community in Rome, or Jerusalem itself, you can always see the foundation: how we came out of the law and how the devil keeps trying to get us back into some kind of legalism.

As Gentiles, we did not come out from under the law as a commandment from God to keep it. But the promise to Abraham was that in his seed all nations of the earth would be blessed. The law was added to that covenant with Abraham because of transgressions until the Seed, which is Christ, should come, to whom the promise was made.

Our heritage really has only one foundation to get straightened out. But when the Judaizers came, those who finally came to terms and agreed that the Gentile could be born again and God could become his Father, they still said that to perfect his faith he had to go on, be instructed in a more perfect way, and keep the law.

So in all of Paul's writings, to keep the foundation of justification by grace under the Church, he had to combat that continually.

We really have one heritage. The promise was to Abraham that we would be saved through his seed, which is Christ. The law was added to that. They kept trying to go back under the law, and this is where the whole argument against legalism came in, when Paul fought to keep the foundation under the Church.

That is why there is so much about the law in his writings.

## The Born-Again Trail Opened the Old Testament Through the New Nature

When God opened all this up to Paul, especially in the book of Romans, what it did with strength was open the potential of everything we became in the new nature when we were born again.

When that opened up, we called it the Born-Again Trail and have been teaching on it for over two years now. I thought it was a six-month adventure. But since I found out it is the foundation of the Church, I do not believe there is an end to it. Now the whole Word of God is lining up. Where do you stop preaching it?

All I know is that now I understand it was not really even the Holy Spirit who set me free. He did the work, but the work was done in me, and what was left in me is what makes me free: the new nature. That is what set me free.

Some of you may remember that I said I could read the Old Testament through, but as far as doing exhaustive studies in it, until I thoroughly understood our dispensation of sonship, I just could not get a spirit of hunger to go in there and research all of those men.

But once He opened up completely the understanding I now have about being born again and how free I am, it freed me to go back and see the old in the light of the new, after I had been born again and had the Teacher on the inside illuminating me.

Then we went way back to Genesis. What a time we have been having.

I understand now. I am getting freer by the moment, and I cannot find any end to this.

Some of the greatest things Paul said about Moses, the veil over his face, and how all of that relates to you and me being born again, are amazing to me. Even under the shadows, I am beginning to understand things that were difficult all my life, like the Sabbath.

## The Sabbath Penalty Protected the Shadow of Salvation by Grace

Why would the penalty be so stringent on the day of rest if a man simply bent over and picked up a stick of firewood? They brought him to the elders and had to put him in a jail of sorts until Moses inquired of God:

"He broke the Sabbath. What do we do with him now?"

God said, "Bring him before the elders of the church and stone him until he is dead."

A rebellious son reached manhood around thirteen. If he would not listen to his mother or father or take instruction, the mother and father would take him to the elders at the gate where they sat in council. She would say, "This son is a drunkard, and he is in rebellion. We have administered correction, and he never takes it."

Then the elders and the people would take that rebellious son and kill him there at the gate, stoning him to death.

Aren't you glad you are not under that dispensation when you have an outburst and run?

The penalty was really stringent for just picking up a piece of firewood on the day of rest. I am thinking the man must have lived in such fear on the Sabbath that he did not get any rest.

See what God had to contend with. He gave the law because of transgression until the time the Seed should come, which is Christ, through whom the promise was made, that through that Seed He would inherit the nations.

God had to deal with the rebellious sin nature, and He was very clear to those who wanted to go back under the law that the law was for the lawless.

Why would you need a commandment to do all those things if you had a new nature that freed you from them?

That is why you are judged now by the law of liberty and the advocate ministry of Jesus Christ. After you are born again and do something wrong, you go before Jesus Christ the Advocate, confessing a sin, and He is faithful and just to cleanse you.

That is what the priest did in the inner court, not the Holy of Holies, but the inner court on the brazen altar, where they brought sacrifices for the sins of the people.

That is what Solomon did first when he took over the kingship from his father David. The first thing King Solomon did was offer a thousand burnt offerings on Mount Moriah. God said, "What do you want?"

Solomon wanted to make sure that, underneath the advocate ministry of the Old Covenant, there was nothing between him and God when he took the throne.

God said, "What do you want?"

He said, "I want wisdom to judge Your people righteously, and I want to build You a house to dwell in."

That is the same thing we want. We want a spirit of wisdom to minister the Word and judgment righteously, and the house we are building is made out of the born-again spirits of just men made perfect.

That is the only house He wants.

Before you had that new nature, it did not matter what you did. You could not give yourself a new nature. You could not save yourself. You could not beat a conscience or new nature into yourself. You cannot discipline one in. You cannot meditate one in. You have to be born again.

You can no more give yourself a new nature than a goldfish can adopt a piranha and beat one into him.

"I was having lunch. I had my friends for lunch."

## Sabbath Rest Is the Shadow of Ceasing From Our Own Works

The penalty was stringent, but when you start understanding the shadow, you see that it was the shadow of things to come. They said the body is of Christ. Meaning, the thing making the shadow is the Body of Christ.

Once the thing making the shadow gets here, it is impossible to live in the shadow and in the thing making it at the same time.

The Sabbath was a type of the rest you and I enter into when we are born again and cease from our own labors. It is not by your works, lest any man should boast. It is the gift of God.

A man working on the Sabbath was saying, in type, that your salvation could be earned. That is an impossibility.

If Joshua had given them rest when they crossed Jordan and entered the promised land, then God would not afterward have spoken of another day. There remains a rest for the people of God.

You enter that rest when you are born again, come out from under the weight and burden of sin, and receive a new nature.

Are you keeping the Sabbath?

Yes, I am, because I am guarding that peace. Glory be to God. I have been born again.

Is anybody hearing me? I have been born again. I have a new nature, and I know the secret of walking in the presence and power of God. It is not what men can give you. It is how you develop that human spirit, born again in the presence of God.

"I am going to fill myself with the Word."

Good, because the Word lays the foundation for faith. But faith takes place in your spirit. It comes from knowing Him who said what you are quoting.

Even when you walk the floor, say something five hundred times, and call yourself healed, when do you get healed? Certainly not by moving God. The one you are really affecting is your own spirit. You are speaking the Word until you conform to it in every area: spirit, soul, and body.

He who spared not His own Son, how shall He not with Him also freely give us all things? He wanted you to think about it. He did not spare Him, so how can you get Him to withhold any good thing from you?

"Why did He not give it to me?"

There is only one reason He cannot, if He cannot get you to receive it.

"I begged for it thirty-five days."

You are a professional unreceiver.

Hallelujah. Thank You, Jesus.

## A Healthy Eye Is to the Body What the New Nature Is to the Spirit

Just two verses further down, it looks like Jesus changed the subject when He talked about why a man lights a candle and brings it into a dark room. But He did not. He was talking about what happens to your spirit when you are born again.

Luke 11:33 says:

> "No man, when he hath lighted a candle,
>
> putteth it in a secret place,
>
> neither under a bushel,
>
> but on a candlestick,
>
> that they which come in
>
> may see the light."

Jesus was saying that the whole purpose of lighting a candle is so a person can see where he could not see before and walk where he could not walk before because of the darkness.

He is talking about what happens to your spirit when you are delivered from the power of darkness and translated into the kingdom of His dear Son. Before the candle was lit and brought into the room, there was nothing but darkness. It was the same with your spirit before you were born again and received the new nature.

There was nothing but darkness. But now that your spirit has been born again and received a new nature, you can see where you could not see and walk where there was once darkness.

Jesus went on to say:

>

When Jesus said the light of the body is the eye, He was saying your eyes are to your body what your new nature is to your spirit. They both supply the light we need to walk in.

Your natural man would be in total darkness if it were not for the eyes. Your spirit man would be in total darkness if it were not for the new nature. As clearly as your natural eyes let light into your body so you can walk by the light, your new nature is what God uses to illuminate your spirit, for it is only through your spirit that you can understand spiritual things.

Proverbs 20:27 says:

> "The spirit of man is the candle of the Lord,
>
> searching all the inward parts of the belly."

When Jesus said, "If thine eye be single, thy whole body shall be full of light," the Greek word for "single" is *haplous* and means a healthy eye, free of disease and able to see clearly. Jesus is using this natural parable to help you understand the spiritual application.

The perfect state of the natural man is to have healthy, disease-free eyes so he can see clearly. Your eyes are to your natural man what your new nature is to your spirit.

On the other hand, Jesus said, "If thine eye be evil, thy whole body shall be full of darkness." The Greek word for evil is *poneros* and means ill, worthless, or diseased. Blind eyes are to the natural man what the darkness of the sin nature is to the spirit of a man who has not been born again.

Jesus said if your whole body is full of light, having no part dark, the whole shall be full of light, as when the bright shining of a candle gives light. In the natural, if the whole body is full of light, having no part dark, it is because you have a healthy set of eyes and can see clearly through a whole range of vision, peripheral and forward.

Because your eyes are to your body what your new nature is to your spirit, the fact that Jesus said, "having no part dark," means there can be a part that is still dark even though you have been born again.

You see that all the time in the Body of Christ: people who do not believe in speaking in tongues, healing, or prophesying. People who have been born again and have a new nature can still be partially blinded to truth by darkness.

But none of that changes the potential we have through our new nature. Even though a person may be taught wrong, you must understand that he still has the capacity and unlimited potential to be taught right.
