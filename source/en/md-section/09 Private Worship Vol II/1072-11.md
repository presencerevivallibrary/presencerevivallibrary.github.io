# Entering the King's Presence With the Gift He Wants

## Praying in Tongues and Worship Must Stay Free From Condemnation

If you would, stand up. You can sit down any time after you want, or leave any time. This is not for condemnation. There is no law here.

I am thinking about praying in tongues. You cannot operate under law praying in tongues, except the law of the Spirit. Even if you are ugly and mean, He is not going to be praying the prayer you want Him to pray. He will be praying about you not being ugly and mean.

God cannot do anything against a person's will unless that person willfully lets Him in, especially to the part where He changes the part that is unwilling, like in praying in tongues.

Listen to me. Any time after we begin this private worship, you can leave if you need to or want to. Otherwise, if you can hang around until ten o'clock, fluctuate between private worship and praying in tongues. Go back into private worship, then pray in tongues a while, then back into private worship. Once in a while, call something out.

Here is a good one:

> You are not a man that You can lie.
>
> You have exalted Your Word above Your name.
>
> Heaven and earth shall pass away,
>
> but Your Word abides forever.
>
> I have what I say.
>
> I call myself dead to this world and alive unto You,
>
> that I might separate to You all the glory.
>
> The blind see, the dead are raised,
>
> the lame walk, the dumb speak,
>
> the poor have the gospel preached to them.
>
> I owe no man anything but to love him,
>
> and I bring no reproach on this gospel through past failures.

Doesn't that sound good? You can shorten it up. Just call something out.

"I thank You for my healing, Jesus. You become to me what I call You. I call myself healed."

Then worship Him for about fifteen minutes because He provided it. Spend the next thirty, forty, or fifty minutes doing that, and you will notice a change, even in your attitude and in your senses.

We are going to sing one song together. Then I am going to lead you for a few minutes, and after that I am going to turn you loose. The building is yours.

## The Gift You Bring Before the King Is Yourself

Right now, I may exhort you at the door of private worship and set you in the right direction before we leave you for these next thirty minutes, which eventually will become several hours.

Remember, you are about to enter into the presence of a King.

Whenever kings are appeared before by ambassadors or princes, there is always some kind of protocol. Sometimes they were required to bring a precious gift. They would present it to the king and receive an audience with him. At the least, protocol required respect, sometimes not even eye contact until they were spoken to.

You are about to enter the presence of the King of kings. You might as well know that anything created does not impress Him. You were born.

You are going to enter His presence with a gift, and you are the only thing He wants. You are it. You are the gift He wants.

When you have a short time like this, because if you have hours you will wear your flesh out, the quickest and most direct route into His presence is to enter with adoration and worship.

Do not leave these next thirty minutes to lip service while your soul wanders down the street trying to solve some problem from yesterday.

My mind, my will, my intellect, and my emotions are all going to pay attention, because they are in the presence of a King.

When I first began breaking in, my mind would wander off and solve something while my lips kept saying, "I love You, I love You." The rest of me had gone down the street. I would stop, go get my soul, and bring it back.

I would say, "You are going to offer yourself up like the rest of me."

Then I would pour everything I was into short statements of adoration:

> I love You.
>
> I glorify You.
>
> I extol and exalt You.
>
> Blessed be the day You saved me.
>
> Hallelujah.

You will feel a response begin in the nature you have. Follow it.

## Music Can Help, but Your Own Priesthood Must Learn to Worship

When you get into long seasons of worship, He will train you. First of all, do not worry about the fight your flesh gives you. It may wander off, and you may have been gone an hour or two. Eventually you will become what you are doing and what you call yourself, which is a worshiper.

Eventually you will wear your flesh out, and it will willingly operate with you and submit itself up.

Music can afford a certain amount of emotion, and the words to a song can help. You can repeat those words, and the music will give you some uplift. But the problem with singing someone else's words, with music providing the emotion, is that you can sing, "Oh, how I love Jesus," and look across the aisle and hate somebody.

It is much harder to choose your own words out of the priesthood of your own sanctuary and stay that way.

What God wants is for all of us to come to the place of able ministers. We are not waiting for an atmosphere to be created for us. My God, we carry the thing in with us. We have cause and effect.

We are not dropping a burden on one particular person up front, saying, "Create an atmosphere so powerful." As an able minister, you will scare the people around you if you do not have music.

Ultimately, this is you.

Language itself is limited. He can listen to a Watusi, a Korean, a German, and you tonight, and even though you do not know what each other is saying, He does.

So if you think the magic is in your words, forget it. Words only try to pass on to Him what is in here. Do not try the big-word thing on Him. Try pouring everything you are into what you say.

## Pour the Whole Soul Into Short Statements of Adoration

Close your eyes and lift those hands for a minute. I will stay with you for a while, and then I am turning you over to yourself. You can walk around the altars or lie on the floor. I do not care. You might as well get used to four hours solid, because that is where we are going.

"Father, which One do I worship?"

Jesus said, "If you have seen Me, you have seen the Father." You worship Jesus; you worship the Father. What makes you think it is not all going where it is supposed to go? That is another teaching.

Come on.

Father, I love You. I glorify You. I magnify You. Hallelujah. I exalt You and lift You up.

Father, I want You. I glorify You, magnify You, and lift You up. I praise You and magnify You.

A chair makes a good altar. It is not how loudly you say it, nor how quietly. It is what is coming out of the heart.

Jesus, I love You. I glorify You, praise You, and magnify You. I worship You. I bless the day that You saved me.

I praise You with all my heart. Blessed be the day I could call You Lord. Glory, glory, glory. I praise You, magnify You, and exalt You.

I am making a conscious effort to begin my worship out of my soul, the part of me I contact you with. With my flesh I contact physical things, like this chair. With my soul, my mind, will, intellect, and emotion, I make contact with my fellow man through accumulated knowledge. But with my Father, I contact Him with my spirit.

I do not care if I start out in my soul. The part of me I contact people with is the part I am reaching out with first.

I love You and glorify You, Father. I magnify You. Blessed be the day You saved me.

From your spirit will come the waves and presence. There will be a day when He will follow you around all day long. When you raise your hands, He will come into a hospital room and take growths off a five-year-old's brain.

## Private Worship Can Lose Track of Time and Invoke Presence

If I spoke every language in the world, I would use them to magnify, exalt, and extol You. How can I say thanks? I can magnify You, glorify You, and lift You up.

At this point, if your tongues burst out, let it be short statements of adoration and try to stick to the offering of your soul.

There is a place in private worship where you get lost in time. Your tea is hot, and you think only a minute has passed, but it is cold.

There is a place, and you will get there if you do not quit. There is a place where time is lost.

You had better watch out, because your spirit will come to a place that invokes God's presence in everything you pray for.

Sometimes you will feel a quickening. You will feel a surge of His life. You are in a good direction. Keep following it.

When collective worship becomes private, from the inner sanctuary of my heart I say, "I love You. I glorify You. I magnify You."

Praise and adoration to the Most High, who was and is and is forever to come, who was dead, is alive, and is alive forevermore.

I love You and praise Your mighty signs and wonders, the people You healed, the things You spoke out of Your mind. I magnify You, glorify You, lift You up, exalt You, extol You.

## Dry Periods Give Way to an Increasing Presence

Now let me choose the words of adoration for you as we go back into corporate worship for a few minutes. Say them from your heart.

I will glorify and extol You, unashamedly, unequivocally, without hesitation. I worship You and call You my Lord.

The dry periods of extended private worship, when you are alone, give way to shorter dry periods, then longer stretches of presence, until eventually you wonder where the old men of God got the presence they carried, men such as Smith Wigglesworth.

If you let Him take you past the barriers, you will finish your life out with an ever-increasing presence that you will not have to tell anybody about. They will tell you.

I magnify and glorify You. Blessed be the day You saved me.

Father, thank You, thank You, thank You. Blessed be the day I could call You Abba. I worship You, glorify You, praise You, and magnify You.

Unashamedly, unrestrainedly, unequivocally, I lift my hands and worship You. I glorify and magnify You with all my heart. With all my heart I tell You that I love You.

It is not the perfection of your statements, the theological essence, or the excellence of the exegesis. It is how much of your heart you can pour in.

For an hour and a half, this is the quickest way in. If you are spending five or six hours, your mind may trail off and wander, but the law of confession kicks in. Eventually saying you love Him over and over will pull you back.

But when you only have an hour and a half left, you need to bring yourself back and worship Him spirit, soul, and body, not just with lip service.

Grab yourself. Pull yourself together. Tell your body, your spirit, and your soul that you are in the presence of a King, and you will not do Him disservice by wandering down the street while your lips are telling Him you love Him.

Practice it for a little while. Learn how to do it.

## Repetition Is Not Vain When It Carries Love

From this point on, if you want to walk, get on your face, sit down, or whatever you want to do, do it.

Jesus, I love You. I glorify and magnify You. Blessed be the day I could call You my Lord and my Savior. I am not ashamed of You. I say it with all my heart: I love You.

I want all creation to know I love You, Jesus. If I cannot keep saying it because something is in the way that will hardly let me love You, I will beat it to death. I will keep telling You how much I love You until it turns into a liquid state and leaves.

Once in a while, you will feel Him shoot through you in a quickening. That means you got on a road you can follow. Sometimes when you say "hallelujah" four or five times, you feel a quickening and it causes you to jump inside. That is a good trail to stay on.

This is not vain repetition. He would not have said vain repetition if there was not a repetition that was not vain. You cannot tell Him how much you love Him enough and have it classified as vain. You cannot do it.

The devil may try to pull this on you: "Which One do I worship, the Father or the Son?"

I will tell you what Jesus said. The Father judges no man, but has committed all judgment to the Son, that all men should honor the Son even as they honor the Father. When you honor the Son in pure, unadulterated worship, you honor the Father. When you honor the Father in pure, unadulterated worship, you honor the Son.

It does not matter how long you give each One. You can give days to One or the Other, and you honor both the Father and the Son. You can give a lifetime to One or the Other, and you have honored the Father and the Son.

Do not let the devil mix you up. Just tell Him how much you love Him. You will get there.

Does it matter how softly you do it? Does it matter if you whisper? Does it matter if you yell? It is not the whispering and it is not the yelling. It is how much you pour yourself into the words you say.

It is not talking fast. It is not talking slow. It is filling your statements with adoration. It will not be long before you hit a baptism of the mind. It will just gush out of you, and they will have to make you quit. You will not want to stop.

The flesh is worthy to fight past. It will fight you, but you are stronger.

You may burst out in tongues, and that is all right, but this time is designated for worship. Pull yourself back into worship. You may burst out again, but try to spend all you can in worship. We will have seasons of praying in tongues. Do it at home. Do it when you are driving. This is worship.

## Worship in Truth Makes the Gospel Unashamed and Powerful

There will be a day when a group of people is around you, and you will say "hallelujah," and they will fall down. They will say, "My God, you know how to testify."

You have that right.

Diseases and devils will cry and leave. Why? Because you can worship Him in truth. Truth will become you, and you will become truth. All things will be possible to him who believes, because there is no limitation to the magnification, understanding, and capacity you have for truth.

Hallelujah, Jesus. I love You and glorify You. I want to tell You I love You. I am not ashamed. I am not ashamed of the gospel of Jesus Christ. I am not ashamed.

Glory, glory, glory. Since God's name is Yah, hallelujah. Say it ten thousand times, and it is one statement: I love You. I worship You.

You are not a man that You can lie. I have what I say. I call myself dead so I can separate to You all the glory and the praise.

Father, I want to tell You how much I love You. I glorify, magnify, and exalt You to high heaven. With all my heart I worship and adore You.

I bless You. I magnify You and give You all the glory. I bless the day I could call You my Father. I bless the day my heart could love You and express from the pureness of the new nature my adoration.

I love You, Holy Spirit. You are supposed to teach me how to glorify Him. Teach me how to worship Him.

Feel free to move around. You can say anything you want to say now.

Sometimes you will jump inside and feel a quickening. Follow it. It is a good line.

## The Room Is Released to Continue Down the Path of Presence

I am breaking into tongues a little bit, and I am letting it happen on purpose. When you break in, let it come. You can fluctuate, but let it come.

The only reason I am telling you about the tongues is because He is pleased. He is very pleased with it, very pleased with all of your hearts reaching out for Him the way they are. Very, very pleased.

Now we are going to leave you on your own for the next twenty minutes. We will come back at ten o'clock.

Some things belong to God, and all the glory is one of them. All the glory for worship belongs to Him too. So lift your hands. Thank Him with all your heart.

You are walking down a path of presence and glory, miracles and faith, answered prayer and all that He is. It is a direct path.

We have many things to walk you into. We will establish them in each of you. This worship will so affect your confession that you will spend one of those hours with the things you are believing for, backed up by forty minutes of receiving worship, another hour of private worship, and then corporate worship.

We will spend five or six hours a week, most of these Friday nights. If we have to move things or change things, we will find time somehow to accomplish this once a week. I am not sure when it will end up.

This was not something He asked me what I thought about. This was: first, find out what it is; establish yourself; then lead them in.

Did you know that some of these things have been hidden for hundreds of years, and you are going to experience them?
