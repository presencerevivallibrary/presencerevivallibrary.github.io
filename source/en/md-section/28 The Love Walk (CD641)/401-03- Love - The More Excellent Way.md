# Love, the More Excellent Way

## The Lord Wants Hearts Seasoned With Love Before Greater Works Come

Friday night we will be doing communion again, and Sunday again, because I know the area He wants to deal with.

"For all those things are not to be left undone. This is high among them: to season your hearts with love and forgiveness, and learn to walk in mercy toward them that are without and grace toward them that are within.

"Let your hearts be seasoned with love and walk in forgiveness toward each other, that I may do among you what I have scheduled to be done, saith the Lord unto you, My seer."

Hallelujah. Hallelujah, Jesus.

How many of you believe that the person next to you is worth wealth? How many know God is willing to expend the wealth of the world to see your soul saved? Do you feel special? You do not have to beg for it either.

Hallelujah.

There is something about walking in love that is stronger than we understand. I do understand that when you and I were lost and undone, on the other side of spiritual death, He sent Jesus across that void. I do understand that there was no guarantee we would accept the blood and be saved, but He sent Him anyway.

It is hard for me to understand the love He had for me when I was yet lost. He called it mercy that caused Him to die, and then grace took me into His family as though nothing had ever happened. I am understanding that some.

But I do understand that hatred, the ability to hurt a believer, is a violation of that very grace you were born again by. That is why God is so hard on it. You did not deserve anything, and He died for you. Then to turn around, as He said, and choke a man over twenty dollars, God just is not too happy about it.

Now we are understanding more and more about that love. As He told the young lawyer, the second commandment is like unto the first: to love your neighbor as yourself. The first is to love God. The same love you love God with, you love your neighbor with.

You say, "I love God, but I do not love my neighbor."

It is impossible, because the same love that loves your neighbor loves God.

That kind of love just reaches out and loves.

"Well, I am directing it."

You cannot direct it, because it encompasses people. If you directed it, it is not God's love.

"Well, I just love Him a whole lot more than people."

You have religion. You have a healthy dose of religion. I should say an unhealthy dose. It will divide you, put you out on a limb, make you right and everybody else wrong, and you will hurt them to prove it.

It was a little disappointing to me to find out that the love of God is developed in me as we love one another. I thought if we could leave them out of the equation, You and I could get along a whole lot better. But then again, I was one of them. I am so glad He did not leave me out of the equation.

I want to develop God's love and His power with it, not without letting the part of that love develop that loves people too.

"Well, I would just like to have the mighty gifts of the Spirit and forget people. Just come out there and do it, and then run back."

What you really want is the exaltation and glory of being used without having to live next to the baser side of people.

Hallelujah.

## Envy, Strife, and Division Reveal That a Person Is Still on Milk

Somebody asked me, "If someone on milk divides himself against people, then what is meat? What is being on meat?"

"Well, I know a whole lot of Word. I am on meat."

You can memorize the whole Bible and still be on milk.

"Well, I can tell you..."

No, you cannot. Notice what he classifies as milk here. 1 Corinthians 3:1:

> And I, brethren, could not speak unto you as unto spiritual, but as unto carnal, even as unto babes in Christ.

He had just taught that the natural man receives not the things of the Spirit of God, neither can he know them, because they are discerned and understood by your new nature. He said, "I could not speak unto you as unto spiritual," that is, somebody discerning with the new nature, "but as unto carnal, even as unto babies in Christ."

> I have fed you with milk, and not with meat: for hitherto ye were not able to bear it, neither yet now are ye able.

I remember several years ago, at the end of that second verse, the Holy Ghost shot through me and said, "Do you understand that this whole first book of Corinthians is to people on milk?"

I said, "How so?"

He said, "Because they could not bear meat. If they could not bear meat, I could not give it to them. So I gave them milk. This whole book is on milk."

That is okay, except it gets pretty heavy. People who really dig into the Word of God preach the depths of 1 Corinthians 13. How about the judgment verse in chapter 5, where Paul turned a man over for the destruction of his flesh so he would not leaven the whole church? This is all milk.

I would kind of like to know what classifies a man as being on milk. I myself am an astounding teacher. I know the Word of God.

"Yes, are you on meat or on milk?"

I know people who hold the limelight in the teaching ministry, and they are on milk.

"How do you know that?"

Because they have divided themselves against other people.

"How do you know?"

Because he said so:

> For ye are yet carnal: for whereas there is among you envying, and strife, and divisions...

Thank God we are on meat. He is certainly not talking to any of us. After all, we have the revelation.

> ...are ye not carnal, and walk as men?

He is talking about walking as mere men, according to the carnal appetites of the flesh, appearing to those within and without as if you had never even been born again.

He classified what milk is like. You can quote the whole New Testament. It does not make any difference to me. You might use that to divide yourself against somebody else.

I do not like cults. I do not like false doctrine. I do not like leaving part of the gospel out. But if somebody is born again, they are my brother, and they are going to hold a place.

Hallelujah.

So this is what it is to be on milk: envy, strife, and divisions.

If envy, strife, and division classify a person as being on milk, then what would you be looking for in the growth of a person? Their astounding knowledge of the Word? Their ability to quote? How much they know? Perhaps the power they walk in?

To a certain degree, a lot of people use the power of God to the detriment of the kingdom of God, to confirm things they are doing. That is called merchandising the anointing. God trusts you with a precious pearl or treasure, and what do we do? Immediately convert it to the flesh.

I can tell you, ministries that do that do not last the duration of their call. Thank God for people like Lester Sumrall and Kenneth Hagin, who spent thirty years putting a root system in. After they put their root system in, a tree grew out of it and affected the whole world. Neither one of those would fall into those other areas, thank goodness.

But what is longevity? What are we looking for? If people used in power are cut off all the time, come under judgment all the time, ministries cut short, falling to this and falling to that, falling to different aspects of the flesh - promiscuousness, adultery, all kinds of things - then what are we looking for?

When he said the love of money is the root of all evil, of course he did not say money itself, but the love of it. He was not talking about everything springing from money itself. He meant if you are so weak that the love of money is your motivation, then you will also have other things wrong. You will be willing to do anything or hurt people for money, in ministry, business, or anything else. If that root is in you, then many times you are undoubtedly weak in the appetites of the flesh department, in promiscuous areas.

You see people falling all the time.

I am looking for maturity. What am I really looking for?

## Spiritual Gifts Qualify Operations, but Love Governs Their Use

Go to 1 Corinthians 12. Let us start with the first verse:

> Now concerning spiritual gifts, brethren, I would not have you ignorant.

Remember, this is all milk.

If there is any area I do not want to be ignorant in, it is the operation of His spiritual gifts.

> Ye know that ye were Gentiles, carried away unto these dumb idols, even as ye were led.
> 
> Wherefore I give you to understand...

Now that you are serving the living God, He has a voice. He is not a dumb idol.

> ...that no man speaking by the Spirit of God calleth Jesus accursed: and that no man can say that Jesus is the Lord, but by the Holy Ghost.

When he said, "Concerning spiritual gifts, brethren, I would not have you ignorant," there are nine gifts of the Spirit that empower eight operations of God. Not only do these nine gifts empower these eight operations, but they qualify the eight operations for what they are.

He lists them beginning with verse 27:

> Now ye are the body of Christ, and members in particular.

As much as you can, remember this lineup. I am an individual member in a vast body called the body of Christ, as he says in Romans 12 and Ephesians 4.

> And God hath set some in the church, first apostles, secondarily prophets, thirdly teachers...

He gives them in the order they come into existence, in divine order.

> ...after that miracles, then gifts of healings...

That encompasses pastor and evangelist. Entry to the fivefold was on the level of miracles and healings. Mostly the traveling man did miracles, and the stationary man had the gifts of healings. That is entry level to the fivefold. That is why he left pastor and evangelist out here.

He did not leave them out in Ephesians 4, but he did here and replaced them with the qualification of those two calls.

"Well, I am a pastor. I am not called to operate in miracles."

Beg your pardon. You are called to operate in healing and miracles. In fact, that is just entry level to the fivefold.

He says thirdly teachers, after that miracles, gifts of healings, and finally the sixth and seventh operations, helps and governments. Then the eighth operation is diversities of tongues.

The nine gifts of the Spirit qualify you for whatever operation of these eight you fulfill. How could you fulfill the operation and calling of diversities of tongues without speaking with tongues?

The mighty apostle has all these offices working in him. He can establish them and operates in every one of them.

Now notice what he goes on to say after he lists diversities of tongues:

> Are all apostles? are all prophets? are all teachers? are all workers of miracles?

Certainly not. Are all apostles? Are all prophets? Are all teachers? Of course not. Are all workers of miracles? Definitely not.

> Have all the gifts of healing? do all speak with tongues? do all interpret?

I did a boyfriend's wedding one time, and the grandma was grilling me. She wanted to know what kind of preacher I was. I was just doing it to be nice anyway. I did not want to.

She said, "What kind of preacher are you?"

I said, "Well, let me see. I am charismatic. How is that?"

"Oh. Like who can you identify with?"

I said, "Have you heard of Kenneth Hagin?"

"No."

"How about Oral Roberts?"

"Yes."

"Kind of like that."

She said, "Oh. You speak with tongues?"

I said, "Yes, I do."

She said, "Well, to each their own. Paul says, 'Are all apostles? Are all teachers? Are all prophets? Do all speak with tongues?' He says no, not all."

I said, "Oh yes, you are. There are four diversities of tongues. The one he was talking about here was ministry to the body. That is why he said, 'Do all speak with tongues? Do all interpret?' Not everybody speaks in a tongue to be interpreted, but everybody speaks in a tongue for personal edification."

I did not mind that she was choking a little bit. I said, "I am perfectly content to wait until you get to heaven to find out."

Hallelujah.

He said:

> But covet earnestly the best gifts: and yet shew I unto you a more excellent way.

What are the best gifts? The ones that qualify your calling. Why would you want other ones? By this time you should know what you are called to. If you are called to evangelize, you are called for a full-blown operation of the workings of miracles. It is yours, along with several other giftings that go with it.

"Well, I thought Benny Hinn was a special vessel."

So are you. So are you.

I remember Benny Hinn was on a program with a nun. He said something about miracles and was testifying about miracles. After a while, she had to get in on it. She said, "But Benny, you are a special vessel."

That is a good way to let yourself out of it, don't you think?

Benny just reached over as kindly as he could and said, "Sister, you can have this. You can have this."

Do you know how they give you life like they do and then say, "You can have this"?

## The More Excellent Way Is to Covet Love With the Same Tenacity as Gifts

He said:

> Covet earnestly the best gifts: and yet shew I unto you a more excellent way.

In each facet He has brought me to, and in the understanding of each call, I have coveted the gifts that go with it. I know how to do that too. My God, I know how to covet these best gifts. I know how to fast and pray, beat the devil to pieces, and stand my ground. I know how to do that.

That is why when He said, "Yet I show you a more excellent way," I asked, a more excellent way to do what? A more excellent way to covet the best gifts. There is a more excellent way than fasting and praying, walking the floor, backing that devil off, and holding my ground.

Covet earnestly. Covet is strong. In fact, it was so strong in the Ten Commandments that He used it as a "thou shalt not." On the positive side, He says, "Covet earnestly the best gifts." That has to be strong.

It is like a man going without eating. What do you want? Food. Food looks better than new cars and everything. After forty days or so, hamburgers dance through your head, not Lexuses.

He said, "Covet earnestly the best gifts. Yet I show you a more excellent way." A more excellent way to do what? A more excellent way to covet.

There was not supposed to be a chapter 13. It was supposed to be one long chapter 12. In other words, he did not change subjects.

> Though I speak with the tongues of men and of angels, and have not charity...

Love in action.

> ...I am become as sounding brass, or a tinkling cymbal.

If I go after the love of God with the same tenacity that I went after giftings that can carry some sort of exaltation along with them - "Look at me; He uses me" - that is the more excellent way.

It is a lot easier to skip past picking up cigarette butts in the parking lot and just head for the pulpit, wouldn't you think?

God forbid that I would do something.

Do not mistake this verse for those who preach it and say, "You can pray in tongues all you want. If you do not have love, you are sounding brass and a tinkling cymbal." What he was saying is that you can have divers tongues and the capacity to pray and speak with tongues and still be a divider, a hater, an envious person who causes a lot of trouble.

What he did not say is that a man who establishes himself consistently in a strong prayer life of edification could stay sounding brass and tinkling cymbal. He will purge the old brass out of you and give you gold.

Hallelujah.

So He says, "I will show you a more excellent way." Do you want to cut your time short? Do you want to go past all the natural avenues? Seek the love of God with the same tenacity, even when it hurts.

That is why God was able to come with judgment here a while back, because we had satisfied Him in how far we extended mercy. This love was the hardest thing to die to the world over.

Sometimes people got so mad at me, and there are still people who left the church because they were real mad that we extended love beyond what they thought was necessary. Well, I cannot help it. If you are going to leave me for doing that, have a happy journey.

One man called me recently. All I had ever done was help him. I helped train him in the early days, helped with meetings, took time, extended myself, did lots and lots of things. When he began to make it in ministry, he fell in with those who were always talking about us. He started running me down, running me down, running me down.

Finally it began to eat at him, and he called me up. He said they had started talking about my family and all that.

I said, "What am I guilty of? Which action did I extend toward you? Which time when we went out of our way to get you and help you in ministry? What am I guilty of?"

He got real quiet.

I said, "If you are going to charge me for believing like a crazy man for my family, then charge me, because my conscience is clean."

He said, "I am sorry."

I said, "That is okay. I got out of that mess a long time ago. I just felt sorry for you."

There is a more excellent way, and the dying process is hard. The choice not to defend myself so many times felt like it was stripping me of my manhood, until God came.

Do you understand? We are never going to stop. Our roots are getting fat. Wait until you see the tree. We are never going to stop. When I go home, fat fruit will be hanging off fat limbs. Whatever you do for God, it is going to be fat: overweight, large, big.

I said we are never going to go away. I am never going to quit. I am never going to quit pushing the devil around. My body feels better. My mind feels better. I feel better than I did in my twenties and thirties. Come on. When does this stop?

I am going to finish my course full of wisdom and power. Our packages and teachings are going to launch preachers, if He tarries a hundred years, wherever they find them. We are a devastating, mountain-moving machine going somewhere to flourish, and nobody or nothing can stop us.

Sure, it was hard dying every time I had to choose to keep my mouth shut and let them rant and rave and leave in a flurry. Each time there is a death process when you choose to do the right thing.

## Peace Cannot Mature While the Capacity to Hurt People Remains

I am supposed to go after love with the same tenacity I would have gone after anything God has that could glorify me. I love the love of God. I am loving it more and more, because I have found that it is not until you get to a certain place in His love that you will even walk in peace.

If you have a capacity to hurt somebody, peace is a little ways from you. You will just admire other people's speeches on peace. The thing that hurts people will keep you from the peace.

He said:

> Peace I leave with you, my peace I give unto you: not as the world giveth...

Especially your own family. If the love of God cannot pour through your family and then to the world, you will find it will not flow through the world and then to your family. Your family is not supposed to be the catch-all for your bad day.

Hallelujah.

Now you can kind of remember this lineup, can't you? First apostle, prophet, teacher, workings of miracles, gifts of healings, helps, governments, and diversities of tongues. We are trying to find out who is on meat.

We know it is somebody who covets the things of God with the tenacity of love. I am finding out there is no place for envy, strife, and division in that. This person just will not do it. You can live in it all you want; he is just not going to be part of it.

Don't you just hate it when you walk up to somebody and want to gossip, and they say, "I do not want to hear it"? It is an indictment against your spirituality.

## Ephesians 4 Shows Gifts Were Given to Build a Functioning Body

Go to Ephesians 4, and let us look at this meat in a little more depth. Begin with verse 7, which again says the same thing as Romans 12 and the same thing as 1 Corinthians 12:

> But unto every one of us is given grace according to the measure of the gift of Christ.

Paul spoke in Romans 12 and said we have graces differing, different callings differing according to the grace God gave us. You can understand this in verse 7. To every one of us is given grace according to the measure of the gift of Christ. The grace he is talking about is your calling.

We have callings or gifts differing according to the grace God gave us. So you ought not think of yourself more highly than you ought to think, but think soberly. As Paul said, "I speak according to the grace given to me." You ought not think of yourself more highly. In other words, you did not call yourself. You did not place yourself.

Why should you think of yourself more highly? Did you give yourself an apostolic call, a prophet's call, a pastor's call, helps, government? Did you call yourself? No. Who chose it? God did.

That is why he says:

> But unto every one of us is given grace according to the measure of the gift of Christ.

That is the measure He has asked us to fill in the body of Christ: hand, foot, leg, eye, whatever your calling is.

He goes on:

> Wherefore he saith, When he ascended up on high, he led captivity captive, and gave gifts unto men.

Then he wanted you to know:

> Now that he ascended, what is it but that he also descended first into the lower parts of the earth?

In other words, He suffered death and went to the lower parts. As Jonah was in the belly of the whale three days and three nights, so must the Son of Man be in the center of the earth. He descended first into the lower parts of the earth. That is another story.

He did so to lead captivity captive and equip you with these gifts by grace. You did not pay for them. You did not earn them. You received them in the measure you are called: hand, foot, eye, apostle, prophet, teacher.

When He said you are called to fill your measure, the gift of faith, the measure of faith, was to fulfill your calling, not mine. That is why you get judged for it. He gave you first the grace for your call, then the measure of faith to fill it.

"Well, I fell short."

He gave it to you anyway. It was yours. Your reason will not stand up.

He continues:

> He that descended is the same also that ascended up far above all heavens, that he might fill all things.
> 
> And he gave some, apostles; and some, prophets; and some, evangelists; and some, pastors and teachers;

Here he listed all five. In 1 Corinthians 12, he said apostle, prophet, teacher, workings of miracles, gifts of healings. Entry level to the fivefold was with pastor and evangelist. You either stand still and teach or travel and teach, but you are supposed to be equipped at entry level with gifts of healings and workings of miracles.

Notice this verse very closely:

> For the perfecting of the saints, for the work of the ministry, for the edifying of the body of Christ.

I was doing a camp meeting overseas and sitting next to a strong teacher in the body. I had mentioned that entry level to the fivefold was workings of miracles and gifts of healings, which replaced pastor and evangelist in the 1 Corinthians 12 list.

He said, "Well, I believe the pastor gift is part of governments."

I said, "No, it is part of the fivefold. Its entry level was miracles and healings."

He said, "No, I believe miracles and healings empower the prophet, apostle, and teacher."

I said, "No, it is pastor and evangelist."

He said, "No, it is part of government, because it says helps and governments."

I said, "No, I can show you because I have assimilated both chapters, 1 Corinthians 12 and Ephesians 4."

Of course, he was waiting for me to disprove it. It had never been done before. We have already upset a lot of people's apple carts. Why stop now? If stopping and conforming to something means stopping my journey, no thank you. I found out people make bad mistakes. I check everything anybody teaches me against the Holy Spirit. I am sorry. I do not have time to take your journey if you are not taking the right one.

I said, "Read this very closely in Ephesians. He gave gifts unto men: apostles, prophets, teachers, pastors, and evangelists for the perfecting of the saints because these ministries nurture and handle the Word. Then He said, 'for the work of the ministry,' and then He separated again and said, 'for the edification of the saints.'"

This is how they taught that in government books: the fivefold are gifts given to the body of Christ for their perfecting. We perfect you so you can do the work of the ministry, and that brings edification to the body of Christ. So we exalted ourselves as those great gifts.

I have had Rolls-Royces next door to me just in case I wanted ice cream at four in the morning. "Hello, ice cream," and they had to go get it, because I am the gift. You are not. I am. I am a gift.

Then for the exit, it was fun. You had something like the Rams football team to block the little ladies who were trying to get through the lines to get prayer. "Tackle that one. Stop that one. That one is getting through. Stop her."

Let me tell you what it is saying when you assimilate and pull these two chapters together.

1 Corinthians 12 says: apostle, prophet, teacher, workings of miracles, gifts of healings, helps, governments, diversities of tongues.

Ephesians 4 says: apostle, prophet, teacher, evangelist, pastor for the perfecting of the saints; helps and governments for the work of the ministry; the eighth operation, diversities of tongues, for the edification of the saints.

In other words, He ascended on high and gave gifts to men. He came back to the body with a handful of gifts and threw them into the body. People stood up all over the place and said, "I am called as a prophet. I am an apostle. I am called as a teacher. I am called as a pastor. I am called as an evangelist." They received these gifts and began to fulfill them in the body.

Then, for the sake of teaching, God went back to heaven a second time, got another handful of gifts, came back, and threw them into the body. People started standing up all over and saying, "I have been called in helps and governments for the work of the ministry."

Then He went back to heaven a third time, grabbed a handful of gifts, diversities of tongues, brought them down, and threw them into the body. Everybody stood up and said, "I received an edification gift." Others stood up and said, "I am called to interpretation, intercession, or to travel the world and preach in other languages as He moves on me supernaturally."

Diversities of tongues was given for the edification of the saints, helps and governments for the work of the ministry, and the fivefold for the perfecting of the saints.

I want you to know, the seat next to me was quiet.

## Meat Is the Whole Body Increasing Itself in Love

He gave these giftings to the body for what purpose?

Who would be somebody on meat? Somebody who does not divide and does not envy.

He gave these gifts:

> Till we all come in the unity of the faith, and of the knowledge of the Son of God, unto a perfect man, unto the measure of the stature of the fullness of Christ:
> 
> That we henceforth be no more children, tossed to and fro, and carried about with every wind of doctrine...

No more children. That is milk.

> ...by the sleight of men, and cunning craftiness, whereby they lie in wait to deceive;
> 
> But speaking the truth in love, may grow up into him in all things, which is the head, even Christ:
> 
> From whom the whole body fitly joined together and compacted by that which every joint supplieth...

He was talking about the whole part of the body represented between the joints: the arm, the hand, the leg.

> ...according to the effectual working in the measure of every part...

Every call, every part.

> ...maketh increase of the body unto the edifying of itself in love.

In 1 Corinthians 3, who is on milk? The divided, carnal, envious person.

Who is on meat? Those who increase in their calling into a unity of the body, in obedience to the Head, and give increase by the edifying of itself, by every measure, in love.

That, my friend, is meat.

Friday night we are going to get another tremendous opportunity to judge ourselves. Pray these next few days. Let Him search our hearts. The more excellent way is to begin to covet forgiveness and peace with the same tenacity with which you would like to be used in power to raise the dead.

Remember, if somebody is fighting you and you want to put the lights out, and you decide for love, you start right there stripping that thing of its power to dominate you. What you are really fighting for is a high-level operation of your measure and calling.

"I thought I was fighting to forgive him."

You are fighting for the increase of that measure of your part in the body of Christ, to come to the fullness of its calling and be obedient to the Head by which every part is compacted.

"I thought I was just fighting to forgive him."

You are fighting to operate in the fullness of the measure of your call.

That makes it a little easier, doesn't it?

"You ugly..."

Yell, "I am filling a measure in the body."

"We do not like anything about you."

"That is okay. He can make up for it. Hallelujah. I am filling the measure in the body, edifying myself in love. I make a decision to love you."

You know that little devil. You know how your mind sharpens up when somebody says something ugly. You can say eight things right there, and one of them wants to jump out of your mouth, with eight more waiting. Something about that anointing just clears your head and you get so sharp.

Sometimes you get away with it a little bit when you are preaching. "You can pray in tongues two months and get weird? You were already weird. I already prayed too much and did not get like you are."

What you are really fighting for is for your measure to come to the fullness of the stature of the operation of the Head. That is what you are fighting for.

It does not feel like it with every decision. It does not feel like it.

## Refusing Compromise Protects the Authority of Your Witness

I can remember one of the last bad compromises I made. It was with a movie star's wife. There was a lot of mileage in having a movie star's wife coming. She was telling everybody wherever she went, including her husband, "Yes, when I go to church now..."

Not only a lot of mileage. I was thinking of the tithes. Glory.

One day she wanted me to go pray for a racehorse they were racing. She smoothed it over this way: "You can pray for my husband," because he was going to race the horse.

I started to say no, but something overpowered it: visions of tithe checks, exhortation, dropping her name, name-dropping. I lost all of that.

I said yes.

Thank God for the weather, because something happened and they could not race the horse. I said, "I got out of it."

God said, "No, you did it. As a man thinketh in his heart, you did it."

I said, "Yes, I did."

"Yes."

I said, "If I had told her no, she would have left."

He said to me, "She is leaving anyway."

She did, two weeks later. Adios.

I said, "You were right. She left."

He said, "The difference is, son, if you had said no, yes, she would have left. But if she had ever wanted help, she would have come back. As it is now, son, you are just part of a long list of preachers she has bought."

Sometimes it is hard to say no.

Glory to God. You can do it. He will help you. He will help you.

Glory be to God forever.

## The More Excellent Way Lets Agape Take Over the Soul

Why don't we stand up and lift our hands? Just thank Him for the more excellent way.

Though I speak with tongues of men and angels, I go on to the more excellent way. Pursue. Seek after. Let the agape escape your spirit with such velocity that it takes that little soul of yours over and renders it submitted.

Hallelujah.

Let Him reason things and let Him see people in different lights. Come on. Let Him do this utmost important work on the inside of us.

In the early service, I prophesied the difference in Mennonite communities who have to separate themselves from sinners to walk in the kind of love they see in the Word. But God wants you right out there with them, walking in that kind of love. He will take care of you. He promised He would.

Most of them resorted to religion after a while because they could not figure out how to walk in those verses. But there is a way. It is with God's strength. He holds you up. You can die before you die. That is where the martyrs come from. They did not run.

Hallelujah.
