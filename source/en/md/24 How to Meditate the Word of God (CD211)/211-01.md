If you are teaching the Word, never just teach it off one Scripture. If you do, you are running about a seventy-five percent chance of being in error.

You can lay it down through prayer and the prayer of faith, yes. But if it is a cross you picked up, then it is a cross you can lay down.

You certainly cannot lay down a wheelchair and a broken body unless you have the Word. Can you see the deception in that?

Did you notice there He said you cannot be My disciple? In other words, if your wife is fighting you, and the land and all that stuff is pulling on you, then you have to pick up your cross and make a decision to follow Him, or you cannot be His disciple.

A disciple is a disciplined follower. Jesus says:

> Why call ye Me, Lord, Lord, and do not the things which I say?

If you do not do His Word, He is not over your all. "Lord" means over all. Boy, when He said Lord, He did not say it once. He said, "Lord, Lord," twice in worship. "Why do you call Me Lord, Lord, and do not the things which I say?"

I thought that when Jesus was talking about me having to choose between Him and everything else, because my wife cannot save me, my land cannot save me, and my oxen cannot save me, He was being hard on me. I thought He was demanding total sacrifice, until I got into the rest of the Scriptures.

Now we are coming up on revelation number two.

In the course of reading through this chapter several times, I ended up at the end of it, on the thirty-third verse, and a bell went off in my spirit that brought me back to the first half. I knew Jesus was still talking about the same thing.

As far as I was concerned, He changed the subject when He started talking about towers and building. But He did not.

Look at verse 33 with me, and listen to your spirit:

> So likewise, whosoever he be of you that forsaketh not all that he hath, he cannot be My disciple.

Did you notice He did not change the subject? Does that agree with your spirit?

Now we are after another revelation. He threw clear back to the twenty-sixth verse in this one. That means everything between here and verse 33 is the "likewise" He is comparing us to. Revelation number two must be right in there.

Now look at verse 34:

> Salt is good: but if the salt have lost his savour, wherewith shall it be seasoned?
>
> It is neither fit for the land, nor yet for the dunghill; but men cast it out. He that hath ears to hear, let him hear.

He is talking about the ears I am telling you to listen with tonight. He is talking about the ears you could have used to go ahead and get the rest of this. That is why He says, "He that hath ears to hear, let him hear."

I said, "Lord, I am going to hear. I am going to hear with my inner man."

Right here in verse 34, there was a part of this that broke this whole chapter open for me.

He said, "Salt is good, but if salt has lost its flavor, wherewith shall it be seasoned?" What is He saying here?

Take a handful of salt. Take the flavor out of it. Now where are you going to go to get something that can season it to make it taste like salt again? There is nothing. There is no substitute you can get to season salt back up and make it taste like salt.

With that in mind, throw back to verse 26:

> If any man come to Me, and hate not his father, and mother, and wife, and children... and his own life also, he cannot be My disciple.

In other words, if you reject Him over land, houses, or anything else, then where are you going to go to get seasoned? Where are you going to go? What are you going to turn to?

Now we are ready for the next verse:

> For which of you, intending to build a tower, sitteth not down first, and counteth the cost, whether he have sufficient to finish it?
>
> Lest haply, after he hath laid the foundation, and is not able to finish it, all that behold it begin to mock him,
>
> Saying, This man began to build, and was not able to finish.

Notice He is asking a question here. You have to realize I went over this and over it and listened to my spirit.

He is saying, "Which of you intending to build a tower will not sit down first and count the cost?"

I am asking you the same question Jesus did. How many of you would build a house, start building it in the middle of the summer, and only have enough money to finish everything but the roof, knowing it would rain?

How many of you are dumb enough to do that?

I am not talking about faith. If you give me a nail, I will tell somebody, "I will put a house on it." There was a bum going around with a button, handing it to people and saying, "Would you sew a shirt on it?"

I am not talking about faith people, because I know, give me a nail and we will put a house on it. I am saying He was speaking strictly in a natural sense.

Is there any of you dumb enough to sit down with half enough material, knowing you only have half enough to finish a house? None of us, really.

Let us say I owned a construction company, and I was building a six-million-dollar complex. There was a company watching me that was going to give me a job worth thirty million dollars. What if I started building, then all of a sudden I ran out of funds, and there it sat by the freeway?

Do you think that other company would give me their job? They would not, because I was too stupid to count the cost and see if I had enough to finish it.

He is asking you this: which of you is that dumb? Literally, none of you.

Then He says:

> Or what king, going to make war against another king, sitteth not down first, and consulteth whether he be able with ten thousand to meet him that cometh against him with twenty thousand?
>
> Or else, while the other is yet a great way off, he sendeth an ambassage, and desireth conditions of peace.

He is asking you, "Which of you would go after twenty thousand with ten thousand?" Just answer directly. How many of you would do that? How many would build a house without enough to finish?

In the natural, none of you.

Now you know who the "likewise" is in the next Scripture.

> So likewise, whosoever he be of you that forsaketh not all that he hath, he cannot be My disciple.

Who is the likewise? He is saying you are like somebody. Who are you like? The person too dumb to count the cost to build a whole building, and the king too dumb to sit down and find out whether he can whip twenty thousand with ten thousand.

He said you are like these dumb people who build a house with no foundation and never complete the job. That is how dumb you literally are. I have got to get this over to you. That is how dumb you are.

In other words, He is putting you in exactly the same boat with the man who did not have enough materials to finish that tower if you do not forsake all that you have to become His disciple. If you do not, you are the guy who fails. You are a half-a-tower builder if you do not sell out to His Word.

Can you see that now? That is the second revelation in this. You fail if you do not. It looks like you are failing if you do.

You say, "I have to choose Jesus over my wife and everything else?"

Yes. You will lose her if you do not.

"You mean I have to choose between Him and oxen and land and houses?"

You will lose them if you do not.

So likewise, whosoever, and that covers you and me, whoever does not forsake all that he has cannot be His disciple. In other words, you are dumb, and you only have half enough to finish anything you start unless you found it on the Word.

Did you notice He said, "Cannot be My disciple"?

Disciple means disciplined follower. The word disciple is derived from the word discipline.

Concerning that, Jesus says:

> If ye continue in My word, then are ye My disciples indeed;
>
> And ye shall know the truth, and the truth shall make you free.

There is only one Scripture that tells you what makes you a disciple. He said, "Why do you call Me Lord and do not what I say?" If you are not doing what He says, you are not His disciple. You might be born again, but you are not His disciple.

He says unless you continue in My Word, when you continue in it, build a tower founded on the Word, and continue in it, then you are My disciples indeed, meaning in your deeds, and you will know the truth, and the truth will set you free.

In other words, your tower will always finish, and you will always win your wars. He says likewise, if you do not forsake, you are as dumb as the guy who had half enough material.

There is only one way you will become His disciple. One way: continue in His Word.

So basically what He is saying here is, "Likewise, whosoever of you does not forsake all that he has for the Word, sell out to the Word, follow it, and continue in it, cannot be My disciple."

Now you say, "I know preachers, lots of them, who have big churches. Everybody is coming, and they look like they are doing real good to me. They are making it."

Yes, dying in their bodies. A lot of them are depressed, sick, and using excuses for why the Word does not work instead of why it does. And you call their house built on a rock?

There were two major revelations in that. Could you see them? The two connected. They tied in with one another. There was the salvation call, the selling out to it, and the consequences for the man who does not do it.

Thank You, Jesus.
