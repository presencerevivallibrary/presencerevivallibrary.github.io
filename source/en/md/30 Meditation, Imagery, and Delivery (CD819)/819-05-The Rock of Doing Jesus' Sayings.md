# The Rock of Doing Jesus' Sayings

We are beginning these messages on meditation with the theme from Psalm 1:1, which we probably will be doing in every service:

> Blessed is the man that walketh not in the counsel of the ungodly,
> nor standeth in the way of sinners,
> nor sitteth in the seat of the scornful.
> But his delight is in the law of the Lord;
> and in His law doth he meditate day and night.

Once again, we found out what qualifies us to be that blessed man of God: if we do not take our counsel from the world, if we do not do things the way sinners do, and if we delight ourselves in His Word through meditation to extract the counsel we need to walk by, so we do not have to do things the way the world does.

Meditation is not study, memorization, or reading, although they all assist meditation. Meditation is when the Holy Spirit gets involved with your spirit in such a way as to extract that counsel from the Word, to take the same meaning that left the heart of God and reconstruct it in your heart in such a form that you can walk by it.

That is the true form of meditation.

How many understood more about that last night after the message?

We are going to get into the actual breaking of verses again. Once again, we have no trouble identifying Jesus as the Master Teacher, the greatest who ever lived. If He were teaching at this pulpit, using the mastery of words, by the time He finished He would have started painting a picture at the top of this pulpit. By the time He reached the bottom, He would have painted a perfect picture of this pulpit within your spirit.

Therefore, every verse is a thought within itself. It is a stroke of the brush, but invariably it is always building toward the completion of the entire picture.

It has been us who have been guilty of taking verses out of context. If I were to take a corn seed, inside of which is the diagramming and programming for a stalk, leaves, and two ears of corn on the average, and fragment it into four pieces, then plant the fragments hoping for a greater harvest, I would get nothing. The reason I would get nothing is because I destroyed the image on the inside of the seed.

Faith comes by hearing, not your pets, not your preconceived ideas, not your theology, not your doctrine. Faith comes by hearing, and hearing by the Word of God.

Glory to God.

If we refuse to take verses out of context, we will come up with what we came up with yesterday morning. Many times the meaning of each individual verse changes when you find out all they are is part of the whole.

We make meditation very difficult for the Holy Spirit by taking a verse out of the middle. We are asking for a miracle, and He can do it. God can take that singular verse and paint the whole picture around it, but that is the exception and not the rule. The rule is for you to meditate on the entire picture until the Holy Spirit can take that last verse and stroke it in.

So we try one verse when we should be meditating the whole image. The Holy Spirit always alerts me to the beginning of the subject and the end.

How many know Luke 6 is the conclusion of His tremendous message, the Sermon on the Mount? Tremendous message. He wound it up with this statement about doing His sayings. He said, "The man that does My sayings, I will show you to whom he is like."

We might as well begin right there, Luke 6:47. I am starting at the end of this, because how could I possibly preach all of the Beatitudes? We just have to demonstrate principles. We will take the last part, from about the twenty-seventh verse over to the forty-ninth verse, just to demonstrate meditation and principles. We would have to go back and take a week or two, as you saw yesterday morning.

Hallelujah.

Luke 6:47:

> Whosoever cometh to Me, and heareth My sayings, and doeth them,
> I will shew you to whom he is like:
> He is like a man which built an house, and digged deep,
> and laid the foundation on a rock:
> and when the flood arose, the stream beat vehemently upon that house,
> and could not shake it: for it was founded upon a rock.

Notice this. Whosoever comes to Me, hears My sayings, and does them, I will show you what he is like. He is like a man who dug deep and laid a foundation on the rock.

You hear people preaching an awful lot about the rock being Jesus Christ. Well, the rock is Him. It is Him. But the foundational rock He is talking about here is doing Jesus' sayings. There are a lot of people on the Rock who have not laid a foundation on the rock. Therefore, anything that comes down that would take any sinner out takes them out: diseases, pain, anything else. When their storm arises, it takes them out because they are not founded on the rock of doing His sayings.

Jesus said, "Let Me show you to whom this man is like, the one who does them. He is like a man who dug deep."

If the rock is doing Jesus' sayings, then what would the deep digging be? The man would be digging deep through meditation into the revelation knowledge of His words so that he could do them. He would be digging deep into His sayings, into His words, through meditation, so he would have that counsel to walk by.

He says he dug deep and laid his foundation on a rock. When the storm came, Jesus did not tell you that you would not have a storm. As a matter of fact, He guaranteed that you would.

How many of you have lived your Christian life without one storm?

He said when the storm arose, it beat vehemently upon the house and could not shake it because it was founded on this rock. It could not shake it. It does not matter how bad the storm gets. What He said would come. He guaranteed you that if you did His sayings, your house would not go down the river.

This made me want to do His sayings probably more than any other time in my life when I first started discovering what He was talking about here.

Of course, coming from an ultra-holiness background, I had my favorite sayings of Jesus:

> The works that I do shall ye do also; and greater works than these shall ye do; because I go unto My Father.

That was one of my favorite sayings. I would have liked to have done that one. Another favorite saying:

> Whosoever shall say unto this mountain, Be thou removed, and be thou cast into the sea...

But when my house kept being shaken and shifted around, and I came out of the trials worse off than when I went in, I thought, "This is not right. I am supposed to come out at least equal."

I said, "But I am doing Your work, raising the dead, praying for the sick, trying to do the greater works. I am doing all Your sayings. How come I am having such trouble?"

In those days, He was teaching me how to meditate. He said, "Roberson, when are you going to quit taking that verse out of context?"

My favorite answer in those days was, "How about now? How about right now?"

He said, "Okay. I will show you what sayings I am talking about. There is no use going to another part of the Word of God and extracting any sayings you want. The sayings I am talking about pertain to My message here."

I did not have to back up very far to find out what sayings He was talking about. We will take it from around verse 27.

Okay, then when your house goes down the river, please do not blame my Father. Please.

Let me show you something. I am going to be jumping back and forth to show you how in harmony these verses are, because what we are studying is more the method of extracting revelation knowledge than even the revelation knowledge contained here itself.

You are going to be known by your fruit. Whether we like that or not, that is a spiritual law. You simply will be known by your fruit.

You do not gather grapes off a bramble bush. If you want grapes, you have to plant grapes. If you get brambles, it is because you planted brambles, and you cannot blame anybody else.

"Throw him!"

You did, when you had the opportunity to do His sayings. He said, "Love your enemies. Do good to them that hate you. Pray for them that despitefully use you. Him that would smite you on one cheek, turn the other. Give to them that ask. If a man takes your cloak, give him your coat also."

For every action, there is a reaction. When that confrontation came to you, you sowed one manner of seed or you sowed the other.

Before we get too far into this, I want to thank God for Jesus Christ the Advocate. As soon as you realize what you are doing, the Advocate, Jesus Christ the righteous, goes between as our lawyer. When you confess it, He not only wipes the seed out, He wipes the harvest out and even destroys the ground it grew in. You would have to replant that thing.

This is what irritates the devil so badly. He can build a well-planned case against you for two years, plotting your failure, and in ten seconds you can take advantage of the Advocate and wipe out two years of work. He really has been defeated, if you can only realize that.

You have to make up your mind to walk in His principles and His truths.

If there is anything I want to be on God's green earth, it is a blessed man of God, a man who has and walks in His counsel. I cannot afford not to do these things. I love my family too much. I love you too much to afford not to walk in these principles.

"Oh God, You know I am not going to be one of them in strife. I am confessing that I love so-and-so. I am not going to be like those who get in strife, because I resist the devil and he flees from me."

I thought, "This is supposed to work, is it not? I am loving my enemy. I am praying for him. I am resisting the devil. I broke his hold over me."

The Lord said, "When are you going to quit taking My verses out of context?"

"How about now?"

He said, "Humble yourself under the mighty hand of God and resist the devil. He will flee from you."

Humble means totally teachable. Humble yourself by casting the whole of your care on Him, by casting it onto the Word. Then He said, "Resist the devil in the faith." Faith comes by hearing, and hearing by the Word.

I said, "God, how do You want me to resist the devil? I am doing it. Satan, get off him. Get off me."

When I went into my seeking time, I found out my nature. I did not want the devil to get off him for his sake. I wanted him off him for my sake. I did not want him doing me wrong. It was dirty, ugly, ugly. It was not him I was concerned about. It was me. I wanted the devil off him so he would get off me.

That is Old Testament kind of love: love your neighbor as yourself. The law said an eye for an eye, a tooth for a tooth. The only reason I did not pluck your eye out and kill your dog is because under the law you could do the same to me. It is not that I liked you. I liked me. So I would not do unto you, so you would not do unto me. That was the law that restrained the flesh. The punishment was simply worse than the sin. Adultery? Die. Homosexuality? Die. So you really had to count the cost.

At the end of this covenant, He says, "Love your brother as I have loved you," which is the supernatural kind of love. I found out it comes from within and is regardless of circumstances. It is a love that seeks not its own, no paychecks, no rewards rendered for services rendered. It is a debt I cannot pay. I do not care how many payments of love I make to you. No matter how you react to them, I cannot pay that payment in full because Jesus put me in debt to you to love.

This is getting interesting.

Do good.

That corresponding action with my faith, with my confession, began to tear the wall down and eventually brought total healing.

I said, "No, I can fix this."

He says, "Do good to them that hate you. Pray for those that despitefully use you."

There are corresponding actions to my faith.

Then He will take care of the storm.

That is some good stuff.

Faith.

Your reward will be great. That reward He is talking about is here. You sow that kind of seed, and your reward will be great, for you shall be called the children of the Most High.

Notice the priorities God puts on these things. "Love your enemies. Do good. Lend, hoping for nothing again. Your reward shall be great. You shall be the children of the Highest, for He is kind unto the unthankful and the evil. Be merciful as your Father also is merciful."

So I began to teach these principles.

> The disciple is not above his master:
> but every one that is perfect shall be as his master.

Do you know what this parable is saying? If I were blind and leading you, I could lead you off a canyon. He said the disciple is not above his master, but he who is mature, perfect, will be as his master. The disciple operates no higher than his master.

Therefore, if I am blind and leading you toward a cliff, you are blinded with the same blindness with which I blinded you. If you are my disciple, you cannot be above me because you are beneath me. You can be as mature or perfect as I am, but if you are my disciple, then you are being taught by me. If I am blind, my teachings will lead you over a cliff, and we will both die.

That is what is happening today in denominations. The blind are leading the blind, and all their disciples are not above their master.

I said, "Lord, how do I keep from falling in a ditch?"

He said, "Son, when all these truths, My sayings, come to light and you walk by them, you will be walking in My light. You will not be leading anybody blind."

Glory to God. Is this not good stuff?

"Brother Roberson, can I ever judge anybody? Can I say anything in judgment?"

As a matter of fact, you can. God has allowed you and me to make spiritual judgments if we do not have a beam in our eye that is blinding our insight. If you do this chapter, then you will not have anything in you that would judge a person wrong.

Look at verse 41:

> And why beholdest thou the mote that is in thy brother's eye,
> but perceivest not the beam that is in thine own eye?
> Either how canst thou say to thy brother,
> Brother, let me pull out the mote that is in thine eye,
> when thou thyself beholdest not the beam that is in thine own eye?
> Thou hypocrite, cast out first the beam out of thine own eye,
> and then shalt thou see clearly to pull out the mote that is in thy brother's eye.

When can I judge? You can judge when you do not have a beam in your eye. If you see your brother walking in error, if you see him following something blind, you can say, "That is wrong."

People say, "You are judging me."

Of course I am, because I have extracted the beam out of my eye.

How do you get the beam out of your eye? You extract it through meditation of these principles we have just taught: love your enemies, do good, until you are walking in the light of what His Word says with no beam. Then when you see your brother in error, when you see him walking blind, you have a right to go over and help him extract the mote out of his own eye.

But if you are judging your brother for what you are guilty of, you have no right. In fact, you bring judgment back on yourself. We hate things in other people that are guilty in us. Have you ever noticed that?

He went on to say every good tree is known by its fruit. What are you planting? Good seeds or bad seeds? Every tree is known by its own fruit. Out of the abundance of the heart, the mouth speaks.

Therefore, whosoever hears My sayings and does them, I will show you what he is like. He is like this man who dug deep and laid his foundation on a rock. Hell or high water, he will not go down the river.

I could tell you testimony after testimony, but I will not.

"You mean if I judge not, Satan cannot bring a judgment against my finances?"

What do you think it is saying? Judge not, and you will not be judged. Condemn not, and you will not be condemned. Forgive, and you will be forgiven. Give, and it will be given.

"If I refuse to make judgments, then when I give, it will be given?"

Yes, because Satan does not have the power to stop it. It will work every time. Better than every time.

I will tell you one vision I had. I was trying to teach this chapter. I got right down to "Give, and it shall be given" and "Judge not." At that time, I noticed in my spirit the correlation. I was prophesying to the people, and the power of God came on. He was talking about treasures that had been intercepted.

When I opened my eyes, I saw a vision. I saw a cloud. It looked like King Solomon's cave. God was illustrating the prophecy for me. It was heaped with precious jewels: strings of pearls and emeralds and diamonds. You have seen cartoons of Solomon's cave, chests of gold and treasures. This cloud's belly was sagging like a window of heaven.

Here all these believers were giving.

I asked God, "What was that?"

He said, "Son, that is the finances and the prosperity that has been intercepted by the evil one, that I sent out. I sent them, and he intercepted them. But you tell them now that if they will straighten their lives out, quit judging, start forgiving instead of condemning, and start walking after Me, then when they start giving, they will strip Satan of his power to bring these kinds of judgments. Everything that was laid up for them will be released. It will come. All the giving you ever did will be taken into account."

Is that not exciting?
