# Separating All the Glory Unto God

In John 17, I am just going to pick on a couple of verses. Let us begin with John 17:17. Once I get into these verses and exhort a few minutes, we will begin to lay a foundation so I can talk about totally separating all the glory to Him.

Glory.

I have had the best time in the prayer closet. When I first locked myself up, it had a certain amount of works in it: obligation, duty. Those things seem to be removed by His fellowship.

Is anybody having a good time?

Hallelujah.

One thing is sure: He wants us to behold His glory.

Jesus said, "That where I am, they may be with Me." If you think He is only talking about a place He is running around in heaven right now, He was not. He was talking about where we are walking now, in a position of truth.

He wants you to see Him in His glory, so that where He is, you can go. We are talking about here. He says, "My glory I give unto them."

In John 16, when He talks about being ready to depart and go to Him who sent Him, He begins to talk about the greater work of John 14 and the Father's house with many rooms. By the time He explains the greater work, He gets to John 16 and talks about the primary ministry of the Holy Spirit: to glorify Jesus.

That is an interesting word. The primary ministry of the Holy Spirit is to glorify Jesus.

Jesus said:

> "He shall not speak of himself...
>
> He shall glorify me:
>
> for he shall receive of mine,
>
> and shall shew it unto you."

In that, He is glorifying Jesus.

Jesus said, "My glory I give unto you." Can you conceive that one facet of the mighty Holy Spirit's ministry is to reveal that glory Jesus has given to you, so that eventually you can behold Him where He is?

He also said:

> "If any man serve me,
>
> let him follow me;
>
> and where I am,
>
> there shall also my servant be."

Just before He went back, He began to talk like this. He wanted to be glorified with the glory He had with the Father before the world was. He said:

> "I have glorified thee on the earth:
>
> I have finished the work
>
> which thou gavest me to do."

We know that Jesus glorified the Father by finishing the work the Father gave Him to do. Then He said, "Glorify Me now with the glory I had with You before the world was."

If finishing the work the Father gave Him to do glorified the Father, then when Jesus says, "My glory I give unto you," and "Where I am, there will My servant be also," you finishing the work He gave you to do, with what He gave you to do it with, will be how you end up glorifying Him at the end of your life.

I know the glory He has given me. I know He wants me to see Him in His glory, that where He is, I may be also.

We talk about the Lord's Prayer a lot:

> "Our Father which art in heaven,
>
> Hallowed be thy name..."

But this prayer in John 17 is the prayer He prayed for us, those who believed on Him through the apostles' word. I guess it is just not taught on very much. I have never heard it taught on in its entirety. I have heard verses from it quoted.

It is like worship. I did not know much about worship until it came time on the Born-Again Trail for Him to put the worship limb on and give it to me as a necessary step to go further with Him.

When I began to understand worship as in the Born-Again Trail, it was because I had first understood enough about the Born-Again Trail to understand worship in that context.

Then came three days on the judgment limb as in the Born-Again Trail. Now He wants me to understand His glory. So what do we call this one, the glory limb?

There is something He wants us to see that the Church, even in her best day as we understand it, has not seen. They will not see it until they start to understand.

I myself tried from time to time to understand this prayer while I was assimilating the book of John. I approached it more from a God aspect: "Glorify Me with the glory I had with You before the world was." So you imagine some sovereign operation with God out there, where once every millennium someone like William Branham comes along.

It is very hard to understand that prayer, and the glory He is trying to get you and me to understand, as one of the last things He said before He went home. He prayed for the Church to be glorified in the glory He was glorified with. One of His last prayers.

The reason it has not been understood is that we try to understand it from the persons of God in the Godhead: Jesus as the second Person, as deity; the Father as deity; the Holy Spirit as deity. Because this kind of talk, "before the foundation of the world," "glorify Me," and "where I am, there may My servant be also," is so easy to put off somewhere into glory.

Even in the best days of teaching faith and trying to get us to understand it is ours, we have not really approached that prayer.

I could not understand it because the Born-Again Trail was not in place. I did not understand who I was. I understood the new creation, the hidden man of the heart, and all those different things we say: "I am a new creation. I have a new nature." Sometimes they become spiritual cliches, part of a religious conversation we learned at church. We speak them, but they are not much a part of us.

The world is more a part of us because we are living in it twenty-two or twenty-three hours a day outside of church, and those cliches do not hold enough to overturn that.

I could not understand John 17 until I understood enough about the position I hold and who He made me as a son with the full inheritance when I was born again. I am not even talking about covenant. I am talking about inheritance in my Father's house.

Now I am beginning to understand it with the first inclinations of what He did for me. For the first time in my born-again life, on this trail, I realized I had been approaching it from a deity standpoint instead of a humanity standpoint, from what He had made me when He made me one with Them while I am still here.

> "Thou in me,
>
> and I in them,
>
> that they may be made perfect in one."

He was not talking about the sweet by and by.

Whatever this place is where I can behold His glory, it is out of what He has made me here and now as a new creation.

Hallelujah.

No wonder I was not taking it in from the standpoint of what He had been made in His glorified humanity and what He made me in this first step of glorifying Him out of what He made me in my spirit.

Hardly any of us had any idea what is available to us. But I can tell you, with the many glimpses I have gotten, that in His inheritance we can reach the world.

Not only can we reach the four corners of the earth with the inheritance He gave us, but also the four corners of Satan's domain where he rules in darkness with disease, torment, and bondage.

If we realize where He is, and that we can see Him in His glory where He is, because right now that is where He wants us to walk while we are here, then we get to reign over disease in the four corners of the earth by His inheritance. We get to find out what His abundant supply is all about as we reach into every dark corner and do something.

Do I feel confidence? Yes, I do. I feel such confidence. It is so nice to invite Him to step underneath and carry this thing.

Jesus glorified the Father by finishing the work the Father sent Him to do. We glorify Jesus by finishing the work He has for us to do. Then we will be glorified in our body with Him, with the glory He has prepared for us from the foundation of the world.

But right now, let me enjoy the fight awhile. Leave me here. Let me run around in that manifested glory while I am here and do wonderful things for Him.

I am finding out it takes about half to three-quarters of that glory just to get you to quit serving yourself.

Hallelujah.

The clearer you see Him, the less you will serve yourself.

He said, "Where My servant is, there I am. Where I am, there My servant will be." You might as well know that "servant" is translated slave. He says, "Where I am, there will My slave be also."

That is not a slave under a taskmaster forcing him to do things he does not want to do. This is a slave who has separated himself because of his love for the family of God.

Let us go to John 5 and begin to trace this life, because if we are going to understand the glory He is talking about, He wants you to know it is a glory you can walk in here. He is talking about your positional truth while you are running around on this earth.

John 5:19 says:

> "Then answered Jesus and said unto them,
>
> Verily, verily, I say unto you,
>
> The Son can do nothing of himself,
>
> but what he seeth the Father do:
>
> for what things soever he doeth,
>
> these also doeth the Son likewise."

Verse 20:

> "For the Father loveth the Son,
>
> and sheweth him all things
>
> that himself doeth:
>
> and he will shew him
>
> greater works than these,
>
> that ye may marvel."

"These" is plural. It refers to all the works done: walking on water, turning water into wine, raising the dead, all the miracles the Old Testament saints were capable of, and all the miracles Jesus did up to that point.

Jesus says the Father loves the Son, shows Him all things He Himself does, and will show Him greater works than all of those Old Testament works and all He had done up to that point.

But notice, He says the Father will show "Him." It goes back to singular. The Father is going to show the Son a greater work than any work ever done under the Old Testament or up to that point.

Verse 21 explains the greater miracle:

> "For as the Father raiseth up the dead,
>
> and quickeneth them;
>
> even so the Son quickeneth
>
> whom he will."

The word "them" is in italics, which means it was not in the original. It would read:

> "For as the Father raiseth up the dead,
>
> and quickeneth;
>
> even so the Son quickeneth
>
> whom he will."

In the Old Testament, they had resurrections, and Jesus had resurrections fulfilling the Old Covenant. He raised people from the dead. He was not the first to do that.

But the word "quickeneth" generally pertains to taking the spirit from death to life. The Father is about to show the Son, singular, a greater miracle than any resurrection from the dead under the old. Even though Jesus could regenerate a body and put the spiritually dead spirit back in it, He could not quicken a human spirit to life or give it a new nature until He Himself was quickened by the Father and came alive in His spirit.

That is why there was no plural here. The Father quickened one Person. The Father brought one spirit from death to life and showed Him the greater work that all might marvel.

No Old Testament saint under the shadows could do that. Jesus Himself could not quicken a man from death to life until the claims of justice were settled and His human spirit became the first since Adam to be quickened and made alive from death to life.

Then John 14 opened up:

> "The works that I do
>
> shall he do also;
>
> and greater than these
>
> shall he do;
>
> because I go unto my Father."

The greater work is not "works" plural, but greater than all these works. You are going to do those works also because I am going to the Father, I am going to be quickened, and then you are going to come alive and operate in the greater work.

You will cause many people to believe on Me through your word. This greater work will spread through the whole earth. You will be administrators of the spirit. You will preach, they will receive, and it will be a glorious day.

> and quickeneth;

The Father raises up the Son and makes alive. Then the Son raises up whom He will.

Verse 22 says:

> "For the Father judgeth no man,
>
> but hath committed all judgment
>
> unto the Son."

The Father judges no man because He judged every man in the Son. When He quickened Him from death to life, every man's judgment was laid on the Son. The Father judges no man because He judged every man in the Son when He quickened Him from death to life.

Now go down to John 5:26:

> "For as the Father hath life in himself;
>
> so hath he given to the Son
>
> to have life in himself."

This life was not only the life that came to Jesus the first time, when His human spirit was born of God in the Virgin Mary and clothed with a body. The real life He is talking about in this verse is the life He gave Him when He brought Him from death to life.

As the Father had life within Himself, He gave to the Son to have life within Himself, and the Son quickens whom He will.

Now this life He received from being quickened from death to life is free to be given to you because He paid the price for you. The life that is in Him, He can put into you. That is the life that makes you one with God.

Hallelujah.

That same life would come into the Son that would come into all those men and women who had promissory notes of righteousness, waiting the dispensations out in Abraham's bosom, waiting for the Firstborn from the dead to be raised.

Then Jesus could take the life the Father put in Him when He quickened Him from death to life and give it to those Old Testament saints, quickening them from death to life.

When Jesus said, "Where My servant is, there I am," and "that you might be one in Us," what made you one with the Father and the Son was that life that came from the Father, was first in the Son, and now is in you.

It has made you one with Them.

If I were you, I would develop that life because it is the only part of you that can go all the way into God. It is the only part of you that can see His glory and glorify Him.

I got a glimpse. It was just a momentary glimpse, but it was enough to alter my life forever. It was not as long a look as I got at the worship limb, when I landed on the floor and He started giving me detailed instructions for how to enter in and break into worship, even telling me how long.

But when I saw myself separating all the glory to Him, and He said, "My glory I give unto you, that where I am you may be also," and that you might see Me in My glory, something opened.

The word "glory" is very difficult to define. A word close to it is what you are separating to Him when you learn to give yourself to extended worship and get past the flesh. You come close to the definition of that glory, but not the whole of it.

They have trouble defining this word because we do not really have anything to define it with. It cannot be truly defined until it is defined by revelation.

When I go back there now, the same thing rushes through me and out my prayers. It never stops.

I do not see how it would be possible to live in a town with most of it going to hell, when it is full of believers and I am beginning to understand this kind of life available in Him. I will not let my town go to hell. I plan on letting Him come into all that He is before this is over.

I stood before the girl. She was like this, never talked. I do not have to play on your emotions. What would you do if that were your daughter, little arm and everything? She is aware of you. She is just a prisoner. She has been to church all her life, five years in our meetings.

It just does not seem right to pack up and go to church all the time and leave it there.

Growing up with this creates such a deep-seated program in the soul, every day you live with it. You say, "I have a covenant of healing." You have a whole lot more than a covenant of healing. You have a birth of incorruptible seed. Nowhere in that birth is there room for a diseased body.

You say, "I have a covenant of healing." I have an inheritance. In my inheritance, I am born again of incorruptible seed. In no part of that birth did I receive the DNA for a diseased body. What I did receive was enough to straighten up the body I have until I get a glorified one.

The closest man on this planet I have seen teaching people how to take a Down syndrome son and, wherever they are walking, one day touch that realm of glory and have Jesus glorified in their son or daughter, was Norvel Hayes in his book *How to Live and Not Die*.

Finally enough transformation takes place that Jesus is able to touch their spirit, just for a moment of time, for that one miracle with His glory. They walk in, like Betty Baxter, and there it unfolds right in front of you. If you are blessed, you are in the room when it happens.

But to receive a realm of glory that stays, what do we do with this? To lay in the atmosphere. To be in the atmosphere.

There are testimonies like Betty Baxter or the sixty-eight-year-old woman who adopted a twenty-two-year-old man. He was just like her, hands like this. Every time I go by, I pet her. That is her contact. Just pet her, because she is mad at the devil.

That sixty-eight-year-old woman adopted a twenty-two-year-old man somebody threw away into a home. They had to dress him and bathe him. That was all he did: lay there.

She dragged him across the floor for four years, four to five hours a day, telling him to get up and walk in the name of Jesus. One day, after they heard music, something like the Fifth Cantata by Bach, they went into the front room. There he was sitting at the piano playing.

Betty Baxter's mother sat there knitting, worshiping, and praying. Betty could talk a little, all twisted up, not this high in her wheelchair.

"Do you ever think Jesus will heal me?"

She said, "I know He will. I do not think. I know He is."

There she was, all twisted up. The day Jesus came, according to what I heard, He stepped out of a silver-dollar-sized cloud of glory. It appeared just above Betty Baxter and got bigger and bigger until it was big enough for a man to step out of, a glory portal of some kind.

Jesus stepped out and stood in front of Betty Baxter. She saw Him and tried to stretch her arm out to touch Him, but of course she could not.

About that time, her mother looked up and saw what was happening. She said, "Oh Jesus, step closer so she can touch You."

In response, Jesus turned around and said, "It is because of your faith that I have come."

He laid His hand on Betty Baxter. She had to eat through a straw because her face was twisted over. When Jesus touched her, that twisted part snapped around front and straightened. Her arms uncurled, her back started popping and cracking, and she came to full height. She put her new dress and shoes on, ran downstairs, told her daddy, "You were wrong," and went and told her preacher, "You were wrong."

There I was standing in front of this girl in Palm Springs. I slipped into the Spirit. He talked to me a little bit first about holding me accountable. Then suddenly I got a glimpse of me separating all the glory to Him as in the Born-Again Trail.
