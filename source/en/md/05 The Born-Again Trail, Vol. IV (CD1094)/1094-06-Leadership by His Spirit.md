# Leadership by His Spirit

We have been doing an in-depth study on hearing the voice of God in the Born-Again Trail. In this part of it, we have been studying the inner witness: how the inner witness works.

Today we are going to take it from Romans 2 on through Romans 7 and Romans 8 in the study of hearing His voice.

There are four basic ways He communicates with His children. Foremost is the inner witness. That is the most common one. That is the one every one of us is called to walk by.

I saw something in Brazil, and I told a lady what I saw. Then I told her exactly what was going to happen to her. Sure enough, when I prayed for her, exactly what we said was wrong was wrong, and exactly what we said was going to happen to her happened to her.

When I am ministering, that is not the inner witness. The vision I see is the fourth level of hearing God's voice: vision.

I will be standing there looking at somebody, and a vision will materialize in my spirit. I know it is there. I can see it, but I can also see through it, totally conscious of what is going on around me. That is the most common kind of ministering vision.

Sometimes I will see a heart suspended in front of me, and I will see something, a discoloration. Sometimes I see a bloodstream. It is not hard, when you are looking at something, to explain to people what you see.

So I was able to explain to her what I saw, which was a double curvature, and exactly what was going to happen. When it happened just the way I said it, they asked, "How did you know?"

I said, "It is easy. Can you read a newspaper?"

There are four different ways He communicates, but the first is the inner witness. It is the one we have been looking at.

The second is revelation. I like that one. That is when He capsules a whole revelation on a computer chip, sends it over to your spirit, and then releases the whole revelation at once on the inside of you. Your mind acts as the computer screen. It can only pull up one sentence at a time, yet you have the whole thing in your spirit.

That operates at times when I first meet somebody, or by word of knowledge. I will call somebody out, and suddenly that revelation just goes off in me. I know them like they are an old friend. I can go back and talk about their life just like they are an old friend.

Most of the time, when I meet somebody new for the first time, they may introduce me and I will hear their name, but it will be in the distance because immediately I switch over to the spirit and start monitoring and listening.

Sometimes He will give me a whole scenario. Sometimes your whole life will just come online right then. Most of the time I will not say anything. He does that to give me information, to let me know where you are and what you need.

One of the easiest spirits to pick up is those perverted twins:

"Hi, how are you doing? You are doing fine, are you? You want to take the children's ministry over?"

So He speaks by vision, by revelation, and another way is in the language you communicate in. He will speak words in your spirit. You will hear words in English, or whatever you speak.

When I first started prophesying, He would give me the first sentence to say, and for the first seven years He gave it to me in King James. That is how He would start all the prophecies, and I learned to trust Him.

"Yea, thus saith the Lord."

I learned to trust Him. But if He did not say, "Yea, thus saith the Lord," I knew it was not Him, because that is how He talked. "How art thou?" I knew He talked like that. I saw The Ten Commandments.

Anyway, if He did not use King James, I would not think it was Him. I remember when He broke me from it. When He broke me, He never used King James again, not one time after that, to prophesy through me.

Do you know how He broke me? He said, "How can Chinese prophesy in King James?"

From that day to this, He has not prophesied King James through me. In fact, about the only way He prophesies through me now is through revelation. He drops the mind of Christ in me, and I get to choose my own words. He just wants me to reveal His mind.

But the predominant way He leads every believer, first and foremost, is with the inner witness.

Unfortunately, I would venture to say that three-quarters or more of the Body of Christ is not led by the inner witness.

Why? Because in the inner witness, for the Holy Spirit to bear witness with you that something is drastically wrong or right, there is a part of you He has to use: your conscience.

I am talking about the conscience resident in your born-again human spirit. The Holy Spirit rises up, and the only thing He has to use to bear witness that something is right or wrong is your conscience.

If you have seared yours, if you are walking after money and do not care, if for you to go to church somebody has to pick you up, give you a pillow, and not say one ugly thing or you will quit going, then you have gone against just about everything He has tried to inner-witness to you.

"This is my life. I am going to do with it what I want. I will go to church on Sunday. The rest of the time, leave me alone."

You think when you sear your whole life and say, "I am going to serve myself and go where I want, when I want," that He has something to work with? The only thing He has to use in an inner witness is your conscience.
