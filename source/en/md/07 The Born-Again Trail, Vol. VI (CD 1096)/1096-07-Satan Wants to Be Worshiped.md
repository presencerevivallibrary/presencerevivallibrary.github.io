Turn to Matthew 16, and put a marker in Ezekiel 28 and Isaiah 14. We will see how far we get this time.

Where we left off about a month ago is Matthew 16. Verse 20 reads this way:

That is an interesting statement in itself. What amazed me here was that Jesus totally bypassed the man and exposed what was working behind him.

Of course, this does not surprise me now. As I get out more in religion, one of the biggest errors I see is the way people use Old Testament saints as examples of our covenant. We cannot do that. God dealt with spiritually dead men differently than He deals with spiritually alive men, which is what we are.

After all these years, I am not surprised now when He says, "Now that you can hear Me enough from praying in tongues, try to go to the Old Testament and use different examples." I never could get the flow to go there. When He told me why, I was not surprised, because of the way we use them as examples for what we do and the way we live.

He said the things that happened to them happened to them as examples for you and me. That does not mean you do not have to understand what happened to them, how it happened under the covenant they were walking under, and according to what He said. God is the same yesterday, today, and forever. He is a righteous God, so He has to deal with things in proportion to that, but He dealt with them differently than He deals with us.

I was talking to different ones, and a teaching seems to be gaining popularity about falling under the power.

Two of his ushers restrained my pilot. They literally held him in his chair when he tried to get out.

After that meeting was over, I said from the pulpit, "This man will trust me to teach the eternal Word of God that can shape your life forever, but he will not let me move in the Spirit. I am closing the meeting now."

He took me in and said, "In our clause we said that we would not allow catchers, and you agreed to it."

I said, "If I did, I did not know it. Did we sign something? I did not know it if I did."

So, according to him, I had bound myself to my word. I only had one more night, so I finished it.

Eventually, instead of Him retreating back to where you are really walking, He lets you worship Him and pay that kind of price, whether you feel Him or do not feel Him. It is a positional truth. You are giving Him what He wants.

In fact, you are actually getting through more when He retreats back to where you really walk and you will not stop anyway.

"I do not care what I feel like. I am not stopping."

He really likes that, because after a while, that will be where you are really walking: right there with Him. You can feel His geographical, manifested, miracle-working presence in whatever house you are in, whatever hospital you go to, or wherever you go.

"Well, I just do not have time to pray."

Come in for counsel. Bring the hours you watch television and a calculator.

I think we need some "He is here."

God knows your heart. It may work on me, but it will not fly with Him.

We want revival. Revival is made out of heart.
