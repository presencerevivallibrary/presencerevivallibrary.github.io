# Jesus Said You Do Not Have to Fear

Open to John 17 as we get ready. Hallelujah. Then we are going to be in a few places, like Luke 11, Mark 4, and would you believe we are even going to shoot for the book of Acts.

When I got a look at that girl, she had been a prisoner of her own body for the twelve or thirteen years she had lived in Palm Springs. As I stood there, He preceded a vision I had inside with the mystery that began to help me understand what the glory of God was. Then I got to see God, or Jesus, in His glory for just a minute.

That moment was the kind of revelation that begins to come forward when you know He is about to give you something, about to do something. It ended up with Him saying things in my spirit like, "I am holding you accountable," and, "No longer will you pass children in that condition without accountability."

He started saying things like that as He began to open up from His Word what I had seen in verses I had read many times.

John 17 is a prayer He prayed for us. He prayed for His disciples and for everyone who would believe on Him through them, which is us. I had read this prayer many times, and I had heard parts of it mentioned. People will quote a statement He made out of this prayer and use it to confirm something. But the whole prayer was hard for me to understand.

He made very difficult statements: "I in them," "Thou in Me," and that we would be "made perfect in one." It was a very difficult prayer to understand.

The one degree I was off, which kept me from understanding it, was that I was trying to link the prayer too much with our oneness with Him in deity and not in His humanity. When I understood that the oneness He was talking about, Him being with the Father and the Father in Him and us in Them, was the position He held with the Father in His glorified humanity, it began to open up.

Through the born-again trail I had already traced that new life you and I received. It began with the Father. As the Father had life within Himself, He gave to the Son to have life within Himself. Then the Son turned around and gave it to us.

The life Jesus had in His spiritually alive spirit is now the same life we have in our spiritually alive spirit, and it came from the Father.

The oneness He is talking about, "I in them, and Thou in Me," is the same kind of oneness you and I had with the first Adam when we were baptized into his spiritual death and the whole human race inherited his nature. Paul said that even those who had not sinned after the similitude of Adam's transgression still inherited it. We did not commit the sin that caused us to have that nature, but since we were born of him, we had that nature. We were all baptized into Adam's death. We were all one with Adam in that nature.

Thank God the second Adam came along, the first Man to stand on this planet in four thousand years spiritually alive.

> In Him was life; and the life was the light of men.

That light shined in the spiritual darkness, the spiritual death of the human race. Now that we have been baptized into Jesus' death, that we might also be like Him in His resurrection, all of us who are born again have been baptized into that new nature. That new nature has made us one with Jesus and also one with the Father.

This is why, when Jesus described going away, He said:

> In My Father's house are many rooms. If it were not so, I would have told you. I go to prepare a place for you, that where I am, there ye may be also.

When He said, "Where I am," He was talking about that place, that room, that dwelling He held with the Father in His humanity, and as it stands now, His glorified humanity.

We know what makes Him different from us is that in eternity past, before His glorified humanity, He was the second Person in the Godhead as deity. This is where people's brains begin to lock up. We have been made one with Him in His humanity. We will never touch His deity. But we are one with Him in His humanity, not His deity.

The room He holds in His humanity is the one He was talking about when He said, "I go away to prepare a place for you." The way He went was by way of the cross. He went to prepare a place for us, that He would come again and receive us unto Himself, and that where He is, that place He occupied with the Father in His humanity, we may be also.

Hallelujah.

I am literally born of God. I do not mind telling you, I am excited. I understand my position more and more because I have gotten to see Jesus in His glorified humanity and am beginning to understand it.

Jesus made statements in Matthew like, "Call no man on this earth master," as in lord, as in final say-so. He said you only have one Master who has the final say-so, and that is Jesus, and you are all brethren.

Then He made another interesting statement: "Call no man father upon the earth." He said you only have one Father, and that is your heavenly Father. He also said, "Call no man rabbi." He was talking about exalted titles and positions. That is why the next verse says that he who is greatest among you will be servant of all, which means slave. That is how you obtain a lofty, exalted position in the kingdom of God: by taking the position of servant and slave.

It is interesting for Him to say, "You are all brethren," and, "You only have one Father." He clarified it: call no man on this earth your father.

People try to explain that away as only religious titles. They say Abraham is the father of faith, or someone fathered a move, so we call men on earth father. There are all kinds of religious titles, and they try to pass that on. In modern commentaries they will use something like a Catholic priest to demonstrate their point.

But when He said, "Call no man on this earth father," I can tell you right now: your natural father is no more your father than Joseph was Jesus' father. Right there, it was broken off. Jesus was born from above. It was broken off right there, the forty-first person from Abraham. Your natural father is no more your father than Joseph was Jesus' father, although Joseph was recorded as His father.

Jesus' only connection with a human being was with Mary, and she was not even in the bloodline. Hallelujah.

Joseph just had to stand by and let the old man die. He was a just man, but he would have put her away. He was willing not to, and of course he started being visited by angels and dreams.

Of course I have a father and mother, because that which is born of flesh is flesh. My natural father and mother caused the birth of my flesh to come about, and in that I inherited a spiritually dead spirit.

But one day, when I bowed my knee to the Lord Jesus Christ and was quickened, I was born from above. I was born again. The old man passed away. Even the law lost its hold over that spiritually dead Jew, because the man it was holding on to died, and it had no nature to hold on to anymore. A new man came into existence, and in that new man I only have one Father, and we are all brethren.

Hallelujah.

I thank my natural father and mother for bringing me into existence in that birth of flesh. There is a big argument about whether, once we are born again, our children are born spiritually alive. You get into a mess there. What if one parent is not saved? Is the child half-saved? You have a merger of DNA. Where would half-saved come from?

I understand this much: what is born of flesh is flesh. The human spirit came into existence at that moment of conception and grew as the body grew. I also know I have to have a birth from above, and that birth from above has to be my decision.

My natural father is no more my father than Joseph was Jesus' father, even though he was recorded in the genealogy as His father.

Hallelujah. I have been born again. My Father is God. Jesus prepared a place for me and received me to Himself, that where He is, I will be also in His glorified humanity. I hold that place now with the Father that He holds as a Son of God in His humanity.

Hallelujah.
