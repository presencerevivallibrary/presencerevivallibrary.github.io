# Our Strength Is in His Grace

Because I have truly been asking my Father to go where I have never been before, consequently I am having the privilege of seeing into things of the Spirit that I have never seen before. And although there are the pros, there are also the cons. Things are being exposed in me that I never knew were there before.

It seems that all of it is a necessary process to the end. Jesus said:

> If ye love me, keep my commandments.

He also said:

> Every branch that beareth fruit, he purgeth it, that it may bring forth more fruit.

If we are fruit bearers, then we can look gladly with great anticipation toward being purged so that we might bear more. In fact, He said:

> Now ye are clean through the word which I have spoken unto you.

Once again, that is the finality we will be judged by in the last days.

He has promised me that if I bear fruit and want fruit, He will purge me so that I bear more. That is all right. But the purging process is cutting off dead limbs. It seems that to produce more, we have to lose. I do not mind.

But along with seeing more, I am being purged more. And along with this purging, evidently it is necessary that I become more acquainted with my enemy, because He said I should not be ignorant of his devices, which I fully intend not to be.

To go where I have never been before, things will try to stop me that have never tried before. Things are being revealed that I never knew were there before. But it is great.

As we take this a step further, we have been studying revival, and it is inevitable that in the study of revival He pulls us around to grace. To stand up against this foe that we are called to stand up against, especially in these last days, the church of God, of whom we are a part, must have a fuller and fuller understanding of grace.

Even though He is taking me in a slightly different direction tonight, this much we must know at this point: if there was ever a man worthy of all the wiles the enemy could muster, connive, scheme, and employ to stop one man, that would have to be the apostle Paul.

Paul outlined the war when he said we do not wrestle against flesh and blood, but against principalities and powers. It is the same war of casting down imaginations and every high thing in this attack that exalts itself against the knowledge of God, with the audacity to replace that knowledge, and bringing into captivity every thought.

This war raged through the thing I could not get past in 2 Corinthians 12. If there was ever a description of this war raging against the apostle Paul, it would have to be when Paul said, "There was given to me a thorn in the flesh because of my abundance of revelation."

That has to be the most descriptive account of a war ever fought by powers and principalities to stop one man. Yet it is recorded.

The most fascinating thing about this war that I have found to this day is that a man of Paul's caliber did not miss a trick. A man responsible for recording grace, a man of this caliber of understanding and revelation knowledge, still had to seek God three times before God was able to get this over to him:

> My grace is sufficient for thee.

Three times. It makes me wonder how many attempts He has made on my life to get grace over to me if it took three for Paul. Paul finally said:

> For this thing I besought the Lord thrice...

Three times I sought God, and He said, "My grace is sufficient." Three times.

It makes me know that contained within this grace, which we have so affectionately labeled the unmerited, unearnable favor of God bestowed upon us, is something tremendous. It means I could not merit it, earn it, or deserve it.

In fact, He said all boasting has been excluded by grace. You cannot earn it no matter how hard you work, no matter how much you do for the kingdom, and not even by how much you give.

Work earns you nothing. It was not until I had fallen in love with Him enough to stand in His presence and reverse the whole order that I could say, "I do not work because I am afraid of You. I do not work because I can make You love me any more. I do not work to earn Your favor. I do not work to put You under some kind of obligation to me that will force You to move on my behalf."

The word grace makes all of that an absolute impossibility.

Grace is so phenomenal. What we could not earn, did not merit, and did not deserve was suddenly freely offered to us. He had to label a certain force that was not used that much in the Old Testament until Hebrews 11 labeled the great annals of faith and said what those men in the Old Testament were doing were exploits by faith, Abraham being the father of it all.

Yet this force used so much in the New Testament is simply called faith.

At times it can almost seem frustrating. We have a word for an invisible force that carries us into the presence of God. Romans 5 labels it:

> Therefore being justified by faith, we have peace with God through our Lord Jesus Christ:
> 
> By whom also we have access by faith into this grace wherein we stand...

We have access into this grace wherein we stand by faith. Before the foundations of the world, in the mind of God, to the day of the cross when He purchased the standing for me, I have this force by which I approach that standing. I cannot obtain it any other way.

Sometimes it almost feels frustrating, because if you put a hammer in my hand and give me a nail, I can drive the nail. If you give me lumber, I can build a house. If you tell me to kill a bullock and shed its blood, there is something physical I can do, some kind of works, where I can leave that point behind me and say with my mouth, "I have done it. I am clean."

No. We have this invisible force to contend with. And as frustrating as it may seem, my friend, there is only one way you can access grace: you must have enough faith to stand up and say, "No, I did not earn it. I did not merit it. But He is offering it to me."

As frustrating as it may seem from time to time, there is only one way I can appropriate His standing to my life. I must simply believe it. I must simply believe it.

I had several things outlined, but when the well opens, I can do nothing else until it is over.

Here we have this force. It is the only force in the universe that makes His standing accessible in my life. That should make us want to know a little more about it, wouldn't you say?

Faith is the ability to believe that which does not exist in the tangible.

> Now faith is the substance of things hoped for, the evidence of things not seen.

My faith is all the evidence of the thing I do not yet see. What an incredible force. And even more incredible than this force is the standing I apprehend, which is grace.

We have been forced into a position to believe in grace as far as heaven is concerned, have we not? After all, you have to believe heaven exists. If you do not, where are you going?

Death is an everyday present reality that we have to reconcile. He said we see all things put under His feet, but as of yet we do not see all things. Yes, positionally He has defeated death. But death is the last sting before victory, and it is inevitable. Everyone around us is dying. We lose our relatives to it. A person becomes ever more aware as age increases.

Regardless, we must hold grace up before us and believe that heaven is. It is. And very shortly, by God's standards, it will be our home.

Our faith has accessed grace to that degree because there is nothing we can do about it except believe we are going. We believe one day I will be glorified, that my body will not see its permanent resting place six feet under the ground. I do believe I am not going to rest there. I believe I am going to be working like crazy one day, and I will hear a trumpet.

Because that is a future event, we have apprehended it by faith, and it has become part of our everyday living.

But evidently Jesus wanted to pull grace into a realm of ever-presence. Grace is a viable, pliable force available for our lives and in our lives in everyday situations. It is not just that we rejoice because our faith has obtained grace and we know heaven is our home. Paul said we rejoice in tribulations also, because tribulation works patience, experience, and hope.

Pulling grace into the everyday affairs of this life by faith will cause you to experience the same kind of power of deliverance Paul experienced.

God had to tell him three times in the middle of what Paul labeled as a messenger straight from the throne room of hell, on assignment to bring his ministry to a standstill because he was causing the kind of revival that had never been caused by a man in the history of the church.

What is the lavish process? How do I know when I am receiving His care? He gives grace to you, you humble outfit, and starts exalting you out of your problem.

First of all, He has to get you out from under Satan's jurisdiction:

> Be sober, be vigilant; because your adversary the devil, as a roaring lion, walketh about, seeking whom he may devour.

How would you like to be out from under his jurisdiction? How would you like to be undevourable? As many inspections as he makes on you day by day, he is still incapable of stopping you.

That is what devour is. He starts with a little cancer and eats your whole life up. He starts with a little poverty and bankrupts you. He starts with a little strife and ruins your marriage. He is the devourer. He enjoys laying under shade for you and eating you a piece at a time.

He is seeking devourable people. I do not want under his authority.

How do I change jurisdiction? Humble yourself under His hand. Become teachable. Turn to what the Word says. It is more than hearing; it is doing.

> Be not conformed to this world: but be ye transformed by the renewing of your mind...

Why?

> ...that ye may prove what is that good, and acceptable, and perfect, will of God.

What does prove mean? It is the same thing God did to Abraham when He proved him. He said, "Give Isaac, because, Abraham, I am telling you something. My Word is true. The promises hang on Isaac. My Word is true."

He proved the strength of the covenant to Abraham, and buddy, she held.

What does proving the good, acceptable, and perfect will mean? It means you have undeniable proof that your God reigns in your life, for it has been proven to you once again.

I like that. I really like that.

So He says, "Humble yourself, for your adversary as a roaring lion walks about seeking whom he may devour," throwing inspections for devourable people.

Who cannot be devoured? The person who humbled himself, took transformation to the Word, started proving the Word, and allowed God to lavish His care on him because he has no care. It took him out of Satan's jurisdiction and made him undevourable.

"Well, this is harder than I thought."

That is why He called it labor.

I do not want to wait until circumstances and tribulation are taking me over to do anything. I would rather go to the Word of God, find myself a mountain of need - needs to be met in the kingdom - and then tear hell up and start meeting it.

Then the enemy will throw at me the same things to stop me because I am invading his territory, the same things he would otherwise systematically use to devour me. I would rather provoke the circumstances myself just for the privilege of kicking his teeth out, rather than having it imposed on me.

I am going to impose it on him.

And that, my friends, is what revivals are made out of.
