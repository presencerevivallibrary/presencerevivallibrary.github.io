# Drink From the Well of Worship

I can remember at the March conference that sometime just before that, He was introducing me to worship by telling me I did not know much about it. That is how you get a prideful preacher into anything. It is all right to have pride if it only exists before God, because He can kill it.

As He set me on fire with it, I went around with the initial revelation. I would to God that I could pull enough out of you and put enough into you to get you into private worship. I am going to feel like I failed if you leave here and do not break through, because if we cannot get this into you, you are going to miss another major key to the walk of power.

Everything else has everything to do with it: the praying in tongues limb, the speaking the Word limb, the meditation limb. All of it has everything to do with it. The fasting limb is major. But then there is the worship limb. That has a lot to do with the anointing and the tangible presence that surrounds you, something you can feel with your senses.

To tell you the truth, God tried to get me into that kind of worship a couple of years ago. That is why I had a visitation. I knew I could not go one step further and accomplish what He wanted without it. I had that visitation because I had prayed myself to it, praying in tongues and fasting.

Since you are the preacher bunch, I was on the ninth day of a fast when He visited me. He knocked me down on the floor and started talking to me.

Since March, I have not been the same. I would to God that we can get this into you: private worship.

I thank God for all our corporate worship, but it is not enough. It is not enough to bring the change. So I am going to enter in this morning and give you a few verses about the ministry of the Holy Spirit in this limb of worship.

Go to John 16:12. He is pacing me, so that is a safety valve that says I will not be very long. Of course, this afternoon we have until the next morning.

Hallelujah.

Brother Tim,

Something unusual happened to me several months ago, and I am just now beginning to understand the effect it is having on my life.

There was a special prayer line to receive the strength for change. In these kinds of lines, it is the grace of God that helps us trade our weaknesses for His strength. When the line was just about over, the Holy Spirit told me to get in. A friend of mine was helping me minister that night. When I got in line, a gentle breeze blew past my ears, and my spirit seemed to get lighter and lighter.

The next thing I knew, I was lying on the floor, and the Holy Spirit was beginning to speak to me. He said, "You do not know much about worship," and went on to instruct me to worship a certain amount of time each day. Not because worship can necessarily be scheduled in and out like an appointment, but so I could initially break through and understand what He wanted to teach me.

Because I was on a certain day of a fast, my mind immediately questioned, "How about fasting? Do You want me to continue fasting?"

God responded, "I did not tell you not to fast. But fasting cannot give you what worship can, and neither can worship give you what fasting does."

Immediately my mind protested a little. God brought me back and reminded me of three revivalists, two of whom I remember would fast two or more forty-day fasts a year. As I looked back over their lives and the effectiveness of their ministries in my mind's eye, God helped me understand that when anyone puts all their confidence in just one key, such as fasting, and believes it is the answer for everything, God will do all He can for them. But unfortunately, there will be other areas of their life He cannot minister to.

As I lay on the floor, I became excited about the prospect of worship the way He was going to teach me. As my mind continued to ponder the different keys He had given me over the years, I could not help but think that if I was guilty of exalting one key as the answer to everything, it would be praying in tongues.

But God comforted me by assuring me that because praying in tongues is a revelation gift, it is something you cannot do too much of. It is the reason He was able to reveal so many other keys that I now understand and hold so close to my heart, such as meditation, confession, fasting, and now worship.

So when He said to me, "You do not know much about worship," I made the necessary arrangements to set myself aside a certain amount of time each day to worship and find out what He wanted me to know.

I went into my sound room where I make radio tapes, closed the door, and began to worship.

Two weeks later I was experiencing a presence that, up until that time, I only knew existed in the realm of possibility. Now I was experiencing firsthand what has got to be what so many of us are looking for but do not quite know what it is, having never experienced drinking from the deep well of worship.

So follow me closely, Tim. This could be what you are looking for. It could change your faith forever and change the way you receive from God.

As many times as I have been over the Word of God, it continues to amaze me how much of it I still miss. In those times when you think you know everything about a verse, it happens again. Something opens up that changes the way you look at things.

This is what happened to me with the Scriptures concerning the Samaritan woman, when Jesus sat down on the edge of Jacob's well and began to reveal His heart and the heart of His Father to her in John 4.

It changed the way I looked at worship forever.

As I listened to my spirit and went over and over the encounter He had with her, it was there at Jacob's well that I began to understand what my Father wanted from me: the closeness you can only have with family.

God is first my Father, and then He is my God. As my God, He has given me everlasting life and secured my future with an abundance as inexhaustible as the limitless power He holds as Creator. But as my Father, He wants from us the fellowship and closeness we can only give Him through being born again and climbing up into His arms with a nature like His own, loving Him with the adoration and worship of a son who believes the sun rises and sets on his father and that there is none other like him.

As Jesus sat on the edge of Jacob's well and talked with the Samaritan woman, I knew He was separating the kind of worship we can have with God the Father from the kind the Old Testament saints could have with Him. They could not give Him what they did not have, and that is the worship that comes from the new nature of the born-again human spirit.

For God is a Spirit, and those who worship Him must worship Him in spirit and in truth.

When Jesus said in John 4:23:

> But the hour cometh, and now is, when the true worshippers shall worship the Father in spirit and in truth: for the Father seeketh such to worship him,

He meant there had never been an hour before this one. Not in the history of man could anyone ever worship God in spirit and in truth. Not since the fall of Adam and the spiritual death of the human race could anyone give Him what He wanted from them: the fellowship and worship that emanates from the new nature.

But you can, because you have been born again.

When He said, "For the Father seeketh such to worship him," it did not mean He was hopping from church to church looking for a good worship team. It means God sought and waited out the generations leading up to the cross, patiently working the redemption plan, longing for the day when He could restore the new nature to the human race and have the kind of fellowship with them He had been waiting for four thousand years. That was made available only through the new birth.

And I can give Him what He wants.

It is not that God did not have a relationship with the Old Testament saints. He did. They worshiped Him out of their mind and soul and gave Him as much of themselves as they could.

But as much as God loved them, there was only a certain closeness He could have with them because of their sin natures. Even that closeness could only be obtained through animal sacrifice as they worshiped Him through the veil of animal blood.

What God longed for, they could not give: the fellowship and worship that comes from being born again and having the same nature as He does.

Hebrews 10:4 says:

Of all the Old Testament saints, the prophets, the men of wisdom such as Moses, David, and King Solomon, Jesus said there had never been one born of woman greater than John. But He went on to say that he who is least in the kingdom is greater than John.

Why? Because before Jesus rose from the dead, the new nature was not yet available. The least in the kingdom of heaven would be greater than John because the Old Testament saints had not yet been given the new birth.

How could Jesus say the least in the kingdom of heaven was greater than John if John already belonged to the kingdom of heaven? He did not, because the new nature was not yet available. John, like Nicodemus, had not yet been born again.

You have. Oh, but you have.

John could not give the Father what He longed for: the fellowship of the new nature. Nor could King Solomon or King David. Their fellowship with God pulled up just short of the unregenerated human spirit. God could not go there.

But we can, Tim. We have been born again.

All the Old Testament saints lived under the shadow of things to come. One thing is sure: there cannot be a shadow without something present to make the shadow. Once a person is born again, they no longer belong to the shadow. They have been baptized into whatever is making the shadow, and that is the Body of Christ.

When the Judaizers came to the Colossians trying to indoctrinate them into keeping the law, Paul said in Colossians 2:16-17:

Have you wondered why, under the shadow of the law, if a man even picked up a stick of firewood on the Sabbath day, he would be stoned to death?

Any work whatsoever on the Sabbath under the shadow took salvation out of grace and put it into works, under which it would be impossible to be born again.
