# The Path Into God's Power and Miracles

Hallelujah. Did the impossible come closer within range?

Part of the foundation is getting the blame off God. In John 5, Jesus said the Father judges no man, but has committed all judgment to the Son. He also said, "I came not to judge the world, but that the world through Me might be saved." The world was already condemned. He came to open the door.

He that believeth not is condemned already. Jesus said, "I judge no man." The Word He has spoken is what will judge in the last day.

He reconciled us by His death when we were yet enemies. The contrast is this: how much more are you saved by His life? If He did that for you when you were His enemy, what is your problem receiving now that you have taken part of His life?

Right now, there is One who judges us in the last day: the Word. You will be judged by the Word He has spoken. Jesus said, "I judge no man" in that present sense.

That is why people can live their lives as serial killers, Hitlers, and worse. You look at things people do to children and think, "If I were God, I would take care of them." But there is a chance for that man to be born again and not exist anymore. The old man passes away. A new creation comes into existence in his place.

If you saw Hitler in heaven, you might be mad. "He killed most of my relatives." They would check the records and say, "There was a man like that who lived. He did those things. But Jesus suffered for them, died under the penalty of them, and suffered in his place. That man does not exist anymore. A new creation has come into existence in his place."

That is why, right now, you can swell your chest and use big, self-exalted words of your own opinion, even doctrinally. But at the great white throne, when the books are opened and He checks for your name, it will be His turn. Everybody gets an audience. You already had your time. You can say whatever you want now, interpret the Word any way you want, teach any way you want, and call yourself a great authority while nothing happens to you.

But in that day there will be absolute judgment and justification. If you will pardon the expertise of my use of English, it will be His turn. You will listen. Everyone He sends back to hell will know why. They will know the moment of their visitation and the form of salvation that reached them. Nobody will be sent back crying, "You are unjust."

He is not that foolish.

Hallelujah.

Where did that come from? Beats me.

I ask Him hard questions, and some of them I have suspended in His presence for a long time in order to arrive at a place of faith where I can take the blame off Him.

When my final exam came from heaven, He said, "Is there any religion in you, son?"

I said, "I do not think so."

He said, "Anything in you that says, 'I can, but I will not'?"

Religion always says, "God can do anything. He just will not do it." But life says, "I can and I will, because it has been paid for."

He said, "I am not talking about your mouth. I am talking about your heart. Is there any religion left in you?"

There are some things I suspended in His presence for some time. One is Romans 1 and 2, where Paul talks about the state of man who did not want to retain God in his knowledge, the pre-flood condition, and the general spiritually dead state of man now. He is talking about salvation in each of those generations and what God afforded the human race.

Shem lived one hundred years before the flood and five hundred years after. Shem, Noah's son, outlived Abraham by twenty-five years. Abraham was born about three hundred years after the flood. Shem lived longer than Abraham on this planet.

If you want to talk about reaching other places, they had ark-building technology because Shem helped build it. Even without that, the tribes of Shem, Ham, and Japheth took the simple commandments of God and messed them up.

God gave simple commands: everything that moves will be food for you, but when you take an animal and eat it, pour the blood out because the life is in the blood and it is for atonement. Then He said not to murder people, or you would end up being killed.

Just a couple of simple commandments, and the tribes overspread the whole world. They made it to isolated islands. Historians talk about closed land masses, but in the first five hundred years they had boat-building technology.

When you get an island of people isolated from anybody, once I settled who God was and what He would do, I knew He was able to communicate even in impossible areas. I did not stand back and mock any part of who He is.

You cannot put a ceiling where there are no walls. You cannot put walls where there is no foundation. Line upon line, precept upon precept, I found out who He was and believed it. It opened a whole new realm.

We think it was a special miracle performed for Philip when he was found at Azotus after baptizing the eunuch in water. There he was, standing in another city, and he went on up the coast causing revival. Quite an evangelist.

There was no difficulty factor between where the eunuch was intercepted and the town on the coast. There were no Alps or Everest ridges that required special climbing tools. There was nothing that would seem to require flight, yet this man had a boarding pass for Trinity Airlines.

He did not fly just because it happened to be convenient that day. God set a standard.

We also classify the clothing that left Paul's body as special miracles. It is embarrassing to be in international ministry and have some man walking around with more power in his clothes than your whole international ministry. They send a cloth to somebody in your meeting and they get healed on the cloth, while all they do in your line is fall down.

So we like to explain it away: "They had a healing ministry."

I am glad you said that. So God decided to leave people sick in your meetings? When someone has a healing ministry and travels to the nations, their whole life introduces God to people through miracles. But that does not mean when the pastor stands up, the sick are supposed to stay sick year after year.

Why did Philip fly? God set a standard. Why did Paul's cloths work? God set a standard. It was not that Paul was special as a person. God is no respecter of persons.

I got to wondering about those islands where people had shipbuilding technology at one time, then became so heathenistic that they turned over to a reprobate mind, killing each other with voodoo and witchcraft and selling out to devils. They found themselves isolated, nobody reaching them, generation after generation.

How will they stand before God? First of all, the fathers failed them. Every father should have taught severely the instructions Noah received and passed them on.

Even in that, when a child born in innocence reaches accountability, there is a transition where they either accept the god of their fathers or look up toward heaven and want to know truth. I can remember wondering, "Where is God?" What people were saying to me did not make much difference. Inside of me, I wanted to know.

There is nobody who lifts his face toward heaven, looks at creation, and wants to know truth, who will not have God get to him in his lifetime and let him know.

I would like to blame somebody for my walk. "I would have done more if I had more money." Why do you think He did the special miracles He did as Head of the Church? When He multiplied the fishes and loaves, He let you know you cannot blame anybody else for what you do not do for Him.

The love of money is the root of all evil. Jesus called it the mammon of unrighteousness. If you are not faithful in that which is least, in the mammon of unrighteousness, who will commit to your trust the true riches?

I used to think selling out to money meant selling out to the almighty dollar and trampling anybody who gets in your way. It generally does. With feigned words, false ministers make merchandise of people. Paul told Timothy that those who will be rich pierce themselves through with many sorrows, supposing gain is godliness.

But I found out another way to serve mammon: when you do not have enough to finish what God told you to do, and money becomes the god that tells you you cannot do it.

"I told you to win old Mexico."

"Well, God, the money never came in."

Do you think that will work? If money kept you from doing it, then it gave you your orders. It played God and told you what you would and would not do.

I am talking about finding out what He has called you to do and standing there until you do it.

It was not until a man was filled with the Holy Spirit that he found out about the gifts. It was not until the Holy Spirit established Himself as Teacher that he began to really find out about the Father. The more you know Him, the more free He is to open you up to more things about Him.

Impossible becomes possible. Cowardice starts standing.

We know a lot about the power twins of fasting and prayer. Matthew 17 says, "This kind goeth not out but by prayer and fasting." We knew about prayer, but we did not know what fasting had to do with the devil coming out.

We labeled them the power twins, fasting and prayer, and they are. We know what prayer has to do with unbelief, because prayer is a form of fellowship. You get to know God better and better. But I could not figure out what going without food had to do with unbelief. If faith comes by hearing, and hearing by the Word of God, then give me a hamburger.

We found out that in the positional truths, my spirit man has been seated in heavenly places in Christ Jesus. Romans says if Christ be in you, the body is dead because of sin, but the spirit is life because of righteousness.

My spirit is seated with Him. My body, positionally, has been declared dead as far as its ability to reign over my spirit. Many times, there is a ceiling of flesh above you, and you bounce off it, never operating above it. Year after year, your walk is the same. You may have better programs and increased knowledge, but the walk is the same.

When the flesh is the ceiling over where you are walking in the spirit, fasting executes the position your flesh has been declared to have, which is dead. You pray in the Holy Ghost and build yourself up, while fasting drives the flesh into its declared position. Finally your spirit rises over the ceiling of your flesh, and suddenly unanswered prayers start coming to pass. You think you moved God. You did not. You lined up with something He already did.

Fasting executes the position your flesh has been declared to have. It does not do Buddha any good to fast because there is no position to execute. But not you. You are executing a position. If the flesh is reigning over your spirit, you can destroy that and have as much of God as you want.

That is why Jesus said if you learn to deal with this kind of unbelief, you could say to the mountain, "Be removed and cast into the sea," and it would obey you. Nothing shall be impossible to you.

As surely as prayer and fasting are power twins, when He opened worship to us, it was to get acquainted with the Father and take the next step into the kind of healing we needed to receive the rest.

Jesus said:

> The Father seeketh such to worship him in spirit and in truth.

We found out that "in spirit" is a positional truth. The Old Testament saints could not give Him what you can because you have been made a partaker of His divine nature. You can worship and have the kind of fellowship with Him that He longed for through four thousand years.

When He said the Father seeketh such to worship Him, He was not hopping from church to church looking for a good worship team. He was waiting for the first Man to appear on this planet with a new nature, Jesus, who could finally give Him the fellowship He wanted. You have been quickened together with Him.

He always says to worship Him in spirit and in truth. My first big hint to what truth meant came in the prayer closet when He whispered to me, "You know how prayer and fasting are power twins? In spirit and in truth, as far as worship is concerned, are power twins too. They fit together and are as powerful as prayer and fasting."

"In spirit" means I can give Him worship and fellowship out of the core of my being, the new nature that has made me His son.

"In truth" means something else. The Old Testament saints were spiritually dead, in the same state as the man in Romans 7. Paul said he was dead to that wherein he was held. The law held the man's sin nature. When the old man passed away and a new man came into its place, he could now serve God in newness of spirit.

How can a man with a death nature know truth? God gave ten simple commandments, and the spiritually dead man could not keep them. He did not even know covetousness was wrong until the law said, "Thou shalt not covet." When he tried to stop, he discovered that sin was his nature.

Now, because you have a new nature, you have the capacity to discern and understand the spiritual attributes of God. You have the capacity to discern and understand truth. Because you can understand truth the Old Testament saint could not understand, you can worship Him out of that truth, which is who He is.

When you find out He is the One who said that mountain can be removed, you can worship Him in the truth of what He said until you come to a place where you can believe it.

To worship Him in truth is to be put within range of what He said. He said He can raise the dead. He said He can move the mountain. He said He can pour His glory out like the waters cover the sea. That is truth.

The Old Testament saint could not understand that the way you can. God said, "They shall not teach every man to know God through concepts, precepts, and laws; all shall know Me from the least to the greatest, because I have written My laws in your nature."

Did He tell you the mountain could be removed? Did He tell you a city could be saved? Did He tell you the dead could be raised and the lame and maimed made whole?

Yes, He did.

Then you can worship Him in the truth of it, and you will come to the place where you did not believe it until you do believe it. The new nature has put you within reach of it, and a pure vein of worship will cause you to believe it.

The difference between Solomon and you is this: Solomon came to the fullness under the shadow. He built God a house. He was wiser than any man who ever lived. Jesus used him as an example of prospering from wisdom. But as wise as he was, he fell to what he was. Jesus said, "A greater than Solomon is here."

Solomon's wisdom under the shadow was a type of what your new nature is to your spirit. He brought them up to the lip of the rebirth, but only the greater One could bring you in.

To worship Him in truth means that all He is and all He said is within your range, because as a new creation you have been equipped with that possibility.

In spirit and in truth are power twins. They are fellowship twins. One is rapport and fellowship with Him. The other, truth, causes you to become the revelation you are starting to understand. You cannot suspend yourself in His presence, worshiping Him in the truth of what He said He is and what He can do, without ending up doing those things.

Let's look at Romans 5 and take this a little further as we approach "in truth."

Romans 5:1-2 says:

> Therefore being justified by faith, we have peace with God through our Lord Jesus Christ:
>
> By whom also we have access by faith into this grace wherein we stand, and rejoice in hope of the glory of God.

Then verse 3 says:

> And not only so, but we glory in tribulations also: knowing that tribulation worketh patience.

We worship God, enjoy His presence, and magnify Him in the middle of tribulation because we know what happens when worship is present. Tribulation goes out and employs patience. Tribulation without rejoicing, thanksgiving, and worship is not the kind of patience God is talking about.

When you worship Him in the middle of tribulation, it hires the God kind of patience. Patience then employs experience. Now you are going to school in this thing. God is showing up, and you are having a wonderful time because patience is working experience.

Then experience employs hope. Now faith is the substance of the thing I am hoping for and the evidence of what I cannot see. Because of how you began this tribulation, you are about to come out of it.

Verse 5 says:

> And hope maketh not ashamed; because the love of God is shed abroad in our hearts by the Holy Ghost which is given unto us.

You started at one end of the tribulation with worship, and it worked its way to hope, the kind of hope faith can put substance to. Then the love of God is shed abroad in your heart by the Holy Spirit.

The Holy Ghost uses your new nature. The way you are victorious in life is when the Holy Ghost comes and begins to magnify what set you free, which is your new nature. That new nature begins to move forward and destroys the dark areas of your life.

The Holy Spirit enforces your freedom by magnifying who He has made you: a new creation.

This person will not murmur or complain. He puts his hands up and says, "He is Lord in the middle of this thing. I will worship Him and glorify Him. He loved me so much that He judged Jesus for this tribulation."

Tribulation hires patience. Patience employs experience. Experience gets hope. The Holy Ghost says, "Good. I have been waiting for you to get here," and sheds the love of God abroad in your heart.

The love of God is shed abroad in our hearts by the Holy Spirit. What part of us does He use? Our hearts, our new nature, the part we love Him with. The part of you that has unlimited capacity to hold the love and compassion of God is the reservoir of your spirit called your new nature.

Paul then uses an opposite to illustrate God's willingness. Jesus did the same thing when He said, "If you, being evil, know how to give good gifts to your children, how much more shall your Father give good things to them that ask Him?"

Romans 5:6-8 says:

> For when we were yet without strength, in due time Christ died for the ungodly.
>
> For scarcely for a righteous man will one die: yet peradventure for a good man some would even dare to die.
>
> But God commendeth his love toward us, in that, while we were yet sinners, Christ died for us.

Then verse 9 says:

> Much more then, being now justified by his blood, we shall be saved from wrath through him.

And verse 10:

> For if, when we were enemies, we were reconciled to God by the death of his Son,
>
> much more, being reconciled, we shall be saved by his life.

When you were His enemy, He did the whole work and reconciled you by the death of His Son. Now that you have accepted Him, how much more shall you be saved by His life?

I am not going to insult Him by sitting around moaning about something the devil is doing to me that Jesus has already been judged for. It will be a cold day in hell before I work the other end of my victory with complaining.

Romans 5 goes on:

> Wherefore, as by one man sin entered into the world, and death by sin;
>
> and so death passed upon all men, for that all have sinned.

The whole human race inherited spiritual death from Adam. Until the law, sin was in the world, but sin is not imputed where there is no law. Nevertheless, death reigned from Adam to Moses, even over those who had not sinned after the similitude of Adam's transgression. They did not cause their own fall. They were born spiritually dead.

Adam is the figure of Him who was to come.

Romans 5:15 says:

> But not as the offense, so also is the free gift.

What about the free gift is not like the fall?

> For if through the offense of one many be dead,
>
> much more the grace of God, and the gift by grace, which is by one man, Jesus Christ, hath abounded unto many.

Then verse 16 says:

> And not as it was by one that sinned, so is the gift:
>
> for the judgment was by one to condemnation,
>
> but the free gift is of many offenses unto justification.

The judgment was by one offense to condemnation. But the free gift, through the second Adam, is of many offenses unto justification.

We all inherited death because Adam sinned. But that death caused billions and billions of offenses. This is what made Jesus different from the first man Adam: He gathered up those billions of offenses, and the judgment was laid on Him for every single one so you could go free.

When you were yet His enemy, He paid the full price for you. If He did that while you were lost, now that you have accepted Him, how much more will He save you by His life?

I am not going to insult Him by complaining about something Jesus has already been judged for.

I think my time is up.
