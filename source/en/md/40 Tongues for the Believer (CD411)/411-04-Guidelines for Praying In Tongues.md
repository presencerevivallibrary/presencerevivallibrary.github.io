I stopped for a minute and listened, and I could actually hear those words of tongues being created, coming into existence, and rising to my mind. I could hear them.

That is when He spoke to me and said, "Welcome to My voice."

The same place where He creates the words of tongues is also where He capsules a revelation, like downloading a whole revelation into a computer chip: a mystery on healing, a mystery on righteousness, a mystery. When He is done downloading that language, He sends it across to your spirit and releases the whole revelation at once.

Suddenly you understand a whole revelation in a millisecond. A moment ago you did not understand it. Suddenly the whole thing dawns upon you. Sometimes it takes me a month to preach what I received in one millisecond.

When you are praying in tongues, He creates those tongues in that same place. Most of the time you do not hear them because the moment they are created, they seize hold of your tongue and your tongue forms the linguistics for the word created in your spirit.

If it comes out your mouth, it does not come up to your mind. It is diverted through a gate that takes it right out. If you shut your mouth, it comes up to your mind. It will not come up very long, because what activates it is your tongue letting it out that gate. It is like a siphon. It will keep one sentence after another going. But if you shut your mouth, eventually it will stop.

Not only will it stop, but you will have to force yourself to keep trying to pray silently in the Spirit, because it will float up to your mind. If you are forcing yourself to do that with your mind, then your mind cannot do anything else. Those words of tongues will occupy your mind if you do not let the tongues out the gate.

That same place where He speaks tongues in my spirit is where I hear Him. If I stop, it feels like it comes from down here somewhere, but it actually comes forward. I hear the tongues.

I have isolated that place through I do not know how many hours of prayer. I have isolated the ear of the spirit, if you will allow me to say it that way. He can stop with the tongues and start speaking English, or God can stop me and my mind quiets down. When my physical mind opens up to the Spirit, I know He is ready to talk to me.

I know it is Him because I have become familiar with that place from hundreds of hours of tongues coming from the same place. I know where that voice comes from, and I know the difference between that voice and the mental energy of my own thinking.

There is a question that arises: Is praying in tongues silently effective? I mean just sitting there praying inside, not moving your tongue, not using anything outward.

Even though there is no ironclad Scripture against it, all the other Scriptures that mention praying in tongues or speaking with tongues connect it with utterance. I have found that when I lock my tongue up, it stops. The tongue seems to finish the sequence.

It does not matter when I am praying quietly. After I just about exhausted my vocal cords, I decided to give that a try. Besides, I got tired of people turning me in at motel rooms and beating on the walls. So I started praying quietly, without using my vocal cords.

But it still finished the circuit. The tongue connected to that language was able to deliver the sequence, and when it did, the next phrase came. If I stopped it, I had to concentrate with mental energy. Faith and the Holy Spirit activate the language in me, but if it is not allowed to finish the sequence because the tongue is connected to it, it generally stops.

I have to exert a lot of mental energy to try to keep it going. Therefore, I cannot even use my mind for anything else because it floats up into my mind and occupies it.

So no, I do not think trying to pray with tongues in your mind and heart without finishing the sequence is effective. That is why He connected utterance with it.

The old thing that passed away was the old covenant. Henceforth we know no man after the flesh. That means there is no more knowing a man through his flesh, his bloodline, or his heritage. Jew, Greek, Scythian, it does not matter. He has abolished the enmity, the partition, and made of all nations one new man in the new creation of Christ Jesus.

You will not pick those things up until you have thoroughly deposited the book of Ephesians. Then when you are over there, you go, "Where have I heard this before?"

Stand up, and let us worship Him in another song.
